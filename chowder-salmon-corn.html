Salmon Corn Chowder

NOTES: This chowder is designed as a full meal in a bowl with lean protein, smart carbs, and 1–2 cups of vegetables per person. Use low-sodium broth and ingredients, avoid added sugars, and keep portions aligned with your goals. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total pot); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 12 oz salmon fillet, cooked and flaked into chunks
  • 2 cups low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup 2% milk or unsweetened almond milk
  • 1/2 cup plain 2% Greek yogurt
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 2 stalks celery, diced
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and diced small
  • 1 cup frozen corn kernels, no salt added
  • 1/2 tsp dried dill
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Cook salmon in advance if needed: bake at 400°F (205°C) for 12–15 minutes until it flakes easily, then cool and flake into chunks.
  2. Dice onion, celery, and potatoes into small, even pieces.
  3. Heat olive oil in a medium soup pot over medium heat for 1–2 minutes.
  4. Add onion and celery. Cook 4–5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened and translucent.
  5. Stir in diced potatoes and corn. Add broth, dill, garlic powder, and black pepper. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low.
  6. Cover and simmer 12–15 minutes, until potatoes are tender when pierced with a fork.
  7. Stir in milk and Greek yogurt, whisking gently to fully combine. Add flaked salmon and cook 3–4 minutes on low heat until chowder is hot but not boiling.
  8. Taste and adjust seasoning with additional pepper if desired. Ladle into three bowls, giving Frank the largest portion of salmon and potatoes.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~590 kcal, ~45 g protein, ~55 g carbs, ~18 g fat.
  • Susan: ~445 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~41 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
  • Elise: ~445 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~41 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
