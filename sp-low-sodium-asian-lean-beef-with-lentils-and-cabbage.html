Low Sodium Asian Lean Beef with Lentils and Cabbage

Servings

3 plates (Frank 1.0×, Susan 0.75×, Elise 0.75×).

Times

  • Prep time: 18 minutes
  • Cook time: 35 minutes
  • Total time: 53 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2.0 lb lean beef steak or roast (top sirloin or top round), trimmed
  • 1.0 cup dry lentils
  • 1 head green cabbage
  • 9 tsp olive oil
  • 6 tsp minced garlic
  • 4 tsp minced ginger
  • 2 tsp black pepper
  • 2 tsp salt substitute
  • 6 tsp low-sodium soy sauce
  • 2 tsp dried parsley

Steps

  1. Lentils: Rinse 1 cup dry lentils. Simmer in water 18–22 minutes until tender; drain and keep warm.
  2. Cabbage: Slice into ribbons. Sauté with 4 tsp olive oil for 8–10 minutes until tender with browned edges; finish with pepper.
  3. Lean beef: Slice into strips. Toss with 3 tsp olive oil + remaining minced garlic + spices. Roast at 450°F for 10–12 minutes on a preheated sheet pan, stirring once, until browned.
  4. Finish: Add 6 tsp low-sodium soy sauce with 1/2 cup water, reduce 2–3 minutes until glossy, then stir in parsley.
  5. Plate portions per Notes.

Notes

Portions (per person):

Frank – 1 plate (8 oz cooked lean beef, 3/4 cup cooked lentils, 1 1/2 cups vegetables).

Susan – 1 plate (6 oz cooked lean beef, 3/4 of Frank’s starch portion, 1 1/4 cups vegetables).

Elise – 1 plate (6 oz cooked lean beef, 3/4 of Frank’s starch portion, 1 1/4 cups vegetables).

Tips (recipe-specific):

Slice beef across the grain for tenderness without needing fatty cuts.

Brown-first rule: don’t crowd the pan; browning is flavor you don’t have to salt.

Storage + Reheat

  • Cool promptly; store in airtight containers in the fridge up to 3–4 days (typical safety guidance).
  • Reheat protein + vegetables in the air fryer at 350°F for 4–6 minutes for edge-crisp; microwave grains/beans 60–90 seconds with a splash of water.
