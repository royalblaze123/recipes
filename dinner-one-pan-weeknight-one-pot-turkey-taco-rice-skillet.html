One-Pot Turkey Taco Rice Skillet

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 650 kcal • P 58g • C 65g • F 14g • Sat 4g • Na 700mg • Fiber 12g
Susan/Elise (each): 488 kcal • P 44g • C 49g • F 10g • Sat 3g • Na 525mg • Fiber 9g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.75 lb 93% lean ground turkey
  • 1 cup dry brown rice
  • 1 can black beans
  • 1 jar salsa
  • 1 bag frozen fajita-style pepper blend
  • low-sodium chicken broth
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • garlic powder
  • onion powder
  • smoked paprika
  • Italian seasoning
  • ground cumin
  • chili powder
  • red pepper flakes
  • extra-virgin olive oil
  • low-sodium chicken broth

Steps

  1. Rice base: in a deep skillet, sauté 1 bag frozen fajita pepper blend with 2 tsp olive oil until water evaporates (6–8 minutes).
  2. Turkey: add ground turkey with 1 tsp chili powder, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp garlic powder, 1/2 tsp onion powder, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper. Brown 7–9 minutes.
  3. Add rice: stir in 1 cup dry brown rice.
  4. Liquid: add 2 1/4 cups low-sodium chicken broth plus 1/2 cup salsa. Bring to a boil.
  5. Simmer: cover and simmer 35–40 minutes until rice is tender. Rest 5 minutes off heat.
  6. Beans: stir in 1 1/2 cups drained/rinsed black beans (from a 15 oz can). Heat 2 minutes.
  7. Serve per Notes.

Notes

Taco-night comfort in one skillet, built on brown rice, peppers, and salsa for moisture. Portions (per person): Frank: About 2 1/2 cups (heaping bowl) Susan: About 2 cups (bowl) Elise: About 2 cups (bowl) Tips (recipe-specific): Evaporate pepper water first so the skillet doesn’t turn soupy. Stir beans in at the end so they stay intact.
