Air Fryer Spiced Cod with Potatoes and Asparagus

Servings

3 plates (Frank 1.0×, Susan 0.75×, Elise 0.75×).

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 25 minutes
  • Total time: 45 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lb cod fillets
  • 1.25 lb baby potatoes
  • 1 bag frozen asparagus spears
  • 1/2 bag frozen bell pepper strips
  • 7 tsp olive oil
  • 2 tsp bottled lemon juice
  • 2 tsp minced garlic
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 2 tsp salt substitute
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp black pepper

Recipe

  1. Preheat the air fryer to 390°F for 3–5 minutes with the empty basket in place so it is hot when you add the food.
  2. Scrub 1.25 lb baby potatoes and cut them into 3/4-inch chunks. Add the potatoes to a large bowl and toss with 3 tsp olive oil, 1 tsp garlic powder, and 1 tsp salt substitute until all pieces are evenly coated.
  3. Spread the seasoned potatoes in a single layer in the air fryer basket. Air fry at 390°F for 12–14 minutes, shaking the basket halfway through, until the edges are browned and the potatoes are just fork-tender. Transfer potatoes to a warm bowl and cover loosely with foil.
  4. While the potatoes cook, microwave 1 bag frozen asparagus spears and 1/2 bag frozen bell pepper strips together in a covered microwave-safe dish until just tender-crisp, following package timing as a guide. Drain off any excess liquid and set the vegetables aside for serving.
  5. Rinse 1.5 lb cod fillets under cold water and pat them very dry with paper towels. Cut the cod into 3 similar portions so each person gets one piece and all pieces are similar in thickness.
  6. In a small bowl, whisk together 4 tsp olive oil, 2 tsp bottled lemon juice, 2 tsp minced garlic, 2 tsp smoked paprika, 1 tsp onion powder, 1 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp dried oregano, 1 tsp salt substitute, and 1 tsp black pepper to make a thick spice rub.
  7. Place the dried cod portions on a plate or tray. Spoon the spice rub over all sides of the fish and gently press it in so it forms a light coating without breaking the fillets. Let the cod rest in the seasoning for about 5 minutes while you prepare the basket.
  8. Return the warm potatoes to one side of the air fryer basket in a loose single layer. Arrange the seasoned cod portions on the other side of the basket, leaving a little space between fillets for air circulation. If your basket is small, cook the cod in two batches and keep finished pieces warm under foil.
  9. Air fry cod and potatoes together at 390°F for 8–10 minutes, depending on thickness, turning the potatoes once halfway through. The cod is done when the thickest piece flakes easily with a fork and reaches 130–135°F in the center. Do not overcook or the fish will dry out.
  10. Right before serving, quickly rewarm the asparagus and peppers in the microwave for 30–60 seconds if needed. Build plates: Frank gets about 8 oz cooked cod, 1 cup roasted potatoes, and 1 1/4 cups mixed asparagus and peppers; Susan and Elise each get about 5.5 oz cooked cod, 3/4 cup potatoes, and 1 cup mixed vegetables.

Notes

Portions (per person):

Frank – 1 plate (about 8 oz cooked cod, 1 cup roasted baby potatoes, 1 1/4 cups mixed asparagus and peppers).

Susan – 1 plate (about 5.5 oz cooked cod, 3/4 cup roasted baby potatoes, 1 cup mixed asparagus and peppers).

Elise – 1 plate (about 5.5 oz cooked cod, 3/4 cup roasted baby potatoes, 1 cup mixed asparagus and peppers).

Tips (recipe-specific):

Pat the cod very dry and press the spice rub in gently so it forms a light crust instead of sliding off.

Check the thickest piece of cod at the earlier end of the cook time and pull it as soon as it flakes easily so it stays moist.
