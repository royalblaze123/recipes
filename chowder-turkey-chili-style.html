Turkey Chili-Style Chowder

NOTES: This chowder is designed as a full meal in a bowl with lean protein, smart carbs, and 1–2 cups of vegetables per person. Use low-sodium broth and ingredients, avoid added sugars, and keep portions aligned with your goals. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total pot); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 12 oz lean ground turkey (93%+ lean)
  • 1 can (15 oz) no-salt-added black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and diced small
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 3 cups low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth
  • 3/4 cup 2% milk or unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp mild chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a soup pot over medium heat for 1–2 minutes.
  2. Add ground turkey and cook 6–7 minutes, breaking it into small pieces, until browned and cooked through.
  3. Add onion and bell pepper. Cook 4–5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened.
  4. Stir in sweet potatoes, black beans, broth, chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, and black pepper.
  5. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer 15–18 minutes until sweet potatoes are tender.
  6. Stir in milk and cook on low heat 3–4 minutes until hot but not boiling.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning. Ladle into three bowls, giving Frank the largest portion of turkey and sweet potatoes.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~620 kcal, ~48 g protein, ~70 g carbs, ~16 g fat.
  • Susan: ~465 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~52 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
  • Elise: ~465 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~52 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
