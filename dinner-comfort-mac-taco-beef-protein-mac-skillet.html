Taco Beef Protein Mac (Skillet)

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 650 kcal • P 58g • C 62g • F 18g • Sat 7g • Na 700mg • Fiber 12g
Susan/Elise (each): 488 kcal • P 44g • C 46g • F 14g • Sat 5g • Na 525mg • Fiber 9g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1 box Barilla Protein+ pasta
  • 1.75 lb 93% lean ground beef
  • 1 can black beans
  • 1 jar salsa
  • 1 carton skim milk
  • 1 container reduced-fat shredded cheddar cheese
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • garlic powder
  • onion powder
  • smoked paprika
  • Italian seasoning
  • chili powder
  • ground cumin
  • crushed red pepper
  • cornstarch
  • extra-virgin olive oil
  • Dijon mustard
  • hot sauce
  • Worcestershire sauce
  • low-sodium soy sauce
  • rice vinegar
  • chili powder
  • ground cumin

Steps

  1. Pasta: cook Protein+ pasta al dente (8–10 minutes). Drain.
  2. Beef: brown 1.75 lb lean ground beef in a large pot 7–9 minutes. Season with chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, onion powder, kosher salt, black pepper.
  3. Beans: Can size note—use a standard 15 oz can black beans.. Drain/rinse. Use 1 1/2 cups beans; refrigerate remainder.
  4. Sauce base: whisk 2 tbsp cornstarch into 2 cups skim milk. Add to pot and simmer 3–5 minutes until thick.
  5. Cheese: stir in 6 oz reduced-fat cheddar until melted.
  6. Salsa: stir in 1/2 cup salsa (measured) for taco flavor.
  7. Combine: fold in pasta and beans. Rest 5 minutes to thicken.
  8. Serve per Notes.

Notes

Cheesy taco mac that still feels like a full dinner (beans help fiber). Portions (per person): Frank: About 2 1/2 cups mac (1/3 pan) + extra veg Susan: About 2 cups mac (1/4 pan) + extra veg Elise: About 2 cups mac (1/4 pan) + extra veg Tips (recipe-specific): Salsa gives flavor without needing lots of salt. Let it rest 5 minutes so it sets up like comfort mac.
