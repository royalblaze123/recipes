Lemon-Garlic Chicken and Lentil Soup with Spinach

Prep: 15 min   Cook: 45 min   Total: 60 min

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lb chicken breast
  • 1 carton low-sodium chicken broth
  • 0.5 carton low-sodium chicken broth
  • 0.5 bag dry lentils
  • 1 onion
  • 2 carrots
  • 2 celery stalks
  • 1 lemon
  • 1 bag spinach
  • olive oil
  • tomato paste
  • cumin
  • oregano
  • black pepper
  • salt substitute

RBM rules: quantities appear before items; fresh proteins use weight. Condiments/spices/seasonings are listed name-only; all measurements are in Steps. Water is only referenced in Steps.

Step-by-step Instructions

  1. Rinse lentils in a fine-mesh strainer until water runs mostly clear; set aside.
  2. Dice onion, carrots, and celery.
  3. Heat a large pot over medium-high. Add 3 tsp olive oil. Add onion, carrots, celery; cook 6–8 minutes until softened.
  4. Stir in 2 tbsp tomato paste + 2 tsp cumin + 2 tsp oregano + 1 tsp black pepper + 2 tsp salt substitute. Cook 60 seconds.
  5. Add broth: pour in 1.5 cartons broth total (48 fl oz). Add lentils. Bring to a simmer and cook 20 minutes.
  6. Add chicken: cut chicken into bite-size pieces. Add to the pot and simmer 10–12 minutes until chicken reaches 165°F and lentils are tender.
  7. Finish: stir in spinach to wilt (60–90 seconds). Add juice of 1 lemon (start with half, then add more to taste). Adjust with black pepper/salt substitute.
  8. Portion: Frank gets ~3 1/2 cups. Susan and Elise each get ~2 3/4 cups.
