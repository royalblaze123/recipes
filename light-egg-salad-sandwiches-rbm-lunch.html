Light Egg Salad Sandwiches - RBM Lunch

Egg salad boosted with egg whites and lightened with Greek yogurt plus a cucumber and carrot side.

Portions (per person):

Frank – 4 sandwich halves (2 sandwiches) + veg side

 

Susan – 3 sandwich halves (1.5 sandwiches) + veg side

 

Elise – 3 sandwich halves (1.5 sandwiches) + veg side

Ingredients

  • 10 slices thin whole wheat bread
  • 6 eggs
  • 1 carton egg whites
  • 1 container plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 bottle Dijon mustard
  • 1 bunch celery
  • 1 romaine heart
  • 1 cucumber
  • 1 lb bag baby carrots
  • 1 bottle olive oil spray

Step-by-step Instructions

  1. Hard-boil the eggs: place 6 eggs in a pot, cover with water, bring to a boil, turn off heat, cover 10–12 minutes, then chill in cold water 5 minutes. Peel when cool.
  2. Cook the egg whites: pour 1 cup egg whites into a nonstick skillet over medium heat and scramble 3–5 minutes until set. Cool slightly.
  3. Chop the peeled eggs. Finely chop the cooked egg whites.
  4. Wash celery stalks from the bunch. Finely chop enough celery to measure 1/2 cup chopped celery for the egg salad; refrigerate remaining celery.
  5. Mix dressing: stir 6 tablespoons plain Greek yogurt with 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard until smooth.
  6. Make egg salad: fold chopped eggs, chopped egg whites, and chopped celery into the dressing until evenly combined.
  7. Wash the romaine heart. Chop enough to measure about 4 cups chopped romaine for the sandwiches; refrigerate remaining romaine if any.
  8. Toast option: lightly mist 10 bread slices with olive oil spray and toast 2–4 minutes, flipping once, until lightly crisp.
  9. Assemble 5 sandwiches: divide egg salad evenly across 5 bread slices. Top with romaine and close with remaining bread slices.
  10. Cut each sandwich in half to make 10 halves. Portion: Frank gets 4 halves; Susan 3 halves; Elise 3 halves.
  11. Veg sides: slice the cucumber and measure 1 cup cucumber slices for Frank and 3/4 cup for Susan and Elise. Measure baby carrots: 1/2 cup for Frank and 1/3 cup for Susan and Elise. Pack separately.
