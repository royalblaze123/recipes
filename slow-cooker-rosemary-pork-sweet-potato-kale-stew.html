Slow Cooker Rosemary Pork Sweet Potato Kale Stew

Rosemary pork tenderloin stew with sweet potato and kale—chefy comfort with slow carbs built in (no side).

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 420 minutes
  • Total time: 440 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 2 lb pork tenderloin
  • 1 carton low sodium chicken broth
  • 2 each sweet potatoes
  • 1 each yellow onion
  • 1 bunch celery
  • 1 lb bag carrots
  • 6 cloves garlic
  • 1 bunch kale
  • 1 can cannellini beans
  • 1 bottle extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 jar dried rosemary
  • 1 jar dried thyme
  • 1 jar black pepper
  • 1 jar cornstarch
  • 1 each lemon
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Prep pork: cut pork tenderloin into 1-inch chunks.
    2. Prep veg: dice 1/2 the onion, slice 3 celery stalks, slice 3 carrots, and mince 3 garlic cloves. Peel and cube 1 sweet potato into 1-inch cubes (measure out about 3 cups; refrigerate remaining sweet potato).
    3. Slow cooker base: add onion, celery, carrots, garlic, sweet potato cubes, 1 tsp dried rosemary, 1 tsp dried thyme, and black pepper to the slow cooker.
    4. Liquid + pork: add the carton of low sodium chicken broth plus 1 1/2 cups water. Add pork chunks and stir once to distribute.
    5. Cook: cook on LOW for 6–7 hours or HIGH for 3–4 hours, until pork is tender.
    6. Beans + kale late: drain and rinse cannellini beans. Chop kale leaves (use about 4 packed cups; refrigerate remaining kale). Stir beans and kale into the stew and cook on HIGH for 20 minutes until kale is tender but still green.
    7. Thicken: whisk 1 tbsp cornstarch with 2 tbsp water. Stir slurry into the stew and cook 5 minutes to thicken lightly.
    8. Finish: squeeze in 1 tsp lemon juice and stir. Serve.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – About 3 1/2 cups stew.


      Susan – About 2 2/3 cups stew.


      Elise – About 2 2/3 cups stew.


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Add kale at the end so it stays bright and not overcooked.


      Cut sweet potato into larger cubes so it holds its shape.


      Lemon is optional—use a very small squeeze for brightness without a citrus-heavy dinner.
