Tex-Mex Turkey Tortilla Lasagna

Layered Tex-Mex lasagna built with whole-grain tortillas, lean turkey, black beans, and a mild spice blend for a comfort dish that stays A1c- and heart-friendly.

PORTION SIZES (3 servings total): Frank = 1.0× (largest square), Susan = 0.75×, Elise = 0.75×. Cut the baked lasagna into 4 equal squares, serve 2 squares to Frank and 1 square each to Susan and Elise.

Ingredients

  • 6 small whole-grain tortillas (soft, 6-inch)
  • 1 lb lean ground turkey (93% lean or better)
  • 1 small yellow onion, finely diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup no-salt-added black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 1/2 cups no-salt-added crushed tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 1/2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp mild chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp kosher salt (optional, to taste)
  • 1 cup part-skim shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 2 tbsp grated reduced-fat cheddar cheese
  • 2 tbsp fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 lime (use 2 tsp juice in the sauce and wedges for serving)

Step-by-step Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Lightly coat a 9×9-inch baking dish with nonstick spray.
  2. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the diced onion and red bell pepper and sauté 4–5 minutes until softened.
  3. Add the minced garlic and cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Add the lean ground turkey and cook 6–8 minutes, breaking it into small crumbles, until browned and cooked through.
  5. Stir in the ground cumin, smoked paprika, mild chili powder, dried oregano, black pepper, and optional salt. Toast the spices with the turkey for 1–2 minutes, stirring constantly.
  6. Pour in the crushed tomatoes and chicken broth. Stir well and simmer 8–10 minutes until slightly thickened.
  7. Fold the black beans and 2 teaspoons of fresh lime juice into the turkey mixture. Remove from the heat.
  8. Spread a thin layer of the turkey mixture on the bottom of the prepared baking dish.
  9. Place two whole-grain tortillas over the sauce, overlapping as needed to cover the surface.
  10. Add a layer of the turkey and bean mixture, then sprinkle with a small amount of shredded mozzarella.
  11. Repeat the layers (tortillas, turkey mixture, mozzarella) until all components are used, finishing with sauce and cheese on top.
  12. Sprinkle the grated reduced-fat cheddar over the final layer.
  13. Cover the dish with foil and bake for 20 minutes. Remove the foil and bake an additional 10 minutes until bubbling and lightly browned.
  14. Rest the Tex-Mex lasagna for 10–15 minutes before cutting into 4 squares.
  15. Cut into 4 equal squares. Serve 2 squares to Frank and 1 square each to Susan and Elise, garnished with chopped fresh cilantro and lime wedges.

Nutrition (per person)

Frank (largest portion): approx 540 kcal, 45 g protein, 48 g carbs, 19 g fat.
Susan (medium portion): approx 405 kcal, 34 g protein, 36 g carbs, 14 g fat.
Elise (medium portion): approx 405 kcal, 34 g protein, 36 g carbs, 14 g fat.
