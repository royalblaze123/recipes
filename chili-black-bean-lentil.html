Black Bean Lentil Chili

NOTES: Each chili is designed as a full meal with lean protein, smart carbs, and plenty of vegetables. Use low-sodium, no-sugar-added canned goods, avoid added sugars, and keep portions aligned with your goals. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total pot); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup dry brown or green lentils, rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) no-salt-added black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 carrot, peeled and diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced or 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 can (15 oz) no-salt-added crushed tomatoes
  • 2 cups low-sodium vegetable broth
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste (no sugar added)
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 1/2 tsp mild chili powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Rinse lentils under cool water and drain.
  2. Dice onion, bell pepper, and carrot into small pieces.
  3. Heat olive oil in a soup pot over medium heat for 1–2 minutes.
  4. Add onion, bell pepper, carrot, and garlic. Cook 5–6 minutes, stirring occasionally, until vegetables are softened.
  5. Stir in chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, and black pepper. Cook 1 minute to bloom spices.
  6. Add lentils, black beans, crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, and vegetable broth. Stir to combine.
  7. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer 25–30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until lentils are tender and chili is thick.
  8. Taste and adjust seasoning. Ladle into three bowls, giving Frank the largest portion. Serve with a side of steamed green beans or a small salad if desired.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~630 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~95 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
  • Susan: ~475 kcal, ~26 g protein, ~71 g carbs, ~9 g fat.
  • Elise: ~475 kcal, ~26 g protein, ~71 g carbs, ~9 g fat.
