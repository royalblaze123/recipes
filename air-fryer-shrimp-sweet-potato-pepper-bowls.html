Air Fryer Shrimp Sweet Potato Pepper Bowls

Fast shrimp bowls with roasted sweet potato and peppers, plus black beans for slow carbs and fiber—built for busy nights.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 16 minutes
  • Total time: 36 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 1 lb raw shrimp
  • 2 each sweet potatoes
  • 1 each yellow onion
  • 2 each bell peppers
  • 1 can black beans
  • 1 bottle extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 bag reduced fat shredded cheese
  • 1 container nonfat Greek yogurt
  • 1 each lime
  • 1 jar chili powder
  • 1 jar ground cumin
  • 1 jar garlic powder
  • 1 jar black pepper
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Prep (sweet potato): peel and cut into 3/4-inch cubes. Measure out about 2 1/2 cups cubes for this recipe; refrigerate remaining sweet potato. Toss with 1 tbsp olive oil, chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, and black pepper.
    2. Prep (peppers/onion): slice 1 bell pepper and 1/2 onion (store remaining). Toss with 1 tsp olive oil and black pepper.
    3. Prep (beans): drain and rinse black beans. Measure out 1 1/4 cups beans for this recipe; refrigerate remaining beans.
    4. Prep (shrimp): pat shrimp dry. Toss with 2 tsp olive oil, chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, and black pepper.
    5. Cosori setup: preheat both baskets to 390°F for 3 minutes. Put sweet potato cubes in Basket A.
    6. Cook Basket A: cook 390°F for 14–16 minutes with shake reminder ON; shake at each reminder.
    7. Cook Basket B: add peppers/onion and cook 390°F for 8 minutes, shaking once.
    8. Add shrimp: when Basket B has 5 minutes left, add shrimp on top of peppers/onion and cook until shrimp are opaque and just curled (about 4–5 minutes).
    9. Warm beans: microwave measured black beans 60–90 seconds, or add to Basket B for the final 2 minutes.
    10. Serve (40/30/30): build bowls with sweet potato, peppers/onion + shrimp, and beans. Sprinkle 3/4 oz reduced-fat cheese total across all bowls. Add a small dollop of Greek yogurt and squeeze lime.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – Shrimp + veg: the largest share. Sweet potato: about 1 1/4 cups. Beans: about 1/2 cup. Yogurt + light cheese sprinkle.


      Susan – Shrimp + veg: medium share. Sweet potato: about 1 cup. Beans: about 3/8 cup. Yogurt + light cheese sprinkle.


      Elise – Shrimp + veg: medium share. Sweet potato: about 1 cup. Beans: about 3/8 cup. Yogurt + light cheese sprinkle.


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Add shrimp for the last 4–5 minutes only so it stays juicy and not rubbery.


      Roast sweet potato cubes with shake reminders to keep edges crisp.


      If you want fewer carbs, reduce sweet potato on the plate and increase peppers/onion.
