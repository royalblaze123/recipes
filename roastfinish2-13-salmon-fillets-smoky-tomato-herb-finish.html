Roast & Finish Salmon Fillets with Cabbage and Quinoa

Servings

3 plates (Frank 1.0×, Susan 0.75×, Elise 0.75×).

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 30 minutes
  • Total time: 50 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1.75 lb salmon fillets
  • 1.0 cup dry quinoa
  • 1 head green cabbage
  • 11 tsp olive oil
  • 6 tsp minced garlic
  • 2 tsp black pepper
  • 2 tsp salt substitute
  • 3 tsp tomato paste
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 2 tsp dried parsley

Steps

  1. Quinoa: Rinse 1 cup dry quinoa. Cook with 2 cups water to yield ~3 cups cooked. Fluff and keep warm.
  2. Oven: Heat to 425°F. Line a large sheet pan with parchment (use two pans if crowded).
  3. Cabbage: Slice into wedges or thick ribbons. Toss with 4 tsp olive oil + 1 tsp pepper + 1 tsp salt substitute. Roast at 425°F for 16–20 minutes, flipping once, until browned edges.
  4. Salmon: Pat dry. Brush with 3 tsp olive oil and season with pepper + salt substitute. Roast at 425°F for 10–13 minutes (thickness dependent) to preferred doneness.
  5. Finish (fresh lift): Immediately after roasting, toss/brush the protein and vegetables with the Finish ingredients. Add black pepper last.
  6. Plate portions per Notes.

Notes

Portions (per person):

Frank – 1 plate (7 oz cooked protein, 1 cup cooked quinoa, 1 1/2 cups cabbage).

Susan – 1 plate (5 1/4 oz cooked protein, 3/4 of Frank’s starch portion, 1 1/4 cups cabbage).

Elise – 1 plate (5 1/4 oz cooked protein, 3/4 of Frank’s starch portion, 1 1/4 cups cabbage).

Tips (recipe-specific):

Roast hard with space; crowding is how gourmet becomes steamed sadness.

Finish with herbs/zest after the oven—heat dulls brightness, and brightness is the ‘restaurant’ signal.

Storage + Reheat

  • Cool promptly; store in airtight containers in the fridge up to 3–4 days (typical safety guidance).
  • Reheat on a sheet pan at 375°F for 8–10 minutes (best for roast texture), or microwave 60–90 seconds with a splash of water for moisture.
