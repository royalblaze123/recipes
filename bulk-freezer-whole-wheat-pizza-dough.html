Bulk Freezer Whole Wheat Pizza Dough

High-hydration whole-wheat pizza dough designed for thin crust, cold fermentation, and bulk freezing.

Yield

6 dough balls (each makes 1 thin 10–12 inch crust).

Times

  • Active prep time: 20 minutes
  • Active cook time: 0 minutes
  • Total active time: 20 minutes

Note: This dough is best with a cold ferment (24–48 hours) and can be frozen.

Ingredients

Dough

  • 720 g whole wheat flour
  • 540 g water
  • 12 g kosher salt
  • 4 g instant yeast
  • 30 g extra-virgin olive oil

Step-by-step Instructions

  1. Mix and rest (autolyse): In a large bowl, stir the whole wheat flour and water until no dry flour remains. Cover and rest 20 minutes.
  2. Add salt and yeast: Sprinkle in the kosher salt and instant yeast. Drizzle in the olive oil. Mix until a cohesive dough forms, then knead 6–8 minutes (by hand or mixer) until smoother and elastic.
  3. First rise: Cover and let rise at room temperature until puffy (about 60–90 minutes).
  4. Divide: Turn dough onto a lightly floured surface and divide into 6 equal pieces (about 219 g each). Shape each piece into a tight ball.
  5. Cold ferment (best flavor/texture): Lightly oil each ball, place into individual containers or zipper bags, and refrigerate 24–48 hours.
  6. Freeze for bulk storage: For freezing, place the oiled dough balls on a parchment-lined tray and freeze 2–3 hours until firm, then transfer to a freezer bag. Label and freeze up to 2 months.
  7. Thaw and use: Thaw a dough ball overnight in the refrigerator (12–18 hours). Bring to room temperature 60–90 minutes. Stretch into a thin 10–12 inch crust.
  8. Bake: Preheat oven with a stone/steel or inverted sheet pan to 500°F for 30 minutes. Top and bake 8–10 minutes until the crust is browned and the center is cooked through.

Portion Sizes

Portions (per person):
Frank: For 1 thin 10–12 inch pizza made from 1 dough ball, cut into 10 slices; eat 4 slices (40% of the crust) and pair with a big salad or roasted vegetables.

Susan: For the same pizza, eat 3 slices (30% of the crust) and pair with the same side.

Elise: For the same pizza, eat 3 slices (30% of the crust) and pair with the same side.
