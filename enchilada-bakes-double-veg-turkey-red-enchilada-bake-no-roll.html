Double-Veg Turkey Red Enchilada Bake (No-Roll)

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 640 kcal • P 60g • C 48g • F 18g
Susan/Elise (each): 480 kcal • P 45g • C 36g • F 14g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.50 lb 93% lean ground turkey
  • 1 package corn tortillas
  • 1 bag reduced-fat shredded Mexican cheese blend
  • 1 each avocado
  • 1/2 jar salsa
  • 1 can enchilada sauce
  • 12 oz fresh broccoli florets
  • 1 bag frozen fajita-style pepper blend
  • 6 tsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 tsp chili powder
  • 2 tsp cumin
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp kosher salt

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F.
  2. Broccoli: cut into bite-size florets. Toss with 2 tsp olive oil and a pinch of kosher salt. Roast at 425°F for 12–15 minutes until browned at edges.
  3. Turkey: cook ground turkey in a skillet 7–9 minutes until browned. Season with the spices and stir 60 seconds.
  4. Veg: cook frozen fajita pepper blend in the same skillet with remaining 4 tsp olive oil 6–8 minutes until browned.
  5. Combine: fold roasted broccoli into the turkey/pepper mix.
  6. Sauce: can size note—use a standard 10 oz can red enchilada sauce. Use all sauce for layering.
  7. Assemble and bake 18–22 minutes at 400°F. Rest 10 minutes.
  8. Toppings: dice avocado and serve salsa on the side. Top each serving.

Notes

Extra vegetables make this enchilada bake big and satisfying without pushing calories. Portions (per person): Frank: 1/3 of the casserole + 1/3 of the avocado + salsa topping Susan: 1/4 of the casserole + 1/4 of the avocado + salsa topping Elise: 1/4 of the casserole + 1/4 of the avocado + salsa topping
