Pork, Cabbage & Carrot Skillet Dinner

NOTES: Ground pork sautéed with cabbage and carrots in a light garlic-ginger sauce. This is a full-plate pork dinner built around lean pork tenderloin/loin or 93% lean ground pork, smart carbs, and plenty of non-starchy vegetables. Uses olive or avocado oil only, keeps sodium moderate, and avoids added sugars to support A1c, cholesterol, blood pressure, and PPPD-friendly eating. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

PORTION SIZES (per person): Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 1 pound 93% lean ground pork
  • 4 cups thinly sliced green cabbage
  • 1 cup shredded carrot
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp grated fresh ginger or 1 tsp ground ginger
  • 1 1/2 tbsp low-sodium tamari or soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar (unseasoned)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp kosher salt or to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large deep skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add onion and cook 2–3 minutes until beginning to soften.
  3. Add ground pork and cook 4–5 minutes, breaking it up, until no longer pink. Drain excess fat if needed.
  4. Stir in garlic and ginger and cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Add cabbage and carrot, tossing to combine with the pork mixture.
  6. Pour in tamari and rice vinegar, then sprinkle with pepper and salt. Cook, stirring frequently, for 6–8 minutes until cabbage is tender but not mushy and liquid has mostly cooked off.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning with a pinch of salt or extra herbs if needed, keeping overall sodium moderate.
  8. Divide the finished meal into three plates or containers: give Frank roughly 1/3 of the total volume, and split the remaining 2/3 between Susan and Elise as slightly smaller but still generous portions.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~500 kcal, ~46 g protein, ~34 g carbs, ~16 g fat.
  • Susan: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
  • Elise: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
