Air Fryer Greek Pork Souvlaki with Potatoes and Cucumber Yogurt

Greek-style pork with crispy potatoes and a cool cucumber-yogurt sauce, cooked with the Cosori dual-basket workflow.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 25 minutes
  • Cook time: 18 minutes
  • Total time: 43 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 1 lb pork tenderloin
  • 1 bag baby potatoes
  • 1 each cucumber
  • 1 each lemon
  • 1 bunch dill
  • 6 cloves garlic
  • 1 bottle extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 container nonfat Greek yogurt
  • 1 jar oregano
  • 1 jar garlic powder
  • 1 jar black pepper
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Prep potatoes: halve baby potatoes. Measure out about 3 1/4 cups potatoes for this recipe; refrigerate remaining. Toss with 1 tbsp olive oil, oregano, garlic powder, and black pepper.
    2. Prep pork: cut pork into 1-inch cubes. Toss with 1 tbsp olive oil, lemon zest, 1 tbsp lemon juice, minced garlic, oregano, and black pepper. Rest 10 minutes.
    3. Cosori setup: preheat both baskets to 400°F for 3 minutes. Put potatoes in Basket A.
    4. Basket A: cook 400°F for 16–18 minutes with shake reminder ON; shake at each reminder.
    5. Basket B (pork): cook pork cubes 400°F for 8–10 minutes, shaking once, until pork reaches 145°F. Rest 3 minutes.
    6. Cucumber yogurt: grate 1/2 cucumber and squeeze out moisture. Stir into 1/4 cup Greek yogurt with chopped dill and 1 tsp lemon juice.
    7. Serve (40/30/30): plate pork with potatoes and add cucumber yogurt on the side.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – Pork: largest share. Potatoes: about 1 1/4 cups. Cucumber yogurt on the side.


      Susan – Pork: medium share. Potatoes: about 1 cup. Cucumber yogurt on the side.


      Elise – Pork: medium share. Potatoes: about 1 cup. Cucumber yogurt on the side.


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Keep pork cubes in a single layer to brown; avoid crowding Basket B.


      Squeeze moisture from cucumber so the sauce stays thick, not watery.


      If potatoes need extra crisp, add 2 minutes at 400°F after shaking.
