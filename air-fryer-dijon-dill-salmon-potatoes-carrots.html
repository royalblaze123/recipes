Air Fryer Dijon Dill Salmon with Potatoes and Carrots

Dijon-dill salmon with roasted potatoes and carrots—simple, filling, and designed for the dual-basket air fryer workflow.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 18 minutes
  • Total time: 38 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 1 lb salmon fillet
  • 1 bag baby potatoes
  • 1 lb bag carrots
  • 1 each lemon
  • 1 bottle extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 jar Dijon mustard
  • 1 jar dried dill
  • 1 jar garlic powder
  • 1 jar black pepper
  • 1 container nonfat Greek yogurt
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Prep (potatoes): halve baby potatoes. Measure out about 2 3/4 cups potatoes for this recipe; refrigerate remaining. Toss with 1 tbsp olive oil, garlic powder, and black pepper.
    2. Prep (carrots): cut carrots into 3-inch sticks. Measure out about 2 cups carrots for this recipe; refrigerate remaining. Toss with 1 tsp olive oil and black pepper.
    3. Prep (salmon): cut salmon into 3 portions (about 5–6 oz each). Mix 1 tbsp Dijon mustard with 1 tsp lemon juice, dried dill, and black pepper. Brush over salmon.
    4. Cosori setup: preheat both baskets to 390°F for 3 minutes. Put potatoes in Basket A. Put carrots in Basket B.
    5. Sync Finish: set Basket A to 390°F for 18 minutes (shake reminder ON). Set Basket B to 390°F for 14 minutes (shake reminder ON). Press Sync Finish.
    6. Shake reminders: shake/flip potatoes and carrots when prompted.
    7. Add salmon: when Basket B has 10 minutes left, push carrots to the side and add salmon pieces skin-side down. Cook remaining time. Salmon is done at 125–130°F for medium or 140°F for well-done.
    8. Yogurt sauce: stir 1/4 cup Greek yogurt with 1 tsp lemon juice and a pinch of dill.
    9. Serve (40/30/30): plate salmon with potatoes and carrots and add a small yogurt drizzle.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – Salmon: largest piece. Potatoes: about 1 1/4 cups. Carrots: about 1 cup. Yogurt drizzle.


      Susan – Salmon: medium piece. Potatoes: about 1 cup. Carrots: about 3/4 cup. Yogurt drizzle.


      Elise – Salmon: medium piece. Potatoes: about 1 cup. Carrots: about 3/4 cup. Yogurt drizzle.


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Add salmon partway through so it doesn’t overcook while vegetables finish.


      If salmon finishes early, pull it and keep warm under foil while potatoes finish.


      For less dryness, cook salmon to 125–130°F and rest 2 minutes before serving.
