Peach Ginger Iced Tea Lite

NOTES: Cool, refreshing drink built for hydration and cardiometabolic goals—no added sugar, no soda, and only small amounts of fruit for flavor. Sparkling water is always plain and unsweetened. Any pinch of salt is kept minimal to keep sodium reasonable.

PORTION SIZES (serves 3 glasses): Prepare about 4 cups total, then divide into three servings. Frank receives the largest glass (≈1/3 of the batch); Susan and Elise receive slightly smaller glasses (≈1/4 of the batch each). Frank = 1.0×; Susan = 0.75×; Elise = 0.75× portions.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups brewed and cooled green tea or decaf green tea
  • 2 cups cold still water
  • 1 small ripe peach, sliced (or 1/2 cup frozen slices)
  • 3–4 thin slices fresh ginger
  • Ice cubes as needed

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. In a large pitcher, combine 2 cups brewed and cooled green tea or decaf green tea, 2 cups cold still water, 1 small ripe peach, sliced (or 1/2 cup frozen slices), 3–4 thin slices fresh ginger, and Ice cubes as needed.
  2. Stir gently so all of the fruit, herbs, and any citrus slices are submerged, then refrigerate for at least 5–10 minutes so the flavors begin to infuse.
  3. For a stronger flavor, infuse up to 1–2 hours, then taste and remove any citrus slices if they start to taste bitter. Stir again before serving.
  4. Divide the drink into three glasses or bottles, giving Frank the largest portion and splitting the remaining amount between Susan and Elise. Keep any leftovers refrigerated and discard fruit after 24 hours for best quality.

Nutrition (approximate per person)

Frank (largest glass): ~5–25 kcal, 0 g protein, 1–6 g carbs, 0 g fat
Susan (medium glass): ~5–20 kcal, 0 g protein, 1–5 g carbs, 0 g fat
Elise (medium glass): ~5–20 kcal, 0 g protein, 1–5 g carbs, 0 g fat
