Buffalo Chicken Jalapeño Poppers

Air Fryer or Sheet Pan • Yield: 24 popper halves

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 12 each fresh jalapeños
  • 16 oz fresh chicken breast
  • 6 oz reduced-fat cream cheese
  • 4 oz part-skim shredded mozzarella
  • hot sauce
  • garlic powder
  • smoked paprika
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper

Steps

  1. Preheat air fryer to 370°F (or oven to 425°F).
  2. Cook chicken: season 16 oz chicken with 1 tsp kosher salt, 1 tsp garlic powder, 1/2 tsp black pepper; air-fry 370°F for 10–12 min (or bake 425°F for 18–22 min) until 165°F. Chop small.
  3. Halve 12 jalapeños lengthwise to make 24 halves; remove seeds/ribs; pat dry.
  4. Mix filling: soften 6 oz cream cheese; stir in 6 tbsp hot sauce (18 tsp) + 1 tsp smoked paprika; fold in chopped chicken and 4 oz mozzarella.
  5. Stuff jalapeño halves; keep filling level (not mounded).
  6. Air-fry 370°F for 7–9 min (or bake 425°F for 12–15 min) until peppers are tender-crisp and filling is hot.

Notes

Flavor-forward holiday poppers designed for snacking or a light meal. Portions (guidance): Snack portion: 4–6 halves per person. If you want less heat, blanch jalapeño halves in simmering water 2 minutes, then dry before stuffing.
