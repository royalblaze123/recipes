Simple Turkey Vegetable Soup (Stovetop, Baseline)

NOTES: Designed as a complete meal soup using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled starch. Use low-sodium broth and avoid extra salt when possible to support blood pressure and kidney health. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~2 cups); Susan: medium portion (~1.5 cups); Elise: medium portion (~1.5 cups).

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 lb 93% lean ground turkey
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 4 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried parsley
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup frozen peas and carrots mix (no added salt)
  • 2 cups chopped baby spinach

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large soup pot over medium heat.
  2. Add ground turkey and cook 6–8 minutes, breaking into crumbles, until no pink remains. Transfer turkey to a plate and set aside.
  3. In the same pot, add diced onion, carrots, and celery and cook 5–6 minutes until vegetables begin to soften.
  4. Stir in minced garlic and diced red bell pepper and cook 2–3 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Pour in low-sodium chicken broth and water and stir.
  6. Stir in dried thyme, dried parsley, smoked paprika, salt, and black pepper.
  7. Return cooked turkey to the pot and stir to combine.
  8. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer 20–25 minutes, stirring occasionally, until vegetables are tender.
  9. Stir in frozen peas and carrots mix and chopped baby spinach and cook 3–5 minutes until peas are heated through and spinach is wilted.
  10. Taste and adjust seasoning with a small pinch more salt or pepper if needed.
  11. Remove from heat and let soup rest a few minutes before ladling into bowls for Frank, Susan, and Elise.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~430 kcal, ~40 g protein, ~32 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
  • Susan: ~320 kcal, ~30 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~10 g fat.
  • Elise: ~320 kcal, ~30 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~10 g fat.
