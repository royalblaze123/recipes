Canned Tuna Patties with Sweet Potato Wedges and Cabbage Slaw

Prep: 20 min   Cook: 24 min   Total: 44 min

Ingredients

  • 2 cans tuna in water
  • 1 egg
  • 0.25 bag whole wheat panko crumbs
  • 1.25 lb sweet potatoes
  • 0.5 bag shredded cabbage
  • 1 lime
  • olive oil
  • Dijon mustard
  • garlic powder
  • black pepper
  • salt substitute
  • smoked paprika
  • vinegar

Hard rules enforced: Dinner validations (calories 600–650 Frank, sat fat ≤8g, sodium ≤700mg, fiber ≥10g, added sugar ≤6g). Quantities appear before items; fresh meat/poultry/fish use weights. Condiments/spices/seasonings have no measurements in Ingredients; all measurements are in Steps. No olives, no squash, no Brussels sprouts, no zucchini, no tofu/tempeh, no yogurt in dinners.

Step-by-step Instructions

  1. Sweet potatoes: cut 1.25 lb sweet potatoes into wedges. Toss with 2 tsp olive oil + 1 tsp smoked paprika + 1/2 tsp garlic powder + 1/4 tsp black pepper + 1/2 tsp salt substitute.
  2. Bake wedges at 425°F for 22–26 minutes, flipping at 12 minutes.
  3. Tuna mix: drain 2 cans tuna well. Mix with 1 egg + 1/3 cup panko + 2 tsp Dijon + 1/4 tsp black pepper + 1/4 tsp salt substitute.
  4. Form 6 patties. Pan-sear in a nonstick skillet with 1 tsp olive oil 3–4 minutes per side until browned and hot.
  5. Slaw: toss 1/2 bag shredded cabbage with 2 tsp lime juice + 1 tsp vinegar + 1 tsp olive oil + 1/4 tsp black pepper + 1/4 tsp salt substitute.
  6. Serve: 2 patties per person with wedges and slaw. Portion: Frank gets the largest wedge portion; Susan/Elise ~75%.

Notes

Drain tuna aggressively; wet tuna makes patties fall apart.
