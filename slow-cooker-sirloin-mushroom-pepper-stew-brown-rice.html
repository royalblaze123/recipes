Slow Cooker Sirloin Mushroom Pepper Stew over Brown Rice

Lean sirloin stew with mushrooms and peppers served over brown rice for a structured, hearty slow-cooker dinner.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 25 minutes
  • Cook time: 420 minutes
  • Total time: 445 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 2 lb sirloin steak
  • 1 package mushrooms
  • 2 each bell peppers
  • 1 each yellow onion
  • 1 carton low sodium beef broth
  • 1 bottle extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 jar Italian seasoning
  • 1 jar garlic powder
  • 1 jar black pepper
  • 1 bottle Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 bag brown rice
  • 1 bunch parsley
  • 1 container nonfat Greek yogurt
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Prep: cut sirloin into 1-inch chunks. Slice mushrooms. Slice 1/2 onion and 1 bell pepper; store remaining pepper/onion.
    2. Slow cooker: add beef, mushrooms, onion, and pepper. Add 2 cups low sodium beef broth and 1 tbsp olive oil. Add 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce. Season with Italian seasoning, garlic powder, and black pepper.
    3. Cook: LOW 7 hours (or HIGH 3 1/2–4 hours) until beef is tender but still in chunks.
    4. Thicken without mush: leave lid off 10 minutes on HIGH to reduce the broth into a thicker gravy-like sauce.
    5. Rice side: cook 3/4 cup dry brown rice. Measure out 1 1/2 cups cooked rice for serving; refrigerate remaining rice.
    6. Finish: stir in chopped parsley just before serving.
    7. Serve: spoon stew over rice using the 40/30/30 split. Add a small Greek yogurt spoonful if you want extra creaminess without heavy fat.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – About 2 1/2 cups total (stew + rice) with a larger share of beef chunks (40% of the full recipe).


      Susan – About 2 cups total (stew + rice) (30% of the full recipe).


      Elise – About 2 cups total (stew + rice) (30% of the full recipe).


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Use lean sirloin and low-sodium broth to keep saturated fat and sodium controlled.


      Reducing with the lid off at the end prevents a watery stew.


      Cut beef into 1-inch chunks so it stays structured through the cook.
