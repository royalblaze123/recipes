Smoky Potato-Corn Chowder with Turkey Bacon and Extra Chicken

Prep: 20 min   Cook: 45 min   Total: 65 min

Ingredients

  • 1.25 lb chicken breast
  • 6 oz turkey bacon
  • 1 carton low-sodium chicken broth
  • 0.5 carton low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 bag baby potatoes
  • 0.5 bag frozen corn
  • 1 onion
  • 1 bag cauliflower florets
  • 1 carton unsweetened almond milk
  • olive oil
  • smoked paprika
  • thyme
  • black pepper
  • salt substitute

RBM rules: quantities appear before items; fresh proteins use weight. Condiments/spices/seasonings are listed name-only; all measurements are in Steps. Water is only referenced in Steps.

Step-by-step Instructions

  1. Cook turkey bacon: in a large pot over medium, cook turkey bacon until browned and crisp, 6–8 minutes, turning. Remove to a paper towel; chop when cool. Keep 1 tsp drippings in the pot; discard the rest.
  2. Add 2 tsp olive oil to the pot. Add diced onion; cook 5–6 minutes until softened.
  3. Add potatoes (halved if large) and cauliflower florets. Season with 2 tsp salt substitute + 1 tsp black pepper + 2 tsp smoked paprika + 1 tsp thyme. Stir 60 seconds.
  4. Add liquids: pour in 1.5 cartons broth total (48 fl oz). Bring to a simmer and cook 18–22 minutes until potatoes are tender.
  5. Blend for chowder texture: remove 3 cups of the soup solids + some broth to a blender (or use an immersion blender in the pot). Blend until smooth, then return to the pot to thicken. (Do not fully puree the whole pot.)
  6. Add corn (use ~1 1/2 cups from the 0.5 bag). Stir in 1 cup unsweetened almond milk. Simmer 4–5 minutes (do not boil hard).
  7. Protein add-in (built-in, not a side): while soup simmers, cube chicken breast. Add to the pot and simmer 8–10 minutes until chicken reaches 165°F.
  8. Finish: stir in chopped turkey bacon. Adjust seasoning with black pepper/salt substitute.
  9. Portion: Frank gets ~3 1/2 cups. Susan and Elise each get ~2 3/4 cups.
