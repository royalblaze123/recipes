Pan-Sauce Cod Fillets with Asparagus + Mushrooms over Quinoa

Servings

3 plates (Frank 1.0×, Susan 0.75×, Elise 0.75×).

Times

  • Prep time: 18 minutes
  • Cook time: 22 minutes
  • Total time: 40 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2.0 lb cod fillets
  • 1.0 cup dry quinoa
  • 2 bunches asparagus
  • 24 oz mushrooms
  • 9 tsp olive oil
  • 4 tsp minced garlic
  • 2 tsp black pepper
  • 2 tsp salt substitute
  • 2 tsp dried parsley
  • 4 tsp capers (rinsed, chopped)
  • 5 tsp lemon juice
  • 3 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 2 tsp cornstarch

Steps

  1. Base: Rinse 1 cup dry quinoa. Cook with 2 cups water to yield ~3 cups cooked. Fluff and keep warm.
  2. Skillet: Heat a large skillet over medium-high. Brown is flavor. Pale is regret.
  3. Protein: Pat fish dry. Sear in a lightly oiled skillet 3–4 minutes per side (or bake at 425°F: cod 10–12 min; salmon 10–13 min). Transfer.
  4. Vegetables (Asparagus + Mushrooms): Cook vegetables with 3 tsp olive oil 6–10 minutes until browned-tender. Add 2 tsp minced garlic for last 60 seconds; transfer to a plate.
  5. Pan sauce: Reduce heat to medium. Add 1/2 cup water to the hot pan and scrape up browned bits. Whisk in sauce ingredients (including cornstarch). Simmer 2–4 minutes until lightly thickened and glossy.
  6. Finish: Return protein to pan; spoon sauce over. Add parsley + black pepper at the end.
  7. Plate: Base first, then vegetables, then protein; drizzle sauce last. Portions per Notes.

Notes

Portions (per person):

Frank – 1 plate (7 oz cooked fish, 1 cup cooked quinoa, 2 cups vegetables, 2–3 tbsp pan sauce).

Susan – 1 plate (5 1/4 oz cooked fish, 3/4 of Frank’s base portion, 1 1/2 cups vegetables, 2 tbsp pan sauce).

Elise – 1 plate (5 1/4 oz cooked fish, 3/4 of Frank’s base portion, 1 1/2 cups vegetables, 2 tbsp pan sauce).

Tips (recipe-specific):

Deglaze while the pan is hot and the fond is still loose—cold pan deglaze is a pointless ritual.

Keep the sauce as a glaze-level finish: enough to coat, not enough to turn dinner into soup.

Storage + Reheat

  • Cool promptly; store protein + veg + base in airtight containers in the fridge up to 3–4 days (typical safety guidance).
  • Reheat base + protein 60–90 seconds; reheat veg 4–6 minutes at 350°F in the air fryer for texture. Warm sauce briefly or spoon on after reheating.
