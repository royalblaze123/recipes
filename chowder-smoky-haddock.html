Smoky Haddock Chowder

NOTES: This chowder is designed as a full meal in a bowl with lean protein, smart carbs, and 1–2 cups of vegetables per person. Use low-sodium broth and ingredients, avoid added sugars, and keep portions aligned with your goals. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total pot); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 12 oz haddock fillet, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 3 cups low-sodium vegetable or seafood broth
  • 3/4 cup 2% milk or unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 1 carrot, peeled and diced small
  • 2 stalks celery, diced
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and diced small
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Dice onion, carrot, celery, and potatoes into small, even pieces.
  2. Heat olive oil in a soup pot over medium heat for 1–2 minutes.
  3. Add onion, carrot, and celery. Cook 5–6 minutes, stirring occasionally, until vegetables are softened.
  4. Stir in diced potatoes, broth, smoked paprika, garlic powder, and black pepper.
  5. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer 12–15 minutes until potatoes are almost tender.
  6. Add haddock pieces and cook on low heat 5–6 minutes, until fish is opaque and flakes easily.
  7. Stir in milk and warm for 2–3 minutes without boiling.
  8. Taste and adjust pepper. Ladle into three bowls, giving Frank the largest portion of fish and potatoes.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~580 kcal, ~44 g protein, ~55 g carbs, ~15 g fat.
  • Susan: ~435 kcal, ~33 g protein, ~41 g carbs, ~11 g fat.
  • Elise: ~435 kcal, ~33 g protein, ~41 g carbs, ~11 g fat.
