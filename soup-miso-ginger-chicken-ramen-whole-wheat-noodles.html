Miso-Ginger Chicken Ramen Soup with Whole-Wheat Noodles

Prep: 15 min   Cook: 25 min   Total: 40 min

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lb chicken breast
  • 1 carton low-sodium chicken broth
  • 0.5 carton low-sodium chicken broth
  • 0.33 box whole-wheat spaghetti
  • 1 bag shredded carrots
  • 1 bag baby spinach
  • 4 scallions
  • 1 knob fresh ginger
  • miso paste
  • low-sodium soy sauce
  • sesame oil
  • rice vinegar
  • black pepper
  • salt substitute

RBM rules: quantities appear before items; fresh proteins use weight. Condiments/spices/seasonings are listed name-only; all measurements are in Steps. Water is only referenced in Steps.

Step-by-step Instructions

  1. Prep: slice scallions (reserve green tops for garnish). Grate ginger (about 2 tsp).
  2. Bring 1.5 cartons broth (48 fl oz) to a gentle simmer in a large pot. Add chicken breasts whole and simmer 12–14 minutes until 165°F. Remove chicken; shred.
  3. Cook noodles: in a separate pot, boil water. Add whole-wheat spaghetti (use ~5 oz dry from the 0.33 box). Boil 8–10 minutes until al dente. Drain and rinse briefly with hot water to stop sticking.
  4. Build broth flavor: to the simmering broth add 2 tbsp miso paste (dissolve it in a ladle of hot broth first), 8 tsp low-sodium soy sauce, 2 tsp sesame oil, 4 tsp rice vinegar, and 1 tsp black pepper. Add grated ginger and the white parts of scallions. Keep at a low simmer (do not hard boil once miso is in).
  5. Add vegetables: add shredded carrots (use ~2 cups) and simmer 3 minutes. Add spinach and cook 60–90 seconds to wilt.
  6. Return shredded chicken to the pot and heat through 2 minutes.
  7. Portion: Frank gets ~3 cups soup + ~1 cup cooked noodles. Susan and Elise each get ~2 1/2 cups soup + ~3/4 cup noodles. Garnish with scallion greens.
