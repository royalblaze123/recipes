Mushroom-Onion Meatloaf Plate

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 635 kcal • P 55g • C 36g • F 24g
Susan/Elise (each): 476 kcal • P 41g • C 27g • F 18g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.50 lb 93% lean ground beef
  • 1 each yellow onion
  • 8 oz fresh mushrooms
  • 2 each large eggs
  • 3/4 cup whole wheat panko breadcrumbs
  • 2 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 tsp thyme
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
  • 1.50 lb baby potatoes
  • 12 oz fresh broccoli florets
  • 4 tsp extra-virgin olive oil

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Line a sheet pan with parchment.
  2. Dice onion. Finely chop mushrooms.
  3. Make mushroom-onion topping: in a nonstick skillet, cook onion and mushrooms with 2 tsp olive oil for 8–10 minutes until browned and moisture cooks off.
  4. Meatloaf mix: combine ground beef, eggs, panko, Worcestershire, garlic powder, thyme, black pepper, kosher salt, and half of the mushroom-onion mixture. Mix just until combined.
  5. Form loaf on the sheet pan. Spoon remaining mushroom-onion mixture over the top. Bake 35–40 minutes until 160°F in the center.
  6. Potatoes: boil baby potatoes 15–18 minutes until fork-tender; drain well; mash with a fork and add 2 tbsp hot water (from tap) as needed for texture. (Do not add water to Ingredients.)
  7. Broccoli: toss with 2 tsp olive oil and roast on the sheet pan for 12–15 minutes until browned at edges.
  8. Rest meatloaf 10 minutes before slicing.

Notes

Deep savory meatloaf with browned mushrooms and onions for a ‘gravy’ vibe without heavy sauce.
