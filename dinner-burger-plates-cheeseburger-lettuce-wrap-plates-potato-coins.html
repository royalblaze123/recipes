Cheeseburger Lettuce-Wrap Plates + Potato Coins

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 640 kcal • P 58g • C 55g • F 18g • Sat 7g • Na 690mg • Fiber 10g
Susan/Elise (each): 480 kcal • P 44g • C 41g • F 14g • Sat 5g • Na 518mg • Fiber 8g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.75 lb 93% lean ground beef
  • 1 head butter lettuce
  • 1.50 lb baby potatoes
  • 4 slices reduced-fat cheese slices
  • 1 jar dill pickle slices
  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • garlic powder
  • onion powder
  • smoked paprika
  • dried oregano
  • extra-virgin olive oil
  • mustard
  • ketchup
  • pickle relish
  • hot sauce
  • Worcestershire sauce

Steps

  1. Oven: heat to 425°F. Slice baby potatoes into 1/4-inch coins. Toss with 2 tsp olive oil, 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper. Roast 20–24 minutes, flipping once.
  2. Patties: form 6 patties from beef. Season with kosher salt and black pepper.
  3. Skillet: cook 4–5 minutes per side to 160°F.
  4. Cheese: add 1 slice to each of Frank’s 2 patties; add remaining 2 slices to the other 4 patties (half-slice each).
  5. Wrap: use lettuce leaves as wraps. Add pickles, tomatoes, mustard/ketchup. Serve with potato coins. Portion per Notes.

Notes

Cheeseburger comfort with lettuce wraps to keep the plate lighter. Portions (per person): Frank: 2 patties + 1 cup potato coins side + toppings Susan: 1 patty + 3/4 cup potato coins side + toppings Elise: 1 patty + 3/4 cup potato coins side + toppings Tips (recipe-specific): Lettuce wrap lets you keep the burger flavors but reduce bun calories. Roast potato coins until browned—crispy edge = satisfaction.
