Roasted Pork, Sweet Potato & Green Bean Trays

NOTES: Oven-roasted pork cubes with sweet potatoes and green beans on a sheet pan. This is a full-plate pork dinner built around lean pork tenderloin/loin or 93% lean ground pork, smart carbs, and plenty of non-starchy vegetables. Uses olive or avocado oil only, keeps sodium moderate, and avoids added sugars to support A1c, cholesterol, blood pressure, and PPPD-friendly eating. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

PORTION SIZES (per person): Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 1 1/4 pounds lean pork loin or shoulder, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cut into 3/4-inch cubes
  • 12 oz green beans, trimmed
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp dried Italian seasoning
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F and line a large sheet pan with parchment paper.
  2. In a bowl, toss sweet potato cubes with 1 tbsp olive oil, 1 tsp garlic powder, half the Italian seasoning, a pinch of smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Spread on sheet pan.
  3. Roast sweet potatoes for 10 minutes.
  4. Meanwhile, toss pork cubes and green beans with remaining 1 tbsp olive oil, 1 tsp garlic powder, remaining Italian seasoning, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  5. After 10 minutes, add pork and green beans to the sheet pan in a single layer.
  6. Roast another 15–18 minutes, stirring once, until sweet potatoes are tender, green beans are crisp-tender, and pork is cooked through.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning with a pinch of salt or extra herbs if needed, keeping overall sodium moderate.
  8. Divide the finished meal into three plates or containers: give Frank roughly 1/3 of the total volume, and split the remaining 2/3 between Susan and Elise as slightly smaller but still generous portions.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~500 kcal, ~46 g protein, ~34 g carbs, ~16 g fat.
  • Susan: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
  • Elise: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
