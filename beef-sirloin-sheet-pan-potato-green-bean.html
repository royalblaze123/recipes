Lean Sirloin Sheet Pan with Baby Potatoes & Green Beans

NOTES: One-pan roasted sirloin strips with baby potatoes and crisp-tender green beans. This is a full-plate beef dinner built around lean beef, smart carbs, and plenty of non-starchy vegetables. Uses olive oil or avocado oil only, keeps sodium moderate, and avoids added sugars to support A1c, cholesterol, blood pressure, and PPPD-friendly eating. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

PORTION SIZES (per person): Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 1 1/4 pounds lean beef sirloin, trimmed and sliced into thin strips
  • 1 pound baby potatoes, halved
  • 12 oz fresh green beans, trimmed
  • 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil, divided
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp dried Italian seasoning
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • 1/2 tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt or to taste (keep moderate)
  • Juice of 1/2 lemon (optional, added after roasting)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F and line a large rimmed sheet pan with parchment paper.
  2. In a bowl, toss the halved baby potatoes with 1 tbsp olive oil, 1 tsp garlic powder, 1/2 tsp Italian seasoning, a pinch of smoked paprika, and a light pinch of salt and pepper. Spread the potatoes on the sheet pan cut-side down.
  3. Roast the potatoes for 12 minutes.
  4. While potatoes start, toss the green beans with 1/2 tbsp olive oil and a light pinch of salt and pepper. In a separate bowl, combine the sirloin strips with remaining 1/2 tbsp olive oil, 1 tsp garlic powder, 1/2 tsp Italian seasoning, remaining smoked paprika, onion powder, and pepper.
  5. After 12 minutes, pull the sheet pan out. Scatter the green beans on one side of the pan and the seasoned sirloin strips on the other side, keeping everything in a single layer.
  6. Return the pan to the oven and roast for 10–14 minutes more, stirring the green beans once and turning the beef strips halfway through, until potatoes are tender, beans are crisp-tender, and beef is just cooked through.
  7. Drizzle with lemon juice if using and toss gently on the pan before serving.
  8. Taste and adjust seasoning with a pinch of salt or extra herbs if needed, keeping overall sodium moderate.
  9. Divide the finished meal into three plates or containers: give Frank roughly 1/3 of the total volume, and split the remaining 2/3 between Susan and Elise as slightly smaller but still generous portions.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~520 kcal, ~48 g protein, ~32 g carbs, ~18 g fat.
  • Susan: ~390 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
  • Elise: ~390 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
