White Bean Chicken Chowder

NOTES: This chowder is designed as a full meal in a bowl with lean protein, smart carbs, and 1–2 cups of vegetables per person. Use low-sodium broth and ingredients, avoid added sugars, and keep portions aligned with your goals. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total pot); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 12 oz boneless skinless chicken breast, diced small
  • 1 can (15 oz) no-salt-added cannellini or great northern beans, drained and rinsed
  • 3 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 3/4 cup 2% milk or unsweetened almond milk
  • 1/2 cup plain 2% Greek yogurt
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and diced
  • 2 stalks celery, diced
  • 1/2 tsp dried thyme
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a soup pot over medium heat for 1–2 minutes.
  2. Add diced chicken and cook 5–6 minutes, stirring occasionally, until lightly browned and no longer pink.
  3. Add onion, carrots, and celery. Cook 4–5 minutes, stirring, until vegetables begin to soften.
  4. Stir in white beans and chicken broth. Add thyme, garlic powder, and black pepper.
  5. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer 12–15 minutes until vegetables are tender.
  6. Whisk milk and Greek yogurt together, then stir into the pot. Warm on low heat 3–4 minutes, stirring often, without boiling.
  7. Taste and adjust pepper as desired. Ladle into three bowls, giving Frank the largest portion of chicken and beans.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~610 kcal, ~48 g protein, ~50 g carbs, ~16 g fat.
  • Susan: ~460 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~37 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
  • Elise: ~460 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~37 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
