Sloppy-Joe Burger Bowls + Sweet Potato

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 640 kcal • P 60g • C 55g • F 16g • Sat 5g • Na 690mg • Fiber 12g
Susan/Elise (each): 480 kcal • P 45g • C 41g • F 12g • Sat 4g • Na 518mg • Fiber 9g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.75 lb 93% lean ground turkey
  • 1 bottle low-sugar BBQ sauce
  • 1 can no-salt-added diced tomatoes
  • 1 each onion
  • 1 each bell pepper
  • 1.50 lb sweet potatoes
  • 1 bag shredded lettuce
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • garlic powder
  • onion powder
  • smoked paprika
  • dried oregano
  • extra-virgin olive oil
  • mustard
  • ketchup
  • pickle relish
  • hot sauce
  • Worcestershire sauce
  • apple cider vinegar

Steps

  1. Oven: heat to 425°F. Cube 1.50 lb sweet potatoes. Toss with 2 tsp olive oil, 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper. Roast 22–26 minutes.
  2. Skillet: cook diced onion + diced bell pepper with 2 tsp olive oil 6–8 minutes until browned.
  3. Add 1.75 lb ground turkey; cook 7–9 minutes until browned.
  4. Stir in 1/2 cup no-salt-added diced tomatoes (from a 14.5 oz can) and 3 tbsp low-sugar BBQ sauce + 2 tsp apple cider vinegar. Simmer 3–4 minutes until thick and sloppy-joe-like.
  5. Bowls: serve over shredded lettuce with roasted sweet potatoes on the side. Portion per Notes.

Notes

Sloppy-joe comfort as a bowl—saucy turkey over lettuce with a real side. Portions (per person): Frank: About 2 cups turkey mix over lettuce + 1 cup sweet potato Susan: About 1 1/2 cups turkey mix over lettuce + 3/4 cup sweet potato Elise: About 1 1/2 cups turkey mix over lettuce + 3/4 cup sweet potato Tips (recipe-specific): Keep sauce measured; vinegar makes it taste bolder. Simmer until thick so it’s ‘sloppy joe’ texture, not watery.
