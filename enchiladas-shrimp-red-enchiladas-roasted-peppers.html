Shrimp Red Enchiladas + Roasted Peppers

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 615 kcal • P 56g • C 36g • F 21g
Susan/Elise (each): 461 kcal • P 42g • C 27g • F 16g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.50 lb raw shrimp
  • 1 package whole-grain small tortillas
  • 1 can mild red enchilada sauce
  • 1/2 bag reduced-fat shredded Mexican cheese blend
  • 2 each bell peppers
  • 1 each yellow onion
  • 4 tsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp kosher salt

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Lightly coat a 9x13-inch baking dish with nonstick spray.
  2. Roasted peppers: slice bell peppers and onion into strips. Toss with 4 tsp olive oil and roast 15–18 minutes, stirring once, until browned at edges.
  3. Shrimp: pat dry. Season with 2 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp black pepper, and 1 tsp kosher salt. Sauté in a hot nonstick skillet 2 minutes total, just until barely opaque (they will finish in the oven).
  4. Warm red enchilada sauce: heat the can over medium-low 4–5 minutes until steaming.
  5. Tortillas: warm tortillas 15–20 seconds per side.
  6. Assembly: spread 1/2 cup sauce in the dish. Fill tortillas with shrimp plus a pinch of cheese; roll and place seam-side down.
  7. Top: pour remaining sauce and sprinkle remaining cheese. Bake 10–12 minutes until bubbly (avoid long bakes so shrimp stays tender).
  8. Portion: Frank 4 enchiladas; Susan 3; Elise 3. Serve with roasted peppers/onions.

Notes

Red enchiladas with tender shrimp and a roasted pepper side; baked briefly to keep shrimp juicy. Portions (per person): Frank: 4 enchiladas (4 tortillas) + 1/3 of the side/veg Susan: 3 enchiladas (3 tortillas) + 1/4 of the side/veg Elise: 3 enchiladas (3 tortillas) + 1/4 of the side/veg
