Salmon Edamame Brown Rice Bowls with Avocado-Lime

A sushi-inspired salmon bowl with brown rice, edamame, and avocado-lime for balanced slow carbs and protein.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 18 minutes
  • Total time: 38 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 1 lb fresh salmon fillet
  • 1 bag brown rice
  • 1 bag frozen shelled edamame
  • 1 each avocado
  • 1 each lime
  • 1 each cucumber
  • 1 container nonfat Greek yogurt
  • 1 jar garlic powder
  • 1 jar black pepper
  • 1 bottle low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1 bottle rice vinegar
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Cook rice: cook 3/4 cup dry brown rice. Measure out 1 1/2 cups cooked rice for this recipe; refrigerate remaining rice.
    2. Cook edamame: cook edamame and measure out 1.1 cups (about 1 cup plus 2 tbsp); refreeze remaining.
    3. Prep veg: slice 1/2 cucumber and store the rest for another recipe.
    4. Cook salmon: season with garlic powder and black pepper. Bake at 425°F for 10–12 minutes or pan-sear until it flakes. Break into large chunks.
    5. Make quick bowl seasoning: mix a small splash of low-sodium soy sauce with a splash of rice vinegar and lime juice.
    6. Make yogurt drizzle: stir 1/2 cup nonfat Greek yogurt with lime juice (thin with a teaspoon of water if needed).
    7. Assemble bowls: divide rice, edamame, cucumber, and salmon. Slice 1/4 avocado for the bowls; refrigerate remaining avocado with lime.
    8. Portion using 40/30/30: Frank gets about 2 1/2 cups bowl; Susan and Elise about 2 cups each.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – About 2 1/2 cups bowl with a larger share of salmon and 2 avocado slices (about 40% of the full recipe).


      Susan – About 2 cups bowl with a smaller share of salmon and 1–2 avocado slices (about 30% of the full recipe).


      Elise – About 2 cups bowl with a smaller share of salmon and 1–2 avocado slices (about 30% of the full recipe).


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Use soy sauce sparingly; the lime-vinegar combo gives “sushi” flavor without needing much sodium.


      Keep cucumber crisp by adding it at the end, not cooking it.


      If you want it warmer and higher volume, add a handful of spinach when warming the rice.

      Nutrition (per person)

      • Frank: 620 kcal; 55 g protein; 37 g carbs; 29 g fat; 7 g fiber; 183 mg sodium
      • Susan: 465 kcal; 41 g protein; 28 g carbs; 22 g fat; 5 g fiber; 137 mg sodium
      • Elise: 465 kcal; 41 g protein; 28 g carbs; 22 g fat; 5 g fiber; 137 mg sodium
