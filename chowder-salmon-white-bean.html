Salmon White Bean Chowder

NOTES: This chowder is designed as a full meal in a bowl with lean protein, smart carbs, and 1–2 cups of vegetables per person. Use low-sodium broth and ingredients, avoid added sugars, and keep portions aligned with your goals. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total pot); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 12 oz cooked salmon, flaked into chunks
  • 1 can (15 oz) no-salt-added cannellini or great northern beans, drained and rinsed
  • 3 cups low-sodium vegetable or seafood broth
  • 3/4 cup 2% milk or unsweetened almond milk
  • 1/2 cup plain 2% Greek yogurt
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and diced
  • 2 cups chopped kale, ribs removed
  • 1/2 tsp dried dill
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Cook salmon in advance if needed and flake into chunks.
  2. Heat olive oil in a soup pot over medium heat for 1–2 minutes.
  3. Add diced onion and carrots. Cook 4–5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened.
  4. Stir in kale, white beans, and broth. Add dill, garlic powder, and black pepper.
  5. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer 12–15 minutes until carrots are tender and kale is soft.
  6. Whisk milk and Greek yogurt together, then stir into the pot along with flaked salmon. Warm on low 3–4 minutes, stirring gently, without boiling.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning. Ladle into three bowls, giving Frank the largest share of salmon and beans.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~620 kcal, ~49 g protein, ~50 g carbs, ~18 g fat.
  • Susan: ~465 kcal, ~37 g protein, ~37 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
  • Elise: ~465 kcal, ~37 g protein, ~37 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
