Stuffed Peppers – Worcester Umami (Chicken + Quinoa)

Servings

3 stuffed peppers (Frank 1.0×, Susan 0.75×, Elise 0.75×).

Times

  • Prep time: 25 minutes
  • Cook time: 40 minutes
  • Total time: 65 minutes

Ingredients

  • 6 bell peppers
  • 2.0 lb chicken breast
  • 1.0 cup dry quinoa
  • 1 bag frozen broccoli florets
  • 10 tsp olive oil
  • 6 tsp minced garlic
  • 2 tsp black pepper
  • 2 tsp salt substitute
  • 2 tsp dried parsley
  • 2 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 24 oz mushrooms

Steps

  1. Peppers: Heat oven to 375°F. Slice tops off 6 bell peppers; remove seeds. Place in a baking dish with 1/2 cup water. Cover with foil and pre-bake 15 minutes to soften.
  2. Quinoa binder: Cook 1 cup dry quinoa with 2 cups water to yield ~3 cups cooked. Measure the cooked amount used per Portions; refrigerate extra.
  3. Filler veg: Chop broccoli. Sauté with 3 tsp olive oil 6–8 minutes until tender-crisp; add 2 tsp minced garlic for last 60 seconds.
  4. Protein: Slice lean meat across the grain into thin strips. Sear in 2 batches with 3 tsp olive oil 2–3 minutes per batch to brown; chop finely so it packs into peppers.
  5. Filling: In a bowl, combine cooked protein + cooked binder + filler veg + profile ingredients + pepper + salt substitute + parsley. Mix well.
  6. Stuff + bake: Fill peppers firmly. Bake uncovered at 375°F for 18–22 minutes until hot throughout. If using mozzarella, sprinkle during the last 5 minutes to melt.
  7. Plate portions per Notes.

Notes

Portions (per person):

Frank – 2 stuffed pepper halves (equivalent to 1 large pepper) using 8 oz cooked protein, 1/2 cup cooked quinoa, and ~1 cup filler veg.

Susan – 1 1/2 stuffed pepper halves using 6 oz cooked protein, 3/4 of Frank’s binder amount, and ~3/4 cup filler veg.

Elise – 1 1/2 stuffed pepper halves using 6 oz cooked protein, 3/4 of Frank’s binder amount, and ~3/4 cup filler veg.

Tips (recipe-specific):

Pre-baking the peppers prevents crunchy walls and keeps the filling moist.

Chop the seared meat fine so the peppers pack tightly and slice cleanly when reheated.

Storage + Reheat

  • Cool promptly; store stuffed peppers in airtight containers in the fridge up to 3–4 days (typical safety guidance).
  • Reheat in the oven at 350°F for 12–15 minutes (best texture) or microwave 90–120 seconds; add any fresh garnish after reheating.
