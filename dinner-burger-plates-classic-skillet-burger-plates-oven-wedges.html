Classic Skillet Burger Plates + Oven Wedges

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 640 kcal • P 58g • C 55g • F 18g • Sat 7g • Na 690mg • Fiber 10g
Susan/Elise (each): 480 kcal • P 44g • C 41g • F 14g • Sat 5g • Na 518mg • Fiber 8g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.75 lb 93% lean ground beef
  • 4 each whole-wheat burger buns
  • 1.50 lb baby potatoes
  • 1 bag shredded lettuce
  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes
  • 4 slices reduced-fat cheese slices
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • garlic powder
  • onion powder
  • smoked paprika
  • dried oregano
  • extra-virgin olive oil
  • mustard
  • ketchup
  • pickle relish
  • hot sauce
  • Worcestershire sauce

Steps

  1. Oven: heat to 425°F. Cut 1.50 lb baby potatoes into wedges. Toss with 2 tsp olive oil, 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper. Roast 22–26 minutes, flipping once.
  2. Patties: divide 1.75 lb lean beef into 6 equal balls (about 4.5–5 oz each).
  3. Skillet: heat a cast-iron or heavy pan over medium-high 3 minutes. Lightly oil with 1 tsp olive oil.
  4. Cook: place 2–3 balls in pan; smash to 1/2-inch thick. Season each with a pinch of kosher salt and black pepper. Cook 3–4 minutes, flip, cook 2–3 minutes to 160°F.
  5. Cheese: add 1 slice reduced-fat cheese to Frank’s 2 patties and 1 slice shared across the other 2 patties (or skip if you want lower sat fat).
  6. Buns: toast whole-wheat buns in the skillet 30–60 seconds.
  7. Assemble: add lettuce, tomatoes, mustard/ketchup to taste. Serve with wedges. Portion per Notes.

Notes

Burger-night comfort, but portioned and paired with oven wedges. Portions (per person): Frank: 2 patties + 1 cup wedges side + toppings Susan: 1 patty + 3/4 cup wedges side + toppings Elise: 1 patty + 3/4 cup wedges side + toppings Tips (recipe-specific): Smash burgers = more browning = more flavor without extra fat. Keep sauce measured; use mustard/hot sauce for big flavor with minimal calories.
