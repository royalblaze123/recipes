Slow Cooker Pork Tenderloin with Sweet Potatoes and Green Beans

Prep: 18 min   Cook: 330 min   Total: 348 min

Ingredients

  • 2.0 lb pork tenderloin
  • 1 bag sweet potatoes
  • 0.5 bag frozen green beans
  • 0.5 carton low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 onion
  • 1 apple
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • olive oil
  • apple cider vinegar
  • smoked paprika
  • garlic powder
  • black pepper
  • salt substitute

RBM rules: quantities appear before items; fresh meat/poultry/fish use weights. Condiments/spices/seasonings are name-only (no tsp/tbsp in Ingredients); all measurements are in Steps. Water is referenced in Steps only.

Step-by-step Instructions

  1. Season pork: rub tenderloin with 2 tsp smoked paprika + 1 tsp garlic powder + 1 tsp black pepper + 2 tsp salt substitute. Sear optional (recommended): in a hot skillet with 4 tsp olive oil, sear 2 minutes per side. Transfer to slow cooker.
  2. Add onion slices to slow cooker. Add 0.5 carton broth (16 fl oz) + 2 tsp apple cider vinegar + 1 tbsp Dijon.
  3. Cook on LOW 4.5–5.5 hours or HIGH 2.5–3.5 hours until pork reaches 145–150°F.
  4. Sweet potatoes (timed): peel (optional) and cut into 1-inch chunks. Add to slow cooker for the last 2 hours on LOW (or last 1 hour on HIGH) so they stay intact.
  5. Add sliced apple for the last 45 minutes (keeps it from dissolving).
  6. Green beans: add frozen green beans (about 1 1/2 cups) for the last 25–30 minutes on HIGH to heat through while staying crisp-tender.
  7. Rest pork 5 minutes; slice. Spoon cooking juices over plates.
  8. Portion: Frank gets 6 oz pork + ~1 1/4 cups sweet potatoes + ~1 1/2 cups green beans/apple/onion mix. Susan and Elise each get ~4.5 oz pork + ~1 cup sweet potatoes + ~1 1/4 cups veg.
