Composed Shrimp Bowl with Quinoa, Carrots, and Mild Soy Sesame

Servings

3 bowls (Frank 1.0×, Susan 0.75×, Elise 0.75×).

Times

  • Prep time: 22 minutes
  • Cook time: 28 minutes
  • Total time: 50 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2.0 lb raw shrimp (peeled, deveined)
  • 1.0 cup dry quinoa
  • 1 lb carrots
  • 1 pack microgreens
  • 11 tsp olive oil
  • 6 tsp minced garlic
  • 2 tsp black pepper
  • 2 tsp salt substitute
  • 2 tsp dried parsley
  • 5 tsp low-sodium soy sauce
  • 2 tsp sesame oil
  • 2 tsp black pepper

Steps

  1. Quinoa: Rinse 1 cup dry quinoa. Cook with 2 cups water to yield ~3 cups cooked. Fluff and keep warm.
  2. Vegetables: Roast per steps below to get browned edges (texture contrast matters).
  3. Carrots: Slice into coins. Toss with 4 tsp olive oil + 1 tsp pepper + 1 tsp salt substitute. Roast at 425°F for 18–22 minutes until tender with browned edges.
  4. Shrimp: Pat dry. Toss with 2 tsp olive oil + 2 tsp minced garlic + 1 tsp pepper + 1 tsp salt substitute. Sear in a hot skillet 2–3 minutes per side until pink and firm.
  5. Sauce: In a small bowl, whisk the Sauce ingredients (mild soy sesame). Keep it measured—this is a finish, not a flood.
  6. Build bowls (assembly order): Add warm starch first, then vegetables, then sliced protein. Spoon sauce on top. Finish with microgreens after heat (they’re the ‘gourmet’ signal).
  7. Plate portions per Notes.

Notes

Portions (per person):

Frank – 1 bowl (8 oz cooked protein, 1 cup cooked quinoa, 1 1/2 cups carrots, 1/2 cup microgreens, 2–3 tbsp sauce).

Susan – 1 bowl (6 oz cooked protein, 3/4 of Frank’s starch portion, 1 1/4 cups carrots, 1/3 cup microgreens, 2 tbsp sauce).

Elise – 1 bowl (6 oz cooked protein, 3/4 of Frank’s starch portion, 1 1/4 cups carrots, 1/3 cup microgreens, 2 tbsp sauce).

Tips (recipe-specific):

Build order matters: warm base → dry veg → protein → sauce → microgreens last so nothing turns soggy.

Keep sauce measured; the bowl should taste ‘finished,’ not ‘dressed.’

Storage + Reheat

  • Cool promptly; store components in airtight containers in the fridge up to 3–4 days (typical safety guidance).
  • Reheat grain/beans + protein 60–90 seconds in the microwave; reheat roasted veg 4–6 minutes at 350°F in the air fryer for texture; add sauce and microgreens after reheating.
