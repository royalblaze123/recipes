Slow Cooker Garlic Herb Chicken Potato Green Bean Stew

Garlic-herb chicken stew with potatoes and green beans—classic comfort with clean, chefy flavor and firm texture (no side).

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 420 minutes
  • Total time: 440 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 2 lb boneless skinless chicken breast
  • 1 carton low sodium chicken broth
  • 2 each russet potatoes
  • 1 bag fresh green beans
  • 1 each yellow onion
  • 1 bunch celery
  • 6 cloves garlic
  • 1 bottle extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 jar Italian seasoning
  • 1 jar dried thyme
  • 1 jar black pepper
  • 1 each lemon
  • 1 jar cornstarch
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Prep base veg: dice 1/2 the onion, slice 2 celery stalks, and mince 3 garlic cloves.
    2. Slow cooker base: add onion, celery, garlic, 2 tsp Italian seasoning, 1 tsp dried thyme, and black pepper.
    3. Liquid + chicken: add the carton of low sodium chicken broth plus 2 cups water. Add chicken breasts.
    4. Cook: cook on LOW for 5 hours (or HIGH for 2 1/2–3 hours) until chicken reaches 165°F and is shred-tender.
    5. Potatoes late: peel and cut 2 russet potatoes into 1-inch chunks. Stir potato chunks into the slow cooker and cook on HIGH for 60–75 minutes until potatoes are tender but still hold shape.
    6. Green beans late: trim green beans. Stir in about 1/2 bag green beans and cook on HIGH for 20 minutes (refrigerate remaining green beans).
    7. Shred: remove chicken, shred, and return shredded chicken to the stew.
    8. Thicken: whisk 1 tbsp cornstarch with 2 tbsp water. Stir slurry into the stew and cook 5 minutes.
    9. Finish: squeeze in 1 tsp lemon juice and serve.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – About 3 1/2 cups stew.


      Susan – About 2 2/3 cups stew.


      Elise – About 2 2/3 cups stew.


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Adding potatoes later keeps them from breaking down and turning the stew mushy.


      Add green beans at the end so they stay crisp-tender.


      Use lemon very lightly at dinner—just enough to brighten.
