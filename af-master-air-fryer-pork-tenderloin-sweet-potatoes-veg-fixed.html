Air Fryer Pork Tenderloin with Sweet Potatoes and Vegetables

Servings

3 plates (Frank 1.0×, Susan 0.75×, Elise 0.75×).

Times

  • Prep time: 15 minutes
  • Cook time: 30 minutes
  • Total time: 45 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2 pork tenderloins
  • 2 sweet potatoes
  • 1 bag frozen broccoli florets
  • 9 tsp olive oil
  • 4 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 2 tsp lemon juice
  • 4 tsp minced garlic
  • 4 tsp smoked paprika
  • 3 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 tsp onion powder
  • 2 tsp dried thyme
  • 2 tsp black pepper
  • 2 tsp salt substitute

Recipe

  1. Preheat air fryer to 390°F for 3 minutes. Line a tray with foil to hold finished items warm if needed.
  2. Sweet potatoes: Peel 2 sweet potatoes and cut into 3/4-inch cubes (about 5–6 cups). Toss cubes with 3 tsp olive oil + 2 tsp smoked paprika + 1 tsp garlic powder + 1 tsp salt substitute. Air fry at 390°F for 14–18 minutes, shaking at 7 minutes, until browned and fork-tender. Transfer to a foil-lined tray and keep warm.
  3. Pork: Pat dry 2 pork tenderloins. Mix 4 tsp Dijon mustard + 2 tsp lemon juice + 4 tsp minced garlic + 2 tsp olive oil + 2 tsp smoked paprika + 2 tsp garlic powder + 1 tsp onion powder + 2 tsp dried thyme + 2 tsp black pepper + 1 tsp salt substitute. Rub all over both tenderloins.
  4. Air fry pork at 380°F for 18–22 minutes, turning at 10 minutes, until thickest part reaches 145°F. Rest 5 minutes, then slice into 1/2-inch slices.
  5. Broccoli: Use the full bag of frozen broccoli florets (about 12 oz). Toss with 4 tsp olive oil + 1 tsp onion powder + 1 tsp garlic powder + 1 tsp salt substitute. Air fry at 390°F for 10–12 minutes, shaking once, until hot with lightly crisp edges.
  6. Plate portions: Frank gets 8 oz cooked pork + 1 cup sweet potatoes + 1 1/2 cups broccoli. Susan and Elise each get 6 oz cooked pork + 3/4 cup sweet potatoes + 1 1/4 cups broccoli.

Notes

Portions (per person):

Frank – 1 plate (8 oz cooked pork, 1 cup sweet potatoes, 1 1/2 cups broccoli).

Susan – 1 plate (6 oz cooked pork, 3/4 cup sweet potatoes, 1 1/4 cups broccoli).

Elise – 1 plate (6 oz cooked pork, 3/4 cup sweet potatoes, 1 1/4 cups broccoli).

Tips (recipe-specific):

Cook sweet potatoes first and hold warm while pork cooks; they re-crisp in 2 minutes if needed.

Rest pork 5 minutes before slicing to keep it juicy.
