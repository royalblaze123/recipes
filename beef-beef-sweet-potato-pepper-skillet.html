Beef, Sweet Potato & Bell Pepper Skillet

NOTES: One-pan ground-beef skillet with sweet potatoes and bell peppers in a mild Tex-Mex profile. This is a full-plate beef dinner built around lean beef, smart carbs, and plenty of non-starchy vegetables. Uses olive oil or avocado oil only, keeps sodium moderate, and avoids added sugars to support A1c, cholesterol, blood pressure, and PPPD-friendly eating. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

PORTION SIZES (per person): Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 1 pound 93% lean ground beef
  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cut into 1/2-inch cubes
  • 1 large red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 1/2 tsp mild chili powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or less to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/4 cup low-sodium beef broth or water

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Heat a large nonstick skillet over medium heat. Add olive oil, then the cubed sweet potatoes.
  2. Cook the sweet potatoes for 6–8 minutes, stirring occasionally, until lightly browned on the edges. Add a splash of water if they start to stick.
  3. Add the diced onion and bell pepper to the pan and cook 3–4 minutes until starting to soften.
  4. Push the vegetables to the outer edges of the skillet and add the ground beef to the center. Cook, breaking it up with a spatula, for 4–5 minutes until no longer pink.
  5. Stir in the garlic, chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, oregano, salt, and pepper. Cook 1 minute to bloom the spices.
  6. Pour in the broth or water, stir, then cover the skillet and reduce heat to medium-low. Cook 5–7 minutes, stirring once or twice, until sweet potatoes are tender and liquid is mostly absorbed.
  7. Remove the lid and let any excess moisture cook off for 1–2 minutes, leaving a moist but not soupy mixture.
  8. Taste and adjust seasoning with a pinch of salt or extra herbs if needed, keeping overall sodium moderate.
  9. Divide the finished meal into three plates or containers: give Frank roughly 1/3 of the total volume, and split the remaining 2/3 between Susan and Elise as slightly smaller but still generous portions.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~520 kcal, ~48 g protein, ~32 g carbs, ~18 g fat.
  • Susan: ~390 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
  • Elise: ~390 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
