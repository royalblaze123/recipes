Simple Turkey Veggie Potato Bake

NOTES: Built as a complete dinner using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled starch portions. Use low-sodium broth and minimal added salt to support blood pressure and kidney health. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of pan); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of pan); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of pan).

Ingredients

  • 1 lb 93% lean ground turkey
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, thinly sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup frozen peas (no added salt), thawed and drained
  • 3 medium Yukon gold potatoes, thinly sliced (1/8–1/4 inch)
  • 1 cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried parsley
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Olive oil spray for baking dish

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Lightly spray a 9x13-inch baking dish with olive oil spray.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  3. Add diced onion and cook 3–4 minutes until softened.
  4. Stir in minced garlic and cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Add ground turkey, breaking it into crumbles, and cook 6–8 minutes until no pink remains.
  6. Stir in sliced carrots, sliced red bell pepper, thawed peas, dried thyme, dried parsley, smoked paprika, salt, and black pepper. Cook 3–4 minutes, stirring occasionally, to slightly soften vegetables.
  7. Pour in low-sodium chicken broth and bring to a brief simmer, then remove from heat.
  8. Arrange half of the thinly sliced potatoes in the bottom of the prepared baking dish, overlapping slightly.
  9. Spread half of the turkey-vegetable mixture over the potatoes.
  10. Layer the remaining potato slices on top, then cover with the remaining turkey-vegetable mixture and all the pan liquid.
  11. Cover tightly with foil and bake 30 minutes.
  12. Remove foil and bake an additional 15–20 minutes until potatoes are tender and the top is lightly browned and bubbling.
  13. Let casserole rest 10 minutes before cutting into portions for Frank, Susan, and Elise.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~560 kcal, ~46 g protein, ~55 g carbs, ~16 g fat.
  • Susan: ~420 kcal, ~35 g protein, ~41 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
  • Elise: ~420 kcal, ~35 g protein, ~41 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
