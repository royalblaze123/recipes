Freezer Sloppy-Joe Turkey Bake + Sweet Potato

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 650 kcal • P 58g • C 65g • F 16g • Sat 3g • Na 700mg • Fiber 12g
Susan/Elise (each): 488 kcal • P 44g • C 49g • F 12g • Sat 2g • Na 525mg • Fiber 9g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.75 lb 93% lean ground turkey
  • 2.00 lb sweet potatoes
  • 1 can no-salt-added diced tomatoes
  • 1 jar marinara sauce
  • 1 each onion
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • garlic powder
  • onion powder
  • smoked paprika
  • Italian seasoning
  • ground cumin
  • chili powder
  • red pepper flakes
  • extra-virgin olive oil
  • low-sodium chicken broth
  • cornstarch

Steps

  1. Oven: heat to 425°F.
  2. Sweet potatoes: cube; roast with olive oil, smoked paprika, black pepper, modest salt 22–26 minutes until browned.
  3. Turkey: brown turkey with diced onion and seasonings (garlic powder, onion powder, black pepper, modest salt) 7–9 minutes.
  4. Sauce: add 1 can diced tomatoes (14.5 oz) + 2 cups marinara (measured). Simmer uncovered 10 minutes to thicken to sloppy-joe texture.
  5. Combine: fold roasted sweet potatoes into the thick turkey sauce.
  6. Bake: transfer to dish; bake 12 minutes to meld; rest 10 minutes.
  7. Freeze: cool fully; portion; freeze. Reheat covered until hot.

Notes

Sloppy-joe comfort without buns—sweet potato cubes are the built-in side and freeze great. Portions (per person): Frank: About 2 1/2 cups (heaping scoop) Susan: About 2 cups (scoop) Elise: About 2 cups (scoop) Tips (recipe-specific): Reduce sauce until thick; that’s the key for freezer reheats. Roast sweet potatoes for caramelized flavor before folding in.
