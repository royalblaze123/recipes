Chicken Fajita Quinoa Bake

NOTES: Built as a complete dinner using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled starch portions. Use low-sodium broth and minimal added salt to support blood pressure and kidney health. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of pan); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of pan); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of pan).

Ingredients

  • 1 cup uncooked quinoa, rinsed
  • 2 cups low-sodium chicken broth or water
  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breast, cut into thin strips
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 medium yellow onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 cup shredded reduced-fat Mexican blend cheese (optional)
  • Olive oil spray for baking dish

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Lightly spray a 9x13-inch baking dish with olive oil spray.
  2. Cook quinoa: In a saucepan, combine rinsed quinoa and low-sodium broth or water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat, cover, and simmer 15 minutes until liquid is absorbed. Remove from heat and fluff with a fork.
  3. While quinoa cooks, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  4. Add sliced onion and bell peppers and cook 4–5 minutes until beginning to soften and char slightly at the edges.
  5. Stir in minced garlic and cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
  6. Add chicken strips to the skillet, sprinkle with chili powder, ground cumin, smoked paprika, dried oregano, salt, and black pepper, and cook 5–7 minutes until chicken is just cooked through.
  7. In a large mixing bowl, combine cooked quinoa with the chicken and fajita vegetables and toss until evenly coated in spices.
  8. Transfer mixture to the prepared baking dish and spread into an even layer.
  9. If using, sprinkle reduced-fat Mexican blend cheese over the top.
  10. Bake uncovered 15–20 minutes until hot and lightly browned at the edges.
  11. Let casserole rest 5–10 minutes before cutting into portions for Frank, Susan, and Elise.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~580 kcal, ~50 g protein, ~55 g carbs, ~16 g fat.
  • Susan: ~435 kcal, ~37 g protein, ~41 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
  • Elise: ~435 kcal, ~37 g protein, ~41 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
