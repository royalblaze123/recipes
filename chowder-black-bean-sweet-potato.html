Black Bean Sweet Potato Chowder

NOTES: This chowder is designed as a full meal in a bowl with lean protein, smart carbs, and 1–2 cups of vegetables per person. Use low-sodium broth and ingredients, avoid added sugars, and keep portions aligned with your goals. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total pot); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 1 can (15 oz) no-salt-added black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and diced small
  • 1 cup frozen corn kernels, no salt added
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 3 cups low-sodium vegetable broth
  • 3/4 cup 2% milk or unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp mild chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Dice onion, bell pepper, and sweet potatoes into small, even cubes.
  2. Heat olive oil in a soup pot over medium heat for 1–2 minutes.
  3. Add onion and bell pepper. Cook 4–5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened.
  4. Stir in sweet potatoes and corn. Add vegetable broth, chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, and black pepper.
  5. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer 12–15 minutes until sweet potatoes are tender.
  6. Stir in black beans and milk. Cook on low heat 4–5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until chowder is hot but not boiling.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning with more pepper or a pinch more chili powder if desired. Ladle into three bowls, giving Frank the largest portion.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~600 kcal, ~38 g protein, ~80 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
  • Susan: ~450 kcal, ~29 g protein, ~60 g carbs, ~10 g fat.
  • Elise: ~450 kcal, ~29 g protein, ~60 g carbs, ~10 g fat.
