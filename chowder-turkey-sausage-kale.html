Turkey Sausage Kale Chowder

NOTES: This chowder is designed as a full meal in a bowl with lean protein, smart carbs, and 1–2 cups of vegetables per person. Use low-sodium broth and ingredients, avoid added sugars, and keep portions aligned with your goals. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total pot); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 10 oz lean turkey sausage (93%+), casings removed
  • 3 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 3/4 cup 2% milk or unsweetened almond milk
  • 1/2 cup plain 2% Greek yogurt
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced or 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 medium potatoes, diced small
  • 2 cups chopped kale, ribs removed
  • 1/2 tsp dried Italian seasoning
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Remove casings from turkey sausage if needed and break sausage into small pieces.
  2. Place a soup pot over medium heat. Add turkey sausage and cook 6–7 minutes, breaking it up with a spoon, until browned and cooked through. Drain any excess fat if necessary.
  3. Add diced onion and minced garlic (or garlic powder). Cook 3–4 minutes, stirring, until onion is softened.
  4. Stir in diced potatoes and kale. Add chicken broth, Italian seasoning, and black pepper.
  5. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer 12–15 minutes, until potatoes are tender and kale is soft.
  6. Whisk together milk and Greek yogurt in a bowl, then stir into the pot. Warm on low heat 3–4 minutes, stirring often, without boiling.
  7. Taste and adjust pepper as needed. Ladle into three bowls, giving Frank the largest portion of sausage and potatoes.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~610 kcal, ~46 g protein, ~50 g carbs, ~18 g fat.
  • Susan: ~460 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~37 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
  • Elise: ~460 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~37 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
