Shrimp, Spinach & Garlic Thin-Crust Pizza

Whole-wheat thin-crust pizza with garlic shrimp, spinach, and vegetables in a light olive-oil and yogurt drizzle for a seafood pizza dinner.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 12 minutes
  • Cook time: 16 minutes
  • Total time: 28 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 large rectangular whole-wheat thin pizza crust
  • 1 lb raw shrimp
  • 1 5 oz bag baby spinach
  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes
  • 1 bell pepper
  • 1 red onion
  • 3/4 cup 2% plain Greek yogurt
  • 2 tbsp water
  • 1 1/2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 tsp dried Italian seasoning
  • 1/4 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp kosher
  • 4 oz part-skim mozzarella cheese
  • 1 oz Parmesan cheese
  • Olive oil spray

Preparation Steps

  1. Measure out about 3 cup baby spinach for the recipe; refrigerate the remaining spinach. Chop the bell pepper and measure out 1 cup for the recipe. Refrigerate any remaining pepper. Dice the red onion and measure out 1/2 cup for the recipe. Refrigerate any remaining onion.
  2. Heat the oven to 425°F (about 220°C). Line a large sheet pan with parchment paper and lightly spray with olive oil.
  3. Rinse and dry the shrimp. Pat dry very well with paper towels. Toss the shrimp in a bowl with 1/2 tbsp olive oil, half of the minced garlic (or half of the garlic powder), a pinch of salt, and a pinch of pepper.
  4. Spread the shrimp in a single layer on a small parchment-lined pan. Bake at 425°F for 5–6 minutes until the shrimp are just pink and opaque. Remove and set aside. When cool enough to handle, roughly chop if large.
  5. Rinse and dry the spinach, grape tomatoes, bell peppers, and red onion (if using). Roughly chop the spinach, halve the tomatoes, and slice the peppers and onion.
  6. In a medium bowl, toss the spinach, tomatoes, peppers, and onion with 1 tsp olive oil, the remaining garlic, a pinch of salt, and pepper. Spread on a separate parchment-lined pan and pre-roast for 5–6 minutes until just beginning to soften.
  7. Make the yogurt drizzle: In a small bowl, whisk together the Greek yogurt, remaining 1 tbsp olive oil, 2 tbsp water, Italian seasoning, black pepper, and a pinch of salt until smooth and pourable.
  8. Place the whole-wheat pizza crust on the prepared main sheet pan. Sprinkle half of the shredded mozzarella over the bare crust.
  9. Scatter the chopped cooked shrimp evenly over the crust. Add the pre-roasted spinach and vegetables evenly on top of the shrimp.
  10. Drizzle about half of the yogurt mixture lightly over the toppings. Sprinkle the remaining mozzarella and the Parmesan evenly over the pizza.
  11. Bake at 425°F for 10–12 minutes, or until the crust is crisp, the cheese is melted, and the edges are lightly browned.
  12. Remove from the oven and let rest for 2–3 minutes. Drizzle a little more of the yogurt sauce over the top if desired (reserve any extra for dipping). Cut the pizza into 10 equal squares.
  13. Place 4 of the 10 squares on a plate for Frank, along with an extra spoonful of vegetables from the pan if needed to keep his vegetable volume high.
  14. Place 3 squares each on plates for Susan and Elise, distributing any remaining vegetables and yogurt drizzle evenly.
