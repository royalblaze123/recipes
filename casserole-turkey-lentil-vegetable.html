Turkey Lentil Vegetable Bake

NOTES: Built as a complete dinner using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled starch portions. Use low-sodium broth and minimal added salt to support blood pressure and kidney health. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of pan); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of pan); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of pan).

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup dry green or brown lentils, rinsed
  • 2 cups low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 lb 93% lean ground turkey
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) no-salt-added diced tomatoes, drained well
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Olive oil spray for baking dish

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Lightly spray an 8x11-inch baking dish with olive oil spray.
  2. In a saucepan, combine rinsed lentils and low-sodium broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat, cover, and simmer 20–25 minutes until lentils are tender but not falling apart. Drain any excess liquid.
  3. While lentils cook, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  4. Add diced onion, carrots, and celery and cook 5–6 minutes until softened.
  5. Stir in minced garlic and diced red bell pepper and cook 2–3 minutes more.
  6. Add ground turkey to the skillet, breaking it into crumbles, and cook 6–8 minutes until no pink remains.
  7. Stir in drained diced tomatoes, dried thyme, dried oregano, smoked paprika, salt, and black pepper. Simmer 3–4 minutes to blend flavors and evaporate excess liquid.
  8. In a large mixing bowl, combine cooked lentils with the turkey-vegetable mixture and stir until evenly mixed.
  9. Transfer mixture to the prepared baking dish and press gently into an even layer.
  10. Bake uncovered 20–25 minutes until the top is lightly crisped and edges are bubbling.
  11. Let casserole rest 5–10 minutes before cutting into portions for Frank, Susan, and Elise.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~540 kcal, ~46 g protein, ~52 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
  • Susan: ~405 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~39 g carbs, ~10 g fat.
  • Elise: ~405 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~39 g carbs, ~10 g fat.
