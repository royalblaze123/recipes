Post-Workout Recharge Electrolyte (3 Bottles)

NOTES: Use only on heavier sweat days (long treadmill sessions or hot days). Provides roughly double the sodium of your baseline electrolyte recipes while remaining within a heart-conscious range. Pair with your normal hydration plan and BP checks.

PORTION SIZES (each recipe makes 3 bottles): Frank: 1 full 20 oz bottle; Susan: 1 full 20 oz bottle; Elise: 1 full 20 oz bottle.

Ingredients

  • 60 fl oz cold filtered water (3 x 20 fl oz bottles)
  • 3/4 tsp fine sea salt (1/4 tsp per bottle)
  • 3 scoops magnesium glycinate powder (about 150–200 mg elemental Mg per scoop, divided)
  • 3 tbsp lemon or lime juice (1 tbsp per bottle, optional for flavor)
  • 12–18 drops liquid stevia or monk fruit sweetener (4–6 drops per bottle)
  • Ice cubes, optional, for serving

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Line up three 20 fl oz bottles. Add 1/4 teaspoon sea salt to each bottle.
  2. Divide the magnesium glycinate powder evenly across the three bottles.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon of lemon or lime juice to each bottle if using citrus.
  4. Add 4–6 drops of liquid stevia or monk fruit to each bottle.
  5. Fill each bottle with cold filtered water up to about 20 fl oz.
  6. Cap tightly, then shake each bottle for 20–30 seconds until the powders fully dissolve.
  7. Chill for at least 30 minutes or add ice before drinking.
  8. Use these bottles after your higher-intensity treadmill sessions, keeping your usual BP monitoring routine.

Nutrition per Person (1 bottle)

  • Frank: ~5–10 kcal, 0 g protein, 0 g fat, 1–2 g carbs, ~550–600 mg sodium, negligible potassium (from sea salt only).
  • Susan: ~5–10 kcal, 0 g protein, 0 g fat, 1–2 g carbs, ~550–600 mg sodium, negligible potassium.
  • Elise: ~5–10 kcal, 0 g protein, 0 g fat, 1–2 g carbs, ~550–600 mg sodium, negligible potassium.
