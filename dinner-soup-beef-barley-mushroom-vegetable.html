Dinner Soup Beef Barley Mushroom Vegetable

Chefy beef-and-barley soup with mushrooms and vegetables—deep flavor without heavy fats, built as a complete dinner.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 18 minutes
  • Cook time: 45 minutes
  • Total time: 63 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 2 lb sirloin steak
  • 1 carton low sodium beef broth
  • 1 each yellow onion
  • 1 bunch celery
  • 1 lb bag carrots
  • 8 oz package mushrooms
  • 6 cloves garlic
  • 1 bag pearl barley
  • 1 jar tomato paste
  • 1 bottle extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 jar dried thyme
  • 1 jar bay leaves
  • 1 jar black pepper
  • 1 jar Worcestershire sauce
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Prep beef: cut sirloin into 3/4-inch cubes.
    2. Prep veg: dice 1/2 the onion, slice 3 celery stalks, slice 2 carrots, slice the mushrooms, and mince 3 garlic cloves.
    3. Brown beef: heat a large pot over medium-high heat. Add 2 tsp olive oil, then add beef in a single layer. Cook 4 minutes, stirring once, until browned on the outside.
    4. Aromatics: add onion, celery, carrots, mushrooms, and garlic. Cook 6 minutes until vegetables soften.
    5. Season: stir in 1 tbsp tomato paste, 1 tsp dried thyme, 1 bay leaf, black pepper, and 2 tsp Worcestershire sauce.
    6. Add broth: add the carton of low sodium beef broth plus 3 cups water. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a steady simmer.
    7. Barley: rinse 1/2 cup dry pearl barley and add. Simmer 30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until barley is tender and soup thickens.
    8. Finish: remove bay leaf. Simmer 3 minutes, then serve.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – About 3 1/2 cups soup.


      Susan – About 2 2/3 cups soup.


      Elise – About 2 2/3 cups soup.


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Browning the beef first gives you a deeper, more chefy flavor without extra fat.


      Stir occasionally once barley is in so it doesn’t stick to the bottom.


      If it thickens too much overnight, add a splash of water when reheating.
