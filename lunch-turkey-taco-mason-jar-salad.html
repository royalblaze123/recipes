Turkey Taco Mason Jar Salad

NOTES: Designed as a portable grab-and-go lunch using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled smart carbs. Use low-sodium ingredients and avoid added sugars where possible. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total volume); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total volume); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total volume) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 12 oz 93% lean ground turkey
  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • 1/2 cup no-salt-added tomato sauce
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 can (15 oz) no-salt-added black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup frozen corn (no added salt), thawed and drained
  • 1 cup halved cherry tomatoes
  • 3 cups chopped romaine or iceberg lettuce
  • 1/2 cup shredded reduced-fat cheddar or Mexican blend cheese
  • 2 tbsp lime juice
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add ground turkey and cook 6–8 minutes, breaking it into crumbles, until no pink remains.
  2. Stir in tomato sauce, chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, and black pepper. Simmer 3–4 minutes until slightly thickened. Cool completely.
  3. Prepare jar layers: In three wide-mouth jars or containers, layer black beans, corn, turkey taco mixture, cherry tomatoes, shredded cheese, and chopped lettuce (lettuce on top).
  4. Squeeze lime juice over the top of each jar just before sealing.
  5. Refrigerate up to 3 days. At lunch, shake the jar to mix and eat straight from the jar or pour into a bowl.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~580 kcal, ~48 g protein, ~52 g carbs, ~18 g fat.
  • Susan: ~435 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~39 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
  • Elise: ~435 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~39 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
