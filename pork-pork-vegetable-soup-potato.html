Hearty Pork & Vegetable Soup with Potatoes

NOTES: Brothy pork soup with potatoes, carrots, celery, and green beans. This is a full-plate pork dinner built around lean pork tenderloin/loin or 93% lean ground pork, smart carbs, and plenty of non-starchy vegetables. Uses olive or avocado oil only, keeps sodium moderate, and avoids added sugars to support A1c, cholesterol, blood pressure, and PPPD-friendly eating. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

PORTION SIZES (per person): Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 1 pound lean pork loin or shoulder, trimmed and cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 pound Yukon Gold potatoes, diced
  • 2 medium carrots, sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup green beans, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 6 cups low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried parsley
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large soup pot over medium-high heat. Add pork cubes and sear 4–5 minutes until browned on most sides.
  2. Add onion, carrots, and celery and cook 3–4 minutes until slightly softened.
  3. Stir in garlic, thyme, parsley, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper and cook 1 minute.
  4. Add potatoes, green beans, and broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer.
  5. Cover and cook 25–30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until pork is tender and potatoes are cooked through.
  6. Taste and adjust seasoning with a pinch of salt or extra herbs if needed, keeping overall sodium moderate.
  7. Divide the finished meal into three plates or containers: give Frank roughly 1/3 of the total volume, and split the remaining 2/3 between Susan and Elise as slightly smaller but still generous portions.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~500 kcal, ~46 g protein, ~34 g carbs, ~16 g fat.
  • Susan: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
  • Elise: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
