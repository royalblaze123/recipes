Lean Beef & Broccoli Brown Rice Skillet (Frank Household – Occasional)

Stir-fry style lean beef and broccoli over brown rice with a light, lower-sodium sauce for an occasional full-flavor dinner.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 10 minutes
  • Cook time: 18 minutes
  • Total time: 28 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 cup cooked brown rice
  • 14 oz raw lean beef sirloin
  • 3 cups small broccoli florets
  • 1 cup sliced bell peppers
  • 1/2 cup sliced onion
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1/4 cup low-sodium beef or chicken broth
  • 1 1/2 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce or coconut aminos
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar or mild vinegar
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp ground ginger
  • 1/4 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp kosher or sea salt
  • 1 tsp cornstarch mixed with 1 tbsp water

Preparation Steps

  1. Cook the brown rice if not already prepared. Use leftover brown rice, cook 1/3 cup dry brown rice according to package directions, or heat a microwave-ready pouch. You need about 1 cup cooked rice total. Set aside.
  2. Trim any visible fat from the lean beef sirloin. Slice thinly against the grain into bite-size strips so they cook quickly.
  3. Rinse and dry the broccoli, bell peppers, and onion. Cut the broccoli into small florets, slice the bell peppers, and slice the onion if using.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the broth, low-sodium soy sauce or coconut aminos, rice vinegar, garlic powder, ground ginger, black pepper, and salt. In a separate small cup, stir the cornstarch with 1 tbsp water to make a slurry.
  5. Heat a large nonstick skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the olive oil. When hot, add the sliced beef in a single layer if possible. Stir-fry for 3–4 minutes until browned on the outside and just cooked through. Transfer the beef to a plate and set aside.
  6. Add the broccoli, bell peppers, and onion to the skillet. Stir-fry over medium-high heat for 4–5 minutes until the vegetables are crisp-tender. If needed, add a tablespoon of water to help them steam.
  7. Stir the sauce once more and pour it into the skillet with the vegetables. Bring to a gentle simmer. Stir the cornstarch slurry again and drizzle it into the skillet while stirring. Cook for 1–2 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly.
  8. Return the cooked beef and any juices to the skillet. Toss everything together for 1–2 minutes over medium heat until heated through and coated with sauce. Turn off the heat.
  9. Add 1/2 cup cooked brown rice to a large bowl or plate for Frank. Top with about 2 to 2 1/4 cups of the beef and broccoli mixture, making sure his portion includes roughly 5 oz beef and about 1 1/2 cups vegetables with sauce.
  10. Divide the remaining rice between two bowls or plates for Susan and Elise, about 1/3 cup each. Top each with about 1 1/2 to 1 3/4 cups of the beef and broccoli mixture, ensuring each has about 3 3/4 oz beef and about 1 cup vegetables.

Portion Sizes (Per Person, Approximate)

  • Frank (F · 1.0×): ~2 to 2 1/4 cups beef and broccoli mixture over 1/2 cup cooked brown rice with ~5 oz beef, ~1 1/2 cups vegetables, and 2–3 tbsp sauce.
  • Susan (S · 0.75×): ~1 1/2 to 1 3/4 cups mixture over 1/3 cup rice with ~3 3/4 oz beef, ~1 cup vegetables, and ~2 tbsp sauce.
  • Elise (E · 0.75×): ~1 1/2 to 1 3/4 cups mixture over 1/3 cup rice with ~3 3/4 oz beef, ~1 cup vegetables, and ~2 tbsp sauce.
