Chophouse Lite: Pork Tenderloin with Broccoli + Carrots + Quinoa (Lemon Dijon Pepper Drizzle)

Servings

3 plates (Frank 1.0×, Susan 0.75×, Elise 0.75×).

Times

  • Prep time: 18 minutes
  • Cook time: 22 minutes
  • Total time: 40 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2 pork tenderloins
  • 1.0 cup dry quinoa
  • 1 bag frozen broccoli florets
  • 1 lb carrots
  • 9 tsp olive oil
  • 2 tsp black pepper
  • 2 tsp salt substitute
  • 2 tsp dried parsley
  • 6 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 6 tsp lemon juice
  • 2 tsp black pepper

Steps

  1. Base: Rinse 1 cup dry quinoa. Cook with 2 cups water to yield ~3 cups cooked. Fluff and keep warm.
  2. Vegetables: Preheat oven to 425°F. Roast vegetables until browned-tender. Add parsley at the end.
  3. Pork: Slice tenderloin into medallions. Season with black pepper + salt substitute.
  4. Sear: Hot skillet + 2 tsp olive oil. Sear 2–3 minutes per side until browned; cook to 145°F; rest 3 minutes.
  5. Finish: Bright, clean. Use after slicing so it reads ‘steakhouse’ not ‘boiled.’
  6. Plate: Base → vegetables → sliced protein → finish drizzle. Portions per Notes.

Notes

Portions (per person):

Frank – 1 plate (8 oz cooked pork, 1 cup cooked quinoa, 2 cups vegetables, 2–3 tbsp finish baste).

Susan – 1 plate (6 oz cooked pork, 3/4 of Frank’s base portion, 1 1/2 cups vegetables, 2 tbsp finish baste).

Elise – 1 plate (6 oz cooked pork, 3/4 of Frank’s base portion, 1 1/2 cups vegetables, 2 tbsp finish baste).

Tips (recipe-specific):

Pat the steak dry. Steam is not a flavor profile.

Slice across the grain every time. Chewing should be optional.

Storage + Reheat

  • Cool promptly; store steak + vegetables + base in airtight containers in the fridge up to 3–4 days (typical safety guidance).
  • Reheat steak gently (microwave 45–60 sec or skillet 1–2 min) to avoid overcooking; reheat vegetables 4–6 minutes at 350°F in the air fryer for texture; add finishing drizzle after reheating.
