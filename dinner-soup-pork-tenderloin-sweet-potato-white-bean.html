Dinner Soup Pork Tenderloin Sweet Potato White Bean

Herby pork tenderloin soup with sweet potato and white beans—comforting, chefy, and engineered as a full dinner.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 18 minutes
  • Cook time: 35 minutes
  • Total time: 53 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 2 lb pork tenderloin
  • 1 carton low sodium chicken broth
  • 2 each sweet potatoes
  • 1 each yellow onion
  • 1 bunch celery
  • 1 lb bag carrots
  • 6 cloves garlic
  • 1 can cannellini beans
  • 1 bottle extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 jar dried rosemary
  • 1 jar dried thyme
  • 1 jar black pepper
  • 1 each lemon
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Prep pork: cut pork tenderloin into 1-inch chunks.
    2. Prep veg: dice 1/2 the onion, slice 2 celery stalks, slice 2 carrots, and mince 3 garlic cloves.
    3. Prep sweet potato: peel and cube 1 sweet potato (measure out about 2 1/2 cups cubes; refrigerate remaining sweet potato).
    4. Start base: heat a large pot over medium heat. Add 2 tsp olive oil, then add onion, celery, carrots, and garlic. Cook 6 minutes until softened.
    5. Season: stir in 1 tsp dried rosemary, 1 tsp dried thyme, and black pepper.
    6. Add broth + pork: add the carton of low sodium chicken broth plus 2 cups water. Add pork chunks. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer and cook 12 minutes.
    7. Add sweet potato: add sweet potato cubes and simmer 15 minutes until tender.
    8. Beans: drain and rinse cannellini beans. Add beans and simmer 5 minutes.
    9. Finish: squeeze in 1 tsp lemon juice and stir. Serve hot.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – About 3 1/2 cups soup.


      Susan – About 2 2/3 cups soup.


      Elise – About 2 2/3 cups soup.


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Add sweet potato after the pork has simmered so both finish tender without overcooking.


      Keep lemon minimal—just enough to brighten without a citrus-heavy dinner.


      If the soup needs more body, mash a few sweet potato cubes and stir back in.
