Turkey Pho-Inspired Noodle Soup (Stovetop, Light)

NOTES: Designed as a complete meal soup using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled starch. Use low-sodium broth and avoid extra salt when possible to support blood pressure and kidney health. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~2 cups); Susan: medium portion (~1.5 cups); Elise: medium portion (~1.5 cups).

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, thinly sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, sliced
  • 2 whole star anise
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 4 whole cloves (optional)
  • 6 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 lb thinly sliced turkey breast or turkey cutlets
  • 3 oz brown rice noodles (thin)
  • 1 tbsp low-sodium tamari or soy sauce
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Fresh cilantro, basil, and green onions for garnish (optional)
  • Bean sprouts and extra lime wedges for serving (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large soup pot over medium heat.
  2. Add sliced onion and cook 5–6 minutes until softened and lightly browned at the edges.
  3. Stir in minced garlic and sliced ginger and cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Add star anise, cinnamon stick, and cloves (if using) and stir for 30 seconds to bloom the spices.
  5. Pour in low-sodium chicken broth and water and stir.
  6. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer 20 minutes to infuse the broth with flavor.
  7. Strain out the whole spices (star anise, cinnamon stick, cloves) using a slotted spoon or mesh strainer and discard.
  8. Add thinly sliced turkey to the hot broth and simmer 5–7 minutes until just cooked through.
  9. Add brown rice noodles and cook according to package directions, usually 3–5 minutes, until tender but not mushy.
  10. Stir in low-sodium tamari or soy sauce and lime juice and taste, adjusting with a bit more lime or tamari if needed.
  11. Remove from heat and let soup sit 2–3 minutes.
  12. Ladle into bowls for Frank, Susan, and Elise and top with fresh cilantro, basil, green onions, and bean sprouts if desired.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~480 kcal, ~42 g protein, ~48 g carbs, ~11 g fat.
  • Susan: ~360 kcal, ~31 g protein, ~36 g carbs, ~8 g fat.
  • Elise: ~360 kcal, ~31 g protein, ~36 g carbs, ~8 g fat.
