Sweet Potato Oat Pancake Bites (Freezer Batch)

Mini pancake bites made with oats and sweet potato for slow carbs that pair cleanly with your shake.

Servings

24 bites (freezer batch).

Times

  • Prep time: 15 minutes
  • Cook time: 15 minutes
  • Total time: 30 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2 each sweet potatoes
  • 1 1/2 cups old-fashioned oats
  • 3/8 carton egg whites
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 2 tsp ground cinnamon

Preparation Steps

  1. Sweet potatoes: Pierce 2 sweet potatoes and microwave until very tender. Cool slightly, then scoop the flesh into a bowl.
  2. Batter: Measure 1 1/2 cups old-fashioned oats. Measure 1 1/2 cups (12 fl oz) egg whites (this is 3/8 of a 32 fl oz carton). Add 2 tsp baking powder and 2 tsp ground cinnamon. Stir until a thick batter forms.
  3. Cook: Heat a nonstick skillet over medium heat. Cook tablespoon-size pancake bites 1–2 minutes per side until set.
  4. Freeze: Cool completely. Freeze on a tray 1 hour, then store in a freezer bag for up to 1 month.
  5. Breakfast use: Grab 6 bites and eat with your morning shake (they thaw quickly).

Notes

Mini pancake bites made with oats and sweet potato for slow carbs that pair cleanly with your shake.

Portions (per person):

Frank – 6 bites with your morning shake (slow-carb add-on).

Susan – Optional: 4 bites with breakfast if desired.

Elise – Optional: 4 bites with breakfast if desired.

Tips (recipe-specific):

If you want faster mornings, move a 2–3 day supply to the fridge.

If you want more protein, eat with a couple spoonfuls of nonfat Greek yogurt.

For a tighter carb load, use 5 bites instead of 6.
