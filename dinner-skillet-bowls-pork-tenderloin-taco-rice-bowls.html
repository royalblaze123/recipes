Pork Tenderloin Taco Rice Bowls

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 635 kcal • P 58g • C 55g • F 18g • Sat 6g • Na 670mg • Fiber 12g
Susan/Elise (each): 476 kcal • P 44g • C 41g • F 14g • Sat 4g • Na 502mg • Fiber 9g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.75 lb pork tenderloin
  • 1 bag frozen fajita-style pepper blend
  • 1 can black beans
  • 1 cup dry brown rice
  • 1 each avocado
  • 1/2 jar salsa
  • 1 each lime
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • chili powder
  • ground cumin
  • garlic powder
  • smoked paprika
  • extra-virgin olive oil

Steps

  1. Rice: cook 1 cup dry brown rice with 2 cups water (35–40 minutes), rest 5 minutes, fluff.
  2. Pork: slice tenderloin into 1/2-inch medallions. Cook in skillet with 2 tsp olive oil 3–4 minutes per side to 145°F. Rest 3 minutes, then chop.
  3. Season pork with 2 tsp chili powder, 2 tsp cumin, 2 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper.
  4. Add frozen fajita pepper blend; cook 6–8 minutes until browned.
  5. Beans: drain/rinse one 15 oz can black beans. Add 1 1/2 cups beans; warm 2 minutes.
  6. Finish with 2 tsp lime juice.
  7. Bowls: divide rice + pork mix; top with salsa and avocado. Portion per Notes.

Notes

Juicy pork + peppers skillet bowls with a measured rice base. Portions (per person): Frank: 1/3 rice + 1/3 skillet mix + 1/3 avocado + salsa Susan: 1/4 rice + 1/4 skillet mix + 1/4 avocado + salsa Elise: 1/4 rice + 1/4 skillet mix + 1/4 avocado + salsa Tips (recipe-specific): Rest pork before chopping to keep it juicy. If reheating, add salsa after microwaving to keep flavors bright.
