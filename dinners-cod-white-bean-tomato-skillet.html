Cod & White Bean Tomato Skillet

Fast skillet cod with white beans, tomatoes, and spinach over a small portion of brown rice for a light but fully satisfying dinner.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 10 minutes
  • Cook time: 18 minutes
  • Total time: 28 minutes

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup cooked brown rice
  • 18 oz raw cod fillets or other white fish
  • 3/4 cup canned white beans
  • 1 1/4 cups canned no-salt-added diced tomatoes
  • 3 cups lightly packed fresh baby spinach
  • 1/2 cup chopped onion
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1/4 cup low-sodium chicken broth or vegetable broth
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp dried Italian seasoning
  • 1/4 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp kosher or sea salt

Preparation Steps

  1. Cook the brown rice if not already prepared. Use leftover brown rice, cook 1/4 cup dry brown rice according to package directions, or heat a microwave-ready pouch. You need about 3/4 cup cooked rice total. Set aside and keep warm.
  2. Pat the cod fillets dry with a paper towel. Cut into large chunks or leave as smaller fillets if they fit in your skillet. Check for and remove any pin bones.
  3. Rinse and dry the spinach. Chop the onion if using. Drain and rinse the white beans. Measure the diced tomatoes with their juices.
  4. Heat a large nonstick skillet with a lid over medium heat. Add the olive oil. Add the onion (if using) and cook for 2–3 minutes until it softens slightly.
  5. Add the diced tomatoes with their juices, broth, lemon juice, garlic powder, Italian seasoning, black pepper, and salt to the skillet. Stir and bring to a gentle simmer.
  6. Nestle the cod pieces into the tomato mixture in a single layer as much as possible. Spoon some of the tomato mixture over the top of the fish. Cover the skillet with a lid and simmer over medium-low heat for 6–8 minutes, or until the cod flakes easily with a fork (145°F internal).
  7. Gently remove the cooked cod pieces to a plate and keep warm. Stir the drained white beans and the baby spinach into the tomato mixture. Cook uncovered over medium heat for 2–3 minutes, stirring, until the beans are warm and the spinach is wilted.
  8. Taste the tomato and bean mixture and adjust with a squeeze more lemon or a pinch of salt or pepper if needed, keeping acidity and sodium modest.
  9. Add about 1/3 cup cooked brown rice to a large bowl or plate for Frank. Top with about 2 to 2 1/4 cups of the tomato, bean, and spinach mixture and about 6 oz of the cod pieces.
  10. Divide the remaining rice between two bowls or plates for Susan and Elise, about 1/4 cup each. Top each with about 1 1/2 to 1 3/4 cups of the tomato-bean-spinach mixture and about 4 1/2 oz cod.

Portion Sizes (Per Person, Approximate)

  • Frank (F · 1.0×): ~2 to 2 1/4 cups tomato, bean, and spinach mixture over 1/3 cup cooked brown rice with ~6 oz cod and ~1 1/2 cups vegetables.
  • Susan (S · 0.75×): ~1 1/2 to 1 3/4 cups mixture over 1/4 cup rice with ~4 1/2 oz cod and ~1 cup vegetables.
  • Elise (E · 0.75×): ~1 1/2 to 1 3/4 cups mixture over 1/4 cup rice with ~4 1/2 oz cod and ~1 cup vegetables.

Nutrition (Approximate, Per Person)

  • Frank | Susan & Elise: 595 | 445 kcal; 56 | 42 g protein; 32 | 24 g carbs; 17 | 13 g fat; 6 | 5 g fiber; 540 | 405 mg sodium.
