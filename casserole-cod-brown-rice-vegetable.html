Cod Brown Rice Vegetable Bake

NOTES: Built as a complete dinner using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled starch portions. Use low-sodium broth and minimal added salt to support blood pressure and kidney health. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of pan); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of pan); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of pan).

Ingredients

  • 1 cup uncooked brown rice, rinsed
  • 2 cups low-sodium vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 1/4 lb cod fillets, cut into large chunks
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 2 cups small broccoli florets
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 lemon, zested and juiced
  • 1 tsp dried dill or 2 tbsp fresh dill, chopped
  • 1 tsp dried parsley
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Olive oil spray for baking dish

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Lightly spray a 9x13-inch baking dish with olive oil spray.
  2. In a saucepan, combine rinsed brown rice and low-sodium broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat, cover, and simmer 25–30 minutes until rice is just tender. Remove from heat and fluff with a fork.
  3. While rice cooks, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  4. Add diced onion, carrots, and red bell pepper and cook 5–6 minutes until vegetables begin to soften.
  5. Stir in minced garlic and cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
  6. Steam or microwave broccoli florets 2–3 minutes until bright green and crisp-tender; drain well.
  7. In a large mixing bowl, combine cooked brown rice, sautéed vegetables, broccoli florets, lemon zest, lemon juice, dried dill, dried parsley, salt, and black pepper. Stir to combine.
  8. Gently fold cod chunks into the rice and vegetable mixture, keeping pieces fairly large so they do not break apart.
  9. Transfer mixture to the prepared baking dish and spread into an even layer.
  10. Cover with foil and bake 18–22 minutes until cod flakes easily with a fork and internal temperature reaches 145°F.
  11. Remove foil and bake an additional 5 minutes to let excess moisture evaporate if needed.
  12. Let casserole rest 5–10 minutes, then portion for Frank, Susan, and Elise.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~560 kcal, ~48 g protein, ~55 g carbs, ~15 g fat.
  • Susan: ~420 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~41 g carbs, ~11 g fat.
  • Elise: ~420 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~41 g carbs, ~11 g fat.
