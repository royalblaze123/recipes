Turkey Lasagna with Carrot-Ginger Tomato Sauce

An Italian-Asian fusion twist with a carrot-ginger infused tomato sauce, keeping flavors bright but gentle for PPPD and GERD.

PORTION SIZES (3 servings total): Frank = 1.0× (largest square), Susan = 0.75×, Elise = 0.75×. Cut the baked lasagna into 4 equal squares, serve 2 squares to Frank and 1 square each to Susan and Elise.

Ingredients

  • 8 whole-wheat lasagna noodles (dry)
  • 1 lb lean ground turkey (93% lean or better)
  • 1 small yellow onion, finely diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup finely grated carrot
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, finely grated
  • 2 cups no-salt-added crushed tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp kosher salt (optional, to taste)
  • 1 tsp rice vinegar
  • 1 cup part-skim ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup part-skim shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 2 tbsp grated reduced-fat Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped

Step-by-step Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Lightly coat a 9×9-inch baking dish with nonstick spray.
  2. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Cook the whole-wheat lasagna noodles until just shy of al dente, about 7–8 minutes. Drain and lay flat on parchment to prevent sticking.
  3. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the diced onion and sauté 4–5 minutes until softened.
  4. Add the minced garlic, grated carrot, and grated ginger. Cook 2–3 minutes, stirring often, until the carrot softens slightly and the mixture is fragrant.
  5. Add the lean ground turkey and cook 6–8 minutes, breaking it into small crumbles, until browned and cooked through.
  6. Stir in the dried oregano, dried basil, black pepper, and optional salt. Toast the herbs with the turkey and vegetables for 1–2 minutes.
  7. Pour in the crushed tomatoes and chicken broth. Stir well and bring to a gentle simmer.
  8. Simmer the sauce for 10–12 minutes, stirring occasionally, until slightly thickened. Stir in the rice vinegar and remove from the heat.
  9. Spread a thin layer of the carrot-ginger turkey sauce on the bottom of the prepared baking dish.
  10. Layer lasagna noodles over the sauce. Add dollops of ricotta, followed by more turkey sauce.
  11. Repeat the layers (noodles, ricotta, sauce) until all components are used, finishing with sauce on top.
  12. Sprinkle the shredded mozzarella and grated Parmesan evenly over the top.
  13. Cover the dish with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake an additional 10–15 minutes until bubbling and lightly golden.
  14. Rest the lasagna for 10–15 minutes, then sprinkle with chopped fresh parsley before serving.
  15. Cut into 4 equal squares. Serve 2 squares to Frank and 1 square each to Susan and Elise.

Nutrition (per person)

Frank (largest portion): approx 525 kcal, 47 g protein, 44 g carbs, 18 g fat.
Susan (medium portion): approx 395 kcal, 35 g protein, 33 g carbs, 14 g fat.
Elise (medium portion): approx 395 kcal, 35 g protein, 33 g carbs, 14 g fat.
