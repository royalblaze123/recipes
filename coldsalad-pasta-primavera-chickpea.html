Cold Chickpea Pasta Primavera Lunch Salad

NOTES: Cold casserole / pasta salad style lunch built around lean protein, whole grains or beans, and plenty of vegetables. Dressings are olive-oil and/or yogurt based, with no heavy cream and no added sugar beyond tiny optional amounts. Everything is designed to be packed in containers for portable work lunches while supporting A1c, cholesterol, blood pressure, and kidney-stone goals.

PORTION SIZES (serves 3 for lunch): Divide the finished salad into three containers. Frank receives the largest portion (about 1/3 of the total volume). Susan and Elise each receive slightly smaller portions (about 1/4 of the total volume each). Frank = 1.0×; Susan = 0.75×; Elise = 0.75× portions.

Ingredients

  • 8 oz dry whole-wheat fusilli pasta
  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup halved grape tomatoes
  • 1 cup diced cucumber
  • 1/2 cup diced yellow bell pepper
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh basil or parsley
  • 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 3 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 tsp dried Italian seasoning
  • 1/4 tsp kosher salt, or to taste
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Cook 8 oz dry whole-wheat fusilli pasta in a pot of boiling water (if pasta or grain) until just tender (al dente for pasta or per grain directions). Drain well, rinse under cool water to stop cooking, and spread on a tray or in a shallow bowl to cool completely.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooled base (if used) with 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed, 1 cup halved grape tomatoes, 1 cup diced cucumber, 1/2 cup diced yellow bell pepper, and 1/4 cup chopped fresh basil or parsley. Gently toss so the protein and vegetables are evenly distributed.
  3. In a separate small bowl or jar with a lid, whisk together 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil, 3 tbsp lemon juice, 1 tsp Dijon mustard, 1/2 tsp dried Italian seasoning, 1/4 tsp kosher salt, or to taste, and 1/4 tsp black pepper until the dressing is smooth and emulsified.
  4. Pour the dressing over the salad and toss gently until everything is lightly and evenly coated. Taste and adjust seasoning with a small pinch of extra salt, pepper, or acid if needed.
  5. Cover the bowl and refrigerate the salad for at least 30–60 minutes so the flavors marry. Then divide into three lunch containers, giving Frank the largest portion and Susan and Elise slightly smaller portions. Keep refrigerated until ready to eat.

Nutrition (approximate per person)

Frank (largest portion): ~450–600 kcal, 35–45 g protein, 45–60 g carbs, 12–20 g fat
Susan (medium portion): ~340–450 kcal, 25–35 g protein, 34–45 g carbs, 9–15 g fat
Elise (medium portion): ~340–450 kcal, 25–35 g protein, 34–45 g carbs, 9–15 g fat
