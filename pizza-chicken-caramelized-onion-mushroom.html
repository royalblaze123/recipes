Chicken Caramelized Onion and Mushroom Pizza

NOTES: Built as a full dinner using ready-made whole-grain or thin crust, lean protein, plenty of vegetables, and controlled cheese to support A1c, cholesterol, and blood pressure goals. Use low-sodium sauce and minimal added salt. PORTION SIZES: Frank: ~40% of the pizza; Susan: ~30% of the pizza; Elise: ~30% of the pizza (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise = 0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 1 ready-made 12-inch thin whole-wheat pizza crust
  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • 1 large yellow onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup plain 2% Greek yogurt
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 8 oz cooked boneless, skinless chicken breast, chopped
  • 1/2 cup shredded reduced-fat mozzarella cheese
  • 2 tbsp grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F and place the crust on a pizza pan or parchment-lined baking sheet.
  2. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium-low heat. Add sliced onion and cook 10–12 minutes, stirring occasionally, until deeply softened and lightly caramelized.
  3. Add sliced mushrooms to the skillet and cook 3–4 minutes more until tender. Remove from heat.
  4. In a small bowl, mix Greek yogurt and minced garlic to create a white sauce.
  5. Spread yogurt sauce evenly over the crust.
  6. Distribute chopped chicken, caramelized onions, and mushrooms evenly over the sauce.
  7. Sprinkle shredded mozzarella, grated Parmesan, dried thyme, and black pepper over the toppings.
  8. Bake 12–15 minutes, until the crust is crisp and the cheese is melted and lightly browned.
  9. Cool 3–5 minutes before slicing and portioning for Frank (~40%) and Susan/Elise (~30% each).

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~610 kcal, ~53 g protein, ~50 g carbs, ~18 g fat.
  • Susan: ~455 kcal, ~40 g protein, ~37 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
  • Elise: ~455 kcal, ~40 g protein, ~37 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
