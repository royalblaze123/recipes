Katsu-Style Chicken Cutlets + Cabbage Slaw + Rice

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 650 kcal • P 62g • C 60g • F 16g • Sat 3g • Na 700mg • Fiber 10g
Susan/Elise (each): 488 kcal • P 46g • C 45g • F 12g • Sat 2g • Na 525mg • Fiber 8g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.75 lb chicken breast cutlets
  • 1 carton egg whites
  • 1 package whole-wheat panko breadcrumbs
  • 1 bag shredded cabbage
  • rice vinegar
  • low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1 cup dry brown rice
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • garlic powder
  • onion powder
  • smoked paprika
  • chili powder
  • ground cumin
  • Italian seasoning
  • crushed red pepper
  • cornstarch
  • low-sodium soy sauce
  • rice vinegar
  • hot sauce
  • Dijon mustard
  • extra-virgin olive oil
  • sesame seeds

Steps

  1. Rice: cook 1 cup dry brown rice with 2 cups water 35–40 minutes. Rest 5 minutes, fluff.
  2. Air fryer: preheat to 400°F.
  3. Breading: Bowl 1: 1/2 cup egg whites. Bowl 2: 1 1/2 cups panko + 1 tsp garlic powder + 1 tsp onion powder + 1/2 tsp black pepper.
  4. Coat cutlets: press into panko firmly. Lightly mist with oil if desired.
  5. Cook: Basket A, 400°F for 10–12 minutes; flip at shake prompt; cook to 165°F.
  6. Slaw: toss shredded cabbage with 2 tbsp rice vinegar + 1 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce + black pepper + 1 tsp sesame seeds.
  7. Serve: cutlets + measured rice + slaw per Notes.

Notes

Katsu comfort with crisp panko and a bright slaw (no mayo). Portions (per person): Frank: 2 cutlets + 1 cup cooked rice + 1 cup slaw Susan: 1 cutlet + 3/4 cup cooked rice + 3/4 cup slaw Elise: 1 cutlet + 3/4 cup cooked rice + 3/4 cup slaw Tips (recipe-specific): Press panko hard so it stays on in the air fryer. Acid slaw keeps the plate feeling ‘restaurant’ without heavy sauces.
