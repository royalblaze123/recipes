Slow Cooker Chicken Sweet Potato Black Bean Chili

Chunky chicken chili with sweet potato for slow carbs and black beans for fiber—built to stay hearty, not mushy.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 390 minutes
  • Total time: 410 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 2 lb boneless skinless chicken breast
  • 2 each sweet potatoes
  • 1 can no salt added diced tomatoes
  • 1 carton low sodium chicken broth
  • 1 each yellow onion
  • 1 each bell pepper
  • 2 cans black beans
  • 1 bottle extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 jar chili powder
  • 1 jar ground cumin
  • 1 jar smoked paprika
  • 1 jar garlic powder
  • 1 jar black pepper
  • 1 container nonfat Greek yogurt
  • 1 each lime
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Prep: peel sweet potatoes and cut into 3/4-inch chunks (larger chunks hold texture better). Dice 1/2 onion and 1/2 bell pepper; store the rest for another recipe.
    2. Slow cooker base: add chicken, sweet potato chunks, diced tomatoes, and 1 cup low sodium broth. Stir in chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, garlic powder, and black pepper.
    3. Cook: LOW 6–7 hours (or HIGH 3–4 hours) until chicken is tender and sweet potatoes are soft but still holding shape.
    4. Shred chicken: remove chicken, shred, then return to the slow cooker.
    5. Beans late: drain and rinse both cans of black beans. Stir 2 cups beans into the chili for the final 20 minutes on LOW (keeps beans intact). Refrigerate remaining beans.
    6. Finish: squeeze lime juice into the pot. Let sit 5 minutes with lid off to slightly thicken.
    7. Serve: top each bowl with a small dollop of nonfat Greek yogurt and use the 40/30/30 split.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – About 2 1/2 cups chili with a small yogurt dollop (40% of the full pot).


      Susan – About 2 cups chili with a small yogurt dollop (30% of the full pot).


      Elise – About 2 cups chili with a small yogurt dollop (30% of the full pot).


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Cut sweet potatoes larger so they don’t dissolve into puree.


      Add beans near the end so they stay firm and the chili stays chunky.


      If you want more vegetable volume, stir in a handful of spinach at serving.
