Chicken Mediterranean Orzo Salad (Whole-Wheat)

NOTES: Designed as a portable grab-and-go lunch using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled smart carbs. Use low-sodium ingredients and avoid added sugars where possible. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total volume); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total volume); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total volume) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 8 oz dry whole-wheat orzo
  • 12 oz cooked boneless, skinless chicken breast, chopped
  • 1 1/2 cups chopped cucumber (seeds removed)
  • 1 cup halved cherry tomatoes
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1/3 cup crumbled reduced-fat feta cheese
  • 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 3 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Cook whole-wheat orzo according to package directions in lightly salted water until al dente. Drain, rinse under cool water, and drain well.
  2. In a large bowl, combine cooled orzo, chopped chicken, cucumber, cherry tomatoes, and bell pepper.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, dried oregano, and black pepper.
  4. Pour dressing over the salad and toss to coat evenly.
  5. Gently fold in crumbled feta cheese.
  6. Divide salad into three containers for grab-and-go, making Frank’s portion largest.
  7. Refrigerate up to 3 days. Serve chilled.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~590 kcal, ~50 g protein, ~60 g carbs, ~17 g fat.
  • Susan: ~445 kcal, ~38 g protein, ~45 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
  • Elise: ~445 kcal, ~38 g protein, ~45 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
