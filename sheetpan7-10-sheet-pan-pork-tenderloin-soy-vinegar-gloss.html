Sheet-Pan Pork Tenderloin: Green Beans + Mushrooms + Brown Rice with Soy Vinegar Gloss

Servings

3 plates (Frank 1.0×, Susan 0.75×, Elise 0.75×).

Times

  • Prep time: 18 minutes
  • Cook time: 27 minutes
  • Total time: 45 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2 pork tenderloins
  • 1.0 cup dry brown rice
  • 1 bag frozen green beans
  • 24 oz mushrooms
  • 9 tsp olive oil
  • 4 tsp minced garlic
  • 2 tsp black pepper
  • 2 tsp salt substitute
  • 2 tsp dried parsley
  • 7 tsp low-sodium soy sauce
  • 4 tsp vinegar
  • 2 tsp black pepper

Steps

  1. Base: Cook 1 cup dry brown rice to yield ~3 cups cooked. Keep warm.
  2. Oven: Heat to 425°F. Line a large sheet pan with parchment or foil for faster cleanup.
  3. Vegetables (Green Beans + Mushrooms): Toss with 4 tsp olive oil + pepper + salt substitute. Roast at 425°F on a sheet pan. Start tougher veg first (carrots/cabbage) 10 minutes; add quicker veg (broccoli/asparagus/green beans/mushrooms) later so everything stays browned-tender.
  4. Aromatics: Add minced garlic during the last 2 minutes of roasting (or it burns and turns bitter).
  5. Protein: Slice into 1-inch strips (or medallions for pork). Toss with 3 tsp olive oil + pepper + salt substitute. Roast at 425°F: chicken/turkey 14–18 min; pork 16–20 min; lean beef 10–14 min. Rest 3 minutes.
  6. Finish sauce: Whisk finishing sauce ingredients. Brush on in the last 2 minutes of roasting for a light lacquer.
  7. Plate: Base first, then vegetables, then protein. Drizzle finishing sauce. Add parsley + black pepper last.
  8. Portions per Notes.

Notes

Portions (per person):

Frank – 1 plate (8 oz roasted protein, 1 cup cooked brown rice, 2 cups roasted vegetables, 2–3 tbsp finish sauce).

Susan – 1 plate (6 oz roasted protein, 3/4 of Frank’s base portion, 1 1/2 cups roasted vegetables, 2 tbsp finish sauce).

Elise – 1 plate (6 oz roasted protein, 3/4 of Frank’s base portion, 1 1/2 cups roasted vegetables, 2 tbsp finish sauce).

Tips (recipe-specific):

Stagger timing: tough veg first, quick veg later. If everything goes in at once, one thing will be mush and one thing will be raw.

Finish sauce goes on after roasting (or last 2 minutes max). That’s how you keep it ‘gourmet’ instead of ‘cafeteria’.

Storage + Reheat

  • Cool promptly; store in airtight containers in the fridge up to 3–4 days (typical safety guidance).
  • Reheat protein + veg at 350°F in the air fryer 4–6 minutes for texture; reheat base in microwave 60–90 seconds; add finishing sauce after reheating.
