Striped Bass Brown Rice Chowder

NOTES: This chowder is designed as a full meal in a bowl with lean protein, smart carbs, and 1–2 cups of vegetables per person. Use low-sodium broth and ingredients, avoid added sugars, and keep portions aligned with your goals. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total pot); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 12 oz striped bass fillet, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1/2 cup uncooked brown rice, rinsed
  • 3 cups low-sodium vegetable or seafood broth
  • 3/4 cup 2% milk or unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and diced
  • 1 cup cut green beans, fresh or frozen
  • 1/2 tsp dried Italian seasoning
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Cook brown rice separately: combine rinsed rice with 1 cup water in a small saucepan, bring to a boil, then reduce heat, cover, and simmer 25–30 minutes until tender. Set aside.
  2. Heat olive oil in a soup pot over medium heat for 1–2 minutes.
  3. Add onion and carrots. Cook 4–5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened.
  4. Stir in green beans, broth, Italian seasoning, garlic powder, and black pepper. Bring to a gentle boil.
  5. Reduce heat to low, add striped bass pieces, and simmer 6–7 minutes until fish is opaque and flakes easily.
  6. Stir in cooked brown rice and milk. Cook on low heat 3–4 minutes until chowder is hot but not boiling.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning. Ladle into three bowls, giving Frank the largest portion of fish and rice.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~620 kcal, ~47 g protein, ~60 g carbs, ~18 g fat.
  • Susan: ~465 kcal, ~35 g protein, ~45 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
  • Elise: ~465 kcal, ~35 g protein, ~45 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
