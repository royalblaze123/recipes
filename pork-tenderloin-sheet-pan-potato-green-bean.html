Sheet-Pan Pork Tenderloin with Baby Potatoes & Green Beans

NOTES: Roasted pork tenderloin with baby potatoes and crisp-tender green beans on a single tray. This is a full-plate pork dinner built around lean pork tenderloin/loin or 93% lean ground pork, smart carbs, and plenty of non-starchy vegetables. Uses olive or avocado oil only, keeps sodium moderate, and avoids added sugars to support A1c, cholesterol, blood pressure, and PPPD-friendly eating. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

PORTION SIZES (per person): Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 1 1/4 pounds pork tenderloin, trimmed
  • 1 pound baby potatoes, halved
  • 12 oz fresh green beans, trimmed
  • 2 tbsp olive oil, divided
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp dried Italian seasoning
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste, keep moderate)
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • Juice of 1/2 lemon (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F and line a large rimmed sheet pan with parchment paper.
  2. Pat the pork tenderloin dry and set aside at room temperature while you prep the vegetables.
  3. In a bowl, toss the halved baby potatoes with 1 tbsp olive oil, 1 tsp garlic powder, half of the Italian seasoning, a pinch of smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Spread on the sheet pan cut-side down.
  4. Roast the potatoes for 12 minutes.
  5. While potatoes start, toss the green beans with 1/2 tbsp olive oil and a light pinch of salt and pepper.
  6. In another small bowl, stir together remaining 1/2 tbsp olive oil, 1 tsp garlic powder, remaining Italian seasoning, smoked paprika, onion powder, salt, and pepper. Rub this mixture all over the pork tenderloin.
  7. After 12 minutes, remove the pan, push potatoes to one side, add green beans to the other, and place the seasoned pork tenderloin in the center.
  8. Return to the oven and roast 14–18 minutes more, until the pork reaches 145°F in the thickest part and the potatoes are tender.
  9. Transfer pork to a cutting board and rest 5 minutes before slicing into medallions. Drizzle the pan with lemon juice if using, then toss vegetables in the pan juices.
  10. Taste and adjust seasoning with a pinch of salt or extra herbs if needed, keeping overall sodium moderate.
  11. Divide the finished meal into three plates or containers: give Frank roughly 1/3 of the total volume, and split the remaining 2/3 between Susan and Elise as slightly smaller but still generous portions.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~500 kcal, ~46 g protein, ~34 g carbs, ~16 g fat.
  • Susan: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
  • Elise: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
