Beef Cauliflower Rice Tex-Mex Bake

NOTES: Built as a complete dinner using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled starch portions. Use low-sodium broth and minimal added salt to support blood pressure and kidney health. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of pan); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of pan); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of pan).

Ingredients

  • 1 lb 93% lean ground beef or sirloin
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup frozen corn (no added salt), thawed and drained
  • 3 cups riced cauliflower (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) no-salt-added diced tomatoes, drained well
  • 2 tsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 cup shredded reduced-fat Mexican blend cheese (optional)
  • Olive oil spray for baking dish

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Lightly spray a 9x13-inch baking dish with olive oil spray.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  3. Add diced onion and cook 3–4 minutes until softened.
  4. Stir in minced garlic and diced red bell pepper and cook 2–3 minutes more.
  5. Add ground beef, breaking it into crumbles, and cook 6–8 minutes until browned and cooked through. Drain any excess fat if needed.
  6. Stir in thawed corn, riced cauliflower, drained diced tomatoes, chili powder, ground cumin, smoked paprika, dried oregano, salt, and black pepper.
  7. Cook 5–7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until cauliflower rice is just tender and most of the excess moisture has evaporated.
  8. Transfer mixture to the prepared baking dish and spread into an even layer.
  9. If using, sprinkle reduced-fat Mexican blend cheese over the top.
  10. Bake uncovered 15–20 minutes until hot and lightly browned on top.
  11. Let casserole rest 5–10 minutes before slicing into portions for Frank, Susan, and Elise.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~560 kcal, ~48 g protein, ~32 g carbs, ~20 g fat.
  • Susan: ~420 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~15 g fat.
  • Elise: ~420 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~15 g fat.
