Pan-Seared Pork Medallions with Garlic Herb Jus and Quinoa

Servings

3 plates (Frank 1.0×, Susan 0.75×, Elise 0.75×).

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 25 minutes
  • Total time: 45 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2 pork tenderloins
  • 1.0 cup dry quinoa
  • 1 lb carrots
  • 10 tsp olive oil
  • 6 tsp minced garlic
  • 2 tsp black pepper
  • 2 tsp salt substitute
  • 2 tsp dried parsley
  • 1/2 cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • 2 tsp Worcestershire sauce

Steps

  1. Quinoa: Rinse 1 cup dry quinoa. Cook with 2 cups water to yield ~3 cups cooked. Fluff and keep warm.
  2. Carrots: Slice into coins. Roast with 4 tsp olive oil + 1 tsp pepper + 1 tsp salt substitute at 425°F for 18–22 minutes until tender with browned edges.
  3. Pork: Slice tenderloin into 1-inch medallions. Season with 1 tsp pepper + 1 tsp salt substitute. Sear in a skillet with 3 tsp olive oil 3–4 minutes per side to 145°F internal; rest 3 minutes.
  4. Pan sauce: Lower heat to medium. Add 1 tsp minced garlic and cook 30 seconds.
  5. Deglaze: Add the broth portion from Ingredients and scrape browned bits.
  6. Reduce: Simmer 2–4 minutes until slightly thickened and sauce clings.
  7. Finish: Deglaze with broth, reduce 2–4 minutes, then finish with Worcestershire and herbs off heat.
  8. Return protein to pan briefly to coat (30–60 seconds), then plate.
  9. Plate portions per Notes.

Notes

Portions (per person):

Frank – 1 plate (8 oz cooked protein, 1 cup cooked quinoa, 1 1/2 cups carrots, ~3 tbsp pan sauce).

Susan – 1 plate (6 oz cooked protein, 3/4 of Frank’s starch portion, 1 1/4 cups carrots, ~2 tbsp pan sauce).

Elise – 1 plate (6 oz cooked protein, 3/4 of Frank’s starch portion, 1 1/4 cups carrots, ~2 tbsp pan sauce).

Tips (recipe-specific):

Do not crowd the skillet—browning is your flavor engine without extra sodium.

Always reduce the sauce 2–4 minutes so it clings; watery sauce reads “cafeteria,” not gourmet.

Storage + Reheat

  • Cool promptly; store in airtight containers in the fridge up to 3–4 days (typical safety guidance).
  • Reheat protein + vegetables in a skillet with a splash of broth (best for sauces) or air fry at 350°F for 4–6 minutes; microwave starch 60–90 seconds with a splash of water.
