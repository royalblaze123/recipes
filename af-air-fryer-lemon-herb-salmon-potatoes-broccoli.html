Air Fryer Lemon Herb Salmon with Potatoes and Broccoli

Servings

3 plates (Frank 1.0×, Susan 0.75×, Elise 0.75×).

Times

  • Prep time: 15 minutes
  • Cook time: 25 minutes
  • Total time: 40 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lb salmon fillets
  • 1 lb baby potatoes
  • 1 bag frozen broccoli florets
  • 6 tsp olive oil
  • 2 tsp bottled lemon juice
  • 2 tsp minced garlic
  • 2 tsp dried dill
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 tsp salt substitute
  • 1 tsp black pepper

Recipe

  1. Preheat the air fryer to 390°F for 3–5 minutes. Place the empty basket in while preheating so it is hot when you add the food.
  2. Rinse 1.5 lb salmon fillets under cold water, then pat very dry with paper towels. Cut into 3 similar portions so each person gets one piece.
  3. Scrub 1 lb baby potatoes and cut into 3/4-inch chunks. Place the potatoes in a bowl and toss with 3 tsp olive oil, 1 tsp garlic powder, and 1 tsp salt substitute until evenly coated.
  4. Spread the seasoned potatoes in a single layer in the air fryer basket. Air fry at 390°F for 10–12 minutes, shaking the basket halfway through, until the edges start to brown and the potatoes are just tender. Transfer potatoes to a warm bowl and cover loosely with foil.
  5. While the potatoes cook, microwave 1 bag frozen broccoli florets according to package directions until just tender-crisp. Drain off any excess water and set aside.
  6. In a small bowl, whisk together 3 tsp olive oil, 2 tsp bottled lemon juice, 2 tsp minced garlic, 2 tsp dried dill, 1 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp salt substitute, and 1 tsp black pepper to make a quick lemon-herb rub.
  7. Place the dried salmon portions on a plate or tray. Brush or spoon the lemon-herb mixture over all sides of the salmon so each piece is well coated. Let sit for about 5 minutes while you get the basket ready.
  8. Return the potatoes to one side of the air fryer basket in a loose single layer. Arrange the salmon portions skin-side down on the other side of the basket, leaving a little space between pieces for air circulation.
  9. Air fry salmon and potatoes together at 390°F for 8–10 minutes, depending on thickness, until the thickest part of the salmon flakes easily with a fork and reaches 125–130°F for medium or 135°F if you prefer more done. If your basket is small, cook salmon in two batches and keep cooked pieces warm under foil.
  10. Right before serving, rewarm the broccoli in the microwave for 30–60 seconds if needed. Plate portions: Frank gets about 8 oz cooked salmon, 1 cup roasted potatoes, and 1 1/4 cups broccoli; Susan and Elise each get about 5.5 oz cooked salmon, 3/4 cup potatoes, and 1 cup broccoli.

Notes

Portions (per person):

Frank – 1 plate (about 8 oz cooked salmon, 1 cup roasted baby potatoes, 1 1/4 cups broccoli).

Susan – 1 plate (about 5.5 oz cooked salmon, 3/4 cup roasted baby potatoes, 1 cup broccoli).

Elise – 1 plate (about 5.5 oz cooked salmon, 3/4 cup roasted baby potatoes, 1 cup broccoli).

Tips (recipe-specific):

Cook potatoes first and hold them warm while the salmon finishes so everything is hot at serving time.

Check the thickest part of the salmon for flaking and internal temperature and adjust air fryer time by 1–2 minutes if your unit runs hotter or cooler.
