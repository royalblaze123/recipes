One-Pan Whole Wheat Pasta with Salmon, Tomatoes, and Spinach

Prep: 18 min   Cook: 24 min   Total: 42 min

Ingredients

  • 1.25 lb salmon fillets
  • 8 oz whole wheat pasta
  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes
  • 1 onion
  • 1 bag spinach
  • 0.5 carton low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 lemon
  • 2 tbsp grated parmesan cheese
  • olive oil
  • garlic
  • black pepper
  • salt substitute

Hard rules enforced: Dinner validations (calories 600–650 Frank, sat fat ≤8g, sodium ≤700mg, fiber ≥10g, added sugar ≤6g). Quantities appear before items; fresh meat/poultry/fish use weights. Condiments/spices/seasonings have no measurements in Ingredients; all measurements are in Steps. No olives, no squash, no Brussels sprouts, no zucchini, no tofu/tempeh, no yogurt in dinners.

Step-by-step Instructions

  1. Salmon: bake 425°F for 10–12 minutes until flaky; cool 5 minutes and flake.
  2. In a deep skillet, sauté sliced onion with 2 tsp olive oil 3 minutes. Add cherry tomatoes; cook 2 minutes.
  3. Add pasta + 1 cup broth (from carton) + 2 cups water. Cover and simmer 10–12 minutes, stirring twice, until al dente.
  4. Stir in spinach to wilt.
  5. Fold in flaked salmon and warm 1 minute. Finish with lemon juice and minced garlic (measured in Steps).
  6. Sprinkle 2 tbsp parmesan.
  7. Portion: Frank largest share; Susan/Elise ~75%.
