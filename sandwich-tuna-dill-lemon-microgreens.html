Tuna Dill-Lemon Salad Sandwiches with Microgreens and Tomato Side

Prep: 15 min   Cook: 0 min   Total: 15 min

Ingredients

  • 6 slices whole wheat bread
  • 2 cans tuna in water
  • 0.25 tub low-fat plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 lemon
  • 1 celery bunch
  • 1 bag microgreens
  • 1 tomato
  • 1 bag cherry tomatoes
  • Dijon mustard
  • dill
  • black pepper
  • salt substitute

RBM Sandwich rules: 3 full sandwiches total (6 slices bread). Deli meats/cheeses listed as slice counts; slicing guidance lives in Steps. Condiments/spices are name-only in Ingredients; all measurements are in Steps. Produce is listed in shoppable units (fractional OK). Microgreens preferred.

Step-by-step Instructions

  1. Drain both tuna cans well. In a bowl, mix tuna with 1/3 cup Greek yogurt + 2 tsp Dijon + 4 tsp lemon juice + 1 tsp dill + 1 tsp black pepper + 1 tsp salt substitute.
  2. Finely dice 2 celery stalks and fold into tuna salad for crunch.
  3. Assemble 3 sandwiches: divide tuna salad across 3 sandwiches. Add a small handful of microgreens to each. Add thin tomato slices if desired. Close and press.
  4. Portioning: Frank gets about 1/2 of the tuna salad total. Susan and Elise each get about 1/4 of the tuna salad total, while each still gets one full sandwich.
  5. Veg side: Frank gets ~2 cups cherry tomatoes. Susan and Elise each get ~1 1/2 cups.

Notes

Bright tuna salad (yogurt-masked) with dill, lemon, and microgreens for a clean, high-protein lunch. Portions (per person): Frank: - 1 full sandwich (about 1/2 of tuna mix total) - Cherry tomatoes: ~2 cups Susan: - 1 full sandwich (about 1/4 of tuna mix total) - Cherry tomatoes: ~1 1/2 cups Elise: - 1 full sandwich (about 1/4 of tuna mix total) - Cherry tomatoes: ~1 1/2 cups Tips (recipe-specific): Drain tuna aggressively; excess water is the main cause of soggy bread. Microgreens go on top of tuna to keep them crisp.
