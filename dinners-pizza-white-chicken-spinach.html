White Chicken & Spinach Thin-Crust Pizza (Frank Household)

Whole-wheat thin-crust white pizza with Greek yogurt sauce, chicken, spinach, and vegetables for a GERD-friendly, high-protein dinner.

Servings

3 servings (Frank, Susan, Elise).

Times

  • Prep time: 12 minutes
  • Cook time: 16 minutes
  • Total time: 28 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 large rectangular whole-wheat thin pizza crust (about 12×16 inches; ~13 oz total)
  • 13 oz cooked chicken breast, chopped (from about 1 lb raw, seasoned lightly)
  • 3 cups lightly packed fresh baby spinach
  • 1 cup thinly sliced mushrooms
  • 1 cup thinly sliced bell peppers
  • 1/2 cup thinly sliced red onion (optional, for tolerance)
  • 3/4 cup 2% plain Greek yogurt
  • 2 tbsp water (to thin sauce as needed)
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp dried Italian seasoning
  • 1/4 tsp ground black pepper (or to taste)
  • 1/4 tsp kosher or sea salt (keep total modest; season to taste)
  • 4 oz part-skim mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 1 oz finely grated Parmesan cheese
  • Olive oil spray (for pan and vegetables)

Preparation Steps

  1. Heat the oven to 425°F (about 220°C). Line a large sheet pan with parchment paper and lightly spray with olive oil.
  2. If the chicken is not yet cooked, season about 1 lb raw boneless skinless chicken breast lightly with salt, pepper, and garlic powder and bake or sauté until cooked through (165°F internal), then chop to make 13 oz cooked chicken breast. Set aside.
  3. Rinse and dry the spinach, mushrooms, bell peppers, and red onion (if using). Roughly chop the spinach. Slice the mushrooms and bell peppers; thinly slice the onion.
  4. In a medium bowl, toss the spinach, mushrooms, bell peppers, and onion with 1 tsp olive oil and a light sprinkle of salt and pepper. Spread the vegetables on a separate parchment-lined pan and pre-roast for 5–6 minutes until just beginning to soften.
  5. Make the white sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together the Greek yogurt, 1 tbsp olive oil, garlic powder, Italian seasoning, black pepper, salt, and 1–2 tbsp water as needed until it is spreadable but not runny.
  6. Place the whole-wheat pizza crust on the prepared sheet pan. Spread the yogurt white sauce evenly over the crust, leaving a small border at the edges.
  7. Sprinkle half of the shredded mozzarella over the sauce. Scatter the chopped cooked chicken evenly over the crust. Add the pre-roasted spinach and vegetables in an even layer on top of the chicken.
  8. Sprinkle the remaining mozzarella and the grated Parmesan evenly over the pizza.
  9. Bake at 425°F for 10–12 minutes, or until the crust is crisp, the cheese is melted with light golden spots, and the edges are browned.
  10. Remove the pizza from the oven and let it rest 2–3 minutes. Cut into 10 equal squares.
  11. Place 4 of the 10 squares on a plate for Frank, making sure his slices include generous chicken and spinach.
  12. Place 3 squares each on plates for Susan and Elise, redistributing any loose toppings as needed.

Portion Sizes (Per Person, Approximate)

  • Frank (F · 1.0×): 4 of 10 squares with ~4.5 oz cooked chicken breast, ~2.6 oz whole-wheat crust, ~1.6 oz part-skim mozzarella, ~2 tbsp yogurt sauce, and ~2 cups vegetables.
  • Susan (S · 0.75×): 3 of 10 squares with ~3.4 oz cooked chicken breast, ~2 oz whole-wheat crust, ~1.2 oz part-skim mozzarella, ~1 1/2 tbsp yogurt sauce, and ~1 1/2 cups vegetables.
  • Elise (E · 0.75×): 3 of 10 squares with ~3.4 oz cooked chicken breast, ~2 oz whole-wheat crust, ~1.2 oz part-skim mozzarella, ~1 1/2 tbsp yogurt sauce, and ~1 1/2 cups vegetables.
