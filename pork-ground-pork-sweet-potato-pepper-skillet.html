Ground Pork, Sweet Potato & Bell Pepper Skillet

NOTES: 93% lean ground pork sautéed with sweet potatoes and bell peppers in a mild chili-garlic profile. This is a full-plate pork dinner built around lean pork tenderloin/loin or 93% lean ground pork, smart carbs, and plenty of non-starchy vegetables. Uses olive or avocado oil only, keeps sodium moderate, and avoids added sugars to support A1c, cholesterol, blood pressure, and PPPD-friendly eating. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

PORTION SIZES (per person): Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 1 pound 93% lean ground pork
  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cut into 1/2-inch cubes
  • 1 large red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 1/2 tsp mild chili powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/4 cup low-sodium chicken broth or water

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Heat a large nonstick skillet over medium heat and add olive oil.
  2. Add the sweet potato cubes and cook 6–8 minutes, stirring occasionally, until lightly browned on the edges. Add a splash of water if they start to stick.
  3. Stir in the onion and bell pepper and cook 3–4 minutes until starting to soften.
  4. Push the vegetables to the edges of the skillet and add the ground pork to the center. Cook 4–5 minutes, breaking it up, until no longer pink.
  5. Stir in the garlic, chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, oregano, salt, and pepper. Cook 1 minute to bloom the spices.
  6. Pour in the broth or water, stir, then cover and reduce heat to medium-low. Cook 5–7 minutes, stirring once or twice, until sweet potatoes are tender and most of the liquid is absorbed.
  7. Remove the lid and cook 1–2 minutes to let any excess moisture cook off, leaving a moist but not soupy mixture.
  8. Taste and adjust seasoning with a pinch of salt or extra herbs if needed, keeping overall sodium moderate.
  9. Divide the finished meal into three plates or containers: give Frank roughly 1/3 of the total volume, and split the remaining 2/3 between Susan and Elise as slightly smaller but still generous portions.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~500 kcal, ~46 g protein, ~34 g carbs, ~16 g fat.
  • Susan: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
  • Elise: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
