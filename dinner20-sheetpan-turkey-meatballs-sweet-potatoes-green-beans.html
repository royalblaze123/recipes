Sheet-Pan Turkey Meatballs with Sweet Potatoes and Green Beans

Servings

3 plates (Frank 1.0×, Susan 0.75×, Elise 0.75×).

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 30 minutes
  • Total time: 50 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2.0 lb 93% lean ground turkey
  • 2 sweet potatoes
  • 1 bag frozen green beans
  • 8 tsp olive oil
  • 1/4 carton egg whites
  • 8 tsp oat flour
  • 4 tsp minced garlic
  • 4 tsp smoked paprika
  • 3 tsp onion powder
  • 2 tsp dried parsley
  • 2 tsp black pepper
  • 2 tsp salt substitute

Steps

  1. Heat oven to 425°F. Line two sheet pans with parchment (crowding rule: use two pans for browning).
  2. Sweet potatoes: Dice sweet potatoes into 3/4-inch cubes (~6 cups). Toss with 3 tsp olive oil + 2 tsp smoked paprika + 1 tsp onion powder + 1 tsp salt substitute. Spread on Pan 1. Roast 15 minutes.
  3. Meatballs: In a bowl, combine ground turkey + 1/4 carton egg whites (measure 1 cup) + 8 tsp oat flour + 2 tsp minced garlic + 2 tsp smoked paprika + 2 tsp onion powder + 2 tsp dried parsley + 1 tsp black pepper + 1 tsp salt substitute. Form 18 meatballs.
  4. Add meatballs to Pan 2. Roast 16–18 minutes until 165°F internal.
  5. Green beans: Toss frozen green beans with 5 tsp olive oil + 2 tsp minced garlic + 1 tsp black pepper + 1 tsp salt substitute. Add to Pan 1 during the last 10 minutes; roast until hot with lightly blistered edges.
  6. Plate portions: Frank gets 6 meatballs (~8 oz cooked) + 1 cup sweet potatoes + 1 1/2 cups green beans. Susan and Elise each get 4 meatballs (~6 oz cooked) + 3/4 cup sweet potatoes + 1 1/4 cups green beans.

Notes

Portions (per person):

Frank – 1 plate (6 meatballs, 1 cup sweet potatoes, 1 1/2 cups green beans).

Susan – 1 plate (4 meatballs, 3/4 cup sweet potatoes, 1 1/4 cups green beans).

Elise – 1 plate (4 meatballs, 3/4 cup sweet potatoes, 1 1/4 cups green beans).

Tips (recipe-specific):

Roast meatballs on a separate pan so they brown instead of steaming in sweet potato moisture.

If meatballs look pale, broil 1–2 minutes at the end (watch closely).

Storage + Reheat

  • Cool promptly; store in airtight containers in the fridge up to 3–4 days (typical safety guidance).
  • For best texture, reheat protein + veg in the air fryer at 350°F for 4–6 minutes, and reheat grains/beans in the microwave 60–90 seconds with a splash of water.
