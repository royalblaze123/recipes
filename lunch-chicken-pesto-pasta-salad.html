Chicken Pesto Whole-Wheat Pasta Salad

NOTES: Designed as a portable grab-and-go lunch using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled smart carbs. Use low-sodium ingredients and avoid added sugars where possible. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total volume); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total volume); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total volume) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 8 oz dry whole-wheat rotini or penne
  • 12 oz cooked boneless, skinless chicken breast, chopped
  • 1 1/2 cups halved cherry tomatoes
  • 1 cup small broccoli florets, lightly steamed and cooled
  • 1/2 red bell pepper, diced
  • 2 tbsp prepared basil pesto
  • 1/4 cup plain 2% Greek yogurt
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Cook pasta according to package directions in lightly salted water until al dente. Drain, rinse under cool water, and drain well.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together basil pesto, Greek yogurt, olive oil, lemon juice, and black pepper to form a light pesto dressing.
  3. In a large bowl, combine cooled pasta, chopped chicken, cherry tomatoes, steamed broccoli, and diced red bell pepper.
  4. Pour dressing over the salad and toss until everything is evenly coated.
  5. Divide pasta salad into three grab-and-go containers, making Frank’s portion slightly larger than Susan’s and Elise’s.
  6. Refrigerate up to 3 days. Serve chilled.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~580 kcal, ~48 g protein, ~60 g carbs, ~16 g fat.
  • Susan: ~435 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~45 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
  • Elise: ~435 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~45 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
