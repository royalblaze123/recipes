Air Fryer Pork Medallions with Parmesan Asparagus and Baby Potatoes

Pork tenderloin medallions with crispy baby potatoes and parmesan asparagus—timed for dual-basket Sync Finish.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 18 minutes
  • Total time: 38 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 1 lb pork tenderloin
  • 1 bag baby potatoes
  • 1 bunch asparagus
  • 1 each lemon
  • 1 bottle extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 container nonfat Greek yogurt
  • 1 jar Italian seasoning
  • 1 jar garlic powder
  • 1 jar black pepper
  • 1 jar parmesan cheese
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Prep (potatoes): halve baby potatoes. Measure out about 3 cups potatoes for this recipe; refrigerate remaining. Toss with 1 tbsp olive oil, garlic powder, Italian seasoning, and black pepper.
    2. Prep (pork): slice pork tenderloin into 12–15 medallions. Toss with 1 tbsp olive oil, Italian seasoning, garlic powder, and black pepper.
    3. Cosori setup: preheat both baskets to 390°F for 3 minutes. Put potatoes in Basket A. Put pork medallions in Basket B in a single layer (cook in two batches if crowded).
    4. Sync Finish: set Basket A to 390°F for 18 minutes (shake reminder ON). Set Basket B to 390°F for 12 minutes. Press Sync Finish.
    5. Shake reminder (Basket A): shake/flip potatoes when prompted.
    6. Flip pork: at the 6-minute mark, flip medallions. Pork is done at 145°F; rest 3 minutes.
    7. Asparagus: trim asparagus. Toss with 1 tsp olive oil and black pepper. When pork is done, cook asparagus in Basket B at 390°F for 6–7 minutes, shaking once.
    8. Finish: squeeze lemon over pork and asparagus. Sprinkle 1 tbsp parmesan total across all plates.
    9. Serve (40/30/30): plate pork with potatoes and asparagus. Optional: 2 tbsp Greek yogurt mixed with lemon for a quick dip.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – Pork: 6–7 medallions. Potatoes: about 1 1/4 cups. Asparagus: about 1 1/2 cups.


      Susan – Pork: 4–5 medallions. Potatoes: about 1 cup. Asparagus: about 1 1/4 cups.


      Elise – Pork: 4–5 medallions. Potatoes: about 1 cup. Asparagus: about 1 1/4 cups.


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Medallions cook quickly—don’t overcook past 145°F or they dry out.


      Cook asparagus after pork so it stays crisp-tender and bright.


      If Basket B is crowded, cook pork in two quick batches and keep the first batch warm under foil.
