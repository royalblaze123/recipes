Slow Cooker White Fish, Potato & Corn Chowder (Lite)

NOTES: Slow cooker one-pot comfort meal built around lean protein, smart carbs, and plenty of vegetables. No heavy cream, no deep-frying, and sodium kept reasonable with low-sodium broths and beans. Each recipe is designed to fit your A1c, cholesterol, blood pressure, and kidney stone goals while still feeling like classic comfort food.

PORTION SIZES (serves 3): This batch is scaled for three people. Frank receives the largest portion (about 1/3 of the total pot). Susan and Elise receive slightly smaller portions (each about 1/4 of the total). Frank = 1.0×; Susan = 0.75×; Elise = 0.75× portions.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb firm white fish (cod, haddock, or pollock), cut into large chunks
  • 1 lb potatoes, peeled if desired and cut into 3/4-inch cubes
  • 1 cup frozen corn kernels
  • 1 medium yellow onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 cups low-sodium fish or chicken broth
  • 1 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp salt, or to taste
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. In a 4- to 6-quart slow cooker, add 1 lb firm white fish (cod, haddock, or pollock), cut into large chunks, 1 lb potatoes, peeled if desired and cut into 3/4-inch cubes, 1 cup frozen corn kernels, 1 medium yellow onion, chopped, 2 cloves garlic, minced, 3 cups low-sodium fish or chicken broth, 1 cup unsweetened almond milk, 1 tbsp olive oil, 1 tsp dried thyme, 1/2 tsp smoked paprika, 1/2 tsp salt, or to taste, and 1/2 tsp black pepper. If any ingredients are frozen, place them on top so they thaw gradually.
  2. Stir gently to distribute the vegetables, protein, grains, beans, and seasonings as evenly as possible. Cover the slow cooker with the lid.
  3. Cook on LOW for the time indicated (usually 6–8 hours for tougher cuts and beans, or 4–6 hours for chicken and fish-based meals) until the protein is cooked through, vegetables are tender, and any grains or lentils are fully cooked.
  4. If using chicken breasts or pork tenderloin left whole, remove them near the end of cooking, shred or slice into bite-sized pieces, and return to the pot. Taste and adjust salt and pepper if needed.
  5. Ladle the stew, chili, or one-pot dinner into three bowls or plates, giving Frank the largest portion and dividing the remaining food between Susan and Elise. Store leftovers in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.

Nutrition (approximate per person)

Frank (largest portion): ~480–650 kcal, 38–55 g protein, 45–65 g carbs, 10–20 g fat
Susan (medium portion): ~360–500 kcal, 28–42 g protein, 34–50 g carbs, 8–15 g fat
Elise (medium portion): ~360–500 kcal, 28–42 g protein, 34–50 g carbs, 8–15 g fat
