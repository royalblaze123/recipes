Air Fryer Herb Beef Medallions with Sweet Potato and Broccoli

Herb-seasoned lean beef medallions with sweet potato and broccoli—simple seasoning, strong macros, and clean timing.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 16 minutes
  • Total time: 36 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 1 lb sirloin steak
  • 2 each sweet potatoes
  • 1 head broccoli
  • 1 bottle extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 jar Italian seasoning
  • 1 jar garlic powder
  • 1 jar black pepper
  • 1 each lemon
  • 1 container nonfat Greek yogurt
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Prep sweet potato: peel and cut into 3/4-inch cubes. Measure out about 2 cups cubes for this recipe; refrigerate remaining sweet potato. Toss with 1 tbsp olive oil, Italian seasoning, and black pepper.
    2. Prep broccoli: cut florets. Measure out 1/2 head broccoli for this recipe; refrigerate remaining. Toss with 1 tsp olive oil, garlic powder, and black pepper.
    3. Prep beef: slice sirloin into 9 medallions. Toss with 2 tsp olive oil, Italian seasoning, garlic powder, and black pepper.
    4. Cosori setup: preheat both baskets to 400°F for 3 minutes. Put sweet potato cubes in Basket A.
    5. Basket A: cook sweet potato cubes 400°F for 14–16 minutes with shake reminder ON; shake at each reminder.
    6. Basket B: cook beef medallions 400°F for 8–10 minutes, flipping at 5 minutes, to desired doneness. Rest 5 minutes.
    7. Broccoli: cook broccoli in Basket B at 400°F for 6–7 minutes, shaking once.
    8. Yogurt lemon drizzle: stir 1/4 cup Greek yogurt with 1 tsp lemon juice.
    9. Serve (40/30/30): plate medallions with sweet potato and broccoli and add a light yogurt drizzle.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – Beef: 4 medallions. Sweet potato: about 1 1/4 cups. Broccoli: about 1 1/2 cups. Yogurt drizzle.


      Susan – Beef: 2–3 medallions. Sweet potato: about 1 cup. Broccoli: about 1 1/4 cups. Yogurt drizzle.


      Elise – Beef: 2–3 medallions. Sweet potato: about 1 cup. Broccoli: about 1 1/4 cups. Yogurt drizzle.


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Rest medallions 5 minutes for a better texture and less juice loss.


      If you prefer more char, cook beef in a single layer and avoid crowding.


      For a lighter dinner, reduce sweet potato and increase broccoli.
