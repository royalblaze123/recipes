Turkey Chickpea Curry Soup (Stovetop, Light)

NOTES: Designed as a complete meal soup using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled starch. Use low-sodium broth and avoid extra salt when possible to support blood pressure and kidney health. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~2 cups); Susan: medium portion (~1.5 cups); Elise: medium portion (~1.5 cups).

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 medium carrot, thinly sliced
  • 1.5 tbsp mild yellow curry powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 4 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 can (15 oz) no-salt-added chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 lb 93% lean ground turkey
  • 1/2 cup light coconut milk (canned)
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Juice of 1/2 lime
  • Chopped cilantro, for garnish (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large soup pot over medium heat.
  2. Add diced onion and cook 4–5 minutes until softened.
  3. Stir in minced garlic and grated ginger and cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Add diced red bell pepper and sliced carrot and cook 3–4 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Sprinkle in curry powder, ground cumin, ground coriander, smoked paprika, salt, and black pepper and stir to coat vegetables.
  6. Add low-sodium chicken broth and chickpeas and stir to combine.
  7. In a separate skillet, cook ground turkey over medium heat 6–8 minutes, breaking into crumbles, until no pink remains. Drain any excess fat if present.
  8. Add cooked turkey to the soup pot and stir well.
  9. Bring soup to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer 20 minutes, stirring occasionally, to blend flavors.
  10. Stir in light coconut milk and simmer another 5 minutes on low; do not let it aggressively boil after adding coconut milk.
  11. Stir in lime juice and taste, adjusting salt and pepper as needed.
  12. Remove from heat and let rest a few minutes before ladling into bowls for Frank, Susan, and Elise.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~500 kcal, ~44 g protein, ~38 g carbs, ~17 g fat.
  • Susan: ~375 kcal, ~33 g protein, ~29 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
  • Elise: ~375 kcal, ~33 g protein, ~29 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
