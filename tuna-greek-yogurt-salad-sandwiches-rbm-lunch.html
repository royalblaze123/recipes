Tuna Greek Yogurt Salad Sandwiches - RBM Lunch

Protein-dense tuna salad lightened with Greek yogurt plus pickle crunch and a cucumber and carrot side.

Portions (per person):

Frank – 4 sandwich halves (2 sandwiches) + veg side

 

Susan – 3 sandwich halves (1.5 sandwiches) + veg side

 

Elise – 3 sandwich halves (1.5 sandwiches) + veg side

Ingredients

  • 10 slices thin whole wheat bread
  • 2 cans tuna
  • 1 container plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 bunch celery
  • 1 lemon
  • 1 bottle Dijon mustard
  • 1 romaine heart
  • 1 cucumber
  • 1 lb bag baby carrots
  • 1 bottle olive oil spray
  • 1 jar dill pickles

Step-by-step Instructions

  1. Drain the tuna cans well. Flake tuna in a bowl.
  2. Wash celery stalks from the bunch. Finely chop enough celery to measure 1/2 cup chopped celery for the tuna salad; refrigerate remaining celery.
  3. Make tuna salad dressing: mix 6 tablespoons plain Greek yogurt with 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard. Zest the lemon and squeeze 1 tablespoon lemon juice into the bowl; stir.
  4. Combine: add chopped celery and tuna to the dressing and stir until evenly coated.
  5. Wash the romaine heart. Chop enough to measure about 4 cups chopped romaine for the sandwiches; refrigerate remaining romaine if any.
  6. Toast option: lightly mist 10 bread slices with olive oil spray and toast 2–4 minutes, flipping once, until lightly crisp.
  7. Assemble 5 sandwiches: divide tuna salad evenly across 5 bread slices. Top each with romaine and 2 dill pickle spears per sandwich. Close with remaining bread slices.
  8. Cut each sandwich in half to make 10 halves. Portion: Frank gets 4 halves; Susan 3 halves; Elise 3 halves.
  9. Veg sides: slice the cucumber and measure 1 cup cucumber slices for Frank and 3/4 cup for Susan and Elise. Measure baby carrots: 1/2 cup for Frank and 1/3 cup for Susan and Elise. Pack separately.
