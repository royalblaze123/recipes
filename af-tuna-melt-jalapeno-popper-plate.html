Tuna Melt Stuffed Jalapeños with Sweet Potato Wedges (Air Fryer)

Dinner • Air Fryer • Cook time: 30 min

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 12 each fresh jalapeños
  • 20 oz canned tuna (drained)
  • 6.5 oz 2% plain Greek yogurt
  • 3 oz reduced-fat shredded cheddar
  • 1.54 lb fresh sweet potatoes
  • garlic powder
  • black pepper
  • kosher salt
  • extra-virgin olive oil
  • mustard

Steps

  1. Heat air fryer to 380°F.
  2. Sweet potato wedges: Cut 1.54 lb sweet potatoes into wedges. Toss with 3 tsp olive oil, 2 tsp kosher salt, 1 tsp garlic powder, and 1/2 tsp black pepper. Air-fry at 380°F for 18–22 minutes, shaking at 10 minutes, until tender and browned.
  3. Jalapeños: Slice 12 jalapeños lengthwise to make 24 halves. Remove seeds and ribs.
  4. Tuna filling: In a bowl, mix 20 oz drained tuna + 6.5 oz Greek yogurt + 6 tsp mustard + 1 tsp garlic powder + 1/2 tsp black pepper + 1 tsp kosher salt. Stir in 2 oz cheddar.
  5. Stuff: Pack filling into the 24 jalapeño halves. Sprinkle remaining 1 oz cheddar over the tops.
  6. Cook poppers: Air-fry stuffed jalapeños at 370°F for 7–9 minutes until peppers are tender-crisp and filling is hot.
  7. Serve: Plate poppers with sweet potato wedges.

Notes

Air Fryer dinner built to be high-protein and satisfying. Portions (per person): Frank: 10 jalapeño halves (5 jalapeños) + 8 oz sweet potato wedges Susan: 8 jalapeño halves (4 jalapeños) + 6.5 oz sweet potato wedges Elise: 8 jalapeño halves (4 jalapeños) + 6.5 oz sweet potato wedges Tips (recipe-specific): If the heat is too much, swap jalapeños for mini sweet peppers and cook 1–2 minutes longer. To reduce sodium, use low-sodium tuna if available and keep added salt to the minimum listed.
