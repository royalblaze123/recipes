Low-Sodium Pizza Dough (12 Balls, 12-inch)

Low-sodium pizza dough designed to keep room for toppings while staying within your household sodium targets. Makes 12 dough balls; each ball bakes into one 12-inch pizza crust.

Servings

12 dough balls (12-inch pizzas).

Times

  • Active prep time: 25 minutes
  • Cold ferment (recommended): 24–72 hours
  • Bake time per pizza: 7–9 minutes

Ingredients

  • 16 cups bread flour
  • 4 tsp instant yeast
  • 2 tsp fine sea salt
  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil

Step-by-step Instructions

  1. Add 5 cups warm water (about 95–105°F) to a large bowl or stand-mixer bowl. Sprinkle in 4 tsp instant yeast and whisk for 10 seconds to distribute.
  2. Add 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil and 2 tsp fine sea salt to the water-yeast mixture and whisk again.
  3. Add about half of the flour (about 8 cups) and mix until a thick batter forms. Add the remaining flour gradually, mixing until a shaggy dough forms and no dry flour remains.
  4. Knead until smooth and elastic: 8–10 minutes by hand on a lightly floured surface, or 6–8 minutes on low speed in a stand mixer. The dough should be slightly tacky but not sticky; if it is very sticky, work in 1–2 tablespoons flour at a time.
  5. Cover and rest 15 minutes, then do 1 quick set of stretch-and-folds in the bowl (4 folds, rotating the bowl). Cover and rest 15 more minutes.
  6. Divide into 12 equal pieces. Shape each piece into a tight dough ball by tucking the edges underneath and rolling against the counter until smooth.
  7. Lightly oil 12 containers or zip-top bags (a thin film of olive oil). Place 1 dough ball in each, seal, and refrigerate 24–72 hours for best flavor and structure.
  8. Freezing option (zip bags): after the dough balls are chilled/firm (at least 30–60 minutes in the fridge), lightly oil if needed, place 1 ball per freezer bag, press out as much air as possible, label, and freeze flat. Best quality 4–8 weeks; still workable up to ~3 months.
  9. Baking day: remove dough balls from the refrigerator 60–90 minutes before stretching so they warm up and relax. Keep covered so the surface does not dry out.
  10. Vacuum-seal option: chill dough balls 30–60 minutes first so the surface firms up. Lightly oil, place 1 ball per vacuum bag, and seal using a gentle/soft or pulse/manual setting so the dough is snug but not crushed. Double-seal the edge, then freeze flat until solid and stack. Do not vacuum-seal dough that is actively proofing at room temperature.
  11. Preheat oven with pizza steel or stone: 500°F for at least 45 minutes (or preheat to your oven’s max).
  12. Thawing: move frozen dough to the refrigerator 12–24 hours. On baking day, let it sit covered at room temperature 60–120 minutes until relaxed and slightly puffy before stretching.
  13. Stretch 1 dough ball into a 12-inch round on a lightly floured surface. Top with low-sodium sauce and measured cheese/toppings (avoid adding extra salt to the crust).
  14. Bake 7–9 minutes on a hot steel or stone until the crust is browned and the cheese is bubbling. Cool 2 minutes before slicing.

Portion Sizes

  • 1 dough ball = 1 (12-inch) pizza crust
