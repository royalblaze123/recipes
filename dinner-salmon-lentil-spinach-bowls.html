Lemon-Dill Salmon & Lentil Spinach Bowls

Seared salmon over warm lentils and spinach with a lemon-dill yogurt drizzle for a high-protein, slow-carb dinner.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 18 minutes
  • Total time: 38 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 1 lb fresh salmon fillet
  • 1 bag dried lentils
  • 1 bag fresh baby spinach
  • 1 each lemon
  • 1 bunch fresh dill
  • 1 container nonfat Greek yogurt
  • 1 bottle extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 jar garlic powder
  • 1 jar black pepper
  • 1 jar smoked paprika
  • 1 container grated parmesan cheese
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Cook lentils: rinse lentils. Simmer 1 cup dry lentils in water until tender (about 20–25 minutes). Drain well. Measure out 2 cups cooked lentils for this recipe; refrigerate remaining lentils for another meal.
    2. Season salmon: pat salmon dry and cut into 3 portions (about 5 oz each). Season with garlic powder, smoked paprika, and black pepper.
    3. Sear salmon: heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a nonstick skillet over medium-high. Cook salmon 3–4 minutes per side (until it flakes and reaches 125–135°F for medium, or your preference).
    4. Warm the bowl base: add the cooked lentils back to the warm pan (or a pot) with a splash of water. Stir in a big handful of spinach until just wilted.
    5. Make lemon-dill yogurt drizzle: stir 1/2 cup nonfat Greek yogurt with lemon juice and chopped fresh dill. Thin with a teaspoon of water if needed.
    6. Plate: divide the lentils/spinach into 3 bowls. Top with salmon and drizzle the yogurt sauce. Finish each bowl with a light sprinkle of parmesan.
    7. Portion guidance: Frank gets about 2 1/2 cups bowl (40%). Susan gets about 2 cups (30%). Elise gets about 2 cups (30%).

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – About 2 1/2 cups bowl (lentils + spinach) topped with 1 salmon portion (about 40% of the full recipe).


      Susan – About 2 cups bowl topped with a smaller salmon portion (about 30% of the full recipe).


      Elise – About 2 cups bowl topped with a smaller salmon portion (about 30% of the full recipe).


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      If you need a little more volume without adding many calories, add an extra handful of spinach while warming the lentils.


      To keep sodium controlled, season with spices and lemon first and salt only at the table if needed.


      For best texture, keep lentils just tender—not mush—by draining promptly when they’re done.

      • Susan: 469 kcal; 45 g protein; 26 g carbs; 21 g fat; 9 g fiber; 180 mg sodium
      • Elise: 469 kcal; 45 g protein; 26 g carbs; 21 g fat; 9 g fiber; 180 mg sodium
