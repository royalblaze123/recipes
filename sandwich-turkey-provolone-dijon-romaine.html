Turkey Provolone Dijon Romaine Sandwich

Servings

3 sandwiches (Frank 1.0×, Susan 0.75×, Elise 0.75×).

Times

  • Prep time: 12 minutes
  • Cook time: 0 minutes
  • Total time: 12 minutes

Ingredients

  • 6 slices whole wheat bread
  • 24 slices deli turkey breast
  • 3 slices reduced-fat provolone cheese
  • 10 tsp light mayonnaise
  • 6 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1/3 package romaine hearts
  • 1 tomato

Recipe

  1. Rinse and dry 1/3 package romaine hearts; pull 6 large leaves (2 per sandwich). Store the remaining romaine hearts package sealed in the fridge.
  2. Slice 1 tomato into 9 thin slices; use 3 slices per sandwich. Store any extra slices in a container.
  3. Frank sandwich: spread 4 tsp light mayonnaise + 2 tsp Dijon mustard across 2 bread slices; add 10 slices deli turkey breast + 1 slice provolone + 2 romaine leaves + 3 tomato slices; close and wrap.
  4. Susan sandwich: spread 3 tsp light mayonnaise + 2 tsp Dijon mustard across 2 bread slices; add 7 slices deli turkey breast + 1 slice provolone + 2 romaine leaves + 3 tomato slices; close and wrap.
  5. Elise sandwich: spread 3 tsp light mayonnaise + 2 tsp Dijon mustard across 2 bread slices; add 7 slices deli turkey breast + 1 slice provolone + 2 romaine leaves + 3 tomato slices; close and wrap.

Notes

Portions (per person):

Frank – 1 sandwich (10 turkey slices, 1 provolone slice, 4 tsp mayo, 2 tsp Dijon).

Susan – 1 sandwich (7 turkey slices, 1 provolone slice, 3 tsp mayo, 2 tsp Dijon).

Elise – 1 sandwich (7 turkey slices, 1 provolone slice, 3 tsp mayo, 2 tsp Dijon).

Tips (recipe-specific):

Make-night-before: keep romaine and tomato between the turkey and cheese to reduce sogginess.

If you’re packing for later, wrap tightly in parchment or foil so it holds shape.

Nutrition (per person)

  • Frank: 510 kcal; Protein 52 g; Carbs 33 g; Fat 16 g; Fiber 5 g; Sodium 1315 mg
  • Susan: 424 kcal; Protein 40 g; Carbs 31 g; Fat 13 g; Fiber 5 g; Sodium 1085 mg
  • Elise: 424 kcal; Protein 40 g; Carbs 31 g; Fat 13 g; Fiber 5 g; Sodium 1085 mg
