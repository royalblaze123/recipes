Instant Pot Pork Loin with Carrots & Potatoes

NOTES: Pressure-cooked lean pork loin roast with carrots and potatoes in a light herb broth. This is a full-plate pork dinner built around lean pork tenderloin/loin or 93% lean ground pork, smart carbs, and plenty of non-starchy vegetables. Uses olive or avocado oil only, keeps sodium moderate, and avoids added sugars to support A1c, cholesterol, blood pressure, and PPPD-friendly eating. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

PORTION SIZES (per person): Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 pounds pork loin roast, trimmed of visible fat
  • 1 pound Yukon Gold potatoes, cut into 1-inch chunks
  • 3 medium carrots, cut into thick coins
  • 1 small onion, cut into wedges
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 cup low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried rosemary, crushed
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set the Instant Pot Pro to Sauté. Add olive oil.
  2. Season the pork loin with thyme, rosemary, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Sear the pork in the hot oil for 2–3 minutes per side until lightly browned, then transfer it to a plate.
  4. Add onion and carrots to the pot and sauté 2–3 minutes. Stir in the garlic and cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Pour in the broth, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot.
  6. Place the pork loin back into the pot on top of the vegetables. Scatter the potato chunks around the sides.
  7. Cancel Sauté. Lock the lid, set the valve to Sealing, and cook on High Pressure for 20 minutes.
  8. Allow a 10-minute natural release, then carefully quick-release any remaining pressure.
  9. Transfer pork to a cutting board and rest 5–10 minutes before slicing. Gently toss the vegetables in the cooking juices.
  10. Taste and adjust seasoning with a pinch of salt or extra herbs if needed, keeping overall sodium moderate.
  11. Divide the finished meal into three plates or containers: give Frank roughly 1/3 of the total volume, and split the remaining 2/3 between Susan and Elise as slightly smaller but still generous portions.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~500 kcal, ~46 g protein, ~34 g carbs, ~16 g fat.
  • Susan: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
  • Elise: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
