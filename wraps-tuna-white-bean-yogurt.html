Tuna & White Bean Yogurt Crunch Wrap

High-protein tuna and white bean salad wrap with Greek yogurt, crisp veggies, and high-fiber tortillas for a balanced, full lunch.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 15 minutes
  • Cook time: 0 minutes (no cooking required)
  • Total time: 15 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 large high-fiber whole-wheat tortilla
  • 2 medium high-fiber whole-wheat tortillas
  • 1 can tuna in water
  • 1/2 cup canned white beans
  • 6 tbsp 2% plain Greek yogurt
  • 2 tsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp dried dill or parsley
  • 1/4 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/8 to 1/4 tsp kosher or sea salt
  • 1 1/2 cups shredded romaine lettuce
  • 1/2 cup thinly sliced celery
  • 1/4 cup finely diced red onion
  • 1/4 cup diced red bell pepper

Preparation Steps

  1. Rinse and pat dry the romaine lettuce, celery, and bell pepper. Shred the romaine and slice or dice the vegetables as directed. If you prefer a milder onion flavor, rinse the diced onion briefly under cool water and pat dry.
  2. In a medium bowl, combine the drained tuna and the drained, rinsed white beans. Use a fork to break them into small flakes and lightly mash some of the beans so the mixture holds together.
  3. In a separate small bowl, whisk together the Greek yogurt, olive oil, lemon juice, Dijon mustard, garlic powder, dried dill or parsley, black pepper, and a pinch of salt until smooth.
  4. Add the yogurt dressing to the tuna and bean mixture. Stir until everything is evenly coated. Fold in the sliced celery, diced onion, and diced red bell pepper. Taste and adjust seasoning with a little more salt, pepper, or lemon if desired.
  5. Gently warm each tortilla in a dry skillet over low heat for 15–20 seconds per side, or in the microwave for about 10 seconds, until just pliable.
  6. Place the large (10-inch) tortilla on a cutting board. Add a generous handful of shredded romaine down the center. Spoon about one-third of the tuna and white bean mixture over the romaine.
  7. Fold the sides inward, then roll tightly from the bottom into a burrito-style wrap. Wrap in foil or parchment if packing to go and slice in half if desired.
  8. Place one medium (8-inch) tortilla on the board. Add a moderate handful of shredded romaine. Spoon about half of the remaining tuna and bean mixture over the romaine.
  9. Fold in the sides and roll firmly from the bottom. Wrap in foil or parchment and slice in half if desired.
  10. Repeat the process with the remaining medium (8-inch) tortilla, romaine, and tuna and bean mixture.
  11. Serve immediately for best texture, or chill the assembled wraps for up to 24 hours. Wrap tightly in foil or parchment and store in a lidded container for lunch on the go.
