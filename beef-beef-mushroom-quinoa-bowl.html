Beef, Mushroom & Quinoa Bowls

NOTES: Savory beef and mushrooms spooned over fluffy quinoa with green beans or asparagus. This is a full-plate beef dinner built around lean beef, smart carbs, and plenty of non-starchy vegetables. Uses olive oil or avocado oil only, keeps sodium moderate, and avoids added sugars to support A1c, cholesterol, blood pressure, and PPPD-friendly eating. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

PORTION SIZES (per person): Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 1 pound lean beef sirloin or flank steak, thinly sliced
  • 8 oz cremini mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 3/4 cup uncooked quinoa, rinsed
  • 1 1/2 cups low-sodium beef or vegetable broth
  • 2 cups green beans or asparagus pieces
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. In a small saucepan, combine quinoa and broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer 15 minutes or until liquid is absorbed. Remove from heat and let stand 5 minutes before fluffing.
  2. While quinoa cooks, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the sliced beef and sear 3–4 minutes, stirring, until just browned. Transfer beef to a plate.
  3. In the same skillet, add onion and mushrooms. Cook 5–6 minutes, stirring, until softened and lightly browned.
  4. Stir in the garlic, thyme, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Cook 1 minute until fragrant.
  5. Return the beef and any juices to the skillet, along with 2–3 tbsp water or broth. Simmer 2–3 minutes on low heat to warm through and create a light pan sauce.
  6. Steam the green beans or asparagus separately for 4–5 minutes until crisp-tender.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning with a pinch of salt or extra herbs if needed, keeping overall sodium moderate.
  8. Divide the finished meal into three plates or containers: give Frank roughly 1/3 of the total volume, and split the remaining 2/3 between Susan and Elise as slightly smaller but still generous portions.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~520 kcal, ~48 g protein, ~32 g carbs, ~18 g fat.
  • Susan: ~390 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
  • Elise: ~390 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
