Air Fryer Ginger Garlic Beef with Slaw and Sweet Potato

Ginger-garlic lean beef strips with crisp slaw and roasted sweet potato—mild, fast, and built for weeknights.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 25 minutes
  • Cook time: 16 minutes
  • Total time: 41 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 1 lb top round steak
  • 2 each sweet potatoes
  • 1 bag coleslaw mix
  • 1 each lime
  • 6 cloves garlic
  • 1 knob fresh ginger
  • 1 bottle extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tbsp low sodium soy sauce
  • 1 container nonfat Greek yogurt
  • 1 jar black pepper
  • 1 jar garlic powder
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Prep sweet potato: peel and cut into 3/4-inch cubes. Measure out about 2 cups cubes for this recipe; refrigerate remaining sweet potato. Toss with 1 tbsp olive oil, garlic powder, and black pepper.
    2. Prep beef: slice top round steak into thin strips across the grain. Toss with 2 tsp olive oil, low sodium soy sauce, grated ginger, minced garlic, and black pepper. Rest 10 minutes.
    3. Prep slaw: measure out 3 cups coleslaw mix for this dinner; refrigerate remaining slaw. Toss slaw with 1 tbsp Greek yogurt and 1 tsp lime juice.
    4. Cosori setup: preheat both baskets to 400°F for 3 minutes. Put sweet potato cubes in Basket A.
    5. Basket A: cook sweet potato cubes 400°F for 14–16 minutes with shake reminder ON; shake at each reminder.
    6. Basket B: cook beef strips 400°F for 6–8 minutes, shaking once, until cooked through. Rest 3 minutes.
    7. Serve (40/30/30): plate beef with sweet potato and the crisp slaw on the side.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – Beef: largest share. Sweet potato: about 1 1/4 cups. Slaw: about 1 1/2 cups.


      Susan – Beef: medium share. Sweet potato: about 1 cup. Slaw: about 1 1/4 cups.


      Elise – Beef: medium share. Sweet potato: about 1 cup. Slaw: about 1 1/4 cups.


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Slice beef thin across the grain so it stays tender in the air fryer.


      Keep slaw cold and separate so the dinner has crunch and contrast.


      Use low sodium soy sauce to keep sodium controlled.
