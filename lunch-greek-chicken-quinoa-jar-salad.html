Greek Chicken Quinoa Jar Salad

NOTES: Designed as a portable grab-and-go lunch using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled smart carbs. Use low-sodium ingredients and avoid added sugars where possible. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total volume); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total volume); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total volume) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup uncooked quinoa, rinsed
  • 1 1/2 cups water or low-sodium chicken broth
  • 12 oz cooked boneless, skinless chicken breast, chopped
  • 1 1/2 cups chopped cucumber (seeds removed)
  • 1 cup halved cherry tomatoes
  • 1 cup chopped bell pepper (any color)
  • 1/3 cup crumbled reduced-fat feta cheese
  • 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 3 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/4 tsp garlic powder
  • Pinch kosher salt (optional, to taste)
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Cook quinoa: Combine rinsed quinoa and water or low-sodium broth in a small saucepan. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat, cover, and simmer 12–15 minutes until liquid is absorbed. Fluff with a fork and let cool completely.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, dried oregano, garlic powder, optional salt, and black pepper to make a simple dressing.
  3. In a large bowl, combine cooled quinoa, chopped chicken, cucumber, cherry tomatoes, and bell pepper. Pour dressing over and toss to coat evenly.
  4. Gently fold in crumbled feta cheese.
  5. Divide the mixture into three grab-and-go containers or wide-mouth jars, making sure Frank’s portion is largest.
  6. Refrigerate up to 3 days. Serve chilled; shake or stir before eating.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~580 kcal, ~50 g protein, ~53 g carbs, ~18 g fat.
  • Susan: ~435 kcal, ~38 g protein, ~40 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
  • Elise: ~435 kcal, ~38 g protein, ~40 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
