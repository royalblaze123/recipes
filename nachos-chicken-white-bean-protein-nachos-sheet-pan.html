Chicken & White Bean Protein Nachos (Sheet Pan)

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 640 kcal • P 60g • C 44g • F 20g
Susan/Elise (each): 480 kcal • P 45g • C 33g • F 15g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.25 lb boneless skinless chicken breast
  • 2/3 bag bean-based tortilla chips
  • 3/4 bag reduced-fat shredded Mexican cheese blend
  • 1/2 tub 2% plain Greek yogurt
  • 1/2 jar salsa
  • 1 can cannellini beans
  • 2 each bell peppers
  • 1 each yellow onion
  • 1 each lime
  • 4 tsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 tsp chili powder
  • 2 tsp cumin
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp kosher salt

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a large sheet pan with parchment.
  2. Chicken: pat chicken dry and cut into 1/2-inch pieces. Season with 2 tsp chili powder, 2 tsp cumin, 2 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp black pepper, and 1 tsp kosher salt.
  3. Cook chicken in a nonstick skillet over medium-high 8–10 minutes until browned and 165°F.
  4. Beans: can size note—use a standard 15 oz can cannellini beans. Drain and rinse. Measure out 1 1/2 cups beans; refrigerate any remainder.
  5. Veg: slice or dice the listed peppers and onion. Toss with the listed olive oil on the sheet pan and roast 10 minutes to start browning.
  6. Add the cooked protein (and any beans/corn listed) over the roasted vegetables and spread into an even layer.
  7. Cheese: sprinkle the listed shredded cheese evenly over the hot topping mix.
  8. Bake 4–6 minutes until the cheese is melted and bubbling at the edges.
  9. Chips: while the pan bakes, portion chips by weight: Frank 1.5 oz; Susan 1.125 oz; Elise 1.125 oz. (Keep chips separate so they stay crunchy.)
  10. Yogurt-lime drizzle: stir 6 tbsp Greek yogurt with 2 tsp lime juice and 1 tsp lime zest.
  11. Salsa: measure 1/2 jar salsa into a bowl for topping.
  12. Serve: spoon the hot cheesy topping mix over each person’s chips. Finish with the listed cold toppings. Portion per Notes.

Notes

Chicken + white beans makes these extra filling and A1c-friendly without increasing chip portions. Portions (per person): Frank: Chips: 1.5 oz + 1/3 of the hot topping mix + 1/3 of the cold toppings Susan: Chips: 1.125 oz + 1/4 of the hot topping mix + 1/4 of the cold toppings Elise: Chips: 1.125 oz + 1/4 of the hot topping mix + 1/4 of the cold toppings
