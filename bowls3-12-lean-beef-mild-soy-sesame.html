Composed Lean Beef Bowl with Cauliflower Rice, Peppers and Onions, and Mild Soy Sesame

Servings

3 bowls (Frank 1.0×, Susan 0.75×, Elise 0.75×).

Times

  • Prep time: 22 minutes
  • Cook time: 28 minutes
  • Total time: 50 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2.0 lb lean beef steak (top sirloin or top round), trimmed
  • 2 bags frozen cauliflower rice
  • 3 bell peppers
  • 1 pack microgreens
  • 11 tsp olive oil
  • 6 tsp minced garlic
  • 2 tsp black pepper
  • 2 tsp salt substitute
  • 2 tsp dried parsley
  • 5 tsp low-sodium soy sauce
  • 2 tsp sesame oil
  • 2 tsp black pepper

Steps

  1. Cauliflower rice: Sauté in a skillet 6–7 minutes until hot and steam-dry. Season lightly with pepper and salt substitute.
  2. Vegetables: Roast per steps below to get browned edges (texture contrast matters).
  3. Peppers/onions: Slice. Toss with 4 tsp olive oil + 2 tsp minced garlic + 1 tsp pepper + 1 tsp salt substitute. Roast at 425°F for 16–18 minutes, stirring once, until edges brown and peppers are tender-crisp.
  4. Lean beef: Slice across the grain into strips. Sear in a hot skillet with 3 tsp olive oil in 2 batches, 2–3 minutes per batch to brown.
  5. Sauce: In a small bowl, whisk the Sauce ingredients (mild soy sesame). Keep it measured—this is a finish, not a flood.
  6. Build bowls (assembly order): Add warm starch first, then vegetables, then sliced protein. Spoon sauce on top. Finish with microgreens after heat (they’re the ‘gourmet’ signal).
  7. Plate portions per Notes.

Notes

Portions (per person):

Frank – 1 bowl (8 oz cooked protein, 2 cups cauliflower rice, 1 1/2 cups peppers and onions, 1/2 cup microgreens, 2–3 tbsp sauce).

Susan – 1 bowl (6 oz cooked protein, 3/4 of Frank’s starch portion, 1 1/4 cups peppers and onions, 1/3 cup microgreens, 2 tbsp sauce).

Elise – 1 bowl (6 oz cooked protein, 3/4 of Frank’s starch portion, 1 1/4 cups peppers and onions, 1/3 cup microgreens, 2 tbsp sauce).

Tips (recipe-specific):

Build order matters: warm base → dry veg → protein → sauce → microgreens last so nothing turns soggy.

Keep sauce measured; the bowl should taste ‘finished,’ not ‘dressed.’

Storage + Reheat

  • Cool promptly; store components in airtight containers in the fridge up to 3–4 days (typical safety guidance).
  • Reheat grain/beans + protein 60–90 seconds in the microwave; reheat roasted veg 4–6 minutes at 350°F in the air fryer for texture; add sauce and microgreens after reheating.
