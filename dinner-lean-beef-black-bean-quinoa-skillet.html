Lean Beef Black Bean Quinoa Skillet with Greek Yogurt

A lean beef skillet with black beans and quinoa for a high-protein dinner that stays within your calorie band.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 20 minutes
  • Total time: 40 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 1 lb lean ground beef
  • 1 bag quinoa
  • 1 can black beans
  • 1 can no-salt-added diced tomatoes
  • 1 each yellow onion
  • 1 each bell pepper
  • 1 jar chili powder
  • 1 jar ground cumin
  • 1 jar garlic powder
  • 1 jar black pepper
  • 1 bottle extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 container nonfat Greek yogurt
  • 1 each lime
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Cook quinoa: cook 3/4 cup dry quinoa. Measure out 1 3/4 cups cooked quinoa for this recipe; refrigerate remaining quinoa.
    2. Prep veg: dice 1/2 onion and 1/2 bell pepper; store remaining.
    3. Brown beef: heat 1/2 tbsp olive oil in a skillet over medium-high. Brown lean ground beef, breaking it up. Drain if needed.
    4. Build skillet: add onion/pepper and cook 3–4 minutes. Stir in drained/rinsed black beans (measure out 1 1/2 cups; refrigerate remaining beans) and the diced tomatoes. Season with chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, and black pepper. Simmer 5 minutes.
    5. Stir in quinoa: fold in the cooked quinoa and warm through. Squeeze lime juice over the skillet.
    6. Serve: divide into 3 bowls using the 40/30/30 split. Top each bowl with a small dollop of nonfat Greek yogurt.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – About 2 1/2 cups skillet bowl with a larger share of beef + 2 tbsp Greek yogurt (about 40% of the full recipe).


      Susan – About 2 cups skillet bowl + 1–2 tbsp Greek yogurt (about 30% of the full recipe).


      Elise – About 2 cups skillet bowl + 1–2 tbsp Greek yogurt (about 30% of the full recipe).


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Use 93% lean beef (or leaner) to keep saturated fat in bounds.


      Rinse beans thoroughly and use no-salt-added tomatoes to keep sodium controlled.


      Keep simmer time short so the skillet stays chunky, not watery.
