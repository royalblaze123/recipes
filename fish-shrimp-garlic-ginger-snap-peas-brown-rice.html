Garlic-Ginger Shrimp with Snap Peas over Brown Rice

Prep: 18 min   Cook: 18 min   Total: 36 min

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lb shrimp
  • 1 lb snap peas
  • 0.5 cup dry brown rice
  • olive oil
  • low-sodium soy sauce
  • rice vinegar
  • sesame oil
  • garlic
  • ginger
  • black pepper
  • salt substitute
  • cornstarch

Hard rules enforced: Dinner validations (calories 600–650 Frank, sat fat ≤8g, sodium ≤700mg, fiber ≥10g, added sugar ≤6g). Quantities appear before items; fresh meat/poultry/fish use weights. Condiments/spices/seasonings have no measurements in Ingredients; all measurements are in Steps. No olives, no squash, no Brussels sprouts, no zucchini, no tofu/tempeh, no yogurt in dinners.

Step-by-step Instructions

  1. Cook rice: cook 1/2 cup dry brown rice with 1 cup water; simmer 35–40 minutes; rest 5 minutes.
  2. Sauce: whisk 2 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce + 1 tbsp rice vinegar + 1 tsp sesame oil + 1 tsp cornstarch + 2 tbsp water + 1 tsp minced garlic + 1 tsp grated ginger + 1/4 tsp black pepper.
  3. Shrimp: pat dry 1.5 lb shrimp. Sauté with 2 tsp olive oil over medium-high 3 minutes.
  4. Add 1 lb snap peas and cook 3 minutes until crisp-tender.
  5. Pour sauce over shrimp/peas and toss 1–2 minutes until glossy and thickened.
  6. Finish: add 1 tsp rice vinegar (optional) for brightness.
  7. Serve over brown rice. Portion: Frank largest shrimp/rice share; Susan/Elise ~75%.
