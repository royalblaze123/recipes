French Onion Meatloaf Plate

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 635 kcal • P 55g • C 36g • F 24g
Susan/Elise (each): 476 kcal • P 41g • C 27g • F 18g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.50 lb 93% lean ground beef
  • 2 each yellow onions
  • 2 each large eggs
  • 3/4 cup whole wheat panko breadcrumbs
  • 2 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 tsp thyme
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
  • 1.50 lb baby potatoes
  • 12 oz fresh asparagus
  • 4 tsp extra-virgin olive oil

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Line a sheet pan with parchment.
  2. Caramelize onions: thinly slice 2 onions. Cook in a nonstick skillet with 2 tsp olive oil over medium heat 12–15 minutes, stirring often, until deep golden and sweet.
  3. Meatloaf mix: in a bowl, combine ground beef, 1/2 of the caramelized onions, eggs, panko, Worcestershire, thyme, garlic powder, black pepper, and kosher salt. Mix just until combined.
  4. Form loaf on the sheet pan. Spoon remaining caramelized onions over the top. Bake 35–40 minutes until 160°F in the center.
  5. Potatoes: quarter baby potatoes; toss with 2 tsp olive oil and roast 25–30 minutes, flipping once, until browned and tender.
  6. Asparagus: toss with 2 tsp olive oil; add for the last 10–12 minutes until tender-crisp.
  7. Rest meatloaf 10 minutes before slicing.

Notes

French-onion style meatloaf with deeply browned onions for big flavor without heavy sauces.
