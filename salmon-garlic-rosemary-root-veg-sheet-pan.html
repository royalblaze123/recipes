Garlic Rosemary Salmon with Root Veggie Sheet Pan

NOTES: Full salmon dinner built as a single-pan or bowl-style meal with cardiometabolic-friendly fats, smart carbs, and plenty of non-starchy vegetables. Salmon is roasted or baked at moderate heat to protect omega-3s while keeping saturated fat very low. Seasonings are kept moderate-sodium and PPPD-friendly.

PORTION SIZES (serves 3 total): Frank gets the largest portion (≈1/3 of the total meal); Susan and Elise each receive slightly smaller portions (≈1/4 of the total meal each). Frank = 1.0×; Susan = 0.75×; Elise = 0.75× portions.

Ingredients

  • 3 salmon fillets (about 4–5 oz each), skin-on or skinless
  • 8 oz carrots, cut into 1/2-inch coins, and 8 oz parsnips, cut into 1/2-inch pieces
  • 1 red onion, cut into wedges
  • 1–2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil (divided, as described)
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste, keeping sodium moderate)
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • Marinade: 1 tbsp olive oil, 1 tbsp lemon juice, 2 cloves minced garlic, and 1 tsp dried rosemary
  • To finish: fresh parsley

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F. Line a large rimmed sheet pan with parchment paper for easy cleanup. Pat the salmon fillets dry with paper towels and set aside in the refrigerator while you prep the vegetables.
  2. Prep the starch: 8 oz carrots, cut into 1/2-inch coins, and 8 oz parsnips, cut into 1/2-inch pieces — cut into 1/2-inch pieces if not already bite-size. Place the potatoes or other starch on the prepared sheet pan. Drizzle with about 1 tablespoon olive oil, sprinkle with a pinch of salt and pepper, and toss to coat. Spread in a single layer. Roast for 10–15 minutes, stirring once, until just starting to soften but not fully tender.
  3. While the starch starts roasting, prep the vegetables: 1 red onion, cut into wedges — cut into bite-size pieces as needed. Set the vegetables in a large mixing bowl.
  4. Make the marinade: In a small bowl, whisk together the ingredients for the marinade (1 tbsp olive oil, 1 tbsp lemon juice, 2 cloves minced garlic, and 1 tsp dried rosemary). Taste and adjust with a splash more lemon or herbs if desired. Spoon 1–2 tablespoons of the marinade over the vegetables in the bowl and toss to coat.
  5. Remove the sheet pan from the oven. Push the par-roasted starch toward one half or the edges of the pan to make room. Spread the marinated vegetables in a single layer in the open space on the pan.
  6. Place the salmon fillets skin-side down (if using skin-on) on top of the vegetables or in an open space on the pan. Brush the tops of the salmon generously with the remaining marinade, making sure each fillet is well coated.
  7. Return the sheet pan to the oven and roast for 12–15 minutes, depending on thickness, until the salmon flakes easily with a fork and reaches 125–130°F in the center, the vegetables are tender-crisp, and the potatoes are fully tender with lightly browned edges.
  8. Remove from the oven and finish with fresh parsley. Taste a vegetable and potato and add a small pinch of extra salt or pepper only if needed.
  9. To serve, divide the potatoes/starch and vegetables among three plates. Top each plate with a salmon fillet, giving Frank the largest portion of starch and vegetables and Susan and Elise slightly smaller portions. Serve immediately.

Nutrition (approximate per person)

Frank (largest portion): ~480–520 kcal, 40–45 g protein, 40–45 g carbs, 16–18 g fat
Susan (medium portion): ~360–400 kcal, 30–34 g protein, 30–34 g carbs, 12–14 g fat
Elise (medium portion): ~360–400 kcal, 30–34 g protein, 30–34 g carbs, 12–14 g fat
