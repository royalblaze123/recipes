Rotisserie Chicken Plate with Creamy Jalapeño Poppers + Roasted Sweet Potatoes & Broccoli (Dinner)
Dinner ATLAS ONE-OFF Creamy poppers (no breadcrumbs) Weeknight ~45 min
ATLAS • ONE-OFF High-protein, comfort dinner using rotisserie chicken as the main, with lighter jalapeño poppers and simple roasted sides. Portions (per person): Frank: 1.0× (about 5.5 oz cooked skinless rotisserie chicken, 4 jalapeño halves, 5.25 oz roasted sweet potato, 7 oz roasted broccoli) Susan: 0.75× (about 4.1 oz cooked skinless rotisserie chicken, 3 jalapeño halves, 4 oz roasted sweet potato, 5.25 oz roasted broccoli) Elise: 0.75× (about 4.1 oz cooked skinless rotisserie chicken, 3 jalapeño halves, 4 oz roasted sweet potato, 5.25 oz roasted broccoli) Tips (recipe-specific): Pull the chicken off the bones and portion it before eating so the rotisserie doesn’t turn into an “eat the whole bird” situation. Keep poppers creamy without going heavy: use more chopped chicken in the filling and keep cheese measured. Roast sweet potatoes hotter (425°F) for browning; add broccoli later so it stays crisp-tender.

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1 rotisserie chicken
  • 8 jalapeños
  • 2 small sweet potatoes
  • 2 heads broccoli
  • 3 oz reduced-fat cream cheese
  • 1 oz reduced-fat cheddar cheese
  • 1 tsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 lemon
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp black pepper

Steps

  1. HEAT: Heat oven to 425°F. Line a sheet pan with foil. Set out a second small pan or oven-safe dish for the jalapeño poppers.
  2. CHICKEN FIRST: Pull all meat from 1 rotisserie chicken. Discard the skin. Reserve 8 oz cooked chicken breast meat for the plates and reserve 4 oz cooked chicken meat to mix into the popper filling. Refrigerate any remaining chicken immediately for sandwiches.
  3. SWEET POTATO: Scrub 2 small sweet potatoes and cut into 3/4-inch cubes. Put cubes on the sheet pan. Drizzle with 1 tsp extra-virgin olive oil and sprinkle with 1 tsp smoked paprika and 1/2 tsp black pepper. Toss to coat.
  4. ROAST START: Roast sweet potatoes at 425°F for 12 minutes.
  5. JALAPEÑOS: While sweet potatoes start, halve 8 jalapeños lengthwise and remove seeds and white ribs. Arrange jalapeño halves cut-side up on the small pan.
  6. POPPER FILLING (CREAMY NOT BREADCRUMBY): In a bowl, mix 3 oz reduced-fat cream cheese with 4 oz cooked chicken (finely chopped). Add 1 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1 tsp onion powder, and 1/2 tsp black pepper. Add 1 tsp lemon juice and 1/2 tsp lemon zest from 1 lemon and stir until smooth.
  7. FILL + TOP: Spoon the filling evenly into the 16 jalapeño halves. Top all poppers with 1 oz reduced-fat cheddar cheese divided evenly.
  8. BROCCOLI: Cut 2 heads broccoli into bite-size florets and slice the stems into thin coins. Put broccoli on the same sheet pan with the sweet potatoes after the first 12 minutes. Sprinkle broccoli with 1 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp smoked paprika, and 1/2 tsp black pepper. Toss broccoli with the pan’s residual oil and moisture (do not add more oil).
  9. ROAST FINISH: Roast sweet potatoes + broccoli for 12–15 more minutes at 425°F, until sweet potatoes are browned and broccoli is crisp-tender with some charred edges.
  10. BAKE POPPERS: While the vegetables finish, bake the jalapeño poppers at 425°F for 10–12 minutes, until the filling is hot and the tops are lightly browned.
  11. PLATE (PORTIONED): Frank gets about 5.5 oz cooked skinless rotisserie chicken plus 4 jalapeño halves plus 5.25 oz sweet potato plus 7 oz broccoli. Susan and Elise each get about 4.1 oz chicken plus 3 jalapeño halves plus 4 oz sweet potato plus 5.25 oz broccoli. Squeeze 1 tsp lemon juice over broccoli on each plate (optional but recommended).
  12. STORAGE + REHEAT: Cool within 2 hours. Refrigerate chicken and vegetables up to 3–4 days. Reheat poppers and vegetables in the air fryer at 350°F for 4–6 minutes for best texture or microwave 60–90 seconds. Reheat chicken gently (microwave 20–30 seconds) to avoid drying out.

Sodium note: rotisserie chicken sodium varies widely by brand and seasoning. The estimates assume a typical skinless portion; label-exact sodium may be higher or lower.
