Instant Pot Shredded Beef Taco Bowls

NOTES: Pressure-cooked shredded beef served over brown rice with peppers and lettuce. This is a full-plate beef dinner built around lean beef, smart carbs, and plenty of non-starchy vegetables. Uses olive oil or avocado oil only, keeps sodium moderate, and avoids added sugars to support A1c, cholesterol, blood pressure, and PPPD-friendly eating. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

PORTION SIZES (per person): Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 pounds lean beef chuck or bottom round, trimmed of visible fat
  • 1 cup uncooked brown rice, rinsed
  • 2 cups low-sodium beef broth, divided
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 1/2 tsp mild chili powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or less to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 2 cups shredded romaine or spring mix for serving

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set the Instant Pot Pro to Sauté. Pour in 1 tbsp of the broth and add the sliced onion and peppers. Sauté 3–4 minutes until slightly softened, then transfer to a plate.
  2. Season the beef with chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, oregano, salt, and pepper. Add the beef to the pot and sear 2–3 minutes per side.
  3. Stir in the garlic, then pour in 1 1/2 cups of the broth. Cancel Sauté.
  4. Lock the lid, set the valve to Sealing, and cook on High Pressure for 35 minutes.
  5. While the beef cooks, prepare the brown rice on the stovetop according to package directions using remaining 1/2 cup broth plus water as needed, keeping sodium moderate.
  6. Allow the Instant Pot to naturally release pressure for 10 minutes, then quick-release any remaining pressure.
  7. Remove the beef to a cutting board and shred with two forks. Return shredded beef to the cooking liquid and stir in the sautéed peppers and onions. Let rest on Warm for 5–10 minutes so the flavors meld.
  8. Taste and adjust seasoning with a pinch of salt or extra herbs if needed, keeping overall sodium moderate.
  9. Divide the finished meal into three plates or containers: give Frank roughly 1/3 of the total volume, and split the remaining 2/3 between Susan and Elise as slightly smaller but still generous portions.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~520 kcal, ~48 g protein, ~32 g carbs, ~18 g fat.
  • Susan: ~390 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
  • Elise: ~390 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
