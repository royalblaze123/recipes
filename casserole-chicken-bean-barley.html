Chicken Bean and Barley Bake

NOTES: Built as a complete dinner using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled starch portions. Use low-sodium broth and minimal added salt to support blood pressure and kidney health. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of pan); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of pan); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of pan).

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup pearl barley, rinsed
  • 2 1/4 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breast, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (15 oz) no-salt-added cannellini or great northern beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup frozen peas (no added salt), thawed and drained
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried parsley
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1/3 cup plain 2% Greek yogurt
  • Olive oil spray for baking dish

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Lightly spray a 9x13-inch baking dish with olive oil spray.
  2. In a saucepan, combine rinsed pearl barley and low-sodium chicken broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat, cover, and simmer 25–30 minutes until barley is tender and most liquid is absorbed. Remove from heat and fluff with a fork.
  3. While barley cooks, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  4. Add diced onion, carrots, and celery and cook 5–6 minutes until vegetables begin to soften.
  5. Stir in minced garlic and cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
  6. Add chicken pieces, season with a small pinch of salt and pepper, and cook 6–8 minutes until just cooked through.
  7. Stir in drained white beans, thawed peas, dried thyme, dried parsley, remaining salt, and black pepper. Cook 3–4 minutes to warm through.
  8. In a large mixing bowl, combine cooked barley with the chicken and vegetable mixture. Let cool 2–3 minutes, then stir in Greek yogurt until creamy.
  9. Transfer mixture to the prepared baking dish and spread into an even layer.
  10. Bake uncovered 20–25 minutes until hot and lightly browned at the edges.
  11. Let casserole rest 5–10 minutes before cutting into portions for Frank, Susan, and Elise.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~580 kcal, ~50 g protein, ~58 g carbs, ~16 g fat.
  • Susan: ~435 kcal, ~37 g protein, ~44 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
  • Elise: ~435 kcal, ~37 g protein, ~44 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
