Slow Cooker Tuscan Chicken White Bean Stew

Chefy Tuscan chicken stew with herbs, white beans, and kale—complete bowl with controlled carbs and strong texture (no side).

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 18 minutes
  • Cook time: 420 minutes
  • Total time: 438 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 2 lb boneless skinless chicken breast
  • 1 carton low sodium chicken broth
  • 1 each yellow onion
  • 1 bunch celery
  • 1 lb bag carrots
  • 6 cloves garlic
  • 1 bunch kale
  • 1 can cannellini beans
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 jar tomato paste
  • 1 bottle extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 jar Italian seasoning
  • 1 jar dried rosemary
  • 1 jar dried thyme
  • 1 jar black pepper
  • 1 jar parmesan cheese
  • 1 jar cornstarch
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Prep veg: dice 1/2 the onion, slice 3 celery stalks, and slice 3 carrots into thick coins. Mince 3 garlic cloves. Chop kale leaves (use about 4 packed cups; refrigerate remaining kale).
    2. Sear flavor (optional but recommended): heat a skillet over medium-high heat. Add 2 tsp olive oil and sear chicken breasts 2 minutes per side to brown the surface (chicken will finish in the slow cooker).
    3. Slow cooker base: add onion, celery, carrots, and garlic to the slow cooker. Stir in 1 tbsp tomato paste, 2 tsp Italian seasoning, 1 tsp dried rosemary, 1 tsp dried thyme, and black pepper.
    4. Liquid: add the carton of low sodium chicken broth plus 1 1/2 cups water. Add the can of diced tomatoes.
    5. Cook: nestle chicken into the liquid. Cook on LOW for 6–7 hours or HIGH for 3–4 hours, until chicken reaches 165°F and is shred-tender.
    6. Shred: remove chicken, shred, and return shredded chicken to the slow cooker.
    7. Beans + kale late: drain and rinse cannellini beans. Stir beans and chopped kale into the stew. Cook on HIGH for 20 minutes until kale is just tender and still green.
    8. Thicken (optional): whisk 1 tbsp cornstarch with 2 tbsp water. Stir slurry into the stew and cook 5 minutes to lightly thicken.
    9. Finish: sprinkle a measured 1 tbsp parmesan total across all bowls and serve.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – About 3 1/2 cups stew.


      Susan – About 2 2/3 cups stew.


      Elise – About 2 2/3 cups stew.


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Add beans and kale at the end so the stew stays structured, not mushy.


      A quick sear adds big flavor even though it’s not required.


      If it thickens overnight, loosen with a splash of water when reheating.
