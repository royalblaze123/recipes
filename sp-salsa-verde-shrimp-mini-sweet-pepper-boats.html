Salsa Verde Shrimp Mini Sweet Pepper Boats with Cauliflower Rice (Sheet Pan)

Dinner • Sheet Pan • Cook time: 25 min

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 18 each fresh mini sweet peppers
  • 24 oz fresh shrimp
  • 8 oz 2% plain Greek yogurt
  • 3 oz part-skim shredded mozzarella
  • 1.32 lb frozen cauliflower rice
  • 1 each fresh lime
  • salsa verde
  • garlic powder
  • cumin
  • black pepper
  • kosher salt
  • extra-virgin olive oil

Steps

  1. Heat oven to 425°F. Line a sheet pan with parchment.
  2. Peppers: Slice 18 mini sweet peppers lengthwise to make 36 boats. Remove seeds.
  3. Shrimp: Chop 24 oz shrimp into small pieces. Season with 2 tsp kosher salt, 2 tsp garlic powder, 2 tsp cumin, and 1/2 tsp black pepper. Sauté with 2 tsp olive oil for 3–4 minutes until just opaque.
  4. Creamy verde binder: In a bowl, whisk 8 oz Greek yogurt + 16 tsp salsa verde + zest of 1 lime. Fold in shrimp and 2 oz mozzarella.
  5. Assemble & bake: Fill each pepper boat. Sprinkle remaining 1 oz mozzarella over the tops. Bake 10–12 minutes until peppers are tender-crisp and filling is hot.
  6. Cauli rice side: Cook 1.32 lb frozen cauliflower rice with 1 tsp olive oil, 1 tsp kosher salt, and 1 tsp garlic powder in a large skillet for 6–8 minutes until hot and moisture cooks off.
  7. Serve: Plate pepper boats with cauliflower rice.

Notes

Sheet Pan dinner built to be high-protein and satisfying. Portions (per person): Frank: 16 pepper boats (8 mini peppers) + 7 oz cauliflower rice Susan: 12 pepper boats (6 mini peppers) + 5.5 oz cauliflower rice Elise: 12 pepper boats (6 mini peppers) + 5.5 oz cauliflower rice Tips (recipe-specific): Do not overcook shrimp during sauté—pull as soon as opaque so it stays juicy after baking. If you want extra brightness without extra acid, add 1 tsp lime zest to the cauliflower rice.
