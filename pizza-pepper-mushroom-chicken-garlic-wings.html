Pepper Mushroom Chicken Pizza + Garlic-Heat Wings

Pepper-and-mushroom chicken pizza matched with garlic wings finished with a measured, mild heat.

Servings

3 servings (Frank, Susan, Elise).

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 35 minutes
  • Total time: 55 minutes

Ingredients

Pizza

  • 1 dough ball pizza dough
  • 1/2 jar no-salt-added pizza sauce
  • 3 oz shredded part-skim mozzarella
  • 4 oz cooked chicken breast
  • 1/2 each red bell pepper
  • 1/2 package mushrooms
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika

Wings

  • 2 lb chicken wings
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper

Wing Finish Sauce

  • 2 tbsp hot sauce
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 each lemon

Preparation Steps

  1. Remove 1 dough ball from the refrigerator (or thawed vacuum-sealed dough) and keep covered at room temperature for 60–90 minutes.
  2. Preheat oven with a pizza steel or stone to 500°F for 45 minutes (or your oven’s maximum).
  3. Wing count note: 1 wing = 1 whole wing (flat + drumette). If buying separated party wings, count 2 pieces = 1 wing.
  4. Wings: Pat wings very dry. Toss with garlic powder, smoked paprika, and black pepper.
  5. Cosori dual-basket setup: Split wings between both baskets in a single layer. Use Sync Cook at 380°F for 18 minutes, shaking halfway.
  6. Crisp finish: Increase to 400°F and cook 6–8 minutes more until browned and 165°F internally.
  7. Garlic-heat finish: Whisk hot sauce, olive oil, and a squeeze of lemon. Toss hot wings lightly to coat.
  8. Pizza topping prep: Slice mushrooms. Thin-slice red bell pepper. Shred or chop cooked chicken.
  9. Stretch dough into a 12-inch round. Spread sauce. Sprinkle mozzarella. Add chicken, mushrooms, and red bell pepper. Dust with garlic powder and smoked paprika.
  10. Bake 7–9 minutes until browned and bubbling. Rest 2 minutes before slicing.
  11. Portion and serve using the Portions split below.

Notes

Pepper-and-mushroom chicken pizza matched with garlic wings finished with a measured, mild heat. Portions (per person): Frank: About 1/3 of the 12-inch pizza + 8 wings (about 4 oz cooked) Susan: About 1/4 of the 12-inch pizza + 6 wings (about 3 oz cooked) Elise: About 1/4 of the 12-inch pizza + 6 wings (about 3 oz cooked) Tips (recipe-specific): Keep the wing sauce light; tossing too heavily can soften the crisp skin. If you prefer zero heat, skip the hot sauce and finish wings with extra lemon and smoked paprika. For best crust, preheat the stone/steel the full 45 minutes.
