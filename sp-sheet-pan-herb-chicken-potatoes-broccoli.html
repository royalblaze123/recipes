Sheet Pan Herb Chicken with Potatoes and Broccoli

Servings

3 plates (Frank 1.0×, Susan 0.75×, Elise 0.75×).

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 30 minutes
  • Total time: 50 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lb boneless skinless chicken breasts
  • 1.5 lb baby potatoes
  • 1 head broccoli
  • 7 tsp olive oil
  • 2 tsp bottled lemon juice
  • 2 tsp minced garlic
  • 2 tsp Italian seasoning
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 2 tsp salt substitute
  • 1 tsp black pepper

Recipe

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F. Line a large rimmed sheet pan with parchment paper or lightly coat it with nonstick spray for easier cleanup.
  2. Trim 1.5 lb boneless skinless chicken breasts of excess fat. Pat the chicken very dry with paper towels, then cut into 3 similar portions so each person gets one piece of similar size and thickness.
  3. Scrub 1.5 lb baby potatoes and cut them into 3/4-inch chunks. Add the potatoes to a large mixing bowl.
  4. Rinse 1 head broccoli, trim off the tough ends of the stalks, and cut the head into small florets about 1 to 1 1/2 inches across. Pat the florets dry with a clean towel or paper towels and add them to the bowl with the potatoes.
  5. Drizzle the potatoes and broccoli in the bowl with 4 tsp olive oil. Sprinkle over 1 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1 tsp salt substitute, and 1/2 tsp black pepper. Toss well with a spatula or your hands until the vegetables are evenly coated in oil and seasoning.
  6. Spread the seasoned potatoes and broccoli out on the prepared sheet pan in a single layer, keeping most of the potatoes toward the center where the heat is strongest and the broccoli more toward the edges so it does not overbrown.
  7. In the now-empty bowl, whisk together 3 tsp olive oil, 2 tsp bottled lemon juice, 2 tsp minced garlic, 2 tsp Italian seasoning, 1 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp onion powder, 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1 tsp salt substitute, and 1/2 tsp black pepper to make an herb rub for the chicken.
  8. Place the dried chicken pieces into the bowl with the herb rub and turn them several times so all sides are coated. Let the chicken rest in the seasoning for 5–10 minutes while the oven finishes heating and the vegetables get a head start.
  9. Place the sheet pan with just the vegetables into the preheated 400°F oven and roast for 10 minutes to begin softening the potatoes and start browning the edges.
  10. Carefully remove the pan from the oven and give the vegetables a quick toss with a spatula to flip most of the potatoes. Make 3 open spaces on the pan by pushing some vegetables aside and lay the seasoned chicken pieces in those spaces so they sit directly on the pan.
  11. Return the sheet pan to the oven and roast at 400°F for another 15–18 minutes, until the thickest part of the chicken reaches 165°F on an instant-read thermometer and the potatoes are tender and golden at the edges. If the broccoli tips are browning too quickly, gently toss them toward the center of the pan for the last few minutes.
  12. Remove the pan from the oven and let the chicken rest for 3–5 minutes on the pan. Build plates: Frank gets about 8 oz cooked chicken, 1 1/4 cups potatoes, and 1 1/4 cups broccoli; Susan and Elise each get about 5.5 oz cooked chicken, 1 cup potatoes, and 1 cup broccoli.

Notes

Portions (per person):

Frank – 1 plate (about 8 oz cooked chicken, 1 1/4 cups roasted baby potatoes, 1 1/4 cups broccoli).

Susan – 1 plate (about 5.5 oz cooked chicken, 1 cup roasted baby potatoes, 1 cup broccoli).

Elise – 1 plate (about 5.5 oz cooked chicken, 1 cup roasted baby potatoes, 1 cup broccoli).

Tips (recipe-specific):

Cut the potatoes into even 3/4-inch pieces so they roast at the same rate and crisp instead of steaming.

Spread the chicken and vegetables out in a single layer; if the pan is crowded, use two pans so everything browns properly.
