Ginger-Lime Arctic Char with Quinoa & Edamame Bowls

Fatty fish bowls with quinoa and edamame for a high-protein, slow-carb dinner with bright ginger-lime flavor.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 18 minutes
  • Total time: 38 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 1 lb fresh arctic char fillets
  • 1 bag quinoa
  • 1 bag frozen shelled edamame
  • 1 carton egg whites
  • 1 each lime
  • 1 each fresh ginger
  • 1 bottle low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1 bottle rice vinegar
  • 1 bottle sesame oil
  • 1 bottle extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 jar garlic powder
  • 1 jar black pepper
  • 1 bag fresh baby spinach
  • 1 container nonfat Greek yogurt
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Cook quinoa: cook 2/3 cup dry quinoa according to package directions. Measure out 1 1/4 cups cooked quinoa for this recipe; refrigerate remaining quinoa.
    2. Cook edamame: microwave or simmer edamame until tender. Measure out 1 cup for this recipe; refreeze remaining.
    3. Quick egg white boost: scramble 1/2 cup egg whites in a nonstick pan until just set (this is a small protein add-on for the bowls).
    4. Season fish: pat arctic char dry. Stir together grated ginger, lime juice, a small splash of low-sodium soy sauce, and black pepper. Brush over the fish.
    5. Cook fish: heat 3/4 tbsp olive oil in a skillet over medium-high. Sear fish 3–4 minutes per side until it flakes.
    6. Build bowls: warm quinoa and toss with edamame, egg whites, and a handful of spinach until spinach wilts.
    7. Optional creamy drizzle: stir 1/2 cup Greek yogurt with lime juice and a few drops of sesame oil; drizzle lightly.
    8. Serve using the 40/30/30 split by volume: Frank ~2 1/2 cups bowl; Susan ~2 cups; Elise ~2 cups.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – About 2 1/2 cups bowl (quinoa + edamame + spinach) topped with a larger char portion (about 40% of the full recipe).


      Susan – About 2 cups bowl topped with a smaller char portion (about 30% of the full recipe).


      Elise – About 2 cups bowl topped with a smaller char portion (about 30% of the full recipe).


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Go light on soy sauce; ginger, lime, and sesame aroma do most of the work for flavor.


      If you want more crunch, add sliced cucumber at serving (store the rest for another recipe).


      Avoid overcooking char—pull it as soon as it flakes so it stays moist.

      • Susan: 476 kcal; 44 g protein; 21 g carbs; 24 g fat; 4 g fiber; 175 mg sodium
      • Elise: 476 kcal; 44 g protein; 21 g carbs; 24 g fat; 4 g fiber; 175 mg sodium
