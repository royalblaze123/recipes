Chicken Wild Rice Mushroom Bake

NOTES: Built as a complete dinner using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled starch portions. Use low-sodium broth and minimal added salt to support blood pressure and kidney health. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of pan); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of pan); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of pan).

Ingredients

  • 1 cup wild rice or wild rice blend, rinsed
  • 2 1/2 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breast, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 8 oz cremini or baby bella mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups small broccoli florets
  • 1/2 cup plain 2% Greek yogurt
  • 1/2 cup shredded reduced-fat Swiss or mozzarella cheese
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1/2 tsp dried rosemary, crushed
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Olive oil spray for baking dish

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Lightly spray a 9x13-inch baking dish with olive oil spray.
  2. In a saucepan, combine rinsed wild rice and low-sodium chicken broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat, cover, and simmer 35–40 minutes until rice is tender and most of the liquid is absorbed. Remove from heat and fluff with a fork.
  3. While rice cooks, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  4. Add sliced mushrooms and diced onion and cook 6–7 minutes until mushrooms release their moisture and begin to brown.
  5. Stir in minced garlic and cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
  6. Add chicken pieces, season with a small pinch of salt and pepper, and cook 6–8 minutes until just cooked through.
  7. Stir in dried thyme, dried rosemary, remaining salt, and black pepper.
  8. Steam or microwave broccoli florets 2–3 minutes until crisp-tender; drain well.
  9. In a large mixing bowl, combine cooked wild rice, chicken-mushroom mixture, and broccoli. Let cool 2–3 minutes, then fold in Greek yogurt until creamy.
  10. Transfer mixture to the prepared baking dish and spread into an even layer.
  11. Sprinkle reduced-fat Swiss or mozzarella cheese evenly over the top.
  12. Bake uncovered 20–25 minutes until bubbly and lightly golden on top.
  13. Let casserole rest 10 minutes before cutting into portions for Frank, Susan, and Elise.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~590 kcal, ~50 g protein, ~58 g carbs, ~17 g fat.
  • Susan: ~445 kcal, ~37 g protein, ~44 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
  • Elise: ~445 kcal, ~37 g protein, ~44 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
