Egg White & Chickpea Crunch Salad

Meat-light salad with egg whites, chickpeas, and crisp veggies over greens with a creamy yogurt-herb dressing for a full lunch.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 10 minutes
  • Total time: 30 minutes

Ingredients

  • 10 large egg whites
  • 3/4 cup canned chickpeas
  • 6 cups mixed salad greens
  • 3/4 cup cucumber
  • 1/2 cup carrot
  • 1 red bell pepper
  • 6 tbsp 2% plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 1/2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp dried parsley
  • 1/4 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp kosher

Preparation Steps

  1. Chop the red bell pepper and measure out 1/3 cup for the recipe. Refrigerate any remaining pepper.
  2. If using whole eggs, separate the whites from the yolks (reserve yolks for another use). Lightly coat a nonstick skillet with a little oil spray if needed. Add the egg whites and cook over medium heat, stirring gently, until just set and tender. Remove from heat, let cool slightly, then chop into bite-size pieces. You can also use carton egg whites cooked the same way.
  3. Rinse and drain the chickpeas well. Rinse and dry the salad greens, cucumber, carrot, and bell pepper. Chop or shred as directed.
  4. In a small bowl or jar, whisk together the Greek yogurt, olive oil, lemon juice, Dijon mustard, garlic powder, dried parsley or dill, black pepper, and salt until smooth. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.
  5. In a large mixing bowl, combine the salad greens, chopped cucumber, shredded carrot, and diced bell pepper.
  6. Add the chopped cooked egg whites and the drained chickpeas to the bowl with the vegetables.
  7. Pour most of the yogurt-herb dressing over the salad (reserve a tablespoon or so). Toss gently until everything is lightly coated.
  8. Add about 2 cups of the salad mixture to a large bowl or container for Frank, ensuring his portion includes roughly 4 egg whites and 1/3 cup chickpeas. Add a teaspoon or two of any reserved dressing on top if desired.
  9. Divide the remaining salad between two containers for Susan and Elise, about 1 1/2 cups each, making sure each has about 3 egg whites and 1/4 cup chickpeas. Drizzle with remaining dressing.
  10. Serve immediately or refrigerate tightly covered for up to 24 hours. Keep chilled for work or school lunches.
