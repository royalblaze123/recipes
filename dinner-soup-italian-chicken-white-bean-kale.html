Dinner Soup Italian Chicken White Bean Kale

Chefy Italian-style chicken soup with white beans and kale—big flavor, high protein, and a complete dinner in one bowl.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 15 minutes
  • Cook time: 35 minutes
  • Total time: 50 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 2 lb boneless skinless chicken breast
  • 1 carton low sodium chicken broth
  • 1 each yellow onion
  • 1 bunch celery
  • 1 lb bag carrots
  • 6 cloves garlic
  • 1 bunch kale
  • 1 can cannellini beans
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 jar tomato paste
  • 1 bottle extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 jar Italian seasoning
  • 1 jar dried thyme
  • 1 jar crushed red pepper
  • 1 jar black pepper
  • 1 jar parmesan cheese
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Prep veg: dice 1/2 the onion, slice 3 celery stalks, and slice 2 carrots into thin coins. Mince 3 garlic cloves. Strip kale leaves from stems and chop leaves (use about 4 packed cups; refrigerate remaining kale).
    2. Start base: heat a large pot over medium heat. Add 2 tsp olive oil, then add onion, celery, carrots, and garlic. Cook 6 minutes, stirring, until softened.
    3. Season: stir in 2 tsp Italian seasoning, 1 tsp dried thyme, 1/8 tsp crushed red pepper, and black pepper.
    4. Add liquid: add the carton of low sodium chicken broth plus 2 cups water. Stir in 1 tbsp tomato paste until dissolved.
    5. Add chicken: add chicken breasts. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer. Cover and simmer 18–20 minutes, until chicken reaches 165°F.
    6. Shred chicken: transfer chicken to a plate, shred, and return shredded chicken to the pot.
    7. Beans + tomatoes: drain and rinse cannellini beans. Add beans and the can of diced tomatoes. Simmer 8 minutes.
    8. Kale finish: add chopped kale and simmer 4–5 minutes until wilted but still bright.
    9. Serve: ladle into bowls and sprinkle a measured 1 tbsp parmesan total across all bowls.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – About 3 1/2 cups soup.


      Susan – About 2 2/3 cups soup.


      Elise – About 2 2/3 cups soup.


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Use a gentle simmer for the chicken so it stays tender when shredded.


      Add kale at the end so it stays bright and not overcooked.


      If you want a thicker soup, mash a few spoonfuls of beans against the pot and stir back in.
