Turkey Chickpea Chili

NOTES: Each chili is designed as a full meal with lean protein, smart carbs, and plenty of vegetables. Use low-sodium, no-sugar-added canned goods, avoid added sugars, and keep portions aligned with your goals. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total pot); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 12 oz lean ground turkey (93%+ lean)
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced or 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 can (15 oz) no-salt-added chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) no-salt-added diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 1/2 tsp mild chili powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Dice onion and bell pepper into small, even pieces.
  2. Heat olive oil in a soup pot over medium heat for 1–2 minutes.
  3. Add ground turkey and cook 5–6 minutes, breaking it into small pieces, until browned and no longer pink.
  4. Add diced onion, bell pepper, and garlic. Cook 4–5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened.
  5. Stir in chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, and black pepper. Cook 1 minute.
  6. Add chickpeas, diced tomatoes with juice, and chicken broth. Stir well.
  7. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer 20–25 minutes, stirring once or twice, until chili thickens and flavors meld.
  8. Taste and adjust seasoning. Ladle into three bowls, giving Frank the largest portion. Serve with a side of steamed broccoli or green beans if desired.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~620 kcal, ~47 g protein, ~55 g carbs, ~16 g fat.
  • Susan: ~465 kcal, ~35 g protein, ~41 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
  • Elise: ~465 kcal, ~35 g protein, ~41 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
