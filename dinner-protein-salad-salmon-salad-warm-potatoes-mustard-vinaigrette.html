Salmon Salad + Warm Potatoes (Mustard Vinaigrette)

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 645 kcal • P 55g • C 50g • F 22g • Sat 5g • Na 620mg • Fiber 10g
Susan/Elise (each): 484 kcal • P 41g • C 38g • F 16g • Sat 4g • Na 465mg • Fiber 8g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.25 lb salmon fillets
  • 1.50 lb baby potatoes
  • 1 bag baby spinach
  • 1 each lemon
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • garlic powder
  • onion powder
  • smoked paprika
  • Italian seasoning
  • ground cumin
  • chili powder
  • red pepper flakes
  • extra-virgin olive oil
  • balsamic vinegar
  • apple cider vinegar
  • Dijon mustard

Steps

  1. Oven: heat to 425°F.
  2. Potatoes: halve and roast with 2 tsp olive oil, black pepper, garlic powder, modest kosher salt 22–26 minutes.
  3. Salmon: season with black pepper, garlic powder, Italian seasoning, and lemon juice. Bake 8–10 minutes until flaky; rest 3 minutes.
  4. Vinaigrette: whisk 2 tbsp Dijon mustard, 2 tbsp lemon juice, 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar, 2 tbsp olive oil, black pepper, and a small pinch of kosher salt.
  5. Salad: toss spinach with vinaigrette, then top with warm potatoes and salmon.
  6. Serve per Notes.

Notes

Warm potato + salmon salad that feels like a real dinner plate. Portions (per person): Frank: 4 cups salad + 6 oz salmon + 1 cup potatoes Susan: 3 cups salad + 4 oz salmon + 3/4 cup potatoes Elise: 3 cups salad + 4 oz salmon + 3/4 cup potatoes Tips (recipe-specific): Dress spinach lightly; warm potatoes and salmon add the comfort. Don’t overbake salmon—pull when it flakes easily.
