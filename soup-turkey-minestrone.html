Lean Turkey Minestrone Soup (Stovetop)

NOTES: Designed as a complete meal soup using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled starch. Use low-sodium broth and avoid extra salt when possible to support blood pressure and kidney health. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~2 cups); Susan: medium portion (~1.5 cups); Elise: medium portion (~1.5 cups).

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 lb 93% lean ground turkey
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) no-salt-added diced tomatoes
  • 5 cups low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 can (15 oz) no-salt-added kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) no-salt-added cannellini beans, drained and rinsed
  • 3/4 cup small whole-wheat pasta (like ditalini)
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 1/2 tsp dried thyme
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 2 cups chopped baby spinach

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large soup pot over medium heat.
  2. Add ground turkey and cook 6–8 minutes, breaking into crumbles, until no pink remains. Transfer turkey to a plate and set aside.
  3. In the same pot, add diced onion, carrots, celery, and red bell pepper. Cook 5–6 minutes until vegetables begin to soften.
  4. Stir in minced garlic and cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Add diced tomatoes with juices, low-sodium broth, and water and stir.
  6. Add kidney beans, cannellini beans, and whole-wheat pasta.
  7. Stir in dried oregano, dried basil, dried thyme, salt, and black pepper.
  8. Return cooked turkey to the pot and stir to combine.
  9. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer 15–18 minutes, stirring occasionally, until pasta is al dente and vegetables are tender.
  10. Stir in chopped baby spinach and cook 2–3 minutes until wilted.
  11. Taste and adjust seasoning with a small pinch more salt or pepper if needed.
  12. Remove from heat and let rest a few minutes before portioning for Frank, Susan, and Elise.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~520 kcal, ~43 g protein, ~60 g carbs, ~10 g fat.
  • Susan: ~390 kcal, ~32 g protein, ~45 g carbs, ~8 g fat.
  • Elise: ~390 kcal, ~32 g protein, ~45 g carbs, ~8 g fat.
