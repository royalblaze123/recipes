Lentil Chili (Fiber Engine)

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 620 kcal • P 60g • C 55g • F 14g • Sat 3g • Na 670mg • Fiber 16g
Susan/Elise (each): 465 kcal • P 45g • C 41g • F 10g • Sat 2g • Na 502mg • Fiber 12g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.50 lb boneless skinless chicken breast
  • 1 cup dry brown lentils
  • 1 can no-salt-added diced tomatoes
  • 1 carton low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 each onion
  • 1 each bell pepper
  • extra-virgin olive oil
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • chili powder
  • ground cumin
  • garlic powder
  • onion powder
  • smoked paprika
  • Italian seasoning
  • crushed red pepper
  • bay leaves
  • bay leaves

Steps

  1. Pot: heat 2 tsp olive oil. Cook diced onion + diced bell pepper 6–8 minutes until browned.
  2. Chicken: add diced chicken; cook 8–10 minutes until 165°F.
  3. Season with chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, onion powder, smoked paprika, kosher salt, black pepper.
  4. Lentils: rinse 1 cup dry brown lentils. Add to pot with 3 cups low-sodium broth.
  5. Tomatoes: Can size note—use a standard 14.5 oz can no-salt-added diced tomatoes.. Add 1 can.
  6. Add 1 bay leaf. Simmer uncovered 22–28 minutes until lentils are tender and chili is thick. Remove bay leaf.
  7. Portion per Notes.

Notes

Thick chili powered by lentils—big comfort, big fiber. Portions (per person): Frank: About 2 1/2 cups chili Susan: About 2 cups chili Elise: About 2 cups chili Tips (recipe-specific): Simmer uncovered; lentils help thicken naturally. If you want it thicker, let it rest 10 minutes before portioning.
