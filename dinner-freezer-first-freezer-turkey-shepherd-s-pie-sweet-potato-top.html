Freezer Turkey Shepherd’s Pie (Sweet Potato Top)

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 650 kcal • P 58g • C 60g • F 16g • Sat 3g • Na 700mg • Fiber 12g
Susan/Elise (each): 488 kcal • P 44g • C 45g • F 12g • Sat 2g • Na 525mg • Fiber 9g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.75 lb 93% lean ground turkey
  • 2.00 lb sweet potatoes
  • 1 bag frozen mixed vegetables
  • 1 carton low-sodium chicken broth
  • cornstarch
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • garlic powder
  • onion powder
  • smoked paprika
  • Italian seasoning
  • ground cumin
  • chili powder
  • red pepper flakes
  • extra-virgin olive oil
  • low-sodium chicken broth
  • cornstarch

Steps

  1. Oven: heat to 400°F.
  2. Sweet potato mash: microwave sweet potatoes until tender; mash with a splash of skim milk (optional) and black pepper; add a modest pinch of kosher salt.
  3. Filling: brown turkey with seasonings (garlic powder, onion powder, black pepper, modest salt) 7–9 minutes.
  4. Veg: add mixed vegetables; cook 5 minutes until heated and water reduces.
  5. Gravy: add 1 cup low-sodium broth. Whisk 1 tbsp cornstarch into 1/4 cup cold water; stir in; simmer 2–3 minutes until thick.
  6. Build: spread filling into baking dish; top with sweet potato mash; rake with fork.
  7. Bake 22–26 minutes; rest 10 minutes.
  8. Freeze: cool fully; portion; freeze. Reheat covered 350°F 30–40 minutes.

Notes

Shepherd’s pie comfort with sweet potato topping that freezes and reheats well. Portions (per person): Frank: About 2 1/2 cups (heaping scoop) Susan: About 2 cups (scoop) Elise: About 2 cups (scoop) Tips (recipe-specific): Thicken the filling before baking so it doesn’t get watery after thawing. Rake the top so it browns slightly and tastes ‘baked,’ not steamed.
