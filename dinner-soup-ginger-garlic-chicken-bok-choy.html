Dinner Soup Ginger Garlic Chicken Bok Choy

A mild, chefy ginger-garlic chicken soup with bok choy and rice noodles—bright and satisfying without heavy spice.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 15 minutes
  • Cook time: 25 minutes
  • Total time: 40 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 2 lb boneless skinless chicken breast
  • 1 carton low sodium chicken broth
  • 1 knob fresh ginger
  • 6 cloves garlic
  • 1 bunch scallions
  • 2 each bok choy
  • 1 bag rice noodles
  • 1 bottle extra virgin olive oil
  • 3 tbsp low sodium soy sauce
  • 1 each lime
  • 1 jar black pepper
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Prep aromatics: mince 3 garlic cloves and grate 1 tbsp ginger. Slice scallions (use 4 scallions; refrigerate remaining).
    2. Prep greens: slice bok choy (use both bok choy) and separate white stems from green leaves.
    3. Prep chicken: cut chicken into 1-inch chunks.
    4. Start base: heat a large pot over medium heat. Add 2 tsp olive oil, then add garlic, ginger, and scallion whites. Cook 60 seconds until fragrant.
    5. Add broth: add the carton of low sodium chicken broth plus 3 cups water and 3 tbsp low sodium soy sauce. Bring to a boil.
    6. Cook chicken: add chicken and simmer 10 minutes until chicken reaches 165°F.
    7. Bok choy stems: add bok choy stems and simmer 3 minutes.
    8. Noodles + leaves: add rice noodles and bok choy leaves and simmer 4–5 minutes until noodles are tender.
    9. Finish: squeeze in 1 tsp lime juice and stir in scallion greens. Serve immediately.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – About 3 1/2 cups soup.


      Susan – About 2 2/3 cups soup.


      Elise – About 2 2/3 cups soup.


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Add noodles near the end so they don’t get mushy.


      Separate bok choy stems and leaves so everything finishes at the right texture.


      Keep lime minimal at dinner—just enough to brighten without being citrus-heavy.
