Cajun Shrimp + Sweet Potatoes + Broccoli

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 645 kcal • P 55g • C 55g • F 18g • Sat 3g • Na 700mg • Fiber 10g
Susan/Elise (each): 484 kcal • P 41g • C 41g • F 14g • Sat 2g • Na 525mg • Fiber 8g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.50 lb shrimp
  • 2.00 lb sweet potatoes
  • 1 bag frozen broccoli florets
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • garlic powder
  • onion powder
  • smoked paprika
  • Italian seasoning
  • ground cumin
  • chili powder
  • red pepper flakes
  • extra-virgin olive oil
  • low-sodium soy sauce
  • hot sauce
  • Dijon mustard
  • balsamic vinegar
  • apple cider vinegar
  • cornstarch

Steps (Cosori dual-basket)

  1. Preheat Cosori to 400°F. Basket A: sweet potatoes. Basket B: shrimp later. Use Sync Finish.
  2. Sweet potatoes (Basket A): cube and toss with 2 tsp olive oil, 2 tsp chili powder, 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1 tsp garlic powder, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, black pepper. Air fry 18–22 minutes with Shake Reminder.
  3. Shrimp (Basket B): toss shrimp with 2 tsp olive oil, 2 tsp chili powder, 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1 tsp garlic powder, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, black pepper. Air fry 6–8 minutes at 400°F; shake once.
  4. Broccoli: microwave broccoli, drain/pat dry. Toss with 2 tsp olive oil, 1 tsp garlic powder, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, black pepper; crisp 6 minutes at 400°F if space or roast 425°F 10–12 minutes.
  5. Serve per Notes.

Notes

Big cajun flavor with sweet-potato balance and a high-volume veggie side. Portions (per person): Frank: 6 oz shrimp + 1 cup sweet potatoes + 1 cup broccoli Susan: 5 oz protein + 1 cup sweet potatoes + 3/4 cup veg Elise: 5 oz protein + 1 cup sweet potatoes + 3/4 cup veg Tips (recipe-specific): Put shrimp in late; overcooking is the only way this gets dry. Pat broccoli dry before crisping.
