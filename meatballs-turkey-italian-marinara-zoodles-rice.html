Italian Turkey Meatballs with Measured Marinara and Spinach over Brown Rice

Prep: 22 min   Cook: 24 min   Total: 46 min

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lb lean ground turkey 93%
  • 1 egg
  • 0.25 bag whole wheat panko crumbs
  • 0.5 jar no-salt-added marinara sauce
  • 0.5 cup dry brown rice
  • 1 bag spinach
  • 1 onion
  • olive oil
  • Italian seasoning
  • garlic powder
  • black pepper
  • salt substitute

Hard rules enforced: Dinner validations (calories 600–650 Frank, sat fat ≤8g, sodium ≤700mg, fiber ≥10g, added sugar ≤6g). Quantities appear before items; fresh meat/poultry/fish use weights. Condiments/spices/seasonings have no measurements in Ingredients; all measurements are in Steps. No olives, no squash, no Brussels sprouts, no zucchini, no tofu/tempeh, no yogurt in dinners.

Step-by-step Instructions

  1. Cook rice: cook 1/2 cup dry brown rice with 1 cup water; simmer 35–40 minutes; rest 5 minutes.
  2. Meatballs: mix turkey + 1 egg + 1/3 cup panko + 2 tsp Italian seasoning + 1 tsp garlic powder + 1/2 tsp black pepper + 1/2 tsp salt substitute + 2 tbsp finely minced onion.
  3. Form 18 meatballs. Air fry at 400°F for 10–12 minutes, shaking once, until 165°F.
  4. Sauce: warm 1/2 jar marinara in a saucepan with 2 tbsp water. Add meatballs and simmer 5 minutes.
  5. Spinach: stir 1 bag spinach into sauce and cook 1–2 minutes until wilted.
  6. Serve meatballs and sauce over brown rice. Portion: Frank gets 7 meatballs; Susan 5; Elise 5 (use the remaining 1 as the ‘buffer’ if sizes vary).
  7. Adjust rice servings so Frank gets the largest scoop; Susan/Elise ~75%.
