Lunch Sandwich Chicken Caesar Yogurt Romaine

Chicken Caesar-style sandwiches with a Greek-yogurt Caesar spread and packed romaine for full lunch volume in the sandwich.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 12 minutes
  • Cook time: 0 minutes
  • Total time: 12 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 6 slices whole wheat bread
  • 18 slices deli chicken breast
  • 3 slices provolone cheese
  • 1 container nonfat Greek yogurt
  • 1 bottle Caesar dressing
  • 1 package romaine hearts
  • 2 each tomatoes
  • 1 each cucumber
  • 1 jar black pepper
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Set up: lay out 6 slices whole wheat bread (3 sandwiches).
    2. Spread: measure 3 tbsp Greek yogurt and 1 tbsp Caesar dressing into a bowl and mix. Use all of this spread for the sandwiches; refrigerate remaining dressing.
    3. Veg: tear romaine. Measure out 6 packed cups romaine for these sandwiches; refrigerate remaining romaine.
    4. Slice: slice cucumber and tomatoes. Use about 1/2 cucumber and 1 tomato for these sandwiches; refrigerate the rest.
    5. Build (Frank): 2 slices bread + 8 slices deli chicken + 1 slice provolone + ~2 cups romaine + cucumber + tomato + ~1 tbsp Caesar-yogurt spread.
    6. Build (Susan): 2 slices bread + 5 slices deli chicken + 1 slice provolone + ~2 cups romaine + cucumber + tomato + ~1 tbsp Caesar-yogurt spread.
    7. Build (Elise): 2 slices bread + 5 slices deli chicken + 1 slice provolone + ~2 cups romaine + cucumber + tomato + ~1 tbsp Caesar-yogurt spread.
    8. Close sandwiches, press gently, and wrap.
      9. 

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – 1 whole sandwich: 8 slices chicken, 1 slice provolone, ~2 cups romaine, cucumber + tomato, ~1 tbsp Caesar-yogurt spread.


      Susan – 1 whole sandwich: 5 slices chicken, 1 slice provolone, ~2 cups romaine, cucumber + tomato, ~1 tbsp Caesar-yogurt spread.


      Elise – 1 whole sandwich: 5 slices chicken, 1 slice provolone, ~2 cups romaine, cucumber + tomato, ~1 tbsp Caesar-yogurt spread.


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Keep Caesar dressing measured—use Greek yogurt for most of the creamy texture.


      Pack romaine tight and press the sandwich so it doesn’t slide.


      If assembling early, keep tomato slices between romaine layers to reduce sogginess.
