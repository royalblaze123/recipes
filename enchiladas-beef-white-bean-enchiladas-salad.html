Beef & White Bean Enchiladas + Salad

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 630 kcal • P 58g • C 38g • F 22g
Susan/Elise (each): 472 kcal • P 44g • C 28g • F 16g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.50 lb 93% lean ground beef
  • 1 package whole-grain small tortillas
  • 1 can cannellini beans
  • 1 can mild red enchilada sauce
  • 1/2 bag reduced-fat shredded Mexican cheese blend
  • 1 bag spring mix
  • 1 each cucumber
  • 8 tsp light salad dressing
  • 2 tsp chili powder
  • 2 tsp cumin
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp kosher salt

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Lightly coat a 9x13-inch baking dish with nonstick spray.
  2. Cook beef: brown ground beef in a nonstick skillet over medium-high 7–9 minutes.
  3. Beans: can size note—use a standard 15 oz can cannellini beans. Drain and rinse. Measure out 1 1/2 cups; refrigerate any remainder.
  4. Season: add chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, smoked paprika, black pepper, and kosher salt to the beef. Stir 60 seconds.
  5. Add beans and 1/3 cup water from the tap (do not add water to Ingredients). Simmer 2–3 minutes until thick.
  6. Warm enchilada sauce: heat the can over medium-low 4–5 minutes until steaming.
  7. Tortillas: warm tortillas 15–20 seconds per side.
  8. Assembly: spread 1/2 cup sauce in the dish. Fill tortillas with beef-bean mix plus a pinch of cheese; roll and place seam-side down.
  9. Top: pour remaining sauce and sprinkle remaining cheese. Bake 14–18 minutes until bubbly.
  10. Salad: slice cucumber. Toss spring mix with cucumber and dressing right before serving.
  11. Portion: Frank 4 enchiladas; Susan 3; Elise 3.

Notes

Beef enchiladas with cannellini beans for extra fiber, served with a simple salad. Portions (per person): Frank: 4 enchiladas (4 tortillas) + 1/3 of the side/veg Susan: 3 enchiladas (3 tortillas) + 1/4 of the side/veg Elise: 3 enchiladas (3 tortillas) + 1/4 of the side/veg
