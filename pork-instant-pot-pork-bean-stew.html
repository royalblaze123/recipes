Instant Pot Pork & White Bean Stew

NOTES: Pressure-cooked pork stew with white beans, carrots, and celery. This is a full-plate pork dinner built around lean pork tenderloin/loin or 93% lean ground pork, smart carbs, and plenty of non-starchy vegetables. Uses olive or avocado oil only, keeps sodium moderate, and avoids added sugars to support A1c, cholesterol, blood pressure, and PPPD-friendly eating. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

PORTION SIZES (per person): Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 1 pound lean pork shoulder or loin, trimmed and cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 cup dry navy or cannellini beans, rinsed (pre-soaked if desired)
  • 2 medium carrots, sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 4 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried rosemary, crumbled
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set the Instant Pot Pro to Sauté and add olive oil.
  2. Add pork cubes and sear 4–5 minutes until lightly browned.
  3. Add onion, carrots, and celery and cook 3–4 minutes until slightly softened.
  4. Stir in garlic, thyme, rosemary, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper and cook 1 minute.
  5. Add beans and broth, stirring to combine and scraping up any browned bits from the bottom.
  6. Cancel Sauté. Lock the lid, set the valve to Sealing, and cook on High Pressure for 25 minutes (for pre-soaked beans) or 35 minutes (for unsoaked beans).
  7. Allow a 10-minute natural release, then quick-release any remaining pressure. Stir and let stew rest a few minutes to thicken slightly.
  8. Taste and adjust seasoning with a pinch of salt or extra herbs if needed, keeping overall sodium moderate.
  9. Divide the finished meal into three plates or containers: give Frank roughly 1/3 of the total volume, and split the remaining 2/3 between Susan and Elise as slightly smaller but still generous portions.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~500 kcal, ~46 g protein, ~34 g carbs, ~16 g fat.
  • Susan: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
  • Elise: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
