Black Bean Sweet Potato Veggie Chili

NOTES: Each chili is designed as a full meal with lean protein, smart carbs, and plenty of vegetables. Use low-sodium, no-sugar-added canned goods, avoid added sugars, and keep portions aligned with your goals. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total pot); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 1 can (15 oz) no-salt-added black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and diced small
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup frozen corn kernels, no salt added
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced or 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 can (15 oz) no-salt-added crushed tomatoes
  • 2 cups low-sodium vegetable broth
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 1/2 tsp mild chili powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Dice onion, bell pepper, and sweet potatoes into small, even pieces.
  2. Heat olive oil in a soup pot over medium heat for 1–2 minutes.
  3. Add onion and bell pepper. Cook 4–5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened.
  4. Add garlic and cook 1 minute, stirring, until fragrant.
  5. Stir in chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, and black pepper. Cook 1 minute.
  6. Add sweet potatoes, black beans, corn, crushed tomatoes, and vegetable broth. Stir well.
  7. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer 25–30 minutes, stirring once or twice, until sweet potatoes are tender and chili is thick.
  8. Taste and adjust seasoning. Ladle into three bowls, giving Frank the largest portion. Serve with a side of steamed green beans or a small salad.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~640 kcal, ~32 g protein, ~95 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
  • Susan: ~480 kcal, ~24 g protein, ~71 g carbs, ~10 g fat.
  • Elise: ~480 kcal, ~24 g protein, ~71 g carbs, ~10 g fat.
