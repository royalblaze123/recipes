Chicken Potato Corn Chowder

NOTES: This chowder is designed as a full meal in a bowl with lean protein, smart carbs, and 1–2 cups of vegetables per person. Use low-sodium broth and ingredients, avoid added sugars, and keep portions aligned with your goals. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total pot); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 12 oz boneless skinless chicken breast, diced into 1/2-inch cubes
  • 2 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 cup 2% milk or unsweetened almond milk
  • 1/2 cup plain 2% Greek yogurt
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 2 stalks celery, diced
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and diced small
  • 1 cup frozen corn kernels, no salt added
  • 1/2 tsp dried thyme
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Dice chicken breast into small, even cubes so it cooks quickly and evenly.
  2. Heat olive oil in a soup pot over medium heat for 1–2 minutes.
  3. Add diced chicken and cook 5–6 minutes, stirring occasionally, until no longer pink on the outside.
  4. Add diced onion and celery to the pot. Cook another 4–5 minutes until vegetables are softened.
  5. Stir in diced potatoes and corn. Add chicken broth, thyme, garlic powder, and black pepper.
  6. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer 12–15 minutes until potatoes are tender and chicken is cooked through.
  7. Whisk together milk and Greek yogurt in a small bowl, then stir into the pot. Warm on low heat 3–4 minutes without boiling.
  8. Taste and adjust pepper as desired. Ladle into three bowls, giving Frank the largest portion of chicken and potatoes.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~600 kcal, ~48 g protein, ~55 g carbs, ~16 g fat.
  • Susan: ~450 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~41 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
  • Elise: ~450 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~41 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
