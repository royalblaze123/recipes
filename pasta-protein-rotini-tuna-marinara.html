Protein+ Rotini Tuna Marinara

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 679 kcal • P 62g • C 71g • F 18g
Susan/Elise (each): 509 kcal • P 46g • C 53g • F 14g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1/3 box Barilla Protein+ rotini
  • 2 cans tuna
  • 0.62 jar no-sugar-added marinara sauce
  • 4 tsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 tbsp grated parmesan
  • 2 each bell peppers
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 tsp Italian seasoning
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp kosher salt

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil for the Barilla Protein+ rotini. (Use enough water to fully cover pasta; do not add water to Ingredients.)
  2. Can size note: use two 5 oz cans tuna. Drain well. Set aside.
  3. Dice the bell peppers. Cook 3–5 minutes until tender-crisp.
  4. Jar size note: assume a standard 24 oz jar marinara. Measure out what you need for this recipe; refrigerate the remainder.
  5. Add marinara to the skillet and simmer 5–7 minutes until hot and slightly thickened.
  6. Cook Barilla Protein+ rotini to al dente per box time. Reserve 1 cup pasta water, then drain.
  7. Toss pasta into the sauce/skillet. Add reserved pasta water 1–2 tbsp at a time until the sauce clings well.
  8. Portion into bowls using the Portions guidance in Notes.

Notes

Tuna marinara rotini with garlic-parmesan finish. Built on Barilla Protein+ for a higher-protein pasta plate with close-approx nutrition. Portions (per person): Frank: 2 oz dry Barilla Protein+ rotini + 1/3 of the skillet/sauce Susan: 1.5 oz dry Barilla Protein+ rotini + 1/4 of the skillet/sauce Elise: 1.5 oz dry Barilla Protein+ rotini + 1/4 of the skillet/sauce
