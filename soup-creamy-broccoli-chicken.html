Creamy Broccoli Chicken Soup (Stovetop, Light)

NOTES: Designed as a complete meal soup using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled starch. Use low-sodium broth and avoid extra salt when possible to support blood pressure and kidney health. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~2 cups); Susan: medium portion (~1.5 cups); Elise: medium portion (~1.5 cups).

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups small broccoli florets
  • 1 medium carrot, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 4 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breast
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 cup plain 2% Greek yogurt
  • 1/2 cup shredded reduced-fat sharp cheddar cheese (optional but limited)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large soup pot over medium heat.
  2. Add diced onion and cook 4–5 minutes until softened.
  3. Stir in minced garlic and cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Add broccoli florets and sliced carrot and cook 3–4 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Pour in low-sodium chicken broth and water and stir.
  6. Place chicken breasts into the pot, submerging them in the liquid.
  7. Stir in dried thyme, smoked paprika, salt, and black pepper.
  8. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low, cover partially, and simmer 20–25 minutes until chicken is cooked through and broccoli is very tender.
  9. Remove chicken to a cutting board and let cool slightly, then shred with two forks.
  10. Use an immersion blender to partially blend the soup base, leaving some broccoli pieces whole for texture.
  11. Return shredded chicken to the pot and stir to combine.
  12. Remove pot from heat and let cool 2–3 minutes, then stir in Greek yogurt until fully incorporated.
  13. Sprinkle in reduced-fat cheddar cheese (if using) and stir until melted and smooth.
  14. Taste and adjust seasoning with additional pepper or a tiny pinch of salt if needed.
  15. Let rest a few minutes, then ladle into bowls for Frank, Susan, and Elise.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~460 kcal, ~48 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~16 g fat.
  • Susan: ~345 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~18 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
  • Elise: ~345 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~18 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
