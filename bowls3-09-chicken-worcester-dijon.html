Composed Chicken Bowl with Sweet Potatoes, Green Beans, and Worcester Dijon

Servings

3 bowls (Frank 1.0×, Susan 0.75×, Elise 0.75×).

Times

  • Prep time: 22 minutes
  • Cook time: 28 minutes
  • Total time: 50 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2.0 lb chicken breast
  • 2 sweet potatoes
  • 1 bag frozen green beans
  • 1 pack microgreens
  • 11 tsp olive oil
  • 6 tsp minced garlic
  • 2 tsp black pepper
  • 2 tsp salt substitute
  • 2 tsp dried parsley
  • 2 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 2 tsp black pepper

Steps

  1. Sweet potatoes: Peel and cube into 3/4-inch pieces (~6 cups). Toss with 3 tsp olive oil + 1 tsp pepper + 1 tsp salt substitute. Roast at 425°F for 20–25 minutes, stirring once, until browned and fork-tender.
  2. Vegetables: Roast per steps below to get browned edges (texture contrast matters).
  3. Green Beans: Toss the full bag with 4 tsp olive oil + 2 tsp minced garlic + 1 tsp pepper + 1 tsp salt substitute. Roast at 425°F for 12–15 minutes, stirring once, until hot with browned tips.
  4. Chicken/turkey: Slice/pound to even thickness. Sear in a skillet with 3 tsp olive oil 4–5 minutes per side to 165°F; rest 3 minutes then slice.
  5. Sauce: In a small bowl, whisk the Sauce ingredients (worcester dijon). Keep it measured—this is a finish, not a flood.
  6. Build bowls (assembly order): Add warm starch first, then vegetables, then sliced protein. Spoon sauce on top. Finish with microgreens after heat (they’re the ‘gourmet’ signal).
  7. Plate portions per Notes.

Notes

Portions (per person):

Frank – 1 bowl (8 oz cooked protein, 1 cup roasted sweet potatoes, 1 1/2 cups green beans, 1/2 cup microgreens, 2–3 tbsp sauce).

Susan – 1 bowl (6 oz cooked protein, 3/4 of Frank’s starch portion, 1 1/4 cups green beans, 1/3 cup microgreens, 2 tbsp sauce).

Elise – 1 bowl (6 oz cooked protein, 3/4 of Frank’s starch portion, 1 1/4 cups green beans, 1/3 cup microgreens, 2 tbsp sauce).

Tips (recipe-specific):

Build order matters: warm base → dry veg → protein → sauce → microgreens last so nothing turns soggy.

Keep sauce measured; the bowl should taste ‘finished,’ not ‘dressed.’

Storage + Reheat

  • Cool promptly; store components in airtight containers in the fridge up to 3–4 days (typical safety guidance).
  • Reheat grain/beans + protein 60–90 seconds in the microwave; reheat roasted veg 4–6 minutes at 350°F in the air fryer for texture; add sauce and microgreens after reheating.
