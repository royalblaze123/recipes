Instant Pot Beef, Brown Rice & Broccoli Bowls

NOTES: Pressure-cooked lean beef with brown rice and broccoli in a light garlic-ginger sauce. This is a full-plate beef dinner built around lean beef, smart carbs, and plenty of non-starchy vegetables. Uses olive oil or avocado oil only, keeps sodium moderate, and avoids added sugars to support A1c, cholesterol, blood pressure, and PPPD-friendly eating. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

PORTION SIZES (per person): Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 1 pound 93% lean ground beef or finely diced lean sirloin
  • 1 cup uncooked brown rice, rinsed
  • 2 cups low-sodium beef or chicken broth
  • 3 cups small broccoli florets
  • 1 medium carrot, finely diced
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp grated fresh ginger or 1 tsp ground ginger
  • 1 tbsp low-sodium tamari or soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp kosher salt (or to taste, keep light)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set the Instant Pot Pro to Sauté. Add olive oil, then the ground beef or diced sirloin. Cook, breaking up the meat, for 3–4 minutes until lightly browned. Drain off any excess fat if needed.
  2. Add the diced onion and carrot. Sauté for 2–3 minutes until slightly softened. Stir in the garlic and ginger and cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Add the rinsed brown rice to the pot and stir to coat with the aromatics and beef.
  4. Pour in the broth, tamari, pepper, and salt. Stir well, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot.
  5. Cancel Sauté. Lock the Instant Pot lid, set the valve to Sealing, and cook on High Pressure for 15 minutes.
  6. While the rice and beef cook, cut the broccoli into small florets.
  7. When the cook time is complete, allow a 5-minute natural release, then carefully quick-release any remaining pressure.
  8. Open the lid and quickly add the broccoli florets, stirring them into the hot rice and beef mixture.
  9. Replace the lid (no need to seal) and let the residual heat steam the broccoli on Keep Warm for 5–7 minutes until crisp-tender, stirring once or twice.
  10. Fluff the rice and beef mixture with a fork before serving.
  11. Taste and adjust seasoning with a pinch of salt or extra herbs if needed, keeping overall sodium moderate.
  12. Divide the finished meal into three plates or containers: give Frank roughly 1/3 of the total volume, and split the remaining 2/3 between Susan and Elise as slightly smaller but still generous portions.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~520 kcal, ~48 g protein, ~32 g carbs, ~18 g fat.
  • Susan: ~390 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
  • Elise: ~390 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
