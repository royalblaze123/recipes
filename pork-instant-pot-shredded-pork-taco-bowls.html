Instant Pot Shredded Pork Taco Bowls

NOTES: Pressure-cooked shredded pork with peppers and onions served over brown rice and lettuce. This is a full-plate pork dinner built around lean pork tenderloin/loin or 93% lean ground pork, smart carbs, and plenty of non-starchy vegetables. Uses olive or avocado oil only, keeps sodium moderate, and avoids added sugars to support A1c, cholesterol, blood pressure, and PPPD-friendly eating. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

PORTION SIZES (per person): Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 pounds pork shoulder or loin, trimmed of visible fat
  • 1 cup uncooked brown rice, rinsed
  • 2 cups low-sodium chicken broth, divided
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 1/2 tsp mild chili powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or less to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 2 cups shredded romaine or spring mix for serving

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set the Instant Pot Pro to Sauté. Add a splash of broth and sauté the sliced onion and peppers 3–4 minutes until slightly softened. Transfer to a plate.
  2. Season pork with chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, oregano, salt, and pepper. Add to the pot and sear 2–3 minutes per side.
  3. Stir in garlic, then pour in 1 1/2 cups broth. Cancel Sauté.
  4. Lock the lid, set the valve to Sealing, and cook on High Pressure for 35 minutes.
  5. While pork cooks, prepare brown rice on the stovetop using remaining 1/2 cup broth plus water as needed, keeping sodium moderate.
  6. Allow a 10-minute natural release, then quick-release any remaining pressure.
  7. Transfer pork to a cutting board, shred with two forks, and return to the cooking liquid along with sautéed peppers and onions. Let rest on Warm 5–10 minutes.
  8. Taste and adjust seasoning with a pinch of salt or extra herbs if needed, keeping overall sodium moderate.
  9. Divide the finished meal into three plates or containers: give Frank roughly 1/3 of the total volume, and split the remaining 2/3 between Susan and Elise as slightly smaller but still generous portions.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~500 kcal, ~46 g protein, ~34 g carbs, ~16 g fat.
  • Susan: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
  • Elise: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
