Pork, Mushroom & Quinoa Bowls

NOTES: Seared pork strips with mushrooms over quinoa and steamed green beans. This is a full-plate pork dinner built around lean pork tenderloin/loin or 93% lean ground pork, smart carbs, and plenty of non-starchy vegetables. Uses olive or avocado oil only, keeps sodium moderate, and avoids added sugars to support A1c, cholesterol, blood pressure, and PPPD-friendly eating. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

PORTION SIZES (per person): Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 1 pound pork tenderloin or loin, cut into thin strips
  • 8 oz cremini mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 3/4 cup uncooked quinoa, rinsed
  • 1 1/2 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 2 cups green beans or asparagus pieces
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Combine quinoa and broth in a small saucepan. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer 15 minutes or until liquid is absorbed. Remove from heat and let stand 5 minutes before fluffing.
  2. While quinoa cooks, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add pork strips and sear 3–4 minutes until just cooked through. Transfer to a plate.
  3. In the same skillet, add onion and mushrooms and cook 5–6 minutes until softened and browned.
  4. Stir in garlic, thyme, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper and cook 1 minute.
  5. Return pork and any juices to the skillet along with 2–3 tbsp water or broth. Simmer 2–3 minutes on low heat to warm through and create a light pan sauce.
  6. Steam green beans or asparagus separately for 4–5 minutes until crisp-tender.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning with a pinch of salt or extra herbs if needed, keeping overall sodium moderate.
  8. Divide the finished meal into three plates or containers: give Frank roughly 1/3 of the total volume, and split the remaining 2/3 between Susan and Elise as slightly smaller but still generous portions.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~500 kcal, ~46 g protein, ~34 g carbs, ~16 g fat.
  • Susan: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
  • Elise: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
