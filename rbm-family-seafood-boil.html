Family Seafood Boil (Pick-Your-Plate Style)

RBM

Ingredients

  • 2 lb baby potatoes, halved
  • 3 ears fresh corn on the cob, cut into thirds
  • 1 package (14 oz) smoked sausage or kielbasa, sliced
  • 1 lb raw medium shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 12 oz raw sea scallops
  • 1 lb fresh clams, scrubbed
  • 1 lb fresh mussels, debearded
  • 6 cups water
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 27 tsp unsalted butter, divided
  • 5 fresh garlic cloves, smashed
  • 1 fresh lemon, halved
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 4.5 tsp Old Bay seasoning
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 0.5 tsp black pepper
  • 0.5 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 4.5 tsp lemon juice
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Steps (Explicit)

  1. Prep shellfish (separate, before the pot): Scrub the fresh clams under cold running water. Remove beards from the fresh mussels. Discard any clams or mussels with cracked shells. Keep clams and mussels cold until cooking.
  2. Start the pot (together): In a very large pot add 6 cups water, 2 cups chicken broth, 9 tsp unsalted butter, 5 smashed garlic cloves, 1 halved lemon, 2 bay leaves, 4.5 tsp Old Bay seasoning, 1 tsp paprika, 0.5 tsp black pepper, and 0.5 tsp salt. Bring to a rolling boil.
  3. Cook potatoes (same pot): Add 2 lb baby potatoes. Boil uncovered 12 minutes.
  4. Cook corn and sausage (same pot): Add 3 ears corn and 1 package (14 oz) sliced smoked sausage. Boil 6 minutes.
  5. Steam clams and mussels (same pot, covered): Reduce to a gentle boil. Add 1 lb clams and 1 lb mussels. Cover and cook 5 minutes, until most shells open. Discard any that do not open.
  6. Steam scallops (same pot, covered): Add 12 oz sea scallops. Cover and cook 3 minutes.
  7. Steam shrimp last (same pot, covered): Add 1 lb shrimp. Cover and cook 2.5–3 minutes, just until shrimp are pink and opaque.
  8. Remove shrimp immediately (recommended): Use a slotted spoon to transfer shrimp to a bowl right away to prevent rubbery texture. Keep the bowl nearby for serving.
  9. Drain everything else (together): Pour the pot into a colander to drain completely. Discard bay leaves and lemon halves.
  10. Butter finish (separate): Melt the remaining 18 tsp unsalted butter. Stir in 1 tsp garlic powder and 4.5 tsp lemon juice.
  11. Toss and serve (together): Return drained potatoes, corn, sausage, clams, mussels, and scallops to the empty pot (off heat). Pour the butter finish over everything and toss gently. Sprinkle fresh parsley if using. Serve shrimp in a side bowl or mix back in at the table.

Notes

Portions: Frank (1.0×): Build a full plate with potatoes, corn, sausage, clams, mussels, scallops, and shrimp. Susan (0.75×): Potatoes + corn + sausage; seafood optional. Elise (0.75×): Potatoes + corn + sausage; seafood optional. Tips: For shrimp texture: chop cooked shrimp into bite-size pieces and toss in the butter finish; serve in a separate bowl so shrimp-sensitive eaters can skip it. For less “fishy” aroma: keep shrimp and scallops in the pot only for the stated time; remove shrimp immediately and do not let seafood sit in hot liquid.
