Roast & Finish Lean Beef Steak Strips with Mushrooms and Black Beans

Servings

3 plates (Frank 1.0×, Susan 0.75×, Elise 0.75×).

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 30 minutes
  • Total time: 50 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2.0 lb lean beef steak (top sirloin or top round), trimmed
  • 1 can canned black beans
  • 24 oz mushrooms
  • 11 tsp olive oil
  • 6 tsp minced garlic
  • 2 tsp black pepper
  • 2 tsp salt substitute
  • 2 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 tsp dried parsley
  • 2 tsp black pepper

Steps

  1. Black beans: Rinse and drain the can well. Warm with 1/2 cup water in a saucepan 4–5 minutes; keep warm.
  2. Oven: Heat to 425°F. Line a large sheet pan with parchment (use two pans if crowded).
  3. Mushrooms: Slice. Toss with 4 tsp olive oil + 2 tsp minced garlic + 1 tsp pepper + 1 tsp salt substitute. Roast at 425°F for 14–16 minutes, stirring once, until browned.
  4. Lean beef: Slice into strips. Toss with 3 tsp olive oil + minced garlic + pepper + salt substitute. Roast on a preheated sheet pan at 450°F for 10–12 minutes, stirring once, until browned.
  5. Finish (fresh lift): Immediately after roasting, toss/brush the protein and vegetables with the Finish ingredients. Add black pepper last.
  6. Plate portions per Notes.

Notes

Portions (per person):

Frank – 1 plate (8 oz cooked protein, 1/2 cup black beans, 1 1/2 cups mushrooms).

Susan – 1 plate (6 oz cooked protein, 3/4 of Frank’s starch portion, 1 1/4 cups mushrooms).

Elise – 1 plate (6 oz cooked protein, 3/4 of Frank’s starch portion, 1 1/4 cups mushrooms).

Tips (recipe-specific):

Roast hard with space; crowding is how gourmet becomes steamed sadness.

Finish with herbs/zest after the oven—heat dulls brightness, and brightness is the ‘restaurant’ signal.

Storage + Reheat

  • Cool promptly; store in airtight containers in the fridge up to 3–4 days (typical safety guidance).
  • Reheat on a sheet pan at 375°F for 8–10 minutes (best for roast texture), or microwave 60–90 seconds with a splash of water for moisture.
