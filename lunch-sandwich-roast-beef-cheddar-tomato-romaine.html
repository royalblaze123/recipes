Lunch Sandwich Roast Beef Cheddar Tomato Romaine

Roast beef and cheddar sandwiches with romaine and tomato for volume, plus a light mustard-yogurt spread to keep it lean.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 12 minutes
  • Cook time: 0 minutes
  • Total time: 12 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 6 slices whole wheat bread
  • 14 slices deli roast beef
  • 3 slices cheddar cheese
  • 1 container nonfat Greek yogurt
  • 1 jar Dijon mustard
  • 1 package romaine hearts
  • 2 each tomatoes
  • 1 each red onion
  • 1 jar black pepper
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Set up: lay out 6 slices whole wheat bread (3 sandwiches).
    2. Spread: mix 3 tbsp Greek yogurt with 2 tsp Dijon mustard and black pepper. Use all of this spread for the sandwiches; refrigerate remaining mustard and yogurt.
    3. Veg: tear romaine. Measure out 6 packed cups romaine for these sandwiches; refrigerate remaining romaine.
    4. Slice: slice tomato and a few thin rings of red onion. Use 1 tomato and about 1/4 onion for these sandwiches; refrigerate the rest.
    5. Build (Frank): 2 slices bread + 6 slices roast beef + 1 slice cheddar + ~2 cups romaine + tomato + onion + ~1 tbsp mustard-yogurt spread.
    6. Build (Susan): 2 slices bread + 4 slices roast beef + 1 slice cheddar + ~2 cups romaine + tomato + onion + ~1 tbsp mustard-yogurt spread.
    7. Build (Elise): 2 slices bread + 4 slices roast beef + 1 slice cheddar + ~2 cups romaine + tomato + onion + ~1 tbsp mustard-yogurt spread.
    8. Close sandwiches and wrap tightly.
      9. 

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – 1 whole sandwich: 6 slices roast beef, 1 slice cheddar, ~2 cups romaine, tomato + onion, ~1 tbsp mustard-yogurt spread.


      Susan – 1 whole sandwich: 4 slices roast beef, 1 slice cheddar, ~2 cups romaine, tomato + onion, ~1 tbsp mustard-yogurt spread.


      Elise – 1 whole sandwich: 4 slices roast beef, 1 slice cheddar, ~2 cups romaine, tomato + onion, ~1 tbsp mustard-yogurt spread.


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Use mustard-yogurt instead of mayo to keep calories and saturated fat controlled.


      If sodium runs high for the day, reduce cheese to 1/2 slice per person and add more romaine.


      Press the sandwich so the romaine stacks tight and holds together.
