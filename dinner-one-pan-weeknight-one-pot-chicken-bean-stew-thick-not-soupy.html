One-Pot Chicken & Bean Stew (Thick, Not Soupy)

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 640 kcal • P 60g • C 55g • F 16g • Sat 3g • Na 700mg • Fiber 12g
Susan/Elise (each): 480 kcal • P 45g • C 41g • F 12g • Sat 2g • Na 525mg • Fiber 9g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.75 lb boneless skinless chicken breast
  • 1 can white beans
  • 1 can no-salt-added diced tomatoes
  • 1 bag frozen mixed vegetables
  • low-sodium chicken broth
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • garlic powder
  • onion powder
  • smoked paprika
  • Italian seasoning
  • ground cumin
  • chili powder
  • red pepper flakes
  • extra-virgin olive oil
  • low-sodium chicken broth

Steps

  1. Chicken: dice chicken. Sauté with 2 tsp olive oil, 1 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp onion powder, black pepper, modest kosher salt 6–8 minutes.
  2. Base: add 1 can diced tomatoes (14.5 oz) plus 3 cups low-sodium broth. Simmer 10 minutes.
  3. Veg: add 2 cups frozen mixed vegetables; simmer 6 minutes.
  4. Beans: add 1 1/2 cups drained white beans (from a 15 oz can). Simmer 3 minutes.
  5. Thicken: simmer uncovered 3–5 minutes until stew is thick.
  6. Serve per Notes.

Notes

Cozy stew that stays thick and structured—beans add body and fiber. Portions (per person): Frank: About 2 1/2 cups (heaping bowl) Susan: About 2 cups (bowl) Elise: About 2 cups (bowl) Tips (recipe-specific): Simmer uncovered at the end so it thickens naturally. Add beans late so they don’t dissolve into mush.
