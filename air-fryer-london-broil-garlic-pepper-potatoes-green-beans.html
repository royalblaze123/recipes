Air Fryer London Broil with Potatoes and Green Beans

Garlic-pepper London broil strips with crispy potatoes and green beans—simple, lean, and built for dual-basket timing.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 25 minutes
  • Cook time: 18 minutes
  • Total time: 43 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 1 lb London broil
  • 2 each russet potatoes
  • 1 bag fresh green beans
  • 1 bottle extra virgin olive oil
  • 6 cloves garlic
  • 1 jar black pepper
  • 1 jar garlic powder
  • 1 jar Italian seasoning
  • 1 each lemon
  • 1 container nonfat Greek yogurt
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Prep potatoes: cut into wedges. Measure out about 3 cups wedges for this recipe; refrigerate remaining potato. Toss with 1 tbsp olive oil, garlic powder, and black pepper.
    2. Prep beef: slice London broil into thin strips across the grain. Toss with 2 tsp olive oil, minced garlic, Italian seasoning, and black pepper.
    3. Prep green beans: trim. Toss 1/2 bag green beans with 1 tsp olive oil and black pepper. Refrigerate remaining green beans.
    4. Cosori setup: preheat both baskets to 400°F for 3 minutes. Put potato wedges in Basket A.
    5. Basket A: cook potatoes 400°F for 16–18 minutes with shake reminder ON; shake at each reminder.
    6. Basket B (beef): cook beef strips 400°F for 6–8 minutes, shaking once, until cooked through. Rest 3 minutes.
    7. Basket B (green beans): cook green beans 400°F for 6–7 minutes, shaking once.
    8. Yogurt lemon drizzle: stir 1/4 cup Greek yogurt with 1 tsp lemon juice and a pinch of black pepper.
    9. Serve (40/30/30): plate beef with potatoes and green beans and add a light yogurt drizzle.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – Beef: largest share. Potato wedges: about 1 1/4 cups. Green beans: about 1 1/2 cups. Yogurt drizzle.


      Susan – Beef: medium share. Potato wedges: about 1 cup. Green beans: about 1 1/4 cups. Yogurt drizzle.


      Elise – Beef: medium share. Potato wedges: about 1 cup. Green beans: about 1 1/4 cups. Yogurt drizzle.


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Always slice London broil across the grain for tenderness.


      If beef browns too fast, drop Basket B to 375°F for the last 2 minutes.


      Cook green beans after beef so they stay crisp-tender.
