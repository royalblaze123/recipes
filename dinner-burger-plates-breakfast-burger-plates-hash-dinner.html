Breakfast Burger Plates + Hash (Dinner)

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 650 kcal • P 58g • C 58g • F 18g • Sat 7g • Na 690mg • Fiber 10g
Susan/Elise (each): 488 kcal • P 44g • C 44g • F 14g • Sat 5g • Na 518mg • Fiber 8g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.75 lb 93% lean ground beef
  • 4 each whole-wheat burger buns
  • 3 each eggs
  • 1.50 lb baby potatoes
  • 1 bag baby spinach
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • garlic powder
  • onion powder
  • smoked paprika
  • dried oregano
  • extra-virgin olive oil
  • mustard
  • ketchup
  • pickle relish
  • hot sauce
  • Worcestershire sauce

Steps

  1. Oven: heat to 425°F. Dice baby potatoes into 1/2-inch cubes. Toss with 2 tsp olive oil, 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper. Roast 22–26 minutes, stirring once.
  2. Patties: form 6 patties. Season with kosher salt and black pepper. Cook 4–5 minutes per side to 160°F.
  3. Eggs: fry or scramble 3 eggs in a nonstick pan. (Aim: 1 egg for Frank, 1 egg split between Susan/Elise as a topper.)
  4. Spinach: sauté 1 bag spinach in the egg pan 60–90 seconds with black pepper.
  5. Assemble: bun + patty + egg topper + spinach. Serve with hash. Portion per Notes.

Notes

Breakfast-for-dinner comfort with burger + hash. Portions (per person): Frank: 2 patties + 1 egg + 1 cup hash + spinach Susan: 1 patty + 1/2 egg (shared) + 3/4 cup hash + spinach Elise: 1 patty + 1/2 egg (shared) + 3/4 cup hash + spinach Tips (recipe-specific): Egg topper scratches the ‘diner’ itch without extra cheese. Roast hash until browned—color = flavor.
