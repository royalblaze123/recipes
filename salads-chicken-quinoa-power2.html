Chicken Quinoa Power Salad

High-protein chicken and quinoa salad with mixed greens and veggies for a full, packable lunch that fits Frank Household lunch targets.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 15 minutes
  • Cook time: 15 minutes (for quinoa and chicken if needed)
  • Total time: 30 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup dry quinoa
  • 10 oz cooked skinless chicken breast
  • 6 cups mixed salad greens
  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes
  • 3/4 cup cucumber
  • 1/2 cup carrot
  • 1 red onion
  • 6 tbsp 2% plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 1/2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/4 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp kosher

Preparation Steps

  1. Dice the red onion and measure out 1/4 cup for the recipe. Refrigerate any remaining onion.
  2. Rinse the dry quinoa under cool water in a fine-mesh strainer. Add it to a small pot with 1 cup of water. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 12–15 minutes until the water is absorbed and the quinoa is tender. Remove from heat, fluff with a fork, and let cool to room temperature.
  3. If not already cooked, season chicken breast lightly with salt and pepper and bake, grill, or sauté until cooked through (165°F internal), then let cool. Chop into bite-size pieces. Measure out about 10 oz cooked chicken for the salad.
  4. Rinse and dry the salad greens, grape tomatoes, cucumber, carrot, and onion. Tear or chop the greens if needed. Halve the tomatoes, chop the cucumber, shred the carrot, and thinly slice the onion if using.
  5. In a small bowl or jar, whisk together the Greek yogurt, olive oil, lemon juice, Dijon mustard, garlic powder, dried oregano, black pepper, and salt until smooth. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.
  6. In a large mixing bowl, combine the salad greens, grape tomatoes, cucumber, shredded carrot, and red onion. Add the cooled quinoa and chopped chicken.
  7. Drizzle most of the yogurt dressing over the salad (reserve a few teaspoons if you like extra on top). Toss gently until everything is lightly coated.
  8. Add about 2 cups of the salad mixture to a large bowl or meal-prep container for Frank, making sure his portion contains roughly 4 oz chicken and 1/3 cup cooked quinoa. Drizzle with an extra teaspoon or two of dressing if reserved.
  9. Divide the remaining salad between two medium bowls or containers for Susan and Elise, about 1 1/2 cups each, ensuring each has about 3 oz chicken and 1/4 cup cooked quinoa. Add a tiny splash of any remaining dressing if desired.
  10. Serve immediately, or cover and refrigerate the portions for up to 24 hours. Keep chilled until ready to take to work or school.

  • Susan (S · 0.75×): ~1 1/2 cups salad with ~3 oz chicken, ~1/4 cup cooked quinoa, ~3/4 cup greens/veggies, and ~2 tbsp dressing.
  • Elise (E · 0.75×): ~1 1/2 cups salad with ~3 oz chicken, ~1/4 cup cooked quinoa, ~3/4 cup greens/veggies, and ~2 tbsp dressing.
