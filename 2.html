Lean Chicken & Veggie Sheet Pan (Frank Household)

High-protein, lower-sodium sheet pan dinner with chicken breast, baby potatoes, and green beans.

Servings

3 servings (Frank, Susan, Elise).

Times

  • Prep time: 15 minutes
  • Cook time: 25 minutes
  • Total time: 40 minutes

Ingredients

  • 18 oz boneless skinless chicken breast
  • 18 oz baby potatoes
  • 12 oz fresh green beans
  • 1 1/2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • 1/4 tsp kosher or sea salt
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper

Preparation Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). Line a large rimmed sheet pan with parchment paper.
  2. Add halved baby potatoes to a large bowl. Drizzle with about 2 teaspoons olive oil and sprinkle with half of the garlic powder, paprika, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper. Toss to coat, then spread potatoes on one third of the sheet pan, cut side down.
  3. Add green beans to the same bowl. Drizzle with about 2 teaspoons olive oil and sprinkle with a little of the remaining seasoning mix. Toss, then arrange green beans on another third of the pan.
  4. Add chicken pieces to the bowl. Drizzle with the remaining olive oil and seasoning. Toss until chicken is evenly coated, then spread chicken on the final third of the sheet pan in a single layer.
  5. Roast for 20–25 minutes, stirring potatoes and green beans once halfway through, until chicken is cooked through (165°F / 74°C) and potatoes are fork-tender. Broil for 1–2 minutes at the end if you want extra browning.
  6. Turn off oven. Divide chicken, potatoes, and green beans into Frank, Susan, and Elise portions as listed below. Serve with optional lemon wedges.

Portion Sizes (Cooked, Approx.)

  • Frank (F · 1.0×): ~8 oz cooked chicken, ~8 oz potatoes, ~5 oz green beans
  • Susan (S · 0.75×): ~6 oz cooked chicken, ~6 oz potatoes, ~4 oz green beans
  • Elise (E · 0.5×): ~4 oz cooked chicken, ~4 oz potatoes, ~3 oz green beans

Notes (for you, outside AnyList)

Tips

  • Keep salt at or below 1/4 tsp total to stay near your sodium targets. You can season individual plates with a light sprinkle of salt-free seasoning if needed.
  • To lower carbs slightly, reduce potatoes by 2–3 oz and add extra green beans or another non-starchy veg.
  • All ingredients follow your household rules: lean protein, no added sugar, no zucchini / olives / Brussels sprouts / squash, baked not fried.
