Scallop Corn Potato Chowder

NOTES: This chowder is designed as a full meal in a bowl with lean protein, smart carbs, and 1–2 cups of vegetables per person. Use low-sodium broth and ingredients, avoid added sugars, and keep portions aligned with your goals. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total pot); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 1 lb sea scallops, cut in half if large
  • 3 cups low-sodium seafood or vegetable broth
  • 3/4 cup 2% milk or unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and diced small
  • 1 cup frozen corn kernels, no salt added
  • 1/2 tsp dried thyme
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Pat scallops dry with paper towels and cut in half if very large so they cook evenly.
  2. Heat olive oil in a soup pot over medium heat for 1–2 minutes.
  3. Add diced onion and cook 4–5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened.
  4. Stir in potatoes, corn, broth, thyme, garlic powder, and black pepper.
  5. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer 12–15 minutes until potatoes are tender.
  6. Add scallops to the pot and cook on low heat 4–5 minutes, stirring gently, until scallops are just opaque and firm.
  7. Stir in milk and warm another 2–3 minutes without boiling.
  8. Taste and adjust pepper. Ladle into three bowls, giving Frank the largest portion of scallops and potatoes.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~610 kcal, ~46 g protein, ~55 g carbs, ~18 g fat.
  • Susan: ~460 kcal, ~35 g protein, ~41 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
  • Elise: ~460 kcal, ~35 g protein, ~41 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
