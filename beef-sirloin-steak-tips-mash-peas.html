Sirloin Steak Tips with Garlic Mashed Potatoes & Green Peas

NOTES: Pan-seared sirloin steak tips served alongside light garlic mashed potatoes and steamed peas. This is a full-plate beef dinner built around lean beef, smart carbs, and plenty of non-starchy vegetables. Uses olive oil or avocado oil only, keeps sodium moderate, and avoids added sugars to support A1c, cholesterol, blood pressure, and PPPD-friendly eating. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

PORTION SIZES (per person): Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 1 1/4 pounds lean sirloin steak, cut into bite-size tips
  • 1 1/2 pounds Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 1 cup frozen green peas
  • 2 tbsp olive oil, divided
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup 1% milk or unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 tbsp light butter or 1 extra tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried thyme or Italian seasoning
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • 3/4 tsp kosher salt, divided (or to taste, keep moderate)
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper, divided

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Place potato chunks in a pot, cover with cool water, and add a light pinch of salt. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer and cook 12–15 minutes until fork-tender.
  2. During the last 4–5 minutes of potato cooking, place the frozen peas in a steamer basket over the potato pot or in a separate small saucepan with a splash of water. Steam until bright green and tender, then drain and set aside.
  3. Drain the potatoes well and return them to the warm pot. Add milk, light butter or extra olive oil, garlic powder, half of the salt, and half of the pepper. Mash until smooth but still slightly rustic. Adjust milk for desired creaminess.
  4. While potatoes cook, pat the sirloin tips dry and season with thyme or Italian seasoning, onion powder, and remaining salt and pepper.
  5. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the seasoned steak tips in a single layer (work in batches if needed) and sear 2–3 minutes per side until browned outside and just cooked to your preferred doneness.
  6. During the last minute of cooking, add the minced garlic and remaining 1 tbsp olive oil to the skillet, tossing the steak tips to coat in the garlicky pan juices.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning with a pinch of salt or extra herbs if needed, keeping overall sodium moderate.
  8. Divide the finished meal into three plates or containers: give Frank roughly 1/3 of the total volume, and split the remaining 2/3 between Susan and Elise as slightly smaller but still generous portions.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~520 kcal, ~48 g protein, ~32 g carbs, ~18 g fat.
  • Susan: ~390 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
  • Elise: ~390 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
