Swedish-Style Turkey Meatballs + Gravy (Light)

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 645 kcal • P 58g • C 55g • F 18g • Sat 6g • Na 700mg • Fiber 9g
Susan/Elise (each): 484 kcal • P 44g • C 41g • F 14g • Sat 4g • Na 525mg • Fiber 7g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.75 lb 93% lean ground turkey
  • 1 package whole-wheat breadcrumbs
  • 2 each eggs
  • 1 package low-sodium gravy mix
  • 1 carton low-sodium beef broth
  • 1 bag frozen mixed vegetables
  • 1 box instant mashed potatoes
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • garlic powder
  • onion powder
  • Italian seasoning
  • smoked paprika
  • chili powder
  • ground cumin
  • crushed red pepper
  • extra-virgin olive oil
  • nutmeg

Steps

  1. Oven: heat to 425°F. Line a sheet pan and lightly oil.
  2. Meatballs: mix turkey with 1/2 cup breadcrumbs, 2 eggs, 1 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp onion powder, 1/4 tsp nutmeg, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper.
  3. Form 18–20 meatballs; bake 12–14 minutes to 165°F.
  4. Veg: sauté 3 cups frozen mixed vegetables in a skillet with 2 tsp olive oil 6–8 minutes until browned.
  5. Gravy: prepare low-sodium gravy mix with 1 cup low-sodium beef broth per package directions. Add meatballs and simmer 2 minutes to coat.
  6. Mash: prepare instant mashed potatoes per box for 3 servings.
  7. Serve: portion meatballs + mash + veg per Notes.

Notes

Swedish-meatball comfort using a light gravy structure. Portions (per person): Frank: 8–9 meatballs + 1 cup mash + 1 cup veg Susan: 6–7 meatballs + 3/4 cup mash + 3/4 cup veg Elise: 6–7 meatballs + 3/4 cup mash + 3/4 cup veg Tips (recipe-specific): Keep gravy measured; the goal is coating, not a bowl of sauce. Bake meatballs so they stay firm in gravy.
