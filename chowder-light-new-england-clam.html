Light New England Clam Chowder

NOTES: This chowder is designed as a full meal in a bowl with lean protein, smart carbs, and 1–2 cups of vegetables per person. Use low-sodium broth and ingredients, avoid added sugars, and keep portions aligned with your goals. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total pot); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 2 (6.5 oz) cans chopped clams in juice, no salt added if possible
  • 2 cups low-sodium seafood or chicken broth
  • 1 cup 2% milk or unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 2 stalks celery, diced
  • 2 medium russet or Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and diced small
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1/2 tsp dried thyme
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Prep all vegetables: dice onion, celery, and potatoes into small, even pieces so they cook at the same rate.
  2. In a medium soup pot, heat olive oil over medium heat for 1–2 minutes.
  3. Add diced onion and celery. Cook 4–5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened and translucent.
  4. Sprinkle flour over the vegetables and stir continuously for 1–2 minutes to form a light roux (this will thicken the chowder).
  5. Slowly whisk in broth and clam juice from the cans while stirring to avoid lumps. Add bay leaf, thyme, garlic powder, and black pepper.
  6. Stir in diced potatoes. Bring mixture just to a gentle boil over medium-high heat, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer 12–15 minutes until potatoes are tender when pierced with a fork.
  7. Stir in milk and chopped clams. Continue cooking 3–4 minutes on low heat, stirring often, until heated through. Do not boil after adding milk.
  8. Remove bay leaf. Taste and adjust pepper if needed. Ladle chowder into three bowls, giving Frank the largest portion.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~560 kcal, ~42 g protein, ~55 g carbs, ~16 g fat.
  • Susan: ~420 kcal, ~31 g protein, ~41 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
  • Elise: ~420 kcal, ~31 g protein, ~41 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
