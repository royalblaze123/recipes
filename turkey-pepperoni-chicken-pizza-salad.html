Turkey Pepperoni & Chicken Pizza with Lemon-Parmesan Salad

Whole wheat pizza night: lower-sodium quick tomato sauce, chicken + turkey pepperoni, and a bright lemon-parmesan side salad.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 17 minutes
  • Total time: 37 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 1 dough ball whole wheat pizza dough
  • 1 lb boneless skinless chicken breast
  • 1 can no-salt-added crushed tomatoes
  • 1 can no-salt-added tomato paste
  • 1 bag part-skim mozzarella cheese
  • 1 package turkey pepperoni
  • 1 each bell pepper
  • 1 each red onion
  • 1 package mushrooms
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1/4 tsp kosher salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper

Optional Side 1

  • 1 bag spring mix
  • 1 each cucumber
  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes
  • 1 each lemon
  • 1 container grated parmesan cheese
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper

Preparation Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 475°F. Place a pizza stone/steel or an upside-down sheet pan in the oven to preheat. Tear off a sheet of parchment for shaping the pizza.
  2. Thaw the dough ball if frozen (overnight in the fridge, or 1–2 hours at room temp). Keep it covered so it doesn’t dry out.
  3. Cook the chicken: pat the chicken breast dry. Season with garlic powder, onion powder, Italian seasoning, kosher salt, and black pepper. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a nonstick skillet over medium-high. Cook chicken 5–6 minutes per side (or until 165°F). Rest 5 minutes, then dice into small bite-size pieces.
  4. Make the quick low-sodium pizza sauce: in a bowl, stir together 3/4 cup crushed tomatoes + 1 tbsp tomato paste + a pinch of Italian seasoning + black pepper. Refrigerate the remaining crushed tomatoes and tomato paste for another recipe.
  5. Prep toppings: thin-slice 1/2 bell pepper, 1/4 red onion, and 4 oz mushrooms. Store the remaining pepper/onion/mushrooms for another recipe.
  6. Shape + par-bake: on parchment, press/stretch the dough ball into a 12–14 inch round. Slide parchment + dough onto the preheated stone/pan. Par-bake 5 minutes.
  7. Top the pizza: spread the sauce evenly, leaving a small border. Sprinkle 3 oz mozzarella over the sauce; reseal and refrigerate/freeze remaining cheese. Scatter the diced chicken, sliced vegetables, and 12 slices turkey pepperoni; reseal the remaining pepperoni.
  8. Bake 10–12 minutes, until the crust is browned and cheese is bubbling. Rest 2 minutes, then slice into 10 slices.
  9. Make the salad: in a large bowl, toss 8 oz spring mix + 1/2 cucumber (sliced) + 1 cup cherry tomatoes (halved). Dress with 1 tbsp olive oil + juice of 1/2 lemon + 2 tbsp grated parmesan + black pepper. Refrigerate remaining cucumber/cherry tomatoes/parmesan as needed.
  10. Portion and serve: Frank gets 4 pizza slices + 2 cups salad. Susan gets 3 slices + 1 1/2 cups salad. Elise gets 3 slices + 1 1/2 cups salad.

Notes

Portions (per person):


Frank – 4 pizza slices (about 40% of the pie) + 2 cups salad.

Susan – 3 pizza slices (about 30% of the pie) + 1 1/2 cups salad.

Elise – 3 pizza slices (about 30% of the pie) + 1 1/2 cups salad.


Tips (recipe-specific):

Use no-salt-added crushed tomatoes + tomato paste for the sauce to keep sodium controlled.


If you want it “full-flavor,” add red pepper flakes at the table (not baked in).


For crispier crust, preheat the pan/stone and par-bake the crust 5 minutes before topping.
