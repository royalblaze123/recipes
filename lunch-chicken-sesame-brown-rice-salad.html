Chicken Sesame Brown Rice Salad

NOTES: Designed as a portable grab-and-go lunch using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled smart carbs. Use low-sodium ingredients and avoid added sugars where possible. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total volume); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total volume); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total volume) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 1 cup uncooked brown rice, rinsed
  • 2 cups water
  • 12 oz cooked boneless, skinless chicken breast, chopped
  • 1 1/2 cups shredded cabbage (green or red)
  • 1 cup shredded carrots
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tbsp low-sodium tamari or soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp rice vinegar (no sugar added)
  • 1 tbsp toasted sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds (optional)
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Cook brown rice: Combine rinsed rice and water in a saucepan. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat, cover, and simmer 25–30 minutes until tender. Cool completely.
  2. In a large bowl, combine cooled rice, chopped chicken, shredded cabbage, shredded carrots, sliced bell pepper, and green onions.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together tamari, rice vinegar, sesame oil, lime juice, and black pepper.
  4. Pour dressing over the salad and toss thoroughly to coat.
  5. Sprinkle sesame seeds over the top and toss again.
  6. Divide into three containers for grab-and-go, giving Frank the largest portion.
  7. Refrigerate up to 3 days. Serve chilled.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~590 kcal, ~50 g protein, ~60 g carbs, ~17 g fat.
  • Susan: ~445 kcal, ~38 g protein, ~45 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
  • Elise: ~445 kcal, ~38 g protein, ~45 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
