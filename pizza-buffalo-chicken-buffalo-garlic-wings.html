Buffalo Chicken Pizza + Buffalo-Garlic Wings

Tomato-based buffalo chicken pizza served with crisp air-fried buffalo-garlic wings.

Servings

3 servings (Frank, Susan, Elise).

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 35 minutes
  • Total time: 55 minutes

Ingredients

Pizza

  • 1 dough ball pizza dough
  • 1/2 jar no-salt-added pizza sauce
  • 3 oz shredded part-skim mozzarella
  • 4 oz cooked chicken breast
  • 1/2 each red onion
  • 1 each celery stalk
  • 2 tsp hot sauce
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil

Wings

  • 2 lb chicken wings
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper

Wing Finish Sauce

  • 3 tbsp hot sauce
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 each lemon

Preparation Steps

  1. Remove 1 dough ball from the refrigerator (or thawed vacuum-sealed dough) and keep covered at room temperature for 60–90 minutes so it relaxes.
  2. Preheat oven with a pizza steel or stone to 500°F for 45 minutes (or your oven’s maximum).
  3. Wing count note: 1 wing = 1 whole wing (flat + drumette). If buying separated party wings, count 2 pieces = 1 wing.
  4. Wings: Pat chicken wings very dry with paper towels. In a large bowl, toss wings with garlic powder, smoked paprika, onion powder, and black pepper until evenly coated.
  5. Cosori dual-basket setup: Split wings between both baskets in a single layer. Select Sync Cook at 380°F for 18 minutes. Shake/flip at the halfway reminder.
  6. Crisp finish: Increase to 400°F and cook 6–8 minutes more (shake once) until deeply browned and the thickest wing hits 165°F.
  7. Buffalo-garlic toss: In a large bowl, whisk hot sauce, olive oil, and juice from 1 lemon. Toss hot wings in the sauce until glossy.
  8. Pizza topping prep: Thin-slice red onion. Thin-slice celery. Shred or chop the cooked chicken into bite-size pieces.
  9. Stretch the dough ball into a 12-inch round on a lightly floured surface. Transfer to a floured peel or parchment.
  10. Spread a thin, even layer of pizza sauce over the dough. Sprinkle mozzarella evenly. Scatter chicken, red onion, and celery over the top. Drizzle 2 teaspoons hot sauce in a light zig-zag.
  11. Bake 7–9 minutes on the hot steel/stone until the crust is browned and cheese is bubbling. Rest 2 minutes before slicing.
  12. Portion and serve: Plate pizza first, then add wings. See Portions below for the standard Frank/Susan/Elise split.

Notes

Tomato-based buffalo chicken pizza served with crisp air-fried buffalo-garlic wings. Portions (per person): Frank: About 1/3 of the 12-inch pizza (about 3 slices if cut into 8) + 8 wings (about 4 oz cooked) Susan: About 1/4 of the 12-inch pizza (about 2 slices if cut into 8) + 6 wings (about 3 oz cooked) Elise: About 1/4 of the 12-inch pizza (about 2 slices if cut into 8) + 6 wings (about 3 oz cooked) Tips (recipe-specific): For lower sodium, use a no-salt-added pizza sauce and keep hot sauce measured. If wings are crowded, run two quick batches; single-layer wings crisp far better than stacked wings. If the dough keeps shrinking back, rest it covered for 10 minutes and stretch again.
