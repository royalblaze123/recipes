Steak with Classic Mashed Potatoes + Roasted Broccoli (Dinner)
Dinner ATLAS ONE-OFF Classic mashed Weeknight ~45 min
ATLAS • ONE-OFF Steakhouse-style dinner built to stay on-plan: measured sirloin, classic slightly-chunky mashed potatoes, and high-volume broccoli. Portions (per person): Frank: 1.0× (about 5.5 oz cooked sirloin steak, 5 oz mashed potatoes, 7 oz roasted broccoli) Susan: 0.75× (about 4.1 oz cooked sirloin steak, 4 oz mashed potatoes, 5.25 oz roasted broccoli) Elise: 0.75× (about 4.1 oz cooked sirloin steak, 4 oz mashed potatoes, 5.25 oz roasted broccoli) Tips (recipe-specific): Mashed potatoes stay A1c-friendlier when hand-mashed slightly chunky (not whipped) and paired with lean protein + big veg. Use 2 Tbsp butter total for the whole pot, then loosen with warm milk or low-sodium broth for creamy texture without extra fat. For tender steak, pat dry, sear hot, and rest 5 minutes before slicing.

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1 lb top sirloin steak
  • 2 lb Yukon Gold potatoes
  • 10 oz 1% milk
  • 2 Tbsp unsalted butter
  • 2 heads broccoli
  • 1 tsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • garlic powder

Seasonings are listed without quantities by design; exact measurements are specified in Steps.

Steps

  1. HEAT: Heat oven to 425°F. Set a large pot of water on the stove for potatoes.
  2. POTATO PREP: Scrub 2 lb Yukon Gold potatoes. Cut into 1.5-inch chunks. Add to the pot, cover with water by 1 inch, and add 1 tsp kosher salt. Bring to a boil, then simmer 12–15 minutes until fork-tender.
  3. BROCCOLI: Cut 2 heads broccoli into bite-size florets and slice stems into thin coins. Put broccoli on a sheet pan. Drizzle with 1 tsp extra-virgin olive oil. Sprinkle with 1 tsp garlic powder, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, and 1/2 tsp black pepper. Toss well.
  4. ROAST BROCCOLI: Roast broccoli at 425°F for 14–18 minutes, tossing once halfway, until edges are browned and stems are crisp-tender.
  5. STEAK PREP: Pat 1 lb top sirloin steak very dry with paper towels. Season both sides with 1 tsp kosher salt, 1 tsp black pepper, and 1 tsp garlic powder.
  6. SEAR: Heat a skillet (cast iron preferred) over medium-high heat for 2 minutes. Sear steak 3–4 minutes per side (time varies by thickness) to 130–135°F for medium-rare or 140–145°F for medium.
  7. REST: Move steak to a plate and rest 5 minutes. Slice against the grain.
  8. DRAIN + DRY POTATOES: When potatoes are tender, drain thoroughly. Return to the hot pot over low heat for 45 seconds, stirring, to steam off moisture.
  9. CLASSIC MASH (MEASURED): Turn heat off. Add 2 Tbsp unsalted butter to the pot and mash by hand until mostly smooth with some small chunks. Warm 10 oz 1% milk in the microwave for 45–60 seconds. Stir in 6 oz warm milk first, mash/stir, then add up to 4 oz more warm milk as needed for creamy texture. Season with 1/2 tsp kosher salt and 1/2 tsp black pepper.
  10. PLATE (PORTIONED): Frank gets about 5.5 oz cooked steak, 5 oz mashed potatoes, and 7 oz roasted broccoli. Susan and Elise each get about 4.1 oz cooked steak, 4 oz mashed potatoes, and 5.25 oz broccoli.
  11. STORAGE + REHEAT: Cool within 2 hours. Refrigerate steak, potatoes, and broccoli up to 3–4 days. Reheat potatoes with 1–2 Tbsp milk stirred in, microwave 60–90 seconds. Reheat broccoli in air fryer at 350°F for 4–6 minutes. Reheat steak gently (microwave 20–30 seconds) to avoid overcooking.
