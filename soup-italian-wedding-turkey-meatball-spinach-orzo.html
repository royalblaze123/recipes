Italian Wedding Soup with Turkey Meatballs, Spinach, and Whole-Wheat Orzo

Prep: 20 min   Cook: 35 min   Total: 55 min

Ingredients

  • 1.25 lb lean ground turkey 93%
  • 0.75 carton low-sodium chicken broth
  • 0.5 carton low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 onion
  • 2 carrots
  • 2 celery stalks
  • 1 bag spinach
  • 0.33 box whole-wheat orzo
  • 1 egg
  • grated Parmesan
  • olive oil
  • tomato paste
  • Italian seasoning
  • garlic powder
  • black pepper
  • salt substitute

RBM rules: quantities appear before items; fresh proteins use weight. Condiments/spices/seasonings are listed name-only; all measurements are in Steps. Water is only referenced in Steps.

Step-by-step Instructions

  1. Make meatballs: in a bowl mix ground turkey + 1 egg + 1/4 cup grated Parmesan + 2 tsp Italian seasoning + 1 tsp garlic powder + 1 tsp black pepper + 2 tsp salt substitute. Form 18–20 small meatballs.
  2. Brown meatballs: heat a large pot over medium-high. Add 3 tsp olive oil. Add meatballs in batches; brown 2–3 minutes per side (they do not need to cook through). Transfer to a plate.
  3. Build base: reduce heat to medium. Add diced onion, sliced carrots, and sliced celery. Cook 6–8 minutes until softened. Stir in 2 tbsp tomato paste; cook 60 seconds.
  4. Add broth: pour in 1.25 cartons broth total (40 fl oz). Bring to a simmer.
  5. Add meatballs back in. Simmer 12–15 minutes until meatballs are cooked through (165°F).
  6. Cook pasta in-soup: add whole-wheat orzo (use ~1 1/4 cups dry from the 0.33 box). Simmer 8–10 minutes, stirring, until al dente.
  7. Finish: stir in spinach and cook 60–90 seconds to wilt. Taste and adjust with black pepper/salt substitute. Add extra Parmesan at serving.
  8. Portion: Frank gets ~3 1/2 cups soup with ~7 meatballs. Susan and Elise each get ~2 3/4 cups soup with ~5 meatballs.
