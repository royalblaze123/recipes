Chili-Lime Shrimp Stuffed Sweet Potatoes

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 640 kcal • P 56g • C 55g • F 18g
Susan/Elise (each): 480 kcal • P 42g • C 41g • F 14g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 2.25 lb sweet potatoes
  • 1.50 lb raw shrimp
  • 1 bag frozen fajita-style pepper blend
  • 1 each avocado
  • 1/2 jar salsa
  • 6 tsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 each lime
  • 2 tsp chili powder
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp kosher salt

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a sheet pan with parchment.
  2. Sweet potatoes: scrub and pierce each potato 6–8 times with a fork. Rub with 2 tsp olive oil and a pinch of kosher salt.
  3. Roast sweet potatoes 40–55 minutes until very tender (knife slides in easily).
  4. Veg: cook frozen fajita pepper blend in a skillet with 2 tsp olive oil 6–8 minutes until browned.
  5. Shrimp: pat dry and season with chili powder, garlic powder, black pepper, and kosher salt.
  6. Cook shrimp in the same skillet with 2 tsp olive oil 2–3 minutes until just opaque. Finish with 2 tsp lime juice.
  7. Combine: toss shrimp with peppers.
  8. Split: slice each potato lengthwise, fluff the insides with a fork, and create a well for the topping.
  9. Stuff: spoon shrimp-pepper mix into potatoes.
  10. Avocado: halve, pit, and dice. Use about 1/2 avocado total; refrigerate remaining avocado with the pit to slow browning.
  11. Serve with salsa and portion per Notes.

Notes

Shrimp + peppers stuffed sweet potatoes—fast and surprisingly light, finished with salsa + avocado. Portions (per person): Frank: 1 stuffed sweet potato (largest) + 1/3 of the topping mix + 1/3 of the avocado + salsa Susan: 1 stuffed sweet potato (medium) + 1/4 of the topping mix + 1/4 of the avocado + salsa Elise: 1 stuffed sweet potato (medium) + 1/4 of the topping mix + 1/4 of the avocado + salsa
