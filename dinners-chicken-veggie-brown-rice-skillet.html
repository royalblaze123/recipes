Chicken & Veggie Brown Rice Skillet (Frank Household)

One-pan chicken, brown rice, and mixed vegetables in a light garlic-herb sauce for a fast weeknight dinner that fits Frank Household dinner targets.

Servings

3 servings (Frank, Susan, Elise).

Times

  • Prep time: 10 minutes
  • Cook time: 18 minutes
  • Total time: 28 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 cup cooked brown rice (from about 1/3 cup dry or a microwave-ready pouch)
  • 15 oz raw boneless skinless chicken breast (yields about 15 oz cooked total; 6 oz for Frank, 4 1/2 oz each for Susan and Elise)
  • 2 cups fresh broccoli florets
  • 1 cup thinly sliced carrots
  • 1 cup sliced bell peppers (any color)
  • 1/2 cup thinly sliced red onion (optional, for tolerance)
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1/4 cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • 2 tbsp 2% plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp dried Italian seasoning or dried basil/oregano blend
  • 1/4 tsp ground black pepper (or to taste)
  • 1/4 tsp kosher or sea salt (keep total modest; season to taste)
  • Olive oil spray (for pan, if needed)

Preparation Steps

  1. Cook the brown rice if not already prepared. Use leftover brown rice, cook 1/3 cup dry brown rice according to package directions, or heat a microwave-ready pouch. You need about 1 cup cooked rice total. Set aside and keep warm.
  2. Trim any visible fat from the chicken breast. Cut the chicken into small, even bite-size pieces about 3/4-inch thick so they cook quickly.
  3. Rinse and dry the broccoli, carrots, bell peppers, and onion. Cut the broccoli into small florets, thinly slice the carrots, slice the bell peppers, and thinly slice the onion if using.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the chicken broth, Greek yogurt, lemon juice, Dijon mustard, garlic powder, Italian seasoning, black pepper, and salt until smooth. Set aside.
  5. Heat a large nonstick skillet over medium-high heat. Add the olive oil and a light spray of olive oil if needed. Add the chicken pieces in a single layer. Cook for 4–5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken is lightly browned and cooked through (165°F internal).
  6. Add the broccoli, carrots, bell peppers, and onion to the skillet with the chicken. Stir and cook for 4–5 minutes over medium heat until the vegetables are crisp-tender. If the pan gets too dry, add a tablespoon of water at a time.
  7. Turn the heat to low. Add the cooked brown rice to the skillet and pour the yogurt-broth sauce over the mixture. Stir well to coat the chicken, rice, and vegetables. Cook for 2–3 more minutes, just until heated through and the sauce lightly thickens.
  8. Taste and add a small pinch of salt or pepper if needed, keeping overall sodium modest.
  9. Spoon about 2 to 2 1/4 cups of the skillet mixture into a large bowl or plate for Frank, making sure his portion includes roughly 6 oz chicken, 1/2 cup cooked brown rice, and about 1 1/2 cups vegetables.
  10. Divide the remaining skillet mixture between two bowls or plates for Susan and Elise, about 1 1/2 to 1 3/4 cups each, ensuring each portion has about 4 1/2 oz chicken, 1/3 cup brown rice, and about 1 cup vegetables.
