Pork Tenderloin Fajita Bowls (Cauliflower Rice)

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 635 kcal • P 58g • C 40g • F 22g
Susan/Elise (each): 476 kcal • P 44g • C 30g • F 16g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.50 lb pork tenderloin
  • 2 each bell peppers
  • 1 each yellow onion
  • 1 bag frozen cauliflower rice
  • 1 each avocado
  • 1/2 jar salsa
  • 1/2 tub 2% plain Greek yogurt
  • 6 tsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 each lime
  • 2 tsp chili powder
  • 2 tsp cumin
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp kosher salt

Steps

  1. Pork: pat tenderloin dry and slice into 1/2-inch medallions. Season with the fajita spices.
  2. Cook pork: heat a skillet over medium-high. Add 2 tsp olive oil. Cook medallions 3–4 minutes per side until 145°F. Rest 3 minutes, then slice into bite-size pieces.
  3. Veg: slice peppers and onion into strips. Cook in the same skillet with remaining 4 tsp olive oil 6–8 minutes until browned at edges.
  4. Finish: add pork back to the skillet with 2 tsp lime juice and toss 30 seconds.
  5. Cauliflower rice: microwave the frozen cauliflower rice per bag directions. Drain any excess moisture. Season with a pinch of black pepper and a squeeze of lime if using.
  6. Yogurt crema: stir 6 tbsp Greek yogurt with 2 tsp lime juice and 1 tsp lime zest until smooth.
  7. Avocado: halve, pit, and dice. For this recipe, plan on using about 1/2 avocado total; refrigerate the remaining avocado with the pit to slow browning.
  8. Build bowls and portion per Notes.

Notes

Pork tenderloin fajita bowls with salsa + avocado; hearty but still controlled. Portions (per person): Frank: 1/3 of the cauliflower rice + 1/3 of the fajita protein/veg + 1/3 of salsa + avocado + yogurt Susan: 1/4 of the cauliflower rice + 1/4 of the fajita protein/veg + 1/4 of salsa + avocado + yogurt Elise: 1/4 of the cauliflower rice + 1/4 of the fajita protein/veg + 1/4 of salsa + avocado + yogurt
