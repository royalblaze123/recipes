High-Protein Turkey & Cottage Cheese Lasagna

A higher-protein lasagna using lean turkey, part-skim cottage cheese, and whole-wheat noodles for a lighter but still comforting bake.

PORTION SIZES (3 servings total): Frank = 1.0× (largest square), Susan = 0.75×, Elise = 0.75×. Cut the baked lasagna into 4 equal squares, serve 2 squares to Frank and 1 square each to Susan and Elise.

Ingredients

  • 8 whole-wheat lasagna noodles (dry)
  • 1 lb lean ground turkey (93% lean or better)
  • 1 small yellow onion, finely diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups no-salt-added crushed tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup no-salt-added tomato sauce
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 1/2 tsp dried thyme
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp kosher salt (optional, to taste)
  • 1 1/2 cups part-skim cottage cheese
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 cup part-skim shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 2 tbsp grated reduced-fat Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped

Step-by-step Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Lightly coat a 9×9-inch baking dish with nonstick spray.
  2. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Cook the whole-wheat lasagna noodles until just shy of al dente, about 7–8 minutes. Drain and lay flat on parchment to prevent sticking.
  3. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the diced onion and sauté 4–5 minutes until softened.
  4. Add the minced garlic and cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Add the lean ground turkey and cook 6–8 minutes, breaking it into small crumbles, until browned and cooked through.
  6. Stir in the dried oregano, dried basil, dried thyme, black pepper, and optional salt. Toast the herbs with the turkey for 1–2 minutes.
  7. Pour in the crushed tomatoes and tomato sauce. Stir well, reduce heat to low, and simmer 8–10 minutes until slightly thickened.
  8. In a medium bowl, whisk the egg. Stir in the cottage cheese and half of the shredded mozzarella until evenly combined.
  9. Spread a thin layer of turkey tomato sauce on the bottom of the prepared baking dish.
  10. Place a layer of lasagna noodles over the sauce. Spread one-third of the cottage cheese mixture over the noodles, then top with a layer of the turkey sauce.
  11. Repeat the layers (noodles, cottage cheese mixture, turkey sauce) until all components are used, finishing with a layer of sauce on top.
  12. Sprinkle the remaining mozzarella and the grated Parmesan evenly over the top.
  13. Cover the dish with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake an additional 10–15 minutes until bubbling and lightly browned.
  14. Remove from the oven and let the lasagna rest for at least 10 minutes to set. Sprinkle with chopped fresh parsley before serving.
  15. Cut into 4 equal squares. Serve 2 squares to Frank and 1 square each to Susan and Elise, with a side of non-starchy vegetables.

Nutrition (per person)

Frank (largest portion): approx 540 kcal, 50 g protein, 42 g carbs, 20 g fat.
Susan (medium portion): approx 405 kcal, 38 g protein, 32 g carbs, 15 g fat.
Elise (medium portion): approx 405 kcal, 38 g protein, 32 g carbs, 15 g fat.
