Beef & Broccoli Stir-Fry over Brown Rice

NOTES: Skillet-seared sirloin strips with broccoli in a light garlic-ginger sauce served over brown rice. This is a full-plate beef dinner built around lean beef, smart carbs, and plenty of non-starchy vegetables. Uses olive oil or avocado oil only, keeps sodium moderate, and avoids added sugars to support A1c, cholesterol, blood pressure, and PPPD-friendly eating. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

PORTION SIZES (per person): Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 1 pound lean beef sirloin, sliced thin against the grain
  • 3 cups small broccoli florets
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp grated fresh ginger or 1 tsp ground ginger
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp low-sodium tamari or soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar (unseasoned)
  • 1 tsp cornstarch (optional, for light thickening)
  • 3/4 cup uncooked brown rice, rinsed
  • 1 1/2 cups water or low-sodium broth
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Cook the brown rice with water or broth according to package directions, keeping sodium moderate. Keep warm.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together tamari, rice vinegar, cornstarch (if using), and pepper. Set aside.
  3. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the sliced beef and sear 3–4 minutes until just browned. Transfer to a plate.
  4. In the same skillet, add onion and broccoli. Stir-fry 4–5 minutes until crisp-tender, adding a splash of water if needed.
  5. Stir in the garlic and ginger and cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
  6. Return the beef to the skillet. Pour in the sauce mixture and cook 1–2 minutes, stirring, until the sauce lightly coats the beef and vegetables.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning with a pinch of salt or extra herbs if needed, keeping overall sodium moderate.
  8. Divide the finished meal into three plates or containers: give Frank roughly 1/3 of the total volume, and split the remaining 2/3 between Susan and Elise as slightly smaller but still generous portions.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~520 kcal, ~48 g protein, ~32 g carbs, ~18 g fat.
  • Susan: ~390 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
  • Elise: ~390 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
