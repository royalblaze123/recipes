Chicken Broccoli Brown Rice Bake

NOTES: Built as a complete dinner using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled starch portions. Use low-sodium broth and minimal added salt to support blood pressure and kidney health. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of pan); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of pan); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of pan).

Ingredients

  • 1 cup uncooked brown rice, rinsed
  • 2 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breast, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 3 cups small broccoli florets
  • 1 medium yellow onion, finely diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup plain 2% Greek yogurt
  • 1/2 cup shredded reduced-fat sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried parsley
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Olive oil spray for baking dish

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Lightly coat a 9x13-inch baking dish with olive oil spray.
  2. In a medium saucepan, combine rinsed brown rice and low-sodium chicken broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat, cover, and simmer 25–30 minutes until rice is just tender but not mushy. Remove from heat and fluff with a fork.
  3. While rice cooks, heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat.
  4. Add diced onion and cook 3–4 minutes until softened. Stir in minced garlic and cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Add chicken cubes to the skillet, season with a small pinch of salt and pepper, and cook 5–7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the outside is opaque (it will finish in the oven).
  6. Steam or microwave broccoli florets 2–3 minutes until bright green and just crisp-tender; drain well to avoid excess moisture.
  7. In a large mixing bowl, combine cooked brown rice, par-cooked chicken, broccoli florets, sautéed onion and garlic, Greek yogurt, thyme, parsley, smoked paprika, remaining salt, and several grinds of black pepper. Stir until everything is evenly coated.
  8. Transfer mixture to the prepared baking dish and spread into an even layer.
  9. Sprinkle reduced-fat cheddar cheese evenly over the top.
  10. Cover dish loosely with foil and bake 20 minutes. Remove foil and bake an additional 10–15 minutes until cheese is melted, edges are bubbling, and chicken is cooked through (internal temp 165°F).
  11. Let casserole rest 5–10 minutes to set, then cut into portions for Frank (largest) and Susan/Elise (medium portions).

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~560 kcal, ~50 g protein, ~55 g carbs, ~15 g fat.
  • Susan: ~420 kcal, ~37 g protein, ~41 g carbs, ~11 g fat.
  • Elise: ~420 kcal, ~37 g protein, ~41 g carbs, ~11 g fat.
