Tuna & Chickpea Crunch Salad

High-protein tuna and chickpea salad over greens with Greek-yogurt lemon dressing for a full, fiber-rich lunch.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 15 minutes
  • Cook time: 0 minutes (no cooking required)
  • Total time: 15 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2 cans tuna in water
  • 3/4 cup canned chickpeas
  • 6 cups mixed salad greens
  • 3/4 cup cucumber
  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes
  • 1/3 cup celery
  • 1 red onion
  • 6 tbsp 2% plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 1/2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp dried dill
  • 1/4 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp kosher

Preparation Steps

  1. Dice the red onion and measure out 1/4 cup for the recipe. Refrigerate any remaining onion.
  2. Rinse and dry the salad greens, cucumber, tomatoes, celery, and onion. Chop or dice as directed so they are ready for the salad.
  3. In a medium bowl, add the drained tuna and the drained, rinsed chickpeas. Use a fork to break the tuna into flakes and lightly mash some of the chickpeas so they hold in the salad but still keep some texture.
  4. In a small bowl or jar, whisk together the Greek yogurt, olive oil, lemon juice, Dijon mustard, garlic powder, dried dill or parsley, black pepper, and salt until smooth. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed.
  5. In a large mixing bowl, add the salad greens, cucumber, grape tomatoes, diced celery, and red onion (if using).
  6. Add the tuna and chickpea mixture to the bowl with the vegetables.
  7. Pour most of the yogurt dressing over the bowl (reserve a tablespoon or so if you like a little extra on top). Toss gently until the greens, vegetables, tuna, and chickpeas are evenly coated.
  8. Add about 2 cups of the salad mixture to a large bowl or container for Frank, ensuring his portion includes roughly 3 1/2 oz tuna and 1/3 cup chickpeas. Add a small spoonful of any reserved dressing on top if desired.
  9. Divide the remaining salad between two bowls or containers for Susan and Elise, about 1 1/2 cups each, making sure each has about 2 1/2 oz tuna and 1/4 cup chickpeas. Top with a small drizzle of any remaining dressing if you like.
  10. Serve immediately or refrigerate the portions for up to 24 hours. Keep covered and chilled for work or school lunches.
