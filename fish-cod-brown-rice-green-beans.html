Baked Cod with Brown Rice and Green Beans

NOTES: Complete dinner with lean seafood, smart carbs, and 1–2 cups of non-starchy vegetables per person. Use low-sodium ingredients, avoid added sugars, and keep portions aligned with goals. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total volume); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total volume); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total volume) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 3 cod fillets (4–5 oz each)
  • 1 cup uncooked brown rice, rinsed
  • 2 cups low-sodium vegetable or chicken broth
  • 3 cups fresh or frozen green beans, trimmed
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp dried parsley or Italian seasoning
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Parchment paper for pan

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Cook brown rice: In a small saucepan, combine rinsed brown rice and broth. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer 25–30 minutes until rice is tender and liquid is absorbed. Fluff with a fork and keep warm.
  2. While rice cooks, preheat oven to 400°F (205°C). Line a sheet pan with parchment paper.
  3. Place cod fillets on one side of the prepared sheet pan. Pat dry and drizzle with half of the olive oil and lemon juice. Sprinkle with half of the garlic powder, dried parsley, and black pepper.
  4. Spread green beans on the other side of the sheet pan. Drizzle with remaining olive oil, then sprinkle with remaining garlic powder and pepper. Toss beans to coat and spread in a single layer.
  5. Bake 12–15 minutes, until cod flakes easily with a fork and green beans are crisp-tender.
  6. Divide brown rice, cod, and green beans among three plates or containers, giving Frank the largest portion of fish and rice. Serve immediately or cool and refrigerate for meal prep dinners.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~580 kcal, ~44 g protein, ~60 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
  • Susan: ~435 kcal, ~33 g protein, ~45 g carbs, ~10 g fat.
  • Elise: ~435 kcal, ~33 g protein, ~45 g carbs, ~10 g fat.
