Light Chicken Tortilla-Style Soup (Stovetop)

NOTES: Designed as a complete meal soup using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled starch. Use low-sodium broth and avoid extra salt when possible to support blood pressure and kidney health. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~2 cups); Susan: medium portion (~1.5 cups); Elise: medium portion (~1.5 cups).

Ingredients

  • 2 small corn tortillas, cut into thin strips
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 jalapeño, seeded and minced (optional)
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) no-salt-added diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breast
  • 1 cup frozen corn kernels (no added salt)
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Chopped cilantro, for garnish (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Arrange tortilla strips on a baking sheet in a single layer and bake 8–10 minutes, flipping once, until crisp and lightly browned. Set aside for garnish.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large soup pot over medium heat.
  3. Add diced onion and red bell pepper and cook 4–5 minutes until softened.
  4. Stir in minced garlic and minced jalapeño (if using) and cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Add chili powder, ground cumin, smoked paprika, dried oregano, salt, and black pepper and stir to coat vegetables.
  6. Add diced tomatoes with juices, low-sodium chicken broth, and water. Stir well.
  7. Place chicken breasts in the pot, submerging them in the liquid.
  8. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low, cover partially, and simmer 20–25 minutes until chicken is cooked through.
  9. Remove chicken to a cutting board and let cool slightly, then shred with two forks.
  10. Return shredded chicken to the pot along with frozen corn. Simmer 5–7 more minutes until corn is heated through.
  11. Stir in lime juice and taste, adjusting salt and pepper as needed.
  12. Remove from heat and let soup sit a few minutes.
  13. Ladle into bowls according to Frank, Susan, and Elise portions. Top each bowl with a small handful of baked tortilla strips and chopped cilantro if desired.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~440 kcal, ~42 g protein, ~32 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
  • Susan: ~330 kcal, ~32 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~9 g fat.
  • Elise: ~330 kcal, ~32 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~9 g fat.
