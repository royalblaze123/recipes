Roast Beef Swiss Sandwiches with Horseradish Spread and Snap Pea Side

Prep: 12 min   Cook: 5 min   Total: 17 min

Ingredients

  • 6 slices whole wheat bread
  • 12 slices deli roast beef
  • 6 slices reduced-fat swiss cheese
  • 1 onion
  • 1 bag spinach
  • 1 bag sugar snap peas
  • horseradish
  • Dijon mustard
  • light mayonnaise
  • black pepper
  • salt substitute

RBM Sandwich rules: 3 full sandwiches total (6 slices bread). Deli meats/cheeses listed as slice counts; slicing guidance lives in Steps. Condiments/spices are name-only in Ingredients; all measurements are in Steps. Produce is listed in shoppable units (fractional OK). Microgreens preferred.

Step-by-step Instructions

  1. Deli request: ask for roast beef **medium-thin sandwich slice** (~2 mm).
  2. Optional toast: toast bread lightly so it doesn’t get soggy.
  3. Quick onion pickle (fast): thin-slice onion. Toss with 6 tsp vinegar (any) + 1 tsp salt substitute + 1 tsp black pepper. Let sit 10 minutes while you assemble.
  4. Make spread: for each sandwich mix 1 tsp mayo + 1 tsp Dijon + 1/2 tsp horseradish (adjust to taste).
  5. Assemble 3 sandwiches: spread on both slices. Add spinach, then roast beef, then swiss, then a small layer of quick-pickled onion. Close and press.
  6. Portion fillings: Frank gets 6 roast beef slices + 2 swiss slices. Susan and Elise each get 3 roast beef slices + 2 swiss slices (or 1 slice if you want lower fat).
  7. Veg side: Frank gets ~2 cups snap peas. Susan and Elise each get ~1 1/2 cups snap peas.

Notes

Medium-thin roast beef with swiss, horseradish bite, and crunchy snap peas. Portions (per person): Frank: - 1 full sandwich (6 roast beef slices, 2 swiss slices) - Sugar snap peas: ~2 cups Susan: - 1 full sandwich (~3 roast beef slices, 1–2 swiss slices) - Sugar snap peas: ~1 1/2 cups Elise: - 1 full sandwich (~3 roast beef slices, 1–2 swiss slices) - Sugar snap peas: ~1 1/2 cups Tips (recipe-specific): Keep horseradish in the spread (not loose) so the flavor is even and controllable. Add spinach first—it acts as a moisture barrier and keeps bread intact.
