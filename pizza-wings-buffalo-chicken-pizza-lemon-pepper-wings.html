Buffalo Chicken Pizza with Lemon-Pepper Wings

Prep: 20 min   Cook: 28 min   Total: 48 min

Ingredients

  • 1 pizza dough ball
  • 0.5 jar low-sodium pizza sauce
  • 8 oz reduced-fat mozzarella cheese
  • 12 oz cooked rotisserie chicken
  • 1 red onion
  • 1 bag microgreens
  • 2.25 lb chicken wings
  • 1 lemon
  • olive oil
  • hot sauce
  • light mayonnaise
  • garlic powder
  • black pepper
  • salt substitute
  • smoked paprika

RBM rules: quantities appear before items; fresh meat/poultry/fish use weights. Condiments/spices/seasonings are listed name-only (no tsp/tbsp in Ingredients); all measurements are in Steps. Water is referenced in Steps only. No olives, no squash, no Brussels sprouts, no yogurt in dinners.

Step-by-step Instructions

  1. Pizza: preheat oven to 475°F with a rack in the lower third. Stretch 1 dough ball into a 12-inch round on parchment.
  2. Make buffalo drizzle (no yogurt): mix 2 tbsp hot sauce + 1 tbsp light mayo + 1 tsp olive oil + 1/2 tsp garlic powder + black pepper. Set aside.
  3. Top pizza: spread ~1/2 jar pizza sauce thinly. Add mozzarella. Add chopped rotisserie chicken (measure ~10 oz for topping; save the rest) and thin-sliced red onion. Bake 10–12 minutes until crust is browned and cheese bubbles.
  4. Finish pizza: drizzle buffalo sauce lightly and top with microgreens after baking. Cut pizza into 10 slices.
  5. Wings (Cosori dual-basket): pat wings very dry. Toss with 2 tsp olive oil + 2 tsp salt substitute + 1 tsp black pepper + 1 tsp garlic powder + 1 tsp smoked paprika + zest of 1 lemon.
  6. Air fry wings: preheat 380°F. Cook 24–26 minutes total, shaking at reminders, then increase to 400°F for 4–6 minutes to crisp. Use Sync Finish if cooking in both baskets.
  7. Serve lemon wedges with wings.
  8. Portion plate: Frank gets 4 pizza slices + 8 wings. Susan and Elise each get 3 pizza slices + 6 wings.

Notes

A spicy buffalo-style pizza paired with crisp lemon-pepper wings for a complete dinner plate. Dry wings thoroughly—this is the single biggest factor for crispness without deep-frying.
