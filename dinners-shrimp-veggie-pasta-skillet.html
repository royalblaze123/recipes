Shrimp & Veggie Whole-Wheat Pasta Skillet

Garlic shrimp and vegetables tossed with whole-wheat pasta and a light lemon-yogurt sauce for a fast, high-protein dinner.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 10 minutes
  • Cook time: 18 minutes
  • Total time: 28 minutes

Ingredients

  • 5 oz dry whole-wheat penne or rotini
  • 1 lb raw shrimp
  • 2 cups small broccoli florets or asparagus pieces
  • 1 cup halved grape tomatoes
  • 1 cup sliced zucchini
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 6 tbsp 2% plain Greek yogurt
  • 2 tbsp pasta cooking water
  • 1 1/2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1/4 tsp dried Italian seasoning
  • 1/4 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp kosher or sea salt
  • Olive oil spray

Preparation Steps

  1. Bring a medium pot of water to a boil. Add the whole-wheat pasta and cook according to package directions until just al dente (usually 9–11 minutes). Reserve 2 tbsp of the pasta cooking water, then drain the pasta.
  2. While the pasta cooks, prep the shrimp and vegetables. Pat the shrimp dry with a paper towel. Rinse and dry the broccoli or asparagus, grape tomatoes, and zucchini. Cut the broccoli or asparagus into small pieces, halve the tomatoes, and slice the zucchini into half-moons.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the Greek yogurt, reserved 2 tbsp pasta cooking water, lemon juice, Italian seasoning, black pepper, and salt until smooth. Set aside off the heat.
  4. Heat a large nonstick skillet over medium-high heat. Add the olive oil and a light spray of olive oil if needed. Add the shrimp in a single layer and cook for 2–3 minutes, stirring once or twice, until they just turn pink and opaque. Transfer the cooked shrimp to a plate and set aside.
  5. In the same skillet, add the broccoli or asparagus, zucchini, and minced garlic (or garlic powder). Cook over medium heat for 4–5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are crisp-tender. If needed, add a tablespoon of water to help them steam.
  6. Add the halved grape tomatoes to the skillet and cook for 1–2 minutes more until they just begin to soften.
  7. Turn the heat down to low. Add the cooked pasta and cooked shrimp back into the skillet with the vegetables. Pour the yogurt-lemon mixture over the top. Toss gently for 1–2 minutes, just until everything is coated and warmed through. Do not boil the sauce or it may separate; keep the heat low.
  8. Taste and adjust seasoning with a pinch of salt or pepper if needed, keeping sodium modest.
  9. Add about 1 cup cooked pasta, 5 oz cooked shrimp, and about 1 cup vegetables with sauce to a large bowl or plate for Frank.
  10. Divide the remaining pasta, shrimp, and vegetables between two bowls or plates for Susan and Elise, about 3/4 cup pasta, 3 3/4 oz shrimp, and 3/4 cup vegetables per person.
