Lunch Sandwich Chicken Hummus Mediterranean

Mediterranean chicken hummus sandwiches with cucumber, tomato, onion, and romaine—big veg and slow carbs built into the sandwich.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 14 minutes
  • Cook time: 0 minutes
  • Total time: 14 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 6 slices whole wheat bread
  • 18 slices deli chicken breast
  • 3 slices provolone cheese
  • 4 tbsp hummus
  • 1 package romaine hearts
  • 1 each cucumber
  • 2 each tomatoes
  • 1 each red onion
  • 1 each lemon
  • 1 jar black pepper
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Veg: tear romaine. Measure out 6 packed cups romaine for these sandwiches; refrigerate remaining romaine.
    2. Slice: slice cucumber and tomatoes. Slice a few thin rings of red onion. Use about 1/2 cucumber, 1 tomato, and 1/4 onion for these sandwiches; refrigerate the rest.
    3. Spread: measure out 4 tbsp hummus for this recipe; refrigerate the rest.
    4. Build (Frank): 2 slices bread + 8 slices deli chicken + 1 slice provolone + about 2 tbsp hummus + ~2 cups romaine + cucumber + tomato + a few onion rings + a squeeze of lemon.
    5. Build (Susan): 2 slices bread + 5 slices deli chicken + 1 slice provolone + about 1 tbsp hummus + ~2 cups romaine + cucumber + tomato + a few onion rings + a squeeze of lemon.
    6. Build (Elise): 2 slices bread + 5 slices deli chicken + 1 slice provolone + about 1 tbsp hummus + ~2 cups romaine + cucumber + tomato + a few onion rings + a squeeze of lemon.
    7. Close sandwiches, press gently, and wrap.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – 1 whole sandwich: 8 slices deli chicken, 1 slice provolone, ~2 tbsp hummus, ~2 cups romaine, cucumber + tomato + onion.


      Susan – 1 whole sandwich: 5 slices deli chicken, 1 slice provolone, ~1 tbsp hummus, ~2 cups romaine, cucumber + tomato + onion.


      Elise – 1 whole sandwich: 5 slices deli chicken, 1 slice provolone, ~1 tbsp hummus, ~2 cups romaine, cucumber + tomato + onion.


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Hummus is calorie-dense—measure it so the lunch band stays on target.


      Use lemon as the acid so you don’t need salty dressings.


      If you’re assembling hours ahead, keep tomato slices between romaine layers to reduce sogginess.
