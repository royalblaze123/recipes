One-Pan Whole Wheat Pasta with Chicken, Pesto, and Peas

Prep: 18 min   Cook: 24 min   Total: 42 min

Ingredients

  • 1.6 lb chicken breast
  • 8 oz whole wheat pasta
  • 1 bag frozen peas
  • 1 onion
  • 0.2 jar pesto
  • 0.5 carton low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 lemon
  • 2 tbsp grated parmesan cheese
  • olive oil
  • black pepper
  • salt substitute
  • garlic powder

Hard rules enforced: Dinner validations (calories 600–650 Frank, sat fat ≤8g, sodium ≤700mg, fiber ≥10g, added sugar ≤6g). Quantities appear before items; fresh meat/poultry/fish use weights. Condiments/spices/seasonings have no measurements in Ingredients; all measurements are in Steps. No olives, no squash, no Brussels sprouts, no zucchini, no tofu/tempeh, no yogurt in dinners.

Step-by-step Instructions

  1. Chicken: cut into bite-size pieces. Season with garlic powder, black pepper, and salt substitute.
  2. Sauté chicken with 2 tsp olive oil 6–8 minutes in a deep skillet. Add sliced onion; cook 3 minutes.
  3. Add pasta + 1 cup broth (from carton) + 2 cups water. Cover and simmer 10–12 minutes, stirring twice.
  4. Peas: microwave peas 3 minutes. Measure 1 cup peas into the pan; refreeze remaining peas.
  5. Stir in measured pesto (about 2 tbsp) and 2 tsp lemon juice. Sprinkle 2 tbsp parmesan.
  6. Portion: Frank largest share; Susan/Elise ~75%.
