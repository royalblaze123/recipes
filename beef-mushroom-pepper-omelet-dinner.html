Beef, Mushroom & Pepper Omelet Plates

NOTES: Breakfast-for-dinner style omelets stuffed with seasoned beef, mushrooms, and peppers plus simple salad. This is a full-plate beef dinner built around lean beef, smart carbs, and plenty of non-starchy vegetables. Uses olive oil or avocado oil only, keeps sodium moderate, and avoids added sugars to support A1c, cholesterol, blood pressure, and PPPD-friendly eating. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

PORTION SIZES (per person): Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 8 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup 1% milk or unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 pound 93% lean ground beef
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1 small bell pepper, diced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 tsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 3 cups spring mix or romaine for side salads

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. In a medium bowl, whisk eggs with milk, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Set aside.
  2. Heat 1 tsp olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add ground beef and cook 4–5 minutes until browned, breaking it up. Drain excess fat if needed.
  3. Add mushrooms, bell pepper, and onion to the beef. Cook 4–5 minutes until vegetables are tender. Stir in smoked paprika and adjust seasoning.
  4. Transfer the beef mixture to a bowl and wipe out the skillet.
  5. Heat remaining 1 tsp olive oil in the skillet over medium heat. Pour in half of the egg mixture and cook, lifting edges to let uncooked egg flow underneath, until just set. Spoon half of the beef mixture over one side, fold the omelet, and cook 1 minute more. Repeat with remaining eggs and filling.
  6. Serve omelet wedges with a simple side salad of spring mix or romaine.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning with a pinch of salt or extra herbs if needed, keeping overall sodium moderate.
  8. Divide the finished meal into three plates or containers: give Frank roughly 1/3 of the total volume, and split the remaining 2/3 between Susan and Elise as slightly smaller but still generous portions.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~520 kcal, ~48 g protein, ~32 g carbs, ~18 g fat.
  • Susan: ~390 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
  • Elise: ~390 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
