Pork, Apple & Cabbage Skillet

NOTES: Skillet pork with cabbage and a small amount of apple for sweetness, plus carrots. This is a full-plate pork dinner built around lean pork tenderloin/loin or 93% lean ground pork, smart carbs, and plenty of non-starchy vegetables. Uses olive or avocado oil only, keeps sodium moderate, and avoids added sugars to support A1c, cholesterol, blood pressure, and PPPD-friendly eating. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

PORTION SIZES (per person): Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 1 pound 93% lean ground pork
  • 4 cups thinly sliced green cabbage
  • 1 medium crisp apple, thinly sliced
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 medium carrot, shredded
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add onion and carrot and cook 3–4 minutes until beginning to soften.
  2. Add ground pork and cook 4–5 minutes, breaking it up, until no longer pink. Drain excess fat if needed.
  3. Stir in garlic, thyme, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper and cook 1 minute.
  4. Add cabbage and apple slices and toss to combine.
  5. Cook 6–8 minutes, stirring frequently, until cabbage is tender but still has some bite and apple slices are softened. Stir in apple cider vinegar during the last minute of cooking.
  6. Taste and adjust seasoning with a pinch of salt or extra herbs if needed, keeping overall sodium moderate.
  7. Divide the finished meal into three plates or containers: give Frank roughly 1/3 of the total volume, and split the remaining 2/3 between Susan and Elise as slightly smaller but still generous portions.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~500 kcal, ~46 g protein, ~34 g carbs, ~16 g fat.
  • Susan: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
  • Elise: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
