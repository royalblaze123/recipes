Shrimp and Corn Chowder

NOTES: This chowder is designed as a full meal in a bowl with lean protein, smart carbs, and 1–2 cups of vegetables per person. Use low-sodium broth and ingredients, avoid added sugars, and keep portions aligned with your goals. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total pot); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 cups low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup 2% milk or unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and diced small
  • 1 cup frozen corn kernels, no salt added
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Cut shrimp into bite-size pieces if large, so each bowl has shrimp in every bite.
  2. Heat olive oil in a soup pot over medium heat for 1–2 minutes.
  3. Add diced onion and cook 4–5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened.
  4. Stir in diced potatoes and corn. Add broth, smoked paprika, garlic powder, and black pepper.
  5. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer 12–15 minutes until potatoes are tender.
  6. Stir in milk and shrimp. Cook on low heat 4–5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until shrimp are pink and opaque and chowder is hot (do not boil).
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning with more pepper if needed. Ladle into three bowls, giving Frank the largest portion of shrimp and potatoes.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~590 kcal, ~47 g protein, ~55 g carbs, ~16 g fat.
  • Susan: ~445 kcal, ~35 g protein, ~41 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
  • Elise: ~445 kcal, ~35 g protein, ~41 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
