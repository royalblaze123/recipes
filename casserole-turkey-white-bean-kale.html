Turkey White Bean Kale Bake

NOTES: Built as a complete dinner using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled starch portions. Use low-sodium broth and minimal added salt to support blood pressure and kidney health. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of pan); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of pan); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of pan).

Ingredients

  • 1 lb 93% lean ground turkey
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 2 cans (15 oz each) no-salt-added cannellini or great northern beans, drained and rinsed
  • 4 cups chopped kale (stems removed) or baby kale
  • 1 cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1/3 cup plain 2% Greek yogurt
  • Olive oil spray for baking dish

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Lightly spray an 8x11-inch baking dish with olive oil spray.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  3. Add diced onion, carrots, and celery and cook 5–6 minutes until vegetables begin to soften.
  4. Stir in minced garlic and diced red bell pepper and cook 2–3 minutes more.
  5. Add ground turkey, breaking it into crumbles, and cook 6–8 minutes until no pink remains.
  6. Stir in dried thyme, dried oregano, smoked paprika, salt, and black pepper and cook 1–2 minutes to bloom spices.
  7. Add drained cannellini beans, chopped kale, and low-sodium chicken broth. Stir and cook 5–7 minutes until kale is wilted and liquid has reduced slightly.
  8. Remove from heat and let mixture cool 2–3 minutes, then stir in Greek yogurt until evenly combined.
  9. Transfer the turkey-bean-kale mixture to the prepared baking dish and spread into an even layer.
  10. Bake uncovered 20–25 minutes until the top is lightly browned at the edges and mixture is bubbling.
  11. Let casserole rest 5–10 minutes before cutting into portions for Frank, Susan, and Elise.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~540 kcal, ~47 g protein, ~45 g carbs, ~15 g fat.
  • Susan: ~405 kcal, ~35 g protein, ~34 g carbs, ~11 g fat.
  • Elise: ~405 kcal, ~35 g protein, ~34 g carbs, ~11 g fat.
