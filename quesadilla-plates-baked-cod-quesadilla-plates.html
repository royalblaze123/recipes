Baked Cod Quesadilla Plates

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 630 kcal • P 58g • C 45g • F 20g
Susan/Elise (each): 472 kcal • P 44g • C 34g • F 15g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.50 lb cod fillets
  • 1 package whole-grain tortillas
  • 1 bag reduced-fat shredded Mexican cheese blend
  • 1 bag spring mix
  • 1 each avocado
  • 1/2 jar salsa
  • 1 each lime
  • 6 tsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 tsp chili powder
  • 2 tsp cumin
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp kosher salt

Steps

  1. Fish: pat cod dry. Rub with 2 tsp olive oil and season with spices.
  2. Bake fish at 425°F for 10–12 minutes until 145°F and flaky. Rest 3 minutes, then break into flakes.
  3. Assembly: lay tortillas on a board. Distribute the filling evenly and top with a measured layer of shredded cheese. Add the second tortilla on top for Frank’s quesadilla; for Susan/Elise, fold a single tortilla over the filling.
  4. Cook: heat a large nonstick skillet over medium. Cook quesadillas 2–3 minutes per side until tortillas are browned and cheese is melted. (Work in batches if needed.)
  5. Tip: use a spatula to press quesadillas gently while cooking so the fish and cheese bind.
  6. Side salad: add spring mix to a large bowl. Drizzle with 2 tsp lime juice and 2 tsp olive oil, then toss. Add diced cucumber/tomato if listed.
  7. Avocado: halve, pit, and dice. Use about 1/2 avocado total; refrigerate remaining avocado with the pit to slow browning.
  8. Serve: slice quesadillas into wedges. Serve with salad, salsa, and avocado. Portion per Notes.

Notes

Flaky cod quesadillas are surprisingly good—especially with salsa + avocado. Portions (per person): Frank: Frank: 1 large quesadilla (made with 2 tortillas) + 1/3 of the salad/veg side + salsa + avocado Susan: Susan: 1 medium quesadilla (made with 1 tortilla folded) + 1/4 of the salad/veg side + salsa + avocado Elise: Elise: 1 medium quesadilla (made with 1 tortilla folded) + 1/4 of the salad/veg side + salsa + avocado
