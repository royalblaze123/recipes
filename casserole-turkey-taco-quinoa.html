Turkey Taco Quinoa Bake

NOTES: Built as a complete dinner using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled starch portions. Use low-sodium broth and minimal added salt to support blood pressure and kidney health. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of pan); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of pan); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of pan).

Ingredients

  • 1 cup uncooked quinoa, rinsed
  • 2 cups low-sodium chicken broth or water
  • 1 lb 93% lean ground turkey
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 can (15 oz) no-salt-added black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup frozen corn kernels (no added salt), thawed and drained
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) no-salt-added diced tomatoes, drained well
  • 1/2 cup shredded reduced-fat Mexican blend cheese
  • Olive oil spray for baking dish

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Lightly spray a 9x13-inch baking dish with olive oil spray.
  2. Cook quinoa: In a saucepan, combine rinsed quinoa and low-sodium broth or water. Bring to a boil, reduce heat, cover, and simmer 15 minutes until liquid is absorbed. Remove from heat and fluff with a fork.
  3. While quinoa cooks, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  4. Add diced onion and red bell pepper and cook 4–5 minutes until softened.
  5. Stir in minced garlic and cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
  6. Add ground turkey, breaking it into crumbles with a spatula, and cook 6–8 minutes until no pink remains.
  7. Stir in chili powder, ground cumin, smoked paprika, dried oregano, salt, and black pepper until turkey and vegetables are evenly coated.
  8. Add drained black beans, thawed corn, and well-drained diced tomatoes to the skillet. Stir and cook 2–3 minutes to warm through and let excess moisture cook off slightly.
  9. In a large mixing bowl, combine cooked quinoa and the turkey-veggie mixture. Stir until evenly distributed.
  10. Transfer mixture to the prepared baking dish and spread into an even layer.
  11. Sprinkle reduced-fat Mexican blend cheese evenly over the top.
  12. Bake uncovered 20–25 minutes until hot, edges are lightly crisp, and cheese is melted and starting to brown.
  13. Let casserole rest 5–10 minutes before cutting into portions for Frank, Susan, and Elise.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~580 kcal, ~48 g protein, ~60 g carbs, ~16 g fat.
  • Susan: ~435 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~45 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
  • Elise: ~435 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~45 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
