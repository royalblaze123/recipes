Lean Turkey Shepherd’s Pie (Potato-Cauliflower Mash)

NOTES: Built as a complete dinner using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled starch portions. Use low-sodium broth and minimal added salt to support blood pressure and kidney health. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of pan); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of pan); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of pan).

Ingredients

  • 1 lb 93% lean ground turkey
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 medium carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 cup frozen peas (no added salt), thawed and drained
  • 1 cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste (no added sugar)
  • 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce (low sodium if available)
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1/2 tsp dried rosemary, crushed
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 3 medium Yukon gold potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 cups small cauliflower florets
  • 2 tbsp plain 2% Greek yogurt
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil (for mash)
  • Olive oil spray for baking dish

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Lightly spray an 8x11- or 9x9-inch baking dish with olive oil spray.
  2. Make the mash: Place cubed potatoes and cauliflower florets in a pot and cover with water. Bring to a boil, reduce heat, and simmer 12–15 minutes until both are very tender. Drain well.
  3. Return drained potatoes and cauliflower to the warm pot. Add Greek yogurt, olive oil, a small pinch of salt, and black pepper. Mash until smooth and creamy, adding a tablespoon of warm water if needed. Set aside.
  4. While mash cooks, heat 1 tablespoon olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  5. Add diced onion, carrots, and celery and cook 5–6 minutes until softened.
  6. Stir in minced garlic and cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
  7. Add ground turkey, breaking it into crumbles, and cook 6–8 minutes until no pink remains.
  8. Stir in tomato paste, Worcestershire sauce, dried thyme, dried rosemary, salt, and black pepper until turkey and vegetables are evenly coated.
  9. Pour in low-sodium chicken broth and simmer 5–7 minutes until slightly thickened and most of the liquid has reduced.
  10. Stir in thawed peas and cook 1–2 minutes more.
  11. Transfer turkey-vegetable mixture to the prepared baking dish and spread evenly.
  12. Gently spread the potato-cauliflower mash over the top, smoothing it to cover the filling completely.
  13. Place the dish on a baking sheet and bake 20–25 minutes until the top is lightly golden and the filling is bubbling around the edges.
  14. Let shepherd’s pie rest 10 minutes before slicing into portions for Frank, Susan, and Elise.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~580 kcal, ~45 g protein, ~58 g carbs, ~17 g fat.
  • Susan: ~435 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~44 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
  • Elise: ~435 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~44 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
