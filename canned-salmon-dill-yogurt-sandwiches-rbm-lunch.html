Canned Salmon Dill Yogurt Sandwiches - RBM Lunch

Canned salmon salad with lemon and dill plus a cucumber and carrot side.

Portions (per person):

Frank – 4 sandwich halves (2 sandwiches) + veg side

 

Susan – 3 sandwich halves (1.5 sandwiches) + veg side

 

Elise – 3 sandwich halves (1.5 sandwiches) + veg side

Ingredients

  • 10 slices thin whole wheat bread
  • 2 cans salmon
  • 1 container plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 lemon
  • 1 bunch dill
  • 1 bunch celery
  • 1 romaine heart
  • 1 cucumber
  • 1 lb bag baby carrots
  • 1 bottle olive oil spray

Step-by-step Instructions

  1. Drain the salmon cans well. Flake salmon in a bowl, removing any large bones or skin as desired.
  2. Wash celery stalks from the bunch. Finely chop enough celery to measure 1/2 cup chopped celery for the salmon salad; refrigerate remaining celery.
  3. Wash dill from the bunch. Finely chop enough to measure 2 tablespoons chopped dill for the salmon salad; refrigerate remaining dill.
  4. Make dressing: mix 6 tablespoons plain Greek yogurt with 1 tablespoon lemon juice and lemon zest from the same lemon.
  5. Combine: stir flaked salmon, chopped celery, and chopped dill into the dressing until evenly coated.
  6. Wash the romaine heart. Chop enough to measure about 4 cups chopped romaine for the sandwiches; refrigerate remaining romaine if any.
  7. Toast option: lightly mist 10 bread slices with olive oil spray and toast 2–4 minutes, flipping once, until lightly crisp.
  8. Assemble 5 sandwiches: divide salmon salad evenly across 5 bread slices. Top each with romaine. Close with remaining bread slices.
  9. Cut each sandwich in half to make 10 halves. Portion: Frank gets 4 halves; Susan 3 halves; Elise 3 halves.
  10. Veg sides: slice the cucumber and measure 1 cup cucumber slices for Frank and 3/4 cup for Susan and Elise. Measure baby carrots: 1/2 cup for Frank and 1/3 cup for Susan and Elise. Pack separately.
