Tuna Verde Protein Nachos + Salad

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 610 kcal • P 60g • C 38g • F 19g
Susan/Elise (each): 458 kcal • P 45g • C 28g • F 14g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 3 cans tuna
  • 2/3 bag bean-based tortilla chips
  • 3/4 bag reduced-fat shredded Mexican cheese blend
  • 1/2 tub 2% plain Greek yogurt
  • 1/2 jar salsa
  • 1 jar salsa verde
  • 1 bag spring mix
  • 1 each cucumber
  • 8 tsp light salad dressing
  • 2 each bell peppers
  • 1 each yellow onion
  • 4 tsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp kosher salt

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a large sheet pan with parchment.
  2. Tuna: can size note—use three 5 oz cans tuna. Drain very well.
  3. Veg: slice bell peppers and onion into thin strips. Toss with 4 tsp olive oil on the sheet pan and roast 10 minutes.
  4. Tuna seasoning: mix drained tuna with 2 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp black pepper, and 1 tsp kosher salt.
  5. Add tuna to the roasted vegetables and spread into an even layer.
  6. Cheese: sprinkle the listed shredded cheese evenly over the topping mix.
  7. Bake 4–6 minutes until cheese is melted and bubbling.
  8. Chips: portion chips by weight: Frank 1.5 oz; Susan 1.125 oz; Elise 1.125 oz. Keep chips separate for crunch.
  9. Verde + yogurt drizzle: stir 1/2 cup salsa verde with 6 tbsp Greek yogurt until smooth.
  10. Salad: slice cucumber. Toss spring mix with cucumber and 8 tsp dressing right before serving.
  11. Serve: spoon hot topping over chips and finish with verde-yogurt drizzle. Portion per Notes. Serve salad alongside.

Notes

Tuna nachos that stay light: no mayo, just tuna + roasted veg + measured chips and cheese. Portions (per person): Frank: Chips: 1.5 oz + 1/3 of the hot topping mix + 1/3 of the cold toppings + Salad: 1/3 (F) or 1/4 (S/E) Susan: Chips: 1.125 oz + 1/4 of the hot topping mix + 1/4 of the cold toppings + Salad: 1/3 (F) or 1/4 (S/E) Elise: Chips: 1.125 oz + 1/4 of the hot topping mix + 1/4 of the cold toppings + Salad: 1/3 (F) or 1/4 (S/E)
