Skillet Pork Chops with Garlic Mashed Potatoes & Green Peas

NOTES: Pan-seared pork chops served with light garlic mashed potatoes and steamed peas. This is a full-plate pork dinner built around lean pork tenderloin/loin or 93% lean ground pork, smart carbs, and plenty of non-starchy vegetables. Uses olive or avocado oil only, keeps sodium moderate, and avoids added sugars to support A1c, cholesterol, blood pressure, and PPPD-friendly eating. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

PORTION SIZES (per person): Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 3 lean center-cut pork chops (about 4–5 oz each)
  • 1 1/2 pounds Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 1 cup frozen green peas
  • 2 tbsp olive oil, divided
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup 1% milk or unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 tbsp light butter or 1 extra tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried thyme or Italian seasoning
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • 3/4 tsp kosher salt, divided (keep moderate)
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper, divided

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Place potatoes in a pot, cover with cool water, and add a light pinch of salt. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer and cook 12–15 minutes until fork-tender.
  2. During the last 4–5 minutes of potato cooking, steam the peas in a steamer basket over the pot or in a separate small saucepan with a splash of water until bright green and tender. Drain and set aside.
  3. Drain potatoes and return to the warm pot. Add milk, light butter or extra olive oil, garlic powder, half of the salt, and half of the pepper. Mash to desired texture.
  4. While potatoes cook, pat pork chops dry and season on both sides with thyme or Italian seasoning, onion powder, and remaining salt and pepper.
  5. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add pork chops and sear 3–4 minutes per side until nicely browned and cooked to 145°F in the center.
  6. During the last minute of cooking, add minced garlic and remaining 1 tbsp olive oil to the skillet, spooning the garlicky pan juices over the chops.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning with a pinch of salt or extra herbs if needed, keeping overall sodium moderate.
  8. Divide the finished meal into three plates or containers: give Frank roughly 1/3 of the total volume, and split the remaining 2/3 between Susan and Elise as slightly smaller but still generous portions.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~500 kcal, ~46 g protein, ~34 g carbs, ~16 g fat.
  • Susan: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
  • Elise: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
