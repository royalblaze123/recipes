Air Fryer Chicken Tenders with Potato Wedges and Green Beans

Servings

3 plates (Frank 1.0×, Susan 0.75×, Elise 0.75×).

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 25 minutes
  • Total time: 45 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lb boneless skinless chicken breasts
  • 1.25 lb baby potatoes
  • 1 bag frozen green beans
  • 6 tsp olive oil
  • 2 tsp bottled lemon juice
  • 2 tsp minced garlic
  • 1 cup whole wheat panko breadcrumbs
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 2 tsp Italian seasoning
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 tsp salt substitute
  • 1 tsp black pepper

Recipe

  1. Preheat the air fryer to 390°F for 3–5 minutes with the empty basket in place so it is hot when you add the food.
  2. Scrub 1.25 lb baby potatoes and cut them into 1/2- to 3/4-inch wedges. Add the wedges to a large bowl and toss with 3 tsp olive oil, 1 tsp garlic powder, and 1 tsp salt substitute until evenly coated.
  3. Spread the seasoned potato wedges in a single layer in the air fryer basket. Air fry at 390°F for 10–12 minutes, shaking the basket halfway through, until the edges are browned and the potatoes are just tender. Transfer potatoes to a warm bowl and cover loosely with foil.
  4. While the potatoes cook, microwave 1 bag frozen green beans according to the package directions until just tender-crisp. Drain any excess liquid and set aside for serving.
  5. Slice 1.5 lb boneless skinless chicken breasts lengthwise into about 10 equal-sized strips to make chicken tenders. Pat the strips very dry with paper towels so the coating adheres.
  6. In a shallow dish, whisk together 3 tsp olive oil, 2 tsp bottled lemon juice, and 2 tsp minced garlic. Add the chicken tenders and turn to lightly coat each piece; let sit 5 minutes.
  7. In a separate shallow dish, combine 1 cup whole wheat panko breadcrumbs, 2 tsp smoked paprika, 2 tsp Italian seasoning, 1 tsp onion powder, 1 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp salt substitute, and 1 tsp black pepper. Stir well so the seasonings are evenly distributed through the crumbs.
  8. Working one piece at a time, lift each marinated chicken strip out of the oil mixture, letting excess drip off, then press it into the seasoned panko on all sides to coat. Place coated tenders on a plate or tray in a single layer.
  9. Lightly spray or brush the air fryer basket with a small amount of olive oil if it tends to stick. Arrange the chicken tenders in a single layer in the basket with a little space between pieces; cook in two batches if needed so they are not crowded.
  10. Air fry the chicken tenders at 390°F for 9–11 minutes total, turning once halfway through, until the thickest pieces reach 165°F on an instant-read thermometer and the coating is golden and crisp. If cooking in batches, keep finished tenders on a rack or plate loosely tented with foil.
  11. Right before serving, quickly rewarm the potato wedges and green beans if needed. Build plates: Frank gets 4 tenders (about 8 oz cooked chicken), 1 1/4 cups potato wedges, and 1 1/4 cups green beans; Susan and Elise each get 3 tenders (about 5.5 oz cooked chicken), 1 cup potato wedges, and 1 cup green beans.

Notes

Portions (per person):

Frank – 1 plate (4 chicken tenders, about 8 oz cooked chicken total, 1 1/4 cups potato wedges, 1 1/4 cups green beans).

Susan – 1 plate (3 chicken tenders, about 5.5 oz cooked chicken total, 1 cup potato wedges, 1 cup green beans).

Elise – 1 plate (3 chicken tenders, about 5.5 oz cooked chicken total, 1 cup potato wedges, 1 cup green beans).

Tips (recipe-specific):

Arrange the chicken tenders in a single layer with space between pieces so the coating crisps instead of steaming.

If your air fryer basket is small, cook the tenders in two batches and hold the first batch on a rack so the bottoms stay crisp.
