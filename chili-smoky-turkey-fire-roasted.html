Smoky Turkey Chili with Fire-Roasted Tomatoes

NOTES: Each chili is designed as a full meal with lean protein, smart carbs, and plenty of vegetables. Use low-sodium, no-sugar-added canned goods, avoid added sugars, and keep portions aligned with your goals. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total pot); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 12 oz lean ground turkey (93%+ lean)
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced or 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 can (15 oz) no-salt-added black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) no-salt-added fire-roasted diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste (no sugar added)
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 1/2 tsp mild chili powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 3/4 tsp smoked paprika
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Dice onion and green bell pepper into small pieces.
  2. Heat olive oil in a soup pot over medium heat for 1–2 minutes.
  3. Add ground turkey and cook 5–6 minutes, breaking it into small pieces, until browned and cooked through.
  4. Add onion, bell pepper, and garlic. Cook 4–5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until vegetables soften.
  5. Stir in chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, and black pepper. Cook 1 minute.
  6. Add black beans, fire-roasted tomatoes with juice, tomato paste, and chicken broth. Stir well.
  7. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer 25–30 minutes, stirring once or twice, until chili thickens and flavors deepen.
  8. Taste and adjust seasoning. Ladle into three bowls, giving Frank the largest portion. Serve with a side of steamed broccoli or green beans if desired.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~630 kcal, ~48 g protein, ~50 g carbs, ~18 g fat.
  • Susan: ~475 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~38 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
  • Elise: ~475 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~38 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
