Smoky Salmon Chickpea-Tomato Skillet over Brown Rice

A one-pan salmon skillet with chickpeas and tomatoes served over brown rice for steady, slow-carb energy.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 20 minutes
  • Total time: 40 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 1 lb fresh salmon fillet
  • 1 bag brown rice
  • 1 can no-salt-added diced tomatoes
  • 1 can chickpeas
  • 1 each yellow onion
  • 1 each bell pepper
  • 1 jar smoked paprika
  • 1 jar ground cumin
  • 1 jar garlic powder
  • 1 jar black pepper
  • 1 bottle extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 container nonfat Greek yogurt
  • 1 each lemon
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Cook rice: cook 3/4 cup dry brown rice according to package directions. Measure out 1 cup cooked rice for this recipe; refrigerate remaining rice.
    2. Prep veg: dice 1/2 onion and 1/2 bell pepper; store remaining onion/pepper for another recipe.
    3. Build the skillet base: heat 1/2 tbsp olive oil in a skillet over medium. Cook onion/pepper until soft.
    4. Add tomatoes + chickpeas: drain and rinse chickpeas. Measure out 1 1/4 cups chickpeas for the skillet; refrigerate remaining chickpeas. Add the full can of no-salt-added diced tomatoes and simmer 5 minutes. Season with smoked paprika, cumin, garlic powder, and black pepper.
    5. Cook salmon: cut salmon into 3 portions (about 5–6 oz each). Nestle into the skillet, cover, and cook 6–8 minutes until salmon flakes.
    6. Make quick yogurt drizzle: stir 1/2 cup nonfat Greek yogurt with lemon juice and a pinch of spices.
    7. Plate: spoon rice into bowls, top with chickpea-tomato mixture and salmon, then drizzle lightly with yogurt.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – About 1 cup cooked brown rice + 1 generous cup chickpea-tomato mixture + 1 salmon portion (about 40% of the full recipe).


      Susan – About 3/4 cup cooked brown rice + 1 cup chickpea-tomato mixture + a smaller salmon portion (about 30% of the full recipe).


      Elise – About 3/4 cup cooked brown rice + 1 cup chickpea-tomato mixture + a smaller salmon portion (about 30% of the full recipe).


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Keep chickpeas firm by simmering briefly—avoid long boiling so the skillet stays chunky, not mushy.


      If sodium is a concern, choose no-salt-added tomatoes and rely on smoked paprika + cumin for flavor.


      If you want more heat, add red pepper flakes at the table so the base stays GERD-friendly.
