Reduce & Glaze Turkey with Cabbage + Peppers/Onions over Roasted Sweet Potatoes

Servings

3 plates (Frank 1.0×, Susan 0.75×, Elise 0.75×).

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 25 minutes
  • Total time: 45 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2.0 lb turkey breast cutlets
  • 2 sweet potatoes
  • 1 head green cabbage
  • 3 bell peppers
  • 9 tsp olive oil
  • 4 tsp minced garlic
  • 2 tsp black pepper
  • 2 tsp salt substitute
  • 2 tsp dried parsley
  • 5 tsp lemon juice
  • 4 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 2 tsp cornstarch

Steps

  1. Base: Peel and cube sweet potatoes into 3/4-inch pieces (~6 cups). Toss with 3 tsp olive oil + pepper + salt substitute. Roast at 425°F for 20–25 minutes, stirring once, until browned and tender.
  2. Skillet: Heat a large nonstick skillet over medium-high. You want sizzle, not sadness.
  3. Vegetables: Add 3 tsp olive oil to a large skillet over medium-high. Add vegetables; cook 6–10 minutes until tender-crisp with browned edges. Add 2 tsp minced garlic for the last 60 seconds.
  4. Protein: Slice lean meat across the grain into strips. Sear in 2 batches with 3 tsp olive oil 2–3 minutes per batch to brown; transfer.
  5. Glaze slurry: Whisk glaze ingredients (including cornstarch) with 1/2 cup water until smooth.
  6. Reduce & glaze: Return protein + veg to the skillet. Pour in slurry. Cook 2–4 minutes, stirring, until glossy and the sauce clings to everything.
  7. Finish: Finish with parsley and black pepper; avoid extra acid at the table if GERD-sensitive.
  8. Plate portions per Notes.

Notes

Portions (per person):

Frank – 1 plate (8 oz cooked protein, 1 cup roasted sweet potatoes, 2 cups vegetables, 3–4 tbsp glaze).

Susan – 1 plate (6 oz cooked protein, 3/4 of Frank’s base portion, 1 1/2 cups vegetables, 3 tbsp glaze).

Elise – 1 plate (6 oz cooked protein, 3/4 of Frank’s base portion, 1 1/2 cups vegetables, 3 tbsp glaze).

Tips (recipe-specific):

The whole point is reduction: 2–4 minutes at the end until it clings. If it’s watery, you stopped early.

Cook vegetables hot and fast for browning; steam-soft veg kills the ‘gourmet’ vibe instantly.

Storage + Reheat

  • Cool promptly; store in airtight containers in the fridge up to 3–4 days (typical safety guidance).
  • Reheat in a skillet with 1–2 tbsp water to re-gloss the glaze, or microwave 60–90 seconds; add any fresh finish (zest/herbs) after reheating.
