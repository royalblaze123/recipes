White Bean & Turkey Sausage Soup (Stovetop)

NOTES: Designed as a complete meal soup using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled starch. Use low-sodium broth and avoid extra salt when possible to support blood pressure and kidney health. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~2 cups); Susan: medium portion (~1.5 cups); Elise: medium portion (~1.5 cups).

Ingredients

  • 1 lb lean turkey sausage (Italian style, casings removed, ~93% lean)
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil (use only if sausage is very lean)
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 2 medium carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cans (15 oz each) no-salt-added cannellini or great northern beans, drained and rinsed
  • 4 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 cups chopped kale or baby spinach
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Heat a large soup pot over medium heat. Add olive oil if sausage is very lean.
  2. Add turkey sausage and cook 6–8 minutes, breaking into crumbles, until fully browned. Transfer sausage to a plate and set aside.
  3. In the same pot, add diced onion, carrots, and celery. Cook 5–6 minutes, stirring occasionally, until vegetables begin to soften.
  4. Stir in minced garlic and cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Add dried oregano, dried thyme, smoked paprika, and bay leaf. Stir to coat vegetables in spices.
  6. Return browned sausage to the pot and stir to combine.
  7. Add drained and rinsed white beans, low-sodium chicken broth, and water. Stir well.
  8. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer uncovered 20–25 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  9. Stir in chopped kale or baby spinach and cook 3–5 minutes until greens wilt and turn bright green.
  10. Taste and adjust seasoning with a small pinch of salt and black pepper as needed.
  11. Remove bay leaf and discard.
  12. Let soup rest 3–5 minutes, then ladle into bowls for Frank, Susan, and Elise.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~480 kcal, ~42 g protein, ~40 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
  • Susan: ~360 kcal, ~31 g protein, ~30 g carbs, ~10 g fat.
  • Elise: ~360 kcal, ~31 g protein, ~30 g carbs, ~10 g fat.
