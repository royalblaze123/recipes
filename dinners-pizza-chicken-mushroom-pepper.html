Chicken, Mushroom & Pepper Pizza

Whole-wheat pizza with chicken, mushrooms, and bell peppers using a light tomato base and part-skim cheese for a classic flavor profile within Frank Household goals.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 12 minutes
  • Cook time: 16 minutes
  • Total time: 28 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 large rectangular whole-wheat thin pizza crust
  • 13 oz cooked chicken breast
  • 2 cups sliced mushrooms
  • 2 cups thinly sliced bell peppers
  • 1 cup small broccoli florets
  • 1/2 cup thinly sliced red onion
  • 3/4 cup no-salt-added tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
  • 4 oz part-skim mozzarella cheese
  • 1 oz finely grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp dried Italian seasoning
  • 1/4 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp kosher or sea salt
  • Olive oil spray

Preparation Steps

  1. Heat the oven to 425°F (about 220°C). Line a large sheet pan with parchment paper and lightly spray with olive oil.
  2. If the chicken is not yet cooked, season about 1 lb raw boneless skinless chicken breast lightly with salt, pepper, and garlic powder and bake or sauté until cooked through (165°F internal), then chop to make 13 oz cooked chicken breast. Set aside.
  3. Rinse and dry the mushrooms, bell peppers, broccoli, and red onion (if using). Slice the mushrooms and peppers, cut the broccoli into small florets, and thinly slice the onion.
  4. In a medium bowl, toss the mushrooms, bell peppers, broccoli, and onion with 1 tsp olive oil and a light sprinkle of salt and pepper. Spread the vegetables on a separate parchment-lined pan and pre-roast for 6–8 minutes until just starting to soften.
  5. In a small bowl, stir together the tomato sauce, garlic powder, Italian seasoning, black pepper, and a pinch of salt.
  6. Place the whole-wheat pizza crust on the prepared sheet pan. Spread the seasoned tomato sauce evenly over the crust, leaving a small border at the edges.
  7. Sprinkle half of the shredded mozzarella over the sauce. Scatter the chopped cooked chicken evenly over the crust. Add the pre-roasted vegetables in an even layer on top of the chicken.
  8. Sprinkle the remaining mozzarella and the Parmesan evenly over the pizza. Drizzle the remaining 2 tsp olive oil lightly over the toppings.
  9. Bake at 425°F for 10–12 minutes, or until the crust is crisp, the cheese is melted, and the edges are lightly browned.
  10. Remove the pizza from the oven and let it rest for 2–3 minutes. Cut into 10 equal squares.
  11. Place 4 of the 10 squares on a plate for Frank, making sure his portion includes plenty of chicken and vegetables.
  12. Place 3 squares each on plates for Susan and Elise, redistributing any loose toppings as needed.

  • Susan (S · 0.75×): 3 of 10 squares with ~3.4 oz cooked chicken breast, ~2 oz whole-wheat crust, ~1.2 oz part-skim mozzarella, and ~1 1/2 cups vegetables.
  • Elise (E · 0.75×): 3 of 10 squares with ~3.4 oz cooked chicken breast, ~2 oz whole-wheat crust, ~1.2 oz part-skim mozzarella, and ~1 1/2 cups vegetables.
