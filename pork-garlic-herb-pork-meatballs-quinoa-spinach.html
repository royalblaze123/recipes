Garlic-Herb Pork Meatballs with Quinoa & Spinach

NOTES: Oven-baked lean pork meatballs served over quinoa with sautéed spinach. This is a full-plate pork dinner built around lean pork tenderloin/loin or 93% lean ground pork, smart carbs, and plenty of non-starchy vegetables. Uses olive or avocado oil only, keeps sodium moderate, and avoids added sugars to support A1c, cholesterol, blood pressure, and PPPD-friendly eating. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

PORTION SIZES (per person): Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 1 1/4 pounds 93% lean ground pork
  • 1/3 cup rolled oats or whole-wheat breadcrumbs
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp dried Italian seasoning
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tbsp olive oil, divided
  • 3/4 cup uncooked quinoa, rinsed
  • 1 1/2 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 6 cups fresh baby spinach
  • Juice of 1/2 lemon (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a bowl, combine ground pork, oats or breadcrumbs, egg, garlic, Italian seasoning, onion powder, salt, and pepper. Mix gently until just combined.
  3. Form into 18–20 small meatballs and arrange on the baking sheet.
  4. Bake 15–18 minutes, turning once halfway through, until browned and cooked through.
  5. While meatballs bake, combine quinoa and broth in a small saucepan. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer 15 minutes or until liquid is absorbed. Remove from heat and let stand 5 minutes before fluffing.
  6. In a large skillet, heat 1 tbsp olive oil over medium heat. Add spinach and sauté 2–3 minutes until wilted. Season with a light pinch of salt and lemon juice if desired.
  7. To serve, divide quinoa among plates, top with spinach, and arrange meatballs over the top.
  8. Taste and adjust seasoning with a pinch of salt or extra herbs if needed, keeping overall sodium moderate.
  9. Divide the finished meal into three plates or containers: give Frank roughly 1/3 of the total volume, and split the remaining 2/3 between Susan and Elise as slightly smaller but still generous portions.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~500 kcal, ~46 g protein, ~34 g carbs, ~16 g fat.
  • Susan: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
  • Elise: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
