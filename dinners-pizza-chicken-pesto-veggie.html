Chicken Pesto Veggie Thin-Crust Pizza

Whole-wheat thin-crust pizza with chicken, light basil pesto yogurt sauce, and plenty of vegetables for a higher-protein, herb-forward dinner.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 12 minutes
  • Cook time: 16 minutes
  • Total time: 28 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 large rectangular whole-wheat thin pizza crust
  • 13 oz cooked chicken breast
  • 3 cups lightly packed fresh baby spinach
  • 1 cup thinly sliced bell peppers
  • 1 cup halved grape tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup thinly sliced red onion
  • 1/2 cup 2% plain Greek yogurt
  • 2 tbsp prepared basil pesto
  • 1 tbsp water
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/4 tsp dried Italian seasoning
  • 1/4 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp kosher or sea salt
  • 4 oz part-skim mozzarella cheese
  • 1 oz finely grated Parmesan cheese
  • Olive oil spray

Preparation Steps

  1. Heat the oven to 425°F (about 220°C). Line a large sheet pan with parchment paper and lightly spray with olive oil.
  2. If the chicken is not yet cooked, season about 1 lb raw boneless skinless chicken breast lightly with salt, pepper, and garlic powder and bake or sauté until cooked through (165°F internal), then chop to make 13 oz cooked chicken breast. Set aside.
  3. Rinse and dry the spinach, bell peppers, grape tomatoes, and red onion (if using). Roughly chop the spinach, slice the peppers, halve the tomatoes, and thinly slice the onion.
  4. In a medium bowl, toss the spinach, peppers, tomatoes, and onion with 1 tsp olive oil and a light sprinkle of salt and pepper. Spread the vegetables on a separate parchment-lined pan and pre-roast for 5–6 minutes until just starting to soften.
  5. Make the pesto yogurt sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together the Greek yogurt, basil pesto, remaining 2 tsp olive oil, garlic powder, Italian seasoning (if using), black pepper, salt, and about 1 tbsp water as needed until smooth and spreadable.
  6. Place the whole-wheat pizza crust on the prepared main sheet pan. Spread the pesto yogurt sauce evenly over the crust, leaving a small border at the edges.
  7. Sprinkle half of the shredded mozzarella over the sauce. Scatter the chopped cooked chicken evenly over the crust. Add the pre-roasted vegetables in an even layer on top of the chicken.
  8. Sprinkle the remaining mozzarella and the grated Parmesan evenly over the pizza.
  9. Bake at 425°F for 10–12 minutes, or until the crust is crisp, the cheese is melted, and the edges are lightly browned.
  10. Remove the pizza from the oven and let it rest for 2–3 minutes. Cut into 10 equal squares.
  11. Place 4 of the 10 squares on a plate for Frank, making sure his slices include plenty of chicken and vegetables.
  12. Place 3 squares each on plates for Susan and Elise, redistributing any loose toppings evenly.
