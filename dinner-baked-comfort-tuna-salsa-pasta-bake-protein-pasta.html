Tuna Salsa Pasta Bake (Protein Pasta)

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 640 kcal • P 62g • C 58g • F 16g • Sat 6g • Na 690mg • Fiber 10g
Susan/Elise (each): 480 kcal • P 46g • C 44g • F 12g • Sat 4g • Na 518mg • Fiber 8g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 3 cans tuna
  • 1 box Barilla Protein+ pasta
  • 1 jar salsa
  • 1 bag baby spinach
  • 6 oz reduced-fat shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 2 oz grated Parmesan cheese
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • garlic powder
  • onion powder
  • smoked paprika
  • chili powder
  • ground cumin
  • Italian seasoning
  • extra-virgin olive oil

Steps

  1. Oven: heat to 400°F. Lightly oil a baking dish.
  2. Pasta: cook Barilla Protein+ pasta to al dente; drain, reserve 1/2 cup pasta water.
  3. Tuna: Can size note—use a standard 5 oz can tuna.. Use three 5 oz cans tuna; drain very well.
  4. Sauce: stir 2 cups salsa + 1/4 cup pasta water in a pot; simmer 2 minutes.
  5. Spinach: stir in 4 cups spinach until wilted.
  6. Combine: fold tuna + pasta into sauce. Spread into dish.
  7. Top: sprinkle 6 oz reduced-fat mozzarella + 2 oz Parmesan.
  8. Bake: 14–16 minutes until bubbly. Rest 8 minutes.
  9. Portion per Notes.

Notes

Cozy baked pasta with a salsa twist—tastes like a pantry casserole, but better. Portions (per person): Frank: 1/3 of pan Susan: 1/4 of pan Elise: 1/4 of pan Tips (recipe-specific): Drain tuna very well or the casserole turns watery. Al dente pasta is non‑negotiable for good leftovers.
