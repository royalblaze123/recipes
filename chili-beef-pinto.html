Lean Beef Pinto Chili

NOTES: Each chili is designed as a full meal with lean protein, smart carbs, and plenty of vegetables. Use low-sodium, no-sugar-added canned goods, avoid added sugars, and keep portions aligned with your goals. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total pot); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 12 oz 90–93% lean ground beef
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced or 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 can (15 oz) no-salt-added pinto beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) no-salt-added crushed tomatoes
  • 1 cup low-sodium beef or chicken broth
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste (no sugar added)
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil (optional if pan is very nonstick)
  • 1 1/2 tsp mild chili powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Dice onion and green bell pepper into small, even pieces.
  2. Place a soup pot over medium heat. If needed, add olive oil, then add lean ground beef.
  3. Cook beef 6–7 minutes, breaking it into small crumbles, until browned and cooked through. Drain any visible fat if necessary.
  4. Add diced onion, bell pepper, and garlic. Cook 4–5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until vegetables are softened.
  5. Stir in chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, and black pepper. Cook 1 minute to bloom the spices.
  6. Add pinto beans, crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, and broth. Stir well to combine.
  7. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer 25–30 minutes, stirring once or twice, until the chili thickens and flavors marry.
  8. Taste and adjust seasoning. Ladle into three bowls, giving Frank the largest portion. Serve with an optional side of steamed green beans or a small salad.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~640 kcal, ~49 g protein, ~50 g carbs, ~20 g fat.
  • Susan: ~480 kcal, ~37 g protein, ~38 g carbs, ~15 g fat.
  • Elise: ~480 kcal, ~37 g protein, ~38 g carbs, ~15 g fat.
