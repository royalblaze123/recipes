Herbed Trout with White Beans and Roasted Broccoli

Pan-seared trout with lemony white beans and roasted broccoli for a high-protein, heart-healthy dinner.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 20 minutes
  • Total time: 40 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 1 lb fresh trout fillets
  • 2 cans cannellini beans
  • 1 bag brown rice
  • 1 head broccoli
  • 1 each lemon
  • 1 bunch fresh parsley
  • 1 bottle extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 jar garlic powder
  • 1 jar black pepper
  • 1 jar Italian seasoning
  • 1 container nonfat Greek yogurt
  • 1 container grated parmesan cheese
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Cook rice: cook 1/3 cup dry brown rice. Measure out 1 cup cooked rice for this recipe; refrigerate remaining rice.
    2. Roast broccoli: preheat oven to 425°F. Cut broccoli into florets. Toss with 1 tbsp olive oil, garlic powder, and black pepper. Roast 14–18 minutes, stirring once.
    3. Beans: drain and rinse both cans. In a pot, warm 1 3/4 cups beans with lemon juice, chopped parsley, Italian seasoning, and a splash of water. Refrigerate remaining beans.
    4. Cook trout: pat dry and season with garlic powder, Italian seasoning, and black pepper. Sear in a nonstick skillet 3–4 minutes per side until it flakes.
    5. Optional drizzle: stir 1/2 cup Greek yogurt with lemon juice and parsley; drizzle lightly.
    6. Plate: divide rice + beans + broccoli across 3 plates, top with trout, and finish with a small sprinkle of parmesan.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – 1 trout portion + about 1 cup white beans + about 2 cups roasted broccoli (about 40% of the full recipe).


      Susan – 1 smaller trout portion + about 3/4 cup beans + about 1 1/2 cups broccoli (about 30% of the full recipe).


      Elise – 1 smaller trout portion + about 3/4 cup beans + about 1 1/2 cups broccoli (about 30% of the full recipe).


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      To keep beans from turning mushy, warm gently and avoid hard boiling.


      If you want more brightness without extra sodium, finish with extra lemon at the table.


      Roast broccoli until edges brown—texture and flavor improve without needing extra oil.

      • Susan: 486 kcal; 42 g protein; 38 g carbs; 18 g fat; 7 g fiber; 160 mg sodium
