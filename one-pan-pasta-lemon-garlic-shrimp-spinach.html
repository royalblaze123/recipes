One-Pan Whole Wheat Pasta with Lemon-Garlic Shrimp and Spinach

Prep: 18 min   Cook: 22 min   Total: 40 min

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lb shrimp
  • 8 oz whole wheat pasta
  • 1 onion
  • 1 bag spinach
  • 0.5 carton low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 lemon
  • 2 tbsp grated parmesan cheese
  • olive oil
  • garlic
  • black pepper
  • salt substitute
  • red pepper flakes

Hard rules enforced: Dinner validations (calories 600–650 Frank, sat fat ≤8g, sodium ≤700mg, fiber ≥10g, added sugar ≤6g). Quantities appear before items; fresh meat/poultry/fish use weights. Condiments/spices/seasonings have no measurements in Ingredients; all measurements are in Steps. No olives, no squash, no Brussels sprouts, no zucchini, no tofu/tempeh, no yogurt in dinners.

Step-by-step Instructions

  1. In a deep skillet, sauté sliced onion with 2 tsp olive oil 3 minutes.
  2. Add pasta + 1 cup broth (from carton) + 2 cups water. Cover and simmer 10–12 minutes, stirring twice, until nearly al dente.
  3. Shrimp: pat dry and season with black pepper and salt substitute.
  4. Stir shrimp into the pasta and simmer 3–4 minutes until pink and curled.
  5. Stir in spinach to wilt. Finish with 2 tsp lemon juice, minced garlic, and red pepper flakes (measured in Steps).
  6. Sprinkle 2 tbsp parmesan over the pan.
  7. Portion: Frank largest share; Susan/Elise ~75%.
