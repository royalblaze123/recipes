Full-Flavor Seafood Boil (Cajun-Style)

RBM — Full-Flavor / Occasional

Ingredients

  • 2 lb baby potatoes, halved
  • 3 ears fresh corn on the cob, cut into thirds
  • 1 package (14 oz) smoked sausage or andouille, sliced
  • 1.5 lb frozen snow crab legs, thawed
  • 1 lb raw medium shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 12 oz raw sea scallops
  • 1 lb fresh clams, scrubbed
  • 1 lb fresh mussels, debearded
  • 6 cups water
  • 12 oz beer (1 bottle) OR 12 oz chicken broth (choose one and use exactly 12 oz)
  • 1 fresh yellow onion, quartered
  • 6 fresh garlic cloves, smashed
  • 1 fresh lemon, halved
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 6 tsp Cajun seasoning
  • 4.5 tsp Old Bay seasoning
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 0.5 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 0.5 tsp dried thyme
  • 0.5 tsp dried oregano
  • 24 tsp unsalted butter, divided
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 6 tsp lemon juice
  • 0.25 bunch fresh parsley, chopped

Steps (Explicit)

  1. Prep shellfish (separate, before the pot): Scrub the fresh clams under cold running water. Remove beards from the fresh mussels. Discard any clams or mussels with cracked shells. Keep clams and mussels cold until cooking.
  2. Start the boil base (together): In a very large pot add 6 cups water, 12 oz beer (or 12 oz chicken broth), 1 quartered yellow onion, 6 smashed garlic cloves, 1 halved lemon, 2 bay leaves, 6 tsp Cajun seasoning, 4.5 tsp Old Bay seasoning, 2 tsp smoked paprika, 1 tsp black pepper, 1 tsp salt, 0.5 tsp cayenne, 0.5 tsp dried thyme, and 0.5 tsp dried oregano. Bring to a rolling boil.
  3. Cook potatoes (same pot): Add 2 lb baby potatoes. Boil uncovered 12 minutes.
  4. Cook corn and sausage (same pot): Add 3 ears corn and 1 package (14 oz) sliced sausage. Boil 6 minutes.
  5. Steam clams and mussels (same pot, covered): Reduce to a steady, gentle boil. Add 1 lb clams and 1 lb mussels. Cover and cook 5 minutes, until most shells open. Discard any that do not open.
  6. Add crab (same pot, covered): Add 1.5 lb thawed snow crab legs. Cover and cook 4 minutes to heat through.
  7. Add scallops (same pot, covered): Add 12 oz sea scallops. Cover and cook 3 minutes.
  8. Add shrimp last (same pot, covered): Add 1 lb shrimp. Cover and cook 2.5–3 minutes, just until shrimp are pink and opaque.
  9. Drain (together): Pour the pot into a colander to drain completely. Discard bay leaves. If you want a cleaner final toss, discard the lemon halves and onion quarters; otherwise leave them in for aroma.
  10. Make Cajun butter (separate): In the empty pot (off heat), melt 24 tsp unsalted butter. Stir in 2 tsp garlic powder, 6 tsp lemon juice, and 0.25 bunch chopped fresh parsley.
  11. Toss and serve (together): Return drained potatoes, corn, sausage, clams, mussels, crab, scallops, and shrimp to the pot (off heat). Toss gently until coated. Serve immediately.

Notes

Portions: Frank (1.0×): 1/3 of the finished boil. Susan (0.75×): 1/4 of the finished boil. Elise (0.75×): 1/4 of the finished boil. Tips: This is labeled “Full-Flavor / Occasional” because Cajun seasoning + sausage + Old Bay typically push sodium higher than a health-goal version. Discard any clams or mussels that do not open after cooking. For best texture, keep the pot at a gentle boil once shellfish go in and follow the timing—shrimp and scallops overcook quickly.
