Light Chicken & Broccoli Alfredo-Style Lasagna

A lighter Alfredo-style lasagna using Greek yogurt, part-skim cheese, chicken, and broccoli to keep things creamy but heart- and A1c-friendly.

PORTION SIZES (3 servings total): Frank = 1.0× (largest square), Susan = 0.75×, Elise = 0.75×. Cut the baked lasagna into 4 equal squares, serve 2 squares to Frank and 1 square each to Susan and Elise.

Ingredients

  • 8 whole-wheat lasagna noodles (dry)
  • 12 oz cooked shredded chicken breast (skinless)
  • 2 cups small broccoli florets
  • 1 small yellow onion, finely diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 1/2 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 cup plain 2% Greek yogurt
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp dried thyme
  • 1/2 tsp dried basil
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp grated nutmeg
  • 1/4 tsp kosher salt (optional, to taste)
  • 1 cup part-skim shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated reduced-fat Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped

Step-by-step Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Lightly coat a 9×9-inch baking dish with nonstick spray.
  2. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Cook the whole-wheat lasagna noodles until just shy of al dente, about 7–8 minutes. Drain and lay flat on parchment to prevent sticking.
  3. Steam the broccoli florets for 2–3 minutes until bright green and just tender. Drain well and set aside.
  4. Heat the olive oil in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Add the diced onion and sauté 4–5 minutes until softened.
  5. Add the minced garlic and cook 30–60 seconds, stirring constantly, until fragrant.
  6. In a small bowl, whisk the cornstarch with 1/4 cup of the chicken broth until smooth. Pour the remaining chicken broth into the saucepan and bring to a gentle simmer.
  7. Slowly whisk in the cornstarch mixture. Cook 2–3 minutes, whisking constantly, until the sauce begins to thicken.
  8. Reduce the heat to low. Whisk in the Greek yogurt, dried thyme, dried basil, black pepper, grated nutmeg, and optional salt. Warm gently without boiling until smooth.
  9. Fold the shredded chicken and steamed broccoli into the yogurt sauce and remove the pan from the heat.
  10. Spread a thin layer of the chicken and broccoli sauce on the bottom of the prepared baking dish.
  11. Layer lasagna noodles over the sauce, followed by more chicken and broccoli sauce, then a light sprinkle of shredded mozzarella.
  12. Repeat the layers (noodles, sauce, mozzarella) until all components are used, finishing with sauce and cheese on top.
  13. Sprinkle the grated Parmesan over the final layer.
  14. Cover with foil and bake for 20 minutes. Remove the foil and bake an additional 10–15 minutes until bubbling and lightly golden.
  15. Rest the lasagna for 10–15 minutes, then sprinkle with chopped fresh parsley before serving.
  16. Cut into 4 equal squares. Serve 2 squares to Frank and 1 square each to Susan and Elise.

Nutrition (per person)

Frank (largest portion): approx 515 kcal, 48 g protein, 36 g carbs, 18 g fat.
Susan (medium portion): approx 385 kcal, 36 g protein, 27 g carbs, 14 g fat.
Elise (medium portion): approx 385 kcal, 36 g protein, 27 g carbs, 14 g fat.
