Tuna Red Enchilada Bake (No-Roll)

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 620 kcal • P 60g • C 46g • F 18g
Susan/Elise (each): 465 kcal • P 45g • C 34g • F 14g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 3 cans tuna
  • 1 package corn tortillas
  • 1 bag reduced-fat shredded Mexican cheese blend
  • 1 each avocado
  • 1/2 jar salsa
  • 1 can enchilada sauce
  • 1 bag baby spinach
  • 4 tsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp kosher salt

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F.
  2. Tuna: can size note—use three 5 oz cans tuna. Drain very well.
  3. Spinach: add 4 cups spinach to a skillet with 4 tsp olive oil and cook 60–90 seconds until just wilted.
  4. Season tuna with garlic powder, black pepper, and kosher salt, then fold into the spinach.
  5. Sauce: can size note—use a standard 10 oz can red enchilada sauce. Use all sauce for layering.
  6. Assemble: spread 1/2 cup sauce in the bottom of a 9x13 dish. Layer 1/2 tortillas, then 1/2 tuna-spinach filling, then 1/3 cheese. Repeat. Top with remaining sauce and last 1/3 cheese.
  7. Bake 18–22 minutes until bubbling. Rest 10 minutes before slicing.
  8. Toppings: dice avocado and serve salsa on the side. Top each serving.

Notes

Fast pantry-style enchilada bake (tuna + spinach) that still tastes like a real comfort dinner. Portions (per person): Frank: 1/3 of the casserole + 1/3 of the avocado + salsa topping Susan: 1/4 of the casserole + 1/4 of the avocado + salsa topping Elise: 1/4 of the casserole + 1/4 of the avocado + salsa topping
