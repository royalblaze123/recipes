Greek Chicken Salad + Chickpeas (No Olives)

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 640 kcal • P 58g • C 45g • F 20g • Sat 6g • Na 650mg • Fiber 12g
Susan/Elise (each): 480 kcal • P 44g • C 34g • F 15g • Sat 4g • Na 488mg • Fiber 9g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.75 lb boneless skinless chicken breast
  • 1 bag baby spinach
  • 1 each cucumber
  • 2 each tomatoes
  • 1 can chickpeas
  • 6 oz reduced-fat feta cheese
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • garlic powder
  • onion powder
  • smoked paprika
  • Italian seasoning
  • ground cumin
  • chili powder
  • red pepper flakes
  • extra-virgin olive oil
  • balsamic vinegar
  • apple cider vinegar
  • Dijon mustard

Steps

  1. Chicken: season with garlic powder, Italian seasoning, black pepper, modest kosher salt. Roast at 425°F 12–15 minutes to 165°F; rest; slice.
  2. Chickpeas: drain/rinse chickpeas (15 oz can). Warm 1 1/2 cups chickpeas in a skillet with black pepper and a splash of balsamic vinegar 2 minutes.
  3. Dressing: whisk 2 tbsp balsamic vinegar, 2 tbsp lemon juice (or apple cider vinegar if no lemon), 2 tbsp olive oil, Dijon mustard, black pepper, and a small pinch of kosher salt.
  4. Salad: toss spinach with chopped cucumber and tomatoes, then dressing.
  5. Top with chicken, chickpeas, and measured feta.
  6. Serve per Notes.

Notes

Greek salad dinner (no olives) with chickpeas for fiber and feta measured for flavor. Portions (per person): Frank: 4 cups salad + 7 oz chicken + 1/2 cup chickpeas + 1 oz feta Susan: 3 cups salad + 5 oz chicken + 1/3 cup chickpeas + 3/4 oz feta Elise: 3 cups salad + 5 oz chicken + 1/3 cup chickpeas + 3/4 oz feta Tips (recipe-specific): Warm chickpeas briefly so the salad doesn’t feel ‘cold diet food.’ Use feta as a topper—don’t mix it in so you keep control.
