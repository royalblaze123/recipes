Garlic-Herb Beef Meatballs with Quinoa & Spinach

NOTES: Oven-baked garlic-herb beef meatballs served over fluffy quinoa with sautéed spinach. This is a full-plate beef dinner built around lean beef, smart carbs, and plenty of non-starchy vegetables. Uses olive oil or avocado oil only, keeps sodium moderate, and avoids added sugars to support A1c, cholesterol, blood pressure, and PPPD-friendly eating. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

PORTION SIZES (per person): Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 1 1/4 pounds 93% lean ground beef
  • 1/3 cup rolled oats or whole-wheat breadcrumbs
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp dried Italian seasoning
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tbsp olive oil, divided
  • 3/4 cup uncooked quinoa, rinsed
  • 1 1/2 cups low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth
  • 6 cups fresh baby spinach
  • Juice of 1/2 lemon (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the ground beef, oats or breadcrumbs, egg, minced garlic, Italian seasoning, onion powder, salt, and pepper. Mix gently with clean hands until just combined.
  3. Form the mixture into 18–20 small meatballs and place them on the prepared baking sheet.
  4. Bake the meatballs for 15–18 minutes, turning once halfway through, until browned and cooked through.
  5. While meatballs bake, combine the rinsed quinoa and broth in a small saucepan. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer 15 minutes or until liquid is absorbed. Remove from heat and let stand 5 minutes before fluffing with a fork.
  6. In a large skillet, heat 1 tbsp olive oil over medium heat. Add the spinach and sauté 2–3 minutes until just wilted. Season with a light pinch of salt and a squeeze of lemon if desired.
  7. To assemble, portion quinoa onto plates, top with sautéed spinach, then arrange meatballs over the top.
  8. Taste and adjust seasoning with a pinch of salt or extra herbs if needed, keeping overall sodium moderate.
  9. Divide the finished meal into three plates or containers: give Frank roughly 1/3 of the total volume, and split the remaining 2/3 between Susan and Elise as slightly smaller but still generous portions.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~520 kcal, ~48 g protein, ~32 g carbs, ~18 g fat.
  • Susan: ~390 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
  • Elise: ~390 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
