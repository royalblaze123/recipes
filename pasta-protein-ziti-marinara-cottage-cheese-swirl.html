Protein+ Ziti Marinara Cottage-Cheese Swirl

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 729 kcal • P 84g • C 76g • F 14g
Susan/Elise (each): 547 kcal • P 63g • C 57g • F 10g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1/3 box Barilla Protein+ ziti
  • 1.25 lb boneless skinless chicken breast
  • 0.62 jar no-sugar-added marinara sauce
  • 1 tub 1% cottage cheese
  • 2 tbsp grated parmesan
  • 1 each yellow onion
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 tsp Italian seasoning
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp kosher salt

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil for the Barilla Protein+ ziti. (Use enough water to fully cover pasta; do not add water to Ingredients.)
  2. Pat chicken breast dry. Season with 2 tsp garlic powder, 2 tsp Italian seasoning, 1 tsp black pepper, and 1 tsp kosher salt. Sear in a nonstick skillet over medium-high 4–5 minutes per side. Reduce to medium, cover, and cook 3–5 minutes more until 165°F. Rest 5 minutes, then chop.
  3. Dice the yellow onion. Add to the skillet and cook 4–6 minutes until softened.
  4. Jar size note: assume a standard 24 oz jar marinara. Measure out what you need for this recipe; refrigerate the remainder.
  5. Add marinara to the skillet and simmer 5–7 minutes until hot and slightly thickened.
  6. If using cottage-cheese sauce: blend cottage cheese with 2–4 tbsp hot pasta water until smooth; stir into the skillet over low heat until just warmed (do not boil).
  7. Cook Barilla Protein+ ziti to al dente per box time. Reserve 1 cup pasta water, then drain.
  8. Toss pasta into the sauce/skillet. Add reserved pasta water 1–2 tbsp at a time until the sauce clings well.
  9. Portion into bowls using the Portions guidance in Notes.

Notes

Creamy marinara (no cream) using blended cottage cheese. Built on Barilla Protein+ for a higher-protein pasta plate with close-approx nutrition. Portions (per person): Frank: 2 oz dry Barilla Protein+ ziti + 1/3 of the skillet/sauce Susan: 1.5 oz dry Barilla Protein+ ziti + 1/4 of the skillet/sauce Elise: 1.5 oz dry Barilla Protein+ ziti + 1/4 of the skillet/sauce
