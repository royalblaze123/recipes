Carrot Ginger Chicken Soup (Stovetop)

NOTES: Designed as a complete meal soup using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled starch. Use low-sodium broth and avoid extra salt when possible to support blood pressure and kidney health. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~2 cups); Susan: medium portion (~1.5 cups); Elise: medium portion (~1.5 cups).

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 4 large carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1.5 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 4 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 cup water, as needed
  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breast
  • 1/2 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1/2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Juice of 1/2 lemon
  • Chopped fresh cilantro or parsley, for garnish (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat.
  2. Add diced onion and cook 3–4 minutes until softened.
  3. Add sliced carrots and cook 4–5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Stir in minced garlic and grated ginger and cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Add turmeric, cumin, salt, and black pepper and stir to coat the vegetables.
  6. Pour in low-sodium chicken broth and enough water to fully cover the carrots.
  7. Place chicken breasts into the pot, submerging them in the liquid.
  8. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low, cover partially, and simmer 20–25 minutes until carrots are very tender and chicken is cooked through.
  9. Remove chicken to a cutting board and let cool slightly.
  10. Use an immersion blender to carefully blend the carrot mixture in the pot until smooth (or blend in batches in a countertop blender and return to pot).
  11. Shred chicken with two forks into bite-sized pieces.
  12. Return shredded chicken to the pot and stir into the carrot-ginger base.
  13. Stir in lemon juice and taste, adjusting salt and pepper as needed.
  14. Simmer 5 more minutes on low, then remove from heat.
  15. Ladle into bowls for Frank, Susan, and Elise and garnish with cilantro or parsley if desired.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~410 kcal, ~45 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~10 g fat.
  • Susan: ~310 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~20 g carbs, ~7 g fat.
  • Elise: ~310 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~20 g carbs, ~7 g fat.
