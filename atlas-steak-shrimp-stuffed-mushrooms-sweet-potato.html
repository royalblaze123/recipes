Steak with Creamy Shrimp-Stuffed Mushrooms + Roasted Sweet Potato & Green Beans (Dinner)
Dinner ATLAS ONE-OFF Creamy (no breadcrumbs) Weeknight ~55 min
ATLAS • ONE-OFF Steakhouse-style dinner plate with creamy, lemon-garlic shrimp-stuffed mushrooms and a balanced sweet potato + green bean side. Portions (per person): Frank: 1.0× (about 3.75 oz cooked steak, 4 stuffed mushrooms, 7 oz roasted sweet potato, 4.25 oz roasted green beans) Susan: 0.75× (about 2.8 oz cooked steak, 3 stuffed mushrooms, 5.25 oz roasted sweet potato, 3.25 oz roasted green beans) Elise: 0.75× (about 2.8 oz cooked steak, 3 stuffed mushrooms, 5.25 oz roasted sweet potato, 3.25 oz roasted green beans) Tips (recipe-specific): Cook the mushroom filling dry: sauté stems until most moisture is gone so the filling stays creamy (not watery). Drain and press the shrimp dry after chopping so the mushrooms brown instead of steaming. For crisp-tender mushrooms, pre-roast caps briefly and pour off the liquid before stuffing.

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 0.8 lb top sirloin steak
  • 0.6 lb raw shrimp
  • 16 oz cremini mushrooms
  • 2 sweet potatoes
  • 1 lb fresh green beans
  • 4 oz reduced-fat cream cheese
  • 1 lemon
  • 1 bunch fresh parsley
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 2 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 2 tsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 0.125 tsp kosher salt

Steps

  1. HEAT + PREP: Heat oven to 425°F (convection if available). Line a sheet pan with foil and lightly mist with cooking spray (optional).
  2. MUSHROOMS: Wipe 16 oz cremini mushrooms clean. Pop stems out of the caps. Finely chop the stems (you’ll use them in the filling). Arrange caps hollow-side up on the sheet pan.
  3. PRE-ROAST CAPS: Bake the empty mushroom caps at 425°F for 6 minutes. Remove pan and carefully pour off any liquid in the caps. Leave oven on.
  4. SHRIMP: Pat 0.6 lb raw shrimp dry. Chop the shrimp into small pieces (pea-size). Pat dry again with a paper towel so the filling browns instead of steaming.
  5. FILLING BASE (DRY IT OUT): In a nonstick skillet over medium heat, add 1 tsp extra-virgin olive oil. Add the chopped mushroom stems and cook 4–5 minutes until most moisture evaporates. Add 4 cloves garlic (minced) and cook 30 seconds.
  6. MAKE IT CREAMY: Reduce heat to low. Add 2 oz reduced-fat cream cheese (measure from the 4 oz package) and stir until melted. Stir in 2 tsp Dijon mustard, 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1 tsp onion powder, and 0.5 tsp black pepper.
  7. ADD SHRIMP: Stir in the chopped shrimp. Cook 2–3 minutes, stirring, just until shrimp turns opaque (do not overcook). Turn off heat. Stir in 1 tbsp chopped fresh parsley and 1 tbsp lemon juice (from 1 lemon).
  8. STUFF + BAKE: Spoon filling into the pre-roasted mushroom caps (aim for 4 caps for Frank and 3 caps each for Susan and Elise). Bake at 425°F for 8–10 minutes, until filling is hot and the tops lightly brown.
  9. SWEET POTATO: While mushrooms bake, scrub 2 sweet potatoes. Cut into 1-inch cubes. On a second sheet pan (or the cleared space on the first pan), toss the sweet potato cubes with 1 tsp extra-virgin olive oil and 0.5 tsp black pepper. Roast at 425°F for 20–25 minutes, flipping once, until browned and tender.
  10. GREEN BEANS: Trim 1 lb fresh green beans. Add to the sweet potato pan for the last 12 minutes of roasting. Toss beans with 0.125 tsp kosher salt and roast until crisp-tender and lightly blistered.
  11. STEAK: While vegetables roast, heat a nonstick or cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat. Pat 0.8 lb top sirloin steak dry. Season with 0.5 tsp black pepper. Sear 3–4 minutes per side (time varies by thickness) to 130–135°F for medium-rare or 140–145°F for medium. Rest 5 minutes, then slice.
  12. PLATE (PORTIONED): Frank gets ~3.75 oz cooked steak, 4 stuffed mushrooms, ~7 oz roasted sweet potato, and ~4.25 oz roasted green beans. Susan and Elise each get ~2.8 oz cooked steak, 3 stuffed mushrooms, ~5.25 oz sweet potato, and ~3.25 oz green beans. Finish each plate with 1 tsp lemon juice (optional) and a sprinkle of chopped parsley (optional).
  13. STORAGE + REHEAT: Cool leftovers within 2 hours. Store mushrooms, steak, and vegetables in airtight containers (fridge up to 3–4 days). Reheat mushrooms and vegetables in the air fryer at 350°F for 4–6 minutes (best texture) or microwave 60–90 seconds. Reheat steak gently (microwave 20–30 seconds or warm in a skillet 1–2 minutes) to avoid overcooking.
