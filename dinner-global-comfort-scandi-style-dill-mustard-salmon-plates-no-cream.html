Scandi-Style Dill Mustard Salmon Plates (No Cream)

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 640 kcal • P 55g • C 55g • F 18g • Sat 5g • Na 650mg • Fiber 10g
Susan/Elise (each): 480 kcal • P 41g • C 41g • F 14g • Sat 4g • Na 488mg • Fiber 8g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.25 lb salmon fillets
  • 1 each lemon
  • 1 jar Dijon mustard
  • fresh dill
  • 1.50 lb baby potatoes
  • 1 bag frozen green beans
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • garlic powder
  • onion powder
  • smoked paprika
  • Italian seasoning
  • ground cumin
  • chili powder
  • red pepper flakes
  • extra-virgin olive oil
  • low-sodium chicken broth

Steps

  1. Oven: heat to 425°F.
  2. Potatoes: roast halved potatoes with 2 tsp olive oil, black pepper, garlic powder, and modest kosher salt 22–26 minutes.
  3. Green beans: roast or microwave and drain; toss with black pepper and lemon.
  4. Salmon: spread 2 tbsp Dijon mustard total across salmon (measured). Sprinkle dill, black pepper, and a squeeze of lemon.
  5. Bake salmon 8–10 minutes until flaky.
  6. Serve per Notes.

Notes

Salmon comfort with dill-mustard flavor (no creamy dill sauce). Portions (per person): Frank: 6 oz salmon + 1 cup potatoes + 1 cup green beans Susan: 5 oz protein + 1 cup potatoes + 3/4 cup veg Elise: 5 oz protein + 1 cup potatoes + 3/4 cup veg Tips (recipe-specific): Dijon + dill gives ‘sauce’ flavor with almost no calories. Don’t overbake salmon—pull when it flakes easily.
