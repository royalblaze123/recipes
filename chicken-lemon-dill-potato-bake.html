Lemon Dill Chicken & Baby Potato Bake

NOTES: Full chicken dinner with lean protein, smart carbs, and plenty of non-starchy vegetables. Seasonings are kept moderate-sodium and PPPD-friendly (no heavy spice, no vinegar overload). Use olive oil only and avoid butter or cream.

PORTION SIZES (serves 3 total): Frank gets the largest portion (≈1/3 of the total meal); Susan and Elise each get slightly smaller portions (≈1/4 of the total meal each). Frank = 1.0×; Susan = 0.75×; Elise = 0.75× portions.

Ingredients

  • 12 oz boneless, skinless chicken breast, cut into bite-size pieces
  • 2 cups mixed non-starchy vegetables (broccoli, peppers, onions, green beans, etc.), chopped
  • 1 1/2 cups cooked whole-grain starch (brown rice, quinoa, whole-wheat pasta, or diced potatoes)
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp dried Italian or mixed herb seasoning
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika or chili powder (optional, to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or less; adjust to needs)
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • Fresh lemon wedges or herbs for serving (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (or heat a large non-stick skillet/Instant Pot sauté mode, depending on recipe style).
  2. If roasting: line a large sheet pan with parchment for easy cleanup. If using skillet or Instant Pot, lightly coat the bottom with cooking spray.
  3. In a large bowl, toss chicken pieces with olive oil, garlic powder, onion powder, herbs, smoked paprika or chili (if using), salt, and pepper until evenly coated.
  4. Add chopped vegetables to the bowl and toss again so everything is lightly coated and seasoned.
  5. Spread chicken and vegetables in an even layer on the sheet pan (or add to skillet/Instant Pot). Avoid overcrowding.
  6. Roast at 400°F for 20–25 minutes, stirring once halfway through, until chicken reaches 165°F and vegetables are tender with lightly browned edges. (If using skillet/Instant Pot, sauté until chicken is cooked through and vegetables are crisp-tender, adding a splash of low-sodium broth as needed.)
  7. Warm the cooked whole-grain starch (brown rice, quinoa, whole-wheat pasta, or potatoes).
  8. To serve, divide starch among three plates, top with roasted/sautéed chicken and vegetables, and finish with fresh lemon and herbs if desired.
  9. Portion for Frank, Susan, and Elise per guidelines below.

Nutrition (approximate per person)

Frank (largest portion): ~520 kcal, 50 g protein, 45 g carbs, 15 g fat
Susan (medium portion): ~390 kcal, 37 g protein, 34 g carbs, 11 g fat
Elise (medium portion): ~390 kcal, 37 g protein, 34 g carbs, 11 g fat
