Turkey Melt Plates + Tomato Soup (Light)

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 645 kcal • P 55g • C 70g • F 16g • Sat 6g • Na 700mg • Fiber 10g
Susan/Elise (each): 484 kcal • P 41g • C 52g • F 12g • Sat 4g • Na 525mg • Fiber 8g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 12 slices whole-wheat sandwich bread
  • 18 slices deli turkey
  • 6 slices reduced-fat cheese slices
  • 1 jar marinara sauce
  • 1 carton low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 bag baby spinach
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • garlic powder
  • onion powder
  • Italian seasoning
  • smoked paprika
  • extra-virgin olive oil
  • Dijon mustard
  • hot sauce
  • rice vinegar

Steps

  1. Soup: in a pot, combine 2 cups marinara + 2 cups low-sodium chicken broth. Simmer 8 minutes. Season with black pepper and a pinch of garlic powder.
  2. Sandwiches: build 3 sandwiches (1 per person). Each sandwich: 4 slices deli turkey + 2 slices cheese total across the 3 sandwiches (use 1 slice on Frank’s, 1 slice split between the other 2). Add spinach.
  3. Cook: toast sandwiches in a nonstick skillet over medium heat 3–4 minutes per side until cheese melts. Use a light mist of oil if needed.
  4. Serve: bowl of soup + sandwich per Notes.

Notes

Soup + melt comfort, but the soup is broth-boosted and the cheese is measured. Portions (per person): Frank: 1 sandwich + 1 1/2 cups soup side Susan: 1 sandwich + 1 1/4 cups soup side Elise: 1 sandwich + 1 1/4 cups soup side Tips (recipe-specific): Boost soup volume with broth so you can keep marinara measured. Use spinach inside the melt for extra volume and fiber.
