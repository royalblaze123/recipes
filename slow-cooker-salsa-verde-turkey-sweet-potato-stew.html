Slow Cooker Salsa Verde Turkey Sweet Potato Stew

Salsa verde turkey stew with sweet potato and white beans—chefy, mild, and engineered as a full dinner bowl (no side).

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 18 minutes
  • Cook time: 420 minutes
  • Total time: 438 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 2 lb turkey breast
  • 1 carton low sodium chicken broth
  • 1 jar salsa verde
  • 2 each sweet potatoes
  • 1 each yellow onion
  • 1 bunch cilantro
  • 6 cloves garlic
  • 1 can cannellini beans
  • 1 bottle extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 jar ground cumin
  • 1 jar black pepper
  • 1 each lime
  • 1 jar cornstarch
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Prep turkey: cut turkey breast into 1-inch chunks.
    2. Prep veg: dice 1/2 the onion and mince 3 garlic cloves. Peel and cube 1 sweet potato into 1-inch cubes (measure out about 3 cups; refrigerate remaining sweet potato). Chop cilantro (use about 1/4 cup; refrigerate remaining cilantro).
    3. Slow cooker base: add turkey, onion, garlic, sweet potato cubes, 1 tsp cumin, and black pepper.
    4. Liquid: add the carton of low sodium chicken broth plus 1 cup water. Add 1 cup salsa verde from the jar and stir.
    5. Cook: cook on LOW for 6–7 hours or HIGH for 3–4 hours, until turkey is tender.
    6. Beans late: drain and rinse cannellini beans. Stir beans into the stew and cook on HIGH for 15 minutes.
    7. Thicken: whisk 1 tbsp cornstarch with 2 tbsp water. Stir slurry into the stew and cook 5 minutes.
    8. Finish: squeeze in 1 tsp lime juice and stir in chopped cilantro. Serve.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – About 3 1/2 cups stew.


      Susan – About 2 2/3 cups stew.


      Elise – About 2 2/3 cups stew.


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Use measured salsa verde so sodium stays moderate.


      Keep sweet potato cubes larger so they hold their shape.


      Add beans near the end so the stew stays structured.
