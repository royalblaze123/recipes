Chophouse Lite: Top Sirloin Steak with Peppers/Onions + Broccoli + Cauliflower Rice (Dijon Worcester Baste)

Servings

3 plates (Frank 1.0×, Susan 0.75×, Elise 0.75×).

Times

  • Prep time: 18 minutes
  • Cook time: 22 minutes
  • Total time: 40 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2.0 lb lean beef steak (top sirloin), trimmed
  • 2 bags frozen cauliflower rice
  • 3 bell peppers
  • 1 bag frozen broccoli florets
  • 9 tsp olive oil
  • 2 tsp black pepper
  • 2 tsp salt substitute
  • 2 tsp dried parsley
  • 5 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 3 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 tsp black pepper

Steps

  1. Base: Sauté cauliflower rice 6–7 minutes until hot and steam-dry. Keep warm.
  2. Vegetables: Preheat oven to 425°F. Roast vegetables until browned-tender. Add parsley at the end.
  3. Steak: Pat dry aggressively. Season with black pepper + salt substitute.
  4. Sear: Heat skillet until very hot. Add 2 tsp olive oil. Sear 2–3 minutes per side until browned.
  5. Finish: Reduce heat to medium; cook to 130–135°F for medium-rare or 135–145°F for medium. Rest 5 minutes; slice across the grain.
  6. Finish: Whisk with 2–3 tbsp hot water; brush on steak during the last minute and again after slicing.
  7. Plate: Base → vegetables → sliced protein → finish drizzle. Portions per Notes.

Notes

Portions (per person):

Frank – 1 plate (8 oz cooked steak, 2 cups cauliflower rice, 2 cups vegetables, 2–3 tbsp finish baste).

Susan – 1 plate (6 oz cooked steak, 3/4 of Frank’s base portion, 1 1/2 cups vegetables, 2 tbsp finish baste).

Elise – 1 plate (6 oz cooked steak, 3/4 of Frank’s base portion, 1 1/2 cups vegetables, 2 tbsp finish baste).

Tips (recipe-specific):

Pat the steak dry. Steam is not a flavor profile.

Slice across the grain every time. Chewing should be optional.

Storage + Reheat

  • Cool promptly; store steak + vegetables + base in airtight containers in the fridge up to 3–4 days (typical safety guidance).
  • Reheat steak gently (microwave 45–60 sec or skillet 1–2 min) to avoid overcooking; reheat vegetables 4–6 minutes at 350°F in the air fryer for texture; add finishing drizzle after reheating.
