Lean Beef & Corn Protein Nachos (Sheet Pan)

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 650 kcal • P 58g • C 42g • F 23g
Susan/Elise (each): 488 kcal • P 44g • C 32g • F 17g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.50 lb 93% lean ground beef
  • 2/3 bag bean-based tortilla chips
  • 3/4 bag reduced-fat shredded Mexican cheese blend
  • 1/2 tub 2% plain Greek yogurt
  • 1/2 jar salsa
  • 1 can corn
  • 2 each bell peppers
  • 1 each yellow onion
  • 4 tsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 tsp chili powder
  • 2 tsp cumin
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp kosher salt

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a large sheet pan with parchment.
  2. Beef: cook ground beef in a nonstick skillet over medium-high 7–9 minutes until browned. Drain excess liquid if needed.
  3. Corn: can size note—use a standard 15 oz can corn. Drain well. Measure out 1 1/2 cups corn for this recipe; refrigerate any remainder.
  4. Season beef with 2 tsp chili powder, 2 tsp cumin, 2 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1 tsp black pepper, and 1 tsp kosher salt. Stir 60 seconds.
  5. Veg: slice or dice the listed peppers and onion. Toss with the listed olive oil on the sheet pan and roast 10 minutes to start browning.
  6. Add the cooked protein (and any beans/corn listed) over the roasted vegetables and spread into an even layer.
  7. Cheese: sprinkle the listed shredded cheese evenly over the hot topping mix.
  8. Bake 4–6 minutes until the cheese is melted and bubbling at the edges.
  9. Chips: while the pan bakes, portion chips by weight: Frank 1.5 oz; Susan 1.125 oz; Elise 1.125 oz. (Keep chips separate so they stay crunchy.)
  10. Greek yogurt: use 6 tbsp as a drizzle across the finished nachos.
  11. Salsa: measure 1/2 jar salsa into a bowl for topping.
  12. Serve: spoon the hot cheesy topping mix over each person’s chips. Finish with the listed cold toppings. Portion per Notes.

Notes

Classic nacho vibe with lean beef, but the base is peppers/onions and measured chips. Portions (per person): Frank: Chips: 1.5 oz + 1/3 of the hot topping mix + 1/3 of the cold toppings Susan: Chips: 1.125 oz + 1/4 of the hot topping mix + 1/4 of the cold toppings Elise: Chips: 1.125 oz + 1/4 of the hot topping mix + 1/4 of the cold toppings
