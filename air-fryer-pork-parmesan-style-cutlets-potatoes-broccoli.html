Air Fryer Pork Parmesan-Style Cutlets with Potatoes and Broccoli

Crispy pork cutlets with potatoes and broccoli, finished with a light parmesan element—no heavy sauce needed.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 25 minutes
  • Cook time: 20 minutes
  • Total time: 45 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 1 lb boneless pork chops
  • 2 each russet potatoes
  • 1 head broccoli
  • 1 bottle extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 bag reduced fat shredded cheese
  • 1 jar parmesan cheese
  • 1 jar garlic powder
  • 1 jar black pepper
  • 1 jar Italian seasoning
  • 1 jar breadcrumbs
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Prep potatoes: cut into wedges. Measure out about 3 cups wedges for this recipe; refrigerate remaining potato. Toss with 1 tbsp olive oil, garlic powder, and black pepper.
    2. Prep broccoli: cut florets. Measure out 1/2 head broccoli for this recipe; refrigerate remaining. Toss with 1 tsp olive oil and black pepper.
    3. Prep pork: pat dry. Coat each chop with 1 tsp olive oil, then press into breadcrumbs mixed with Italian seasoning and black pepper.
    4. Cosori setup: preheat both baskets to 390°F for 3 minutes. Put potatoes in Basket A.
    5. Basket A: cook potatoes 390°F for 20 minutes with shake reminder ON; shake at each reminder.
    6. Basket B: cook pork cutlets 390°F for 12–14 minutes, flipping at 7 minutes, until 145°F internal.
    7. Broccoli finish: when pork finishes, cook broccoli in Basket B at 390°F for 6–7 minutes, shaking once.
    8. Finish: sprinkle 1 tbsp parmesan total across all plates and add 1/2 oz reduced-fat shredded cheese total across all plates (optional).
    9. Serve (40/30/30): plate pork with potatoes and broccoli.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – Pork: largest cutlet. Potato wedges: about 1 1/4 cups. Broccoli: about 1 1/2 cups.


      Susan – Pork: smaller cutlet. Potato wedges: about 1 cup. Broccoli: about 1 1/4 cups.


      Elise – Pork: smaller cutlet. Potato wedges: about 1 cup. Broccoli: about 1 1/4 cups.


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Breadcrumb coating browns best with a light oil film—don’t soak it.


      Cook broccoli at the end so it stays crisp-tender, not soft.


      If the pork is thick, add 2 minutes after flipping and verify 145°F.
