Salmon Veggie Rice Bowl (Canned Salmon)

NOTES: Designed as a portable grab-and-go lunch using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled smart carbs. Use low-sodium ingredients and avoid added sugars where possible. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total volume); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total volume); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total volume) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 1 cup uncooked brown rice, rinsed
  • 2 cups low-sodium vegetable or chicken broth
  • 2 (6 oz) cans wild salmon, drained and flaked (skinless/boneless if possible)
  • 1 1/2 cups steamed broccoli florets
  • 1 cup shredded carrots
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp dried dill
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Cook brown rice: Combine rinsed rice and low-sodium broth in a saucepan. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat, cover, and simmer 25–30 minutes until tender. Fluff with a fork and cool slightly.
  2. Steam broccoli florets until crisp-tender, then cool slightly.
  3. In a large bowl, combine cooked rice, flaked canned salmon, steamed broccoli, shredded carrots, and sliced bell pepper.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, dried dill, and black pepper.
  5. Pour dressing over the bowl and toss gently until evenly coated.
  6. Divide into three containers with lids, giving Frank the largest portion, for grab-and-go lunches.
  7. Refrigerate up to 3 days. Enjoy chilled or gently rewarmed at home.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~600 kcal, ~50 g protein, ~55 g carbs, ~18 g fat.
  • Susan: ~450 kcal, ~38 g protein, ~41 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
  • Elise: ~450 kcal, ~38 g protein, ~41 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
