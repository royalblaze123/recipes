Turkey Pepperoni Veggie Pizza + Smoky Paprika Wings

Turkey pepperoni veggie pizza with smoky paprika wings for full flavor without sugary sauces.

Servings

3 servings (Frank, Susan, Elise).

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 35 minutes
  • Total time: 55 minutes

Ingredients

Pizza

  • 1 dough ball pizza dough
  • 1/2 jar no-salt-added pizza sauce
  • 3 oz shredded part-skim mozzarella
  • 2 oz turkey pepperoni
  • 1/2 each green bell pepper
  • 1/2 package mushrooms
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • 3 tsp smoked paprika
  • 32 oz chicken wings
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 3 tsp extra-virgin olive oil

Wings

  • 2 lb chicken wings
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper

Wing Finish Sauce

  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika

Preparation Steps

  1. Remove 1 dough ball from the refrigerator (or thawed vacuum-sealed dough) and keep covered at room temperature for 60–90 minutes.
  2. Preheat oven with a pizza steel or stone to 500°F for 45 minutes (or your oven’s maximum).
  3. Wings: Pat wings very dry. Toss with smoked paprika, garlic powder, cumin, and black pepper until evenly coated.
  4. Cosori dual-basket setup: Split wings between both baskets in a single layer. Use Sync Cook at 380°F for 18 minutes, shaking halfway.
  5. Crisp finish: Increase to 400°F and cook 6–8 minutes more until browned and 165°F internally.
  6. Finish: Toss hot wings with 1 tablespoon olive oil and an extra 1 teaspoon smoked paprika for a glossy, smoky finish.
  7. Pizza topping prep: Slice mushrooms. Slice green bell pepper.
  8. Stretch dough into a 12-inch round. Spread sauce. Sprinkle mozzarella. Add turkey pepperoni, mushrooms, and bell pepper. Dust with Italian seasoning and smoked paprika.
  9. Bake 7–9 minutes until browned and bubbling. Rest 2 minutes before slicing.
  10. Portion and serve using the Portions split below.

Notes

Turkey pepperoni veggie pizza with smoky paprika wings for full flavor without sugary sauces.

Portions (per person):

Frank: 4 slices (about 1/3 of the 12-inch pizza) + 8 wings (about 4 oz cooked wings)

Susan: 3 slices (about 1/4 of the 12-inch pizza) + 6 wings (about 3 oz cooked wings)

Elise: 3 slices (about 1/4 of the 12-inch pizza) + 6 wings (about 3 oz cooked wings)

Tips (recipe-specific):

Keep pepperoni measured; it is the main sodium driver in this combo.

If you want more volume without more sodium, add extra mushrooms and bell pepper.

For crispier wings, do not crowd the baskets and shake at the reminder.
