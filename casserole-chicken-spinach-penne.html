Chicken Spinach Whole-Wheat Penne Bake

NOTES: Built as a complete dinner using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled starch portions. Use low-sodium broth and minimal added salt to support blood pressure and kidney health. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of pan); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of pan); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of pan).

Ingredients

  • 8 oz dry whole-wheat penne pasta
  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breast, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups fresh baby spinach
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) no-salt-added crushed or finely diced tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup plain 2% Greek yogurt
  • 1/2 cup shredded reduced-fat mozzarella cheese
  • 1 tsp dried Italian seasoning
  • 1/2 tsp dried basil
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Olive oil spray for baking dish

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Lightly spray a 9x13-inch baking dish with olive oil spray.
  2. Cook whole-wheat penne in a large pot of lightly salted water according to package directions until just al dente. Drain well and set aside.
  3. While pasta cooks, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  4. Add diced onion and cook 4–5 minutes until softened.
  5. Stir in minced garlic and cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
  6. Add chicken pieces to the skillet, season with a small pinch of salt and pepper, and cook 6–8 minutes, stirring occasionally, until chicken is just cooked through.
  7. Add baby spinach to the skillet in batches, stirring until wilted and bright green.
  8. Stir in crushed or finely diced tomatoes, Italian seasoning, dried basil, remaining salt, and black pepper. Simmer 3–4 minutes to blend flavors.
  9. Remove skillet from heat and let mixture cool 2–3 minutes, then stir in Greek yogurt until smooth and evenly distributed.
  10. In a large mixing bowl, combine cooked penne with the chicken-spinach sauce and toss until everything is coated.
  11. Transfer mixture to the prepared baking dish and spread into an even layer.
  12. Sprinkle reduced-fat mozzarella evenly over the top.
  13. Bake uncovered 20–25 minutes until bubbling around the edges and cheese is melted and lightly golden.
  14. Let casserole rest 5–10 minutes, then slice into portions for Frank, Susan, and Elise.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~590 kcal, ~50 g protein, ~58 g carbs, ~17 g fat.
  • Susan: ~445 kcal, ~37 g protein, ~44 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
  • Elise: ~445 kcal, ~37 g protein, ~44 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
