Air Fryer Ginger Garlic Chicken with Sweet Potato and Slaw

Ginger-garlic chicken with roasted sweet potato and crisp slaw for contrast—fast, mild, and high protein.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 25 minutes
  • Cook time: 16 minutes
  • Total time: 41 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 1 lb boneless skinless chicken breast
  • 2 each sweet potatoes
  • 1 bag coleslaw mix
  • 1 knob fresh ginger
  • 6 cloves garlic
  • 1 bottle extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tbsp low sodium soy sauce
  • 1 container nonfat Greek yogurt
  • 1 each lime
  • 1 jar black pepper
  • 1 jar garlic powder
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Prep sweet potato: peel and cut into 3/4-inch cubes. Measure out about 2 cups cubes for this recipe; refrigerate remaining sweet potato. Toss with 1 tbsp olive oil, garlic powder, and black pepper.
    2. Prep chicken: slice chicken into thin strips. Toss with 2 tsp olive oil, low sodium soy sauce, grated ginger, minced garlic, and black pepper. Rest 10 minutes.
    3. Prep slaw: measure out 3 cups coleslaw mix for this dinner; refrigerate remaining slaw. Toss slaw with 1 tbsp Greek yogurt and 1 tsp lime juice.
    4. Cosori setup: preheat both baskets to 400°F for 3 minutes. Put sweet potato cubes in Basket A.
    5. Basket A: cook sweet potato cubes 400°F for 14–16 minutes with shake reminder ON; shake at each reminder.
    6. Basket B: cook chicken strips 400°F for 8–10 minutes, shaking once, until 165°F internal.
    7. Serve (40/30/30): plate chicken with sweet potato and the crisp slaw on the side.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – Chicken: largest share. Sweet potato: about 1 1/4 cups. Slaw: about 1 1/2 cups.


      Susan – Chicken: medium share. Sweet potato: about 1 cup. Slaw: about 1 1/4 cups.


      Elise – Chicken: medium share. Sweet potato: about 1 cup. Slaw: about 1 1/4 cups.


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Keep slaw cold and separate so the dinner has crunch and contrast.


      Use low sodium soy sauce to keep sodium controlled.


      If you want fewer carbs, reduce sweet potato on the plate and add more slaw.
