Southwest Black Bean & Chicken Salad (Frank Household)

Southwest-style salad with chicken, black beans, corn, and veggies over greens with a mild yogurt-lime dressing for a full lunch.

Servings

3 servings (Frank, Susan, Elise).

Times

  • Prep time: 15 minutes
  • Cook time: 10 minutes (for chicken if needed)
  • Total time: 25 minutes

Ingredients

  • 10 oz cooked skinless chicken breast, chopped (about 4 oz for Frank, 3 oz each for Susan and Elise)
  • 3/4 cup canned black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1/3 cup frozen corn, thawed and patted dry
  • 6 cups chopped romaine or green leaf lettuce
  • 3/4 cup chopped cucumber
  • 1/2 cup halved grape tomatoes
  • 1/3 cup diced red bell pepper
  • 6 tbsp 2% plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 1/2 tbsp lime juice
  • 1/2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika or sweet paprika
  • 1/4 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/4 tsp ground black pepper (or to taste)
  • 1/4 tsp kosher or sea salt (keep total modest; season to taste)

Preparation Steps

  1. Use cooked chicken breast from grilling, baking, or rotisserie (white meat). Trim any skin and visible fat. Chop into bite-size pieces and measure about 10 oz.
  2. Rinse and drain the black beans well. Thaw the frozen corn and pat dry with a paper towel so it does not water down the salad.
  3. Rinse and dry the lettuce, cucumber, tomatoes, and bell pepper. Chop the lettuce, chop the cucumber, halve the tomatoes, and dice the bell pepper.
  4. In a small bowl or jar, whisk together the Greek yogurt, olive oil, lime juice, ground cumin, smoked paprika, garlic powder, black pepper, and salt until smooth. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed, keeping spice level mild for tolerance.
  5. In a large mixing bowl, combine the chopped lettuce, cucumber, grape tomatoes, and diced red bell pepper.
  6. Add the chopped chicken, drained black beans, and thawed corn to the bowl with the vegetables.
  7. Pour most of the yogurt-lime dressing over the salad (reserve a tablespoon or so for drizzling). Toss gently until everything is evenly coated.
  8. Add about 2 cups of the salad mixture to a large bowl or container for Frank, ensuring his portion includes roughly 4 oz chicken, 1/3 cup black beans, and about 2 tbsp corn. Add a teaspoon or two of any reserved dressing if desired.
  9. Divide the remaining salad between two containers for Susan and Elise, about 1 1/2 cups each, making sure each has about 3 oz chicken, 1/4 cup black beans, and about 1 1/2 tbsp corn. Drizzle with remaining dressing.
  10. Serve immediately or refrigerate tightly covered for up to 24 hours. Keep chilled for work or school lunches.
