Lunch Sandwich Buffalo Chicken Yogurt Crunch

Buffalo-style chicken sandwiches with a yogurt hot-sauce spread and crunchy veg packed inside (no side needed).

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 12 minutes
  • Cook time: 0 minutes
  • Total time: 12 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 6 slices whole wheat bread
  • 18 slices deli chicken breast
  • 3 slices provolone cheese
  • 1 container nonfat Greek yogurt
  • 2 tbsp hot sauce
  • 1 lb bag carrots
  • 1 bunch celery
  • 1 bag spring mix
  • 1 jar black pepper
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Spread: mix 4 tbsp Greek yogurt with 2 tbsp hot sauce and black pepper. Measure out 3 tbsp spread for this recipe; refrigerate the rest.
    2. Veg: shred 1 carrot into ribbons (use about 1 cup ribbons). Slice 2 celery stalks thin. Measure out 6 packed cups spring mix for these sandwiches; refrigerate remaining spring mix.
    3. Build (Frank): 2 slices bread + 8 slices deli chicken + 1 slice provolone + ~2 cups spring mix + 1/3 of the carrot ribbons + 1/3 of the celery slices + ~1 tbsp buffalo-yogurt spread.
    4. Build (Susan): 2 slices bread + 5 slices deli chicken + 1 slice provolone + ~2 cups spring mix + 1/3 of the carrot ribbons + 1/3 of the celery slices + ~1 tbsp buffalo-yogurt spread.
    5. Build (Elise): 2 slices bread + 5 slices deli chicken + 1 slice provolone + ~2 cups spring mix + 1/3 of the carrot ribbons + 1/3 of the celery slices + ~1 tbsp buffalo-yogurt spread.
    6. Close sandwiches, press gently, and wrap.
      7. 

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – 1 whole sandwich: 8 slices deli chicken, 1 slice provolone, ~2 cups spring mix, carrot + celery, ~1 tbsp buffalo-yogurt spread.


      Susan – 1 whole sandwich: 5 slices deli chicken, 1 slice provolone, ~2 cups spring mix, carrot + celery, ~1 tbsp buffalo-yogurt spread.


      Elise – 1 whole sandwich: 5 slices deli chicken, 1 slice provolone, ~2 cups spring mix, carrot + celery, ~1 tbsp buffalo-yogurt spread.


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Choose a lower-sodium hot sauce when possible to keep the sandwich sodium moderate.


      Carrot ribbons add bulk and crunch without extra calories—use them to keep the sandwich satisfying.


      If you want less heat, reduce hot sauce and add extra black pepper instead.
