Lemon-Dill Salmon with Quinoa & Asparagus (RBM Dinner)

Oven-roasted salmon served with fluffy quinoa and roasted asparagus for a heart-healthy, omega-3-rich dinner.

Servings

3 servings (Frank, Susan, Elise).

Times

  • Prep time: 15m
  • Cook time: 20m
  • Total time: 35m

Ingredients

  • 18 oz salmon fillet (skin on or off), cut into 3 even pieces
  • 1/2 cup dry quinoa, rinsed
  • 1 cup low-sodium chicken broth or water
  • 12 oz fresh asparagus, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 6 oz fresh cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 1/2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 medium lemon, zested and juiced
  • 1 tsp dried dill (or 1 tbsp fresh chopped dill)
  • 1/4 tsp kosher or sea salt (or less, to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 small garlic clove, minced

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (205°C). Line a sheet pan with parchment paper.
  2. In a small saucepan, combine the rinsed quinoa and low-sodium chicken broth (or water). Bring to a gentle boil over medium-high heat, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 12–15 minutes until the liquid is absorbed and the quinoa is tender. Turn off heat, fluff with a fork, and keep covered.
  3. While the quinoa cooks, place the salmon pieces, asparagus, and cherry tomatoes on the prepared sheet pan.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon zest, lemon juice, dried (or fresh) dill, minced garlic, salt, and pepper.
  5. Brush or spoon about half of the lemon-dill mixture over the salmon pieces and drizzle the remaining mixture over the asparagus and tomatoes. Toss the vegetables lightly so they are coated.
  6. Roast for 12–15 minutes, depending on thickness of the salmon, until the salmon flakes easily with a fork and reaches 145°F (63°C) in the center, and the asparagus is crisp-tender.
  7. Turn off the oven. Let the salmon rest on the pan for a couple of minutes while you uncover and fluff the quinoa again.
  8. Divide the salmon, quinoa, asparagus, and tomatoes into portions as listed below and serve immediately.

Portion Sizes (Cooked Approx.)

  • Frank (F · 1.0×): ~8 oz cooked salmon, ~1/3 cup cooked quinoa, ~1 1/2 cups asparagus and tomatoes
  • Susan (S · 0.75×): ~6 oz cooked salmon, ~1/4 cup cooked quinoa, ~1 cup asparagus and tomatoes
  • Elise (E · 0.5×): ~4 oz cooked salmon, ~3 tbsp cooked quinoa, ~3/4 cup asparagus and tomatoes

Nutrition (Per Person)

  • Frank: 640 kcal, 55 g protein, 25 g carbs, 30 g fat, 5 g fiber, 520 mg sodium
  • Susan: 480 kcal, 41 g protein, 19 g carbs, 22 g fat, 4 g fiber, 390 mg sodium
  • Elise: 320 kcal, 28 g protein, 12 g carbs, 15 g fat, 2.5 g fiber, 260 mg sodium
