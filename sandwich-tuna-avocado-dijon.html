Tuna Avocado Dijon Sandwich

Servings

3 sandwiches (Frank 1.0×, Susan 0.75×, Elise 0.75×).

Times

  • Prep time: 15 minutes
  • Cook time: 0 minutes
  • Total time: 15 minutes

Ingredients

  • 6 slices whole wheat bread
  • 2 cans tuna
  • 11 tsp light mayonnaise
  • 6 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 avocado
  • 3 slices reduced-fat provolone cheese
  • 1/5 bunch celery
  • 1 lemon

Recipe

  1. Open 2 cans tuna and drain very well. In a bowl, flake the tuna.
  2. From 1/5 bunch celery, finely chop 2 large stalks (about 1 cup). Store the remaining celery bunch.
  3. Add to the bowl: chopped celery + 11 tsp light mayonnaise + 6 tsp Dijon mustard + juice of 1 lemon; mix until creamy.
  4. Slice 1 avocado. Portion avocado slices: Frank 1/2 avocado; Susan 1/4 avocado; Elise 1/4 avocado.
  5. Frank sandwich: use 2 bread slices; add 1/2 of the tuna salad mixture (about 1 heaping cup) + 1 slice provolone + 1/2 avocado slices; close and wrap.
  6. Susan sandwich: use 2 bread slices; add 1/4 of the tuna salad mixture + 1 slice provolone + 1/4 avocado slices; close and wrap.
  7. Elise sandwich: use 2 bread slices; add 1/4 of the tuna salad mixture + 1 slice provolone + 1/4 avocado slices; close and wrap.

Notes

Portions (per person):

Frank – 1 sandwich (about 1/2 of tuna salad, 1 provolone slice, 1/2 avocado, 4 tsp mayo).

Susan – 1 sandwich (about 1/4 of tuna salad, 1 provolone slice, 1/4 avocado, 3 1/2 tsp mayo).

Elise – 1 sandwich (about 1/4 of tuna salad, 1 provolone slice, 1/4 avocado, 3 1/2 tsp mayo).

Tips (recipe-specific):

Drain tuna aggressively (press in the strainer) so the sandwich stays firm for make-ahead lunches.

Add avocado on top of the tuna layer so the bread stays drier.
