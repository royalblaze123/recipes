Air Fryer Beef Tenderloin Medallions with Potatoes and Asparagus

Lean beef tenderloin medallions with crispy potatoes and asparagus, timed with Sync Finish for a clean weeknight plate.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 18 minutes
  • Total time: 38 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 1 lb beef tenderloin
  • 1 bag baby potatoes
  • 1 bunch asparagus
  • 1 bottle extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 each lemon
  • 1 jar Italian seasoning
  • 1 jar garlic powder
  • 1 jar black pepper
  • 1 container nonfat Greek yogurt
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Prep potatoes: halve baby potatoes. Measure out about 3 cups potatoes for this recipe; refrigerate remaining. Toss with 1 tbsp olive oil, garlic powder, and black pepper.
    2. Prep beef: slice beef tenderloin into 9 medallions. Toss with 2 tsp olive oil, Italian seasoning, garlic powder, and black pepper.
    3. Cosori setup: preheat both baskets to 390°F for 3 minutes. Put potatoes in Basket A. Put beef medallions in Basket B in a single layer.
    4. Sync Finish: set Basket A to 390°F for 18 minutes (shake reminder ON). Set Basket B to 390°F for 10 minutes. Press Sync Finish.
    5. Shake reminder (Basket A): shake/flip potatoes when prompted.
    6. Flip beef: at the 5-minute mark, flip medallions. Cook to 135–140°F for medium or 145°F for medium-well. Rest 5 minutes.
    7. Asparagus: trim asparagus. Toss with 1 tsp olive oil and black pepper. Cook in Basket B at 390°F for 6–7 minutes, shaking once.
    8. Yogurt lemon drizzle: stir 1/4 cup Greek yogurt with 1 tsp lemon juice (thin with 1 tsp water if needed).
    9. Serve (40/30/30): plate medallions with potatoes and asparagus and add a light yogurt drizzle.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – Beef: 4 medallions. Potatoes: about 1 1/4 cups. Asparagus: about 1 1/2 cups. Yogurt drizzle.


      Susan – Beef: 2–3 medallions. Potatoes: about 1 cup. Asparagus: about 1 1/4 cups. Yogurt drizzle.


      Elise – Beef: 2–3 medallions. Potatoes: about 1 cup. Asparagus: about 1 1/4 cups. Yogurt drizzle.


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Tenderloin cooks fast—start checking temperature early to avoid overcooking.


      Resting the medallions 5 minutes keeps them juicy.


      Cook asparagus after beef so it stays crisp-tender.
