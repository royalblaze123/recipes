Tuna Patties + Sweet Potato Fries + Green Beans

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 650 kcal • P 60g • C 55g • F 18g • Sat 3g • Na 700mg • Fiber 10g
Susan/Elise (each): 488 kcal • P 45g • C 41g • F 14g • Sat 2g • Na 525mg • Fiber 8g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 4 cans tuna
  • 2.00 lb sweet potatoes
  • 1 bag frozen green beans
  • egg whites
  • panko breadcrumbs
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • garlic powder
  • onion powder
  • smoked paprika
  • Italian seasoning
  • ground cumin
  • chili powder
  • red pepper flakes
  • extra-virgin olive oil
  • Dijon mustard
  • balsamic vinegar
  • apple cider vinegar
  • low-sodium soy sauce
  • cornstarch
  • hot sauce

Steps

  1. Oven: heat to 425°F. Line two sheet pans if available.
  2. Sweet potato fries: cut into fries. Toss with 2 tsp olive oil, smoked paprika, garlic powder, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, black pepper. Roast 24–28 minutes, flipping once.
  3. Tuna: drain tuna (4 standard 5 oz cans). Mix with 1/2 cup egg whites, 1/2 cup panko, 1 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp onion powder, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, black pepper. Form 6 patties.
  4. Bake tuna patties 12–14 minutes, flipping once, until set and lightly browned.
  5. Green beans: microwave; drain well; toss with 2 tsp olive oil, garlic powder, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, black pepper; roast 10 minutes.
  6. Serve per Notes.

Notes

Fast pantry protein with real sides—baked tuna patties stay moist via egg whites. Portions (per person): Frank: 2 tuna patties + 1 cup sweet potato fries + 1 1/2 cups green beans Susan: 5 oz protein + 3/4 cup starch + 1 cup veg Elise: 5 oz protein + 3/4 cup starch + 1 cup veg Tips (recipe-specific): Flip patties once so both sides brown. Roast fries longer for crisp edges (don’t crowd the pan).
