Slow Cooker Lemon Herb Chicken with White Beans and Brown Rice

Bright lemon-herb chicken with white beans over brown rice plus broccoli for a high-protein slow-cooker dinner.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 360 minutes
  • Total time: 380 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 2 lb boneless skinless chicken breast
  • 2 cans cannellini beans
  • 1 carton low sodium chicken broth
  • 1 each lemon
  • 1 bunch parsley
  • 1 each yellow onion
  • 6 cloves garlic
  • 1 bottle extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 jar Italian seasoning
  • 1 jar garlic powder
  • 1 jar black pepper
  • 1 bag brown rice
  • 1 head broccoli
  • 1 container nonfat Greek yogurt
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Prep: slice 1/2 onion and mince garlic. Zest and juice the lemon.
    2. Slow cooker: add chicken, onion, 3/4 cup low sodium broth, lemon juice, and 1 tbsp olive oil. Season with Italian seasoning, garlic powder, and black pepper. Cook LOW 6 hours (or HIGH 3–4 hours).
    3. Shred chicken in the pot. Leave lid off 10 minutes on HIGH to tighten texture.
    4. Beans late: drain and rinse cannellini beans. Measure out 1 cup beans and stir into the slow cooker for the final 20 minutes. Refrigerate remaining beans.
    5. Rice side: cook 3/4 cup dry brown rice. Measure out 1 cup cooked rice for this dinner; refrigerate remaining rice.
    6. Broccoli side: cut broccoli into florets. Roast at 425°F for 14–18 minutes with 1 tsp olive oil, garlic powder, and black pepper.
    7. Finish: stir chopped parsley into the chicken-bean mixture. Serve over rice with roasted broccoli and a small dollop of Greek yogurt.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – About 2 1/2 cups total (chicken + beans + rice + broccoli) with a larger share of chicken (40% of the full recipe).


      Susan – About 2 cups total (chicken + beans + rice + broccoli) (30% of the full recipe).


      Elise – About 2 cups total (chicken + beans + rice + broccoli) (30% of the full recipe).


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Add beans near the end so they stay intact and the dish stays structured.


      Use lemon zest at the end for flavor without sodium.


      If you want an even thicker sauce, leave the lid off 10–15 minutes before serving.
