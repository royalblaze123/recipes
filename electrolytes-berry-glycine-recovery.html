Berry Glycine Recovery Electrolyte (3 Bottles)

NOTES: Best used after treadmill sessions or VRT days when you want a slightly sweeter, recovery-focused bottle without sugar. Glycine adds a mild sweetness and may support sleep and recovery without spiking blood sugar.

PORTION SIZES (each recipe makes 3 bottles): Frank: 1 full 20 oz bottle; Susan: 1 full 20 oz bottle; Elise: 1 full 20 oz bottle.

Ingredients

  • 60 fl oz cold filtered water (3 x 20 fl oz bottles)
  • 3/8 tsp lite salt (divided, 1/8 tsp per bottle)
  • 3 scoops magnesium glycinate powder (about 150–200 mg elemental Mg per scoop, divided)
  • 6 g glycine powder (about 2 g per bottle, measuring with an accurate scale)
  • 3/4 tsp unsweetened berry flavor powder or a few drops berry flavor extract (evenly divided)
  • Additional liquid stevia or monk fruit sweetener, to taste (optional)
  • Ice cubes, optional, for serving

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Line up three clean 20 fl oz bottles. Add 1/8 teaspoon lite salt to each bottle.
  2. Divide the magnesium glycinate powder evenly across the three bottles.
  3. Weigh 6 g glycine powder and divide evenly, adding about 2 g to each bottle.
  4. Add the unsweetened berry flavor powder or extract evenly across the three bottles.
  5. Add a few drops of stevia or monk fruit to each bottle if you prefer a slightly sweeter taste.
  6. Fill each bottle with cold filtered water up to about 20 fl oz total volume.
  7. Cap tightly and shake each bottle for 20–30 seconds until all powders are completely dissolved.
  8. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes before drinking. Shake gently before each use.

Nutrition per Person (1 bottle)

  • Frank: ~10 kcal, 2 g protein (from glycine), 0 g fat, 0–1 g net carbs, ~250–300 mg sodium, ~300–350 mg potassium.
  • Susan: ~10 kcal, 2 g protein, 0 g fat, 0–1 g net carbs, ~250–300 mg sodium, ~300–350 mg potassium.
  • Elise: ~10 kcal, 2 g protein, 0 g fat, 0–1 g net carbs, ~250–300 mg sodium, ~300–350 mg potassium.
