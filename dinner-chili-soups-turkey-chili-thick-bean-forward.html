Turkey Chili (Thick, Bean-Forward)

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 630 kcal • P 60g • C 50g • F 18g • Sat 5g • Na 680mg • Fiber 14g
Susan/Elise (each): 472 kcal • P 45g • C 38g • F 14g • Sat 4g • Na 510mg • Fiber 10g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.75 lb 93% lean ground turkey
  • 1 can black beans
  • 1 can kidney beans
  • 1 can no-salt-added diced tomatoes
  • 1 carton low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 each onion
  • 1 each bell pepper
  • extra-virgin olive oil
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • chili powder
  • ground cumin
  • garlic powder
  • onion powder
  • smoked paprika
  • Italian seasoning
  • crushed red pepper
  • bay leaves

Steps

  1. Pot: heat 2 tsp olive oil over medium-high. Add 1 diced onion and 1 diced bell pepper; cook 6–8 minutes until browned and softened.
  2. Add 1.75 lb ground turkey; cook 7–9 minutes until fully browned.
  3. Season with chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, onion powder, smoked paprika, kosher salt, and black pepper (measure for your taste, keeping salt modest).
  4. Tomatoes: Can size note—use a standard 14.5 oz can no-salt-added diced tomatoes.. Add 1 can with juices.
  5. Beans: Can size note—use a standard 15 oz can black beans. and Can size note—use a standard 15 oz can kidney beans.. Drain/rinse both; add all beans.
  6. Liquid: add 1 cup low-sodium broth. Simmer uncovered 18–22 minutes, stirring, until thick.
  7. Finish: stir 1 tsp crushed red pepper if you want heat. Taste and adjust seasoning.
  8. Serve: portion per Notes.

Notes

Classic thick chili that reheats like a dream. Portions (per person): Frank: About 2 1/2 cups chili (1/3 pot) Susan: About 2 cups chili (1/4 pot) Elise: About 2 cups chili (1/4 pot) Tips (recipe-specific): Simmer uncovered so it reduces and stays thick. If it’s still thin, mash 1/2 cup beans in the pot to thicken without flour.
