Light Chicken Enchilada Bake

NOTES: Built as a complete dinner using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled starch portions. Use low-sodium broth and minimal added salt to support blood pressure and kidney health. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of pan); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of pan); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of pan).

Ingredients

  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breast
  • 4 cups low-sodium chicken broth (for poaching and sauce)
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste (no added sugar)
  • 1 tbsp whole-wheat flour
  • 6 small corn tortillas, cut into strips or wedges
  • 1 cup frozen corn (no added salt), thawed and drained
  • 1/2 cup shredded reduced-fat Mexican blend cheese
  • Olive oil spray for baking dish

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Place chicken breasts in a saucepan and cover with 3 cups of the low-sodium chicken broth. Bring just to a gentle simmer, cover partially, and cook 15–18 minutes until chicken is cooked through. Remove chicken to a plate to cool, then shred with two forks. Reserve 1 cup of the poaching broth for the sauce.
  2. Preheat oven to 375°F. Lightly spray an 8x11-inch baking dish with olive oil spray.
  3. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add diced onion and red bell pepper and cook 4–5 minutes until softened.
  4. Stir in minced garlic and cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Sprinkle in chili powder, ground cumin, smoked paprika, dried oregano, salt, and black pepper and stir to coat vegetables.
  6. Add tomato paste and whole-wheat flour to the skillet and cook 1–2 minutes, stirring constantly, to form a thick paste.
  7. Slowly whisk in the reserved 1 cup of chicken broth, stirring to smooth out lumps. Simmer 3–4 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly.
  8. Stir in shredded chicken and thawed corn. Cook 2–3 minutes to warm through, then remove from heat.
  9. Layer half of the corn tortilla pieces in the bottom of the prepared baking dish.
  10. Spread half of the chicken-vegetable mixture over the tortillas.
  11. Add the remaining tortilla pieces in an even layer, then top with the remaining chicken mixture.
  12. Sprinkle reduced-fat Mexican blend cheese over the top.
  13. Bake uncovered 20–25 minutes until bubbling around the edges and cheese is melted and lightly browned.
  14. Let casserole rest 10 minutes before slicing into portions for Frank, Susan, and Elise.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~570 kcal, ~48 g protein, ~50 g carbs, ~17 g fat.
  • Susan: ~430 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~38 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
  • Elise: ~430 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~38 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
