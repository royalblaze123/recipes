Beef & Bok Choy Brown Rice Bowls

NOTES: Ginger-garlic beef strips with bok choy and carrots over brown rice. This is a full-plate beef dinner built around lean beef, smart carbs, and plenty of non-starchy vegetables. Uses olive oil or avocado oil only, keeps sodium moderate, and avoids added sugars to support A1c, cholesterol, blood pressure, and PPPD-friendly eating. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

PORTION SIZES (per person): Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 1 pound lean beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 3 small heads baby bok choy, sliced
  • 1 medium carrot, matchstick-cut
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp grated fresh ginger or 1 tsp ground ginger
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp low-sodium tamari or soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar (unseasoned)
  • 3/4 cup uncooked brown rice, rinsed
  • 1 1/2 cups water or broth
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Cook the brown rice in water or broth according to package directions, then keep warm.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add sliced beef and sear 3–4 minutes until just cooked through. Transfer to a plate.
  3. In the same skillet, add bok choy stems and carrot. Cook 3–4 minutes until starting to soften.
  4. Stir in bok choy leaves, garlic, ginger, and green onions. Cook 2–3 minutes until leaves wilt.
  5. Return beef and any juices to the skillet. Stir in tamari, rice vinegar, and pepper. Cook 1–2 minutes, tossing to coat everything in the sauce.
  6. Taste and adjust seasoning with a pinch of salt or extra herbs if needed, keeping overall sodium moderate.
  7. Divide the finished meal into three plates or containers: give Frank roughly 1/3 of the total volume, and split the remaining 2/3 between Susan and Elise as slightly smaller but still generous portions.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~520 kcal, ~48 g protein, ~32 g carbs, ~18 g fat.
  • Susan: ~390 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
  • Elise: ~390 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
