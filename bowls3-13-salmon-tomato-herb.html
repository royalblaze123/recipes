Composed Salmon Bowl with Quinoa, Cabbage, and Tomato Herb

Servings

3 bowls (Frank 1.0×, Susan 0.75×, Elise 0.75×).

Times

  • Prep time: 22 minutes
  • Cook time: 28 minutes
  • Total time: 50 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1.75 lb salmon fillets
  • 1.0 cup dry quinoa
  • 1 head green cabbage
  • 1 pack microgreens
  • 11 tsp olive oil
  • 6 tsp minced garlic
  • 2 tsp black pepper
  • 2 tsp salt substitute
  • 2 tsp dried parsley
  • 3 tsp tomato paste
  • 2 tsp dried parsley
  • 2 tsp black pepper

Steps

  1. Quinoa: Rinse 1 cup dry quinoa. Cook with 2 cups water to yield ~3 cups cooked. Fluff and keep warm.
  2. Vegetables: Roast per steps below to get browned edges (texture contrast matters).
  3. Cabbage: Slice into wedges or thick ribbons. Toss with 4 tsp olive oil + 1 tsp pepper + 1 tsp salt substitute. Roast at 425°F for 16–20 minutes, flipping once, until browned edges.
  4. Salmon: Pat dry. Brush with 3 tsp olive oil; season with pepper + salt substitute. Bake at 425°F for 10–13 minutes to preferred doneness.
  5. Sauce: In a small bowl, whisk the Sauce ingredients (tomato herb). Keep it measured—this is a finish, not a flood.
  6. Build bowls (assembly order): Add warm starch first, then vegetables, then sliced protein. Spoon sauce on top. Finish with microgreens after heat (they’re the ‘gourmet’ signal).
  7. Plate portions per Notes.

Notes

Portions (per person):

Frank – 1 bowl (7 oz cooked protein, 1 cup cooked quinoa, 1 1/2 cups cabbage, 1/2 cup microgreens, 2–3 tbsp sauce).

Susan – 1 bowl (5 1/4 oz cooked protein, 3/4 of Frank’s starch portion, 1 1/4 cups cabbage, 1/3 cup microgreens, 2 tbsp sauce).

Elise – 1 bowl (5 1/4 oz cooked protein, 3/4 of Frank’s starch portion, 1 1/4 cups cabbage, 1/3 cup microgreens, 2 tbsp sauce).

Tips (recipe-specific):

Build order matters: warm base → dry veg → protein → sauce → microgreens last so nothing turns soggy.

Keep sauce measured; the bowl should taste ‘finished,’ not ‘dressed.’

Storage + Reheat

  • Cool promptly; store components in airtight containers in the fridge up to 3–4 days (typical safety guidance).
  • Reheat grain/beans + protein 60–90 seconds in the microwave; reheat roasted veg 4–6 minutes at 350°F in the air fryer for texture; add sauce and microgreens after reheating.
