Pork, Spinach & Tomato Penne (Light)

NOTES: Whole-wheat penne with lean ground pork, tomato-garlic sauce, and baby spinach. This is a full-plate pork dinner built around lean pork tenderloin/loin or 93% lean ground pork, smart carbs, and plenty of non-starchy vegetables. Uses olive or avocado oil only, keeps sodium moderate, and avoids added sugars to support A1c, cholesterol, blood pressure, and PPPD-friendly eating. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

PORTION SIZES (per person): Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 8 oz whole-wheat penne pasta
  • 1 pound 93% lean ground pork
  • 3 cups baby spinach
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 1/2 cups no-added-sugar tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 tsp dried Italian seasoning
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Cook whole-wheat penne in lightly salted water according to package directions until al dente. Reserve 1/4 cup cooking water, then drain.
  2. While pasta cooks, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add ground pork and cook 4–5 minutes, breaking it up, until no longer pink. Drain excess fat if needed.
  3. Add onion and cook 3–4 minutes until softened. Stir in garlic and cook 30–60 seconds.
  4. Pour in tomato sauce and broth, then add Italian seasoning, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Simmer 5–7 minutes on low.
  5. Add spinach and stir until wilted.
  6. Add cooked penne and a splash of reserved cooking water to the skillet, tossing until everything is combined and lightly saucy.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning with a pinch of salt or extra herbs if needed, keeping overall sodium moderate.
  8. Divide the finished meal into three plates or containers: give Frank roughly 1/3 of the total volume, and split the remaining 2/3 between Susan and Elise as slightly smaller but still generous portions.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~500 kcal, ~46 g protein, ~34 g carbs, ~16 g fat.
  • Susan: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
  • Elise: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
