Chili-Lime Trout Taco Bowls with Lentils and Black Beans

Taco-style bowls built on lentils and beans with chili-lime trout for slow carbs and high protein without tortillas.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 20 minutes
  • Total time: 40 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 1 lb fresh trout fillets
  • 1 bag dried lentils
  • 1 can black beans
  • 1 each lime
  • 1 each red onion
  • 1 each bell pepper
  • 1 jar chili powder
  • 1 jar ground cumin
  • 1 jar garlic powder
  • 1 jar black pepper
  • 1 bottle extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 container nonfat Greek yogurt
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Cook lentils: simmer 3/4 cup dry lentils until just tender. Measure out 1 3/4 cups cooked lentils for this recipe; refrigerate remaining lentils.
    2. Beans: drain and rinse black beans. Measure out 1 cup beans for this recipe; refrigerate remaining beans.
    3. Veg: dice 1/2 red onion and 1/2 bell pepper; store the rest.
    4. Warm base: heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a skillet. Cook onion/pepper until soft. Stir in lentils and beans with chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, and black pepper. Warm through.
    5. Cook trout: season with chili powder and lime juice. Sear 3–4 minutes per side until it flakes.
    6. Finish: stir Greek yogurt with lime juice for a quick crema.
    7. Serve: bowl the lentil-bean base and top with trout and a light yogurt drizzle, using the 40/30/30 split.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – About 2 1/2 cups lentil-bean base topped with a larger trout portion and a light yogurt drizzle (about 40% of the full recipe).


      Susan – About 2 cups base topped with a smaller trout portion and a light yogurt drizzle (about 30% of the full recipe).


      Elise – About 2 cups base topped with a smaller trout portion and a light yogurt drizzle (about 30% of the full recipe).


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Keep the seasoning mild in the base; add extra heat at the table to stay PPPD/GERD-friendly.


      Don’t over-simmer lentils—pull them when just tender so the bowl stays chunky.


      If sodium is a concern, rinse beans thoroughly and rely on lime + cumin for flavor.
