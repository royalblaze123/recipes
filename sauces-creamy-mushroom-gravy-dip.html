Creamy Mushroom Gravy Dip

A savory, creamy mushroom gravy for meatballs, chicken, pork, or roasted vegetables.
Servings: 6
Prep: 5 min | Cook: 10 min | Total: 15 min

Ingredients

  • 8 oz package mushrooms
  • 1 cup 2% milk
  • 1 cup low sodium chicken broth
  • 2 tbsp cornstarch
  • 2 tsp olive oil
  • 2 tsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp black pepper

Steps

  1. Slice mushrooms. In a small bowl, whisk cornstarch into the milk until smooth (no lumps).
  2. Heat olive oil in a nonstick skillet over medium-high heat. Add mushrooms and cook 5–6 minutes, stirring, until browned and most moisture cooks off.
  3. Add chicken broth, soy sauce, Dijon, garlic powder, onion powder, and black pepper. Bring to a gentle simmer.
  4. Whisk the milk-cornstarch slurry again, then slowly pour it into the skillet while whisking constantly.
  5. Simmer 2–3 minutes, whisking, until thickened. If too thick, add a splash of broth; if too thin, simmer 1–2 minutes longer.
  6. Serve warm. Store refrigerated up to 4 days; rewarm gently and whisk smooth.

Notes

Optional one-line dish description: Creamy, savory mushroom gravy with a clean, dip-friendly thickness. Portions (per person): Frank: 1/3 of recipe (about 1/2 cup) Susan: 1/4 of recipe (about 6 tbsp) Elise: 1/4 of recipe (about 6 tbsp)
