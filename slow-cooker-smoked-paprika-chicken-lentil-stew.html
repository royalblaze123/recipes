Slow Cooker Smoked Paprika Chicken Lentil Stew

Chefy smoked-paprika chicken and lentil stew with vegetables—high protein, slow carbs, and hearty texture (no side).

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 420 minutes
  • Total time: 440 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 2 lb boneless skinless chicken breast
  • 1 carton low sodium chicken broth
  • 1 each yellow onion
  • 1 bunch celery
  • 1 lb bag carrots
  • 6 cloves garlic
  • 1 bag lentils
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 bottle extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 jar smoked paprika
  • 1 jar dried thyme
  • 1 jar bay leaves
  • 1 jar black pepper
  • 1 bunch parsley
  • 1 jar cornstarch
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Rinse lentils: rinse 3/4 cup dry lentils and set aside.
    2. Prep veg: dice 1/2 the onion, slice 3 celery stalks, slice 3 carrots, and mince 3 garlic cloves. Chop parsley (use about 1/4 cup; refrigerate remaining parsley).
    3. Slow cooker base: add onion, celery, carrots, and garlic. Stir in 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1 tsp dried thyme, 1 bay leaf, and black pepper.
    4. Liquid: add the carton of low sodium chicken broth plus 2 cups water and the can of diced tomatoes.
    5. Cook chicken: add chicken breasts. Cook on LOW for 6–7 hours or HIGH for 3–4 hours, until chicken reaches 165°F and is shred-tender.
    6. Shred: remove chicken, shred, and return shredded chicken to the slow cooker.
    7. Lentils late: stir in rinsed lentils and cook on HIGH for 35–40 minutes until lentils are tender but not falling apart.
    8. Thicken (optional): whisk 1 tbsp cornstarch with 2 tbsp water. Stir slurry into the stew and cook 5 minutes.
    9. Finish: remove bay leaf, stir in chopped parsley, and serve.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – About 3 1/2 cups stew.


      Susan – About 2 2/3 cups stew.


      Elise – About 2 2/3 cups stew.


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Add lentils at the end so the stew stays hearty instead of turning soft and muddy.


      Smoked paprika adds depth without making the stew spicy.


      Stir in parsley at the end for a fresh, chefy finish.
