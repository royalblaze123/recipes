Shepherd’s Pie-Style Bake (Sweet Potato Mash)

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 640 kcal • P 58g • C 60g • F 16g • Sat 6g • Na 680mg • Fiber 12g
Susan/Elise (each): 480 kcal • P 44g • C 45g • F 12g • Sat 4g • Na 510mg • Fiber 9g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.50 lb 93% lean ground beef
  • 2.25 lb sweet potatoes
  • 1 bag frozen mixed vegetables
  • 1 carton low-sodium beef broth
  • 1 package low-sodium gravy mix
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • garlic powder
  • onion powder
  • smoked paprika
  • chili powder
  • ground cumin
  • Italian seasoning
  • extra-virgin olive oil

Steps

  1. Oven: heat to 400°F. Lightly oil a baking dish.
  2. Sweet potatoes: peel and cube. Boil in salted water 14–18 minutes until very tender. Drain well.
  3. Mash: add 2 tsp olive oil, 1/2 tsp garlic powder, and 1/4 tsp black pepper to potatoes. Mash until smooth.
  4. Beef: brown 1.50 lb lean ground beef 7–9 minutes. Season with 1 tsp onion powder, 1 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper.
  5. Veg: stir in 3 cups frozen mixed vegetables; cook 6–8 minutes until hot.
  6. Gravy: whisk low-sodium gravy mix with 1 cup low-sodium beef broth per package directions. Stir into beef/veg and simmer 2 minutes until thick.
  7. Assemble: spread beef mixture in dish. Spread sweet potato mash over top and rough up with a fork.
  8. Bake: 15–18 minutes until edges bubble. Broil 2–3 minutes for browned peaks. Rest 10 minutes.
  9. Portion per Notes.

Notes

Classic comfort: savory beef base under a sweet-potato mash top. Portions (per person): Frank: 1/3 of pan Susan: 1/4 of pan Elise: 1/4 of pan Tips (recipe-specific): Thicken the base before baking so it cuts clean (not soupy). Fork-texture on top browns better and tastes more ‘comfort-food’.
