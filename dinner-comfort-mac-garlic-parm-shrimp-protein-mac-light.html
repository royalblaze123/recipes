Garlic-Parm Shrimp Protein Mac (Light)

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 640 kcal • P 60g • C 58g • F 16g • Sat 6g • Na 690mg • Fiber 10g
Susan/Elise (each): 480 kcal • P 45g • C 44g • F 12g • Sat 4g • Na 518mg • Fiber 8g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1 box Barilla Protein+ pasta
  • 1.50 lb raw shrimp
  • 1 bag frozen broccoli florets
  • 1 carton skim milk
  • 2 oz grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 container reduced-fat shredded mozzarella cheese
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • garlic powder
  • onion powder
  • smoked paprika
  • Italian seasoning
  • chili powder
  • ground cumin
  • crushed red pepper
  • cornstarch
  • extra-virgin olive oil
  • Dijon mustard
  • hot sauce
  • Worcestershire sauce
  • low-sodium soy sauce
  • rice vinegar
  • lemon

Steps

  1. Pasta: cook Protein+ pasta al dente. Drain.
  2. Shrimp: pat shrimp dry. Sauté with 2 tsp olive oil, 2 tsp garlic powder, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper 3–4 minutes until pink.
  3. Broccoli: steam 3 cups frozen broccoli (microwave or pot). Drain well.
  4. Sauce base: whisk 2 tbsp cornstarch into 2 cups skim milk. Simmer 3–5 minutes until thick.
  5. Cheese: stir in 4 oz reduced-fat mozzarella + 2 oz Parmesan until melted.
  6. Finish: add 2 tsp lemon juice and black pepper.
  7. Combine: fold in pasta, shrimp, and broccoli.
  8. Serve per Notes.

Notes

Garlic-parm comfort without a heavy cream sauce—shrimp keeps it light. Portions (per person): Frank: About 2 1/2 cups mac (1/3 pan) + extra veg Susan: About 2 cups mac (1/4 pan) + extra veg Elise: About 2 cups mac (1/4 pan) + extra veg Tips (recipe-specific): Pat shrimp dry so it sears instead of steaming. Lemon at the end makes the cheesy sauce taste brighter.
