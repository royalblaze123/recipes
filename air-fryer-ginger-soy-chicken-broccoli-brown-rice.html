Air Fryer Ginger Soy Chicken with Broccoli and Brown Rice

Ginger-soy chicken with broccoli over brown rice—savory, low-sugar, and designed to hit your dinner macro band.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 25 minutes
  • Cook time: 16 minutes
  • Total time: 41 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 1 lb boneless skinless chicken breast
  • 1 bag brown rice
  • 1 head broccoli
  • 1 knob fresh ginger
  • 6 cloves garlic
  • 1 bottle extra virgin olive oil
  • 3 tbsp low sodium soy sauce
  • 1 container nonfat Greek yogurt
  • 1 each lime
  • 1 jar black pepper
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Rice: cook 1 cup dry brown rice. Measure out 2 cups cooked rice for this dinner; refrigerate remaining rice.
    2. Prep broccoli: cut florets. Measure out 1/2 head broccoli for this dinner; refrigerate remaining. Toss with 1 tsp olive oil and black pepper.
    3. Prep chicken: cut into bite-size pieces. Toss with 2 tsp olive oil, low sodium soy sauce, grated ginger, minced garlic, and black pepper. Rest 10 minutes.
    4. Cosori setup: preheat both baskets to 400°F for 3 minutes.
    5. Basket A (broccoli): cook broccoli 400°F for 8 minutes, shaking once. Remove to a plate.
    6. Basket B (chicken): cook chicken 400°F for 8–10 minutes, shaking once, until 165°F internal.
    7. Quick sauce: stir 2 tbsp Greek yogurt with 1 tsp lime juice and 1 tsp water for a light drizzle (optional).
    8. Serve (40/30/30): build bowls with brown rice, chicken, and broccoli.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – Rice: about 1/2 cup cooked. Chicken: largest share. Broccoli: about 1 1/2 cups.


      Susan – Rice: about 3/8 cup cooked. Chicken: medium share. Broccoli: about 1 1/4 cups.


      Elise – Rice: about 3/8 cup cooked. Chicken: medium share. Broccoli: about 1 1/4 cups.


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Cook broccoli first, then chicken—this keeps broccoli crisp and chicken hot.


      Use low sodium soy sauce to keep sodium controlled.


      If you want fewer carbs, reduce rice and add extra broccoli.
