Haddock with Lemon Herb Potatoes and Asparagus

NOTES: Complete dinner with lean seafood, smart carbs, and 1–2 cups of non-starchy vegetables per person. Use low-sodium ingredients, avoid added sugars, and keep portions aligned with goals. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total volume); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total volume); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total volume) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 3 haddock fillets (4–5 oz each)
  • 12 oz baby potatoes, halved
  • 1 lb asparagus, woody ends trimmed
  • 1 1/2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 1/2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp dried Italian seasoning
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Parchment paper for pan

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (205°C). Line a sheet pan with parchment paper.
  2. Place baby potatoes on the pan. Drizzle with 1 tbsp olive oil, sprinkle with half the garlic powder, half the Italian seasoning, and black pepper. Toss and spread in a single layer.
  3. Roast potatoes for 10 minutes.
  4. Remove pan from oven. Add asparagus spears to the other side of the pan. Drizzle with remaining 1/2 tbsp olive oil and sprinkle with remaining garlic powder, Italian seasoning, and black pepper. Toss lightly.
  5. Place haddock fillets in the center of the pan. Brush with lemon juice and a little of the pan oil if needed.
  6. Return pan to the oven and roast 10–12 more minutes, until haddock flakes easily with a fork and potatoes are tender.
  7. Divide haddock, potatoes, and asparagus into three portions, giving Frank the largest share of fish and potatoes.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~600 kcal, ~45 g protein, ~55 g carbs, ~17 g fat.
  • Susan: ~450 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~41 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
  • Elise: ~450 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~41 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
