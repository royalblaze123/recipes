Dinner Soup Chicken Lentil Vegetable

Chefy chicken and lentil soup with vegetables—slow carbs, high protein, and a full dinner with strong texture.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 18 minutes
  • Cook time: 45 minutes
  • Total time: 63 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 2 lb boneless skinless chicken breast
  • 1 carton low sodium chicken broth
  • 1 each yellow onion
  • 1 bunch celery
  • 1 lb bag carrots
  • 6 cloves garlic
  • 1 bag lentils
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 bottle extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 jar smoked paprika
  • 1 jar dried thyme
  • 1 jar bay leaves
  • 1 jar black pepper
  • 1 bunch parsley
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Rinse lentils: rinse 3/4 cup dry lentils and set aside.
    2. Prep veg: dice 1/2 the onion, slice 3 celery stalks, slice 2 carrots, and mince 3 garlic cloves. Chop parsley (use about 1/4 cup; refrigerate remaining parsley).
    3. Start base: heat a large pot over medium heat. Add 2 tsp olive oil, then add onion, celery, carrots, and garlic. Cook 6 minutes until softened.
    4. Season: stir in 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1 tsp dried thyme, 1 bay leaf, and black pepper.
    5. Add liquid + tomatoes: add the carton of low sodium chicken broth plus 3 cups water and the can of diced tomatoes. Bring to a boil.
    6. Add chicken + lentils: add chicken breasts and rinsed lentils. Reduce to a gentle simmer, cover, and simmer 20 minutes until chicken reaches 165°F and lentils are tender.
    7. Shred chicken: transfer chicken to a plate, shred, and return shredded chicken to the pot.
    8. Finish: remove bay leaf, stir in chopped parsley, and simmer 2 minutes.
    9. Serve hot.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – About 3 1/2 cups soup.


      Susan – About 2 2/3 cups soup.


      Elise – About 2 2/3 cups soup.


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Simmer chicken gently so it stays tender when shredded.


      Rinsing lentils reduces foam and improves texture.


      This soup thickens as it sits—add a splash of water when reheating.
