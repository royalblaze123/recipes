Protein Baked Oatmeal Squares (Freezer Batch)

Freezer-friendly baked oatmeal squares for slow carbs and steady energy alongside your morning shake.

Servings

16 squares.

Times

  • Prep time: 10 minutes
  • Cook time: 35 minutes
  • Total time: 45 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 1 container old-fashioned oats
  • 1 carton egg whites
  • 1 tub nonfat Greek yogurt
  • 1 jar unsweetened applesauce
  • 1 container whey protein isolate
  • 1 container baking powder
  • 1 jar ground cinnamon
  • 1 bag frozen blueberries
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Line a 9x13 pan with parchment.
    2. In a large bowl, mix 3 cups oats, 1 scoop whey, 2 tsp baking powder, and 2 tsp cinnamon.
    3. Whisk in 2 cups egg whites, 1 cup Greek yogurt, and 1 cup unsweetened applesauce until evenly combined.
    4. Fold in 1 cup frozen blueberries. Reseal and freeze the remaining blueberries.
    5. Pour into the pan and bake 30–35 minutes until set. Cool completely.
    6. Cut into 16 squares. Wrap squares individually and freeze in a labeled freezer bag for up to 1 month.
    7. Breakfast use: grab 1 square and eat with your morning shake (it will thaw by mid-morning).

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – 1 square with your morning shake (slow-carb add-on).


      Susan – Optional: 1 square with breakfast if desired.


      Elise – Optional: 1 square with breakfast if desired.


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      If you want even steadier energy, eat the square first, then drink the shake.


      For best texture, thaw in the fridge overnight (or just eat at room temp).


      If mornings run long, keep 2 squares in the work fridge as backup.
