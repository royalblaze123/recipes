Protein Baked Oatmeal Squares (Freezer Batch)

Freezer-friendly baked oatmeal squares for slow carbs and steady energy alongside your morning shake.

Servings

16 squares (freezer batch).

Times

  • Prep time: 10 minutes
  • Cook time: 35 minutes
  • Total time: 45 minutes

Ingredients

  • 3 cups old-fashioned oats
  • 1/2 carton egg whites
  • 1/4 tub nonfat Greek yogurt
  • 1/3 jar unsweetened applesauce
  • 1 scoop whey protein isolate
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 5/12 bag frozen blueberries

Preparation Steps

  1. Oven: Preheat to 350°F. Line a 9x13 pan with parchment.
  2. Dry mix: Measure 3 cups old-fashioned oats. Stir oats + 1 scoop whey protein isolate + 2 tsp baking powder + 2 tsp ground cinnamon in a large bowl.
  3. Wet mix: Measure 2 cups (16 fl oz) egg whites (this is 1/2 of a 32 fl oz carton). Measure 1 cup nonfat Greek yogurt (this is 1/4 of a 32 oz tub). Measure 1 cup unsweetened applesauce (this is 1/3 of a 24 oz jar). Whisk into the dry mix until evenly combined.
  4. Blueberries: Measure 1 cup frozen blueberries (this is about 5/12 of a 12 oz bag). Fold in. Reseal and freeze the remaining blueberries.
  5. Bake: Pour into the pan and bake 30–35 minutes until set. Cool completely.
  6. Freeze: Cut into 16 squares. Wrap squares individually and freeze in a labeled freezer bag for up to 1 month.
  7. Breakfast use: Grab 1 square and eat with your morning shake (it will thaw by mid-morning).

Notes

Tips (recipe-specific):

If you want even steadier energy, eat the square first, then drink the shake.

For best texture, thaw in the fridge overnight (or just eat at room temp).

If mornings run long, keep 2 squares in the work fridge as backup.
