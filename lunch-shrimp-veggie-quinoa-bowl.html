Shrimp Veggie Quinoa Bowl

NOTES: Designed as a portable grab-and-go lunch using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled smart carbs. Use low-sodium ingredients and avoid added sugars where possible. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total volume); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total volume); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total volume) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup uncooked quinoa, rinsed
  • 1 1/2 cups low-sodium vegetable or chicken broth
  • 12 oz cooked shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 1/2 cups steamed broccoli florets
  • 1 cup halved cherry tomatoes
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp dried Italian seasoning
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Cook quinoa: Combine rinsed quinoa and broth in a saucepan. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat, cover, and simmer 12–15 minutes until liquid is absorbed. Cool slightly.
  2. Steam broccoli florets until crisp-tender, then cool.
  3. In a large bowl, combine cooked quinoa, shrimp, broccoli, cherry tomatoes, and sliced bell pepper.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, Italian seasoning, and black pepper.
  5. Pour dressing over the bowl and toss gently until evenly coated.
  6. Divide into three containers, giving Frank the largest portion for his protein needs.
  7. Refrigerate up to 2–3 days. Eat chilled or lightly rewarmed at home.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~570 kcal, ~48 g protein, ~52 g carbs, ~16 g fat.
  • Susan: ~430 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~39 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
  • Elise: ~430 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~39 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
