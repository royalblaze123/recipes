Protein Oat Muffins (Freezer Batch)

Grab-and-go oat muffins engineered as a slow-carb add-on to your shake for rushed mornings.

Servings

12 muffins (freezer batch).

Times

  • Prep time: 10 minutes
  • Cook time: 22 minutes
  • Total time: 32 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups old-fashioned oats
  • 3/8 carton egg whites
  • 1/8 tub nonfat Greek yogurt
  • 2 each ripe bananas
  • 1 scoop whey protein isolate
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 2 tsp ground cinnamon

Preparation Steps

  1. Oven: Preheat to 350°F. Line a 12-cup muffin tin.
  2. Dry base: Measure 1 1/2 cups old-fashioned oats. In a bowl, stir oats + 1 scoop whey protein isolate + 2 tsp baking powder + 2 tsp ground cinnamon.
  3. Wet base: Mash 2 ripe bananas. Measure 1 1/2 cups (12 fl oz) egg whites (this is 3/8 of a 32 fl oz carton). Measure 1/2 cup nonfat Greek yogurt (this is 1/8 of a 32 oz tub). Whisk into the dry base until uniform.
  4. Bake: Divide into 12 muffin cups. Bake 18–22 minutes until set. Cool completely.
  5. Freeze: Freeze muffins on a tray 1 hour, then move to a freezer bag. Freeze up to 1 month.
  6. Breakfast use: Grab 1 muffin and eat with your morning shake.

Notes

Grab-and-go oat muffins engineered as a slow-carb add-on to your shake for rushed mornings.

Portions (per person):

Frank – 1 muffin with your morning shake (slow-carb add-on).

Susan – Optional: 1 muffin with breakfast if desired.

Elise – Optional: 1 muffin with breakfast if desired.

Tips (recipe-specific):

If lunch will be 2–3 PM, grab 2 muffins instead of 1.

If you want less sweetness, use 1 banana and add 1/2 cup unsweetened applesauce in its place.

Store 2–3 muffins in the fridge as a 3-day backup so you never run out.
