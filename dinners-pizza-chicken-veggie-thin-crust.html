Chicken & Veggie Thin-Crust Sheet Pan Pizza

Whole-wheat thin-crust sheet pan pizza loaded with chicken and vegetables for a high-protein, lighter pizza night dinner.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 12 minutes
  • Cook time: 16 minutes
  • Total time: 28 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 large rectangular whole-wheat thin pizza crust
  • 14 oz cooked chicken breast
  • 2 cups thinly sliced bell peppers
  • 1 1/2 cups small broccoli florets
  • 1 cup thinly sliced mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup thinly sliced red onion
  • 3/4 cup no-salt-added tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
  • 4 oz part-skim mozzarella cheese
  • 1 oz finely grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp dried Italian seasoning
  • 1/4 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp kosher or sea salt
  • Olive oil spray

Preparation Steps

  1. Heat the oven to 425°F (about 220°C). Line a large sheet pan with parchment paper and lightly spray with olive oil.
  2. If the chicken is not yet cooked, season 1 lb raw boneless skinless chicken breast lightly with salt, pepper, and garlic powder and bake or sauté until cooked through (165°F internal), then chop to make 14 oz cooked chicken breast. Set aside.
  3. Place the whole-wheat pizza crust on the prepared sheet pan. If using a very thin lavash-style crust, you can use two overlapping pieces to cover the pan; press them together slightly so they bake as one base.
  4. In a medium bowl, toss the bell peppers, broccoli florets, mushrooms, and red onion (if using) with 1 tsp olive oil and a light sprinkle of salt and pepper. Spread the vegetables on a second parchment-lined pan and pre-roast for 6–8 minutes, just until starting to soften.
  5. In a small bowl, stir together the tomato sauce, garlic powder, dried Italian seasoning, and a pinch of salt and pepper.
  6. Spread the seasoned tomato sauce evenly over the pizza crust, leaving a small border at the edges. Sprinkle half of the shredded mozzarella over the sauce.
  7. Scatter the chopped cooked chicken evenly over the crust. Add the pre-roasted vegetables in an even layer on top of the chicken.
  8. Sprinkle the remaining mozzarella and the grated Parmesan evenly over the top. Drizzle the remaining 2 tsp olive oil very lightly over the pizza surface.
  9. Bake the pizza at 425°F for 10–12 minutes, or until the crust is crisp, the cheese is melted, and the edges are lightly browned. Rotate the pan halfway through if needed for even browning.
  10. Remove the pizza from the oven and let it rest for 2–3 minutes. Using a sharp knife or pizza wheel, cut the sheet pan pizza into 10 equal squares.
  11. Place 4 of the 10 squares on a plate for Frank, making sure his portion includes plenty of chicken and vegetables.
  12. Place 3 squares each on plates for Susan and Elise. If any toppings slide, use a spatula to scoop the extra vegetables and chicken back onto each person's slices so portions stay balanced.

Portion Sizes (Per Person, Approximate)

  • Frank (F · 1.0×): 4 of 10 squares with ~4.8 oz cooked chicken breast, ~2.6 oz whole-wheat crust, ~1.6 oz part-skim mozzarella, and ~2 cups vegetables.
  • Susan (S · 0.75×): 3 of 10 squares with ~3.6 oz cooked chicken breast, ~2 oz whole-wheat crust, ~1.2 oz part-skim mozzarella, and ~1 1/2 cups vegetables.
  • Elise (E · 0.75×): 3 of 10 squares with ~3.6 oz cooked chicken breast, ~2 oz whole-wheat crust, ~1.2 oz part-skim mozzarella, and ~1 1/2 cups vegetables.
