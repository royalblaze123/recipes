Salmon Super Greens Lunch Salad

Omega-3 rich salmon salad over super greens with light yogurt Dijon dressing for a heart-healthy full lunch.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 15 minutes
  • Cook time: 15 minutes (for brown rice, if needed)
  • Total time: 30 minutes

Ingredients

  • 10 oz cooked salmon
  • 1/2 cup dry brown rice
  • 6 cups mixed super greens
  • 3/4 cup cucumber
  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup carrot
  • 6 tbsp 2% plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 1/2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp dried dill
  • 1/4 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp kosher

Preparation Steps

  1. Rinse the brown rice under cool water. Add to a small pot with 1 cup of water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 25–30 minutes until tender and water is absorbed. Fluff with a fork and let cool to room temperature.
  2. Use cooked salmon from baking, air-frying, or grilling. Remove any skin and visible bones. Flake the salmon into bite-size pieces and let cool.
  3. Rinse and dry the super greens, cucumber, tomatoes, and carrot. Chop or shred as directed.
  4. In a small bowl or jar, whisk together the Greek yogurt, olive oil, lemon juice, Dijon mustard, garlic powder, dried dill, black pepper, and salt until smooth. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed, keeping acidity gentle.
  5. In a large mixing bowl, combine the super greens, cucumber, grape tomatoes, and shredded carrot.
  6. Add the flaked salmon and the cooled brown rice to the bowl with the vegetables.
  7. Pour most of the yogurt Dijon dressing over the salad (reserve a tablespoon or so for drizzling). Toss gently until everything is lightly coated, trying not to break up the salmon too much.
  8. Add about 2 cups of the salad mixture to a large bowl or container for Frank, making sure his portion includes roughly 4 oz salmon and 1/3 cup cooked brown rice. Add a teaspoon or two of any reserved dressing on top if desired.
  9. Divide the remaining salad between two bowls or containers for Susan and Elise, about 1 1/2 cups each, ensuring each has about 3 oz salmon and 1/4 cup cooked brown rice. Drizzle with any remaining dressing.
  10. Serve right away or refrigerate tightly covered for up to 24 hours. Keep chilled for work or school lunches.
