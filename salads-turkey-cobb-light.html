Light Turkey Cobb Lunch Salad (Frank Household – Occasional)

Cobb-style salad with turkey breast, egg, a little turkey bacon, and Greek-yogurt ranch for an occasional full-flavor lunch that still fits your targets.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 10 minutes
  • Total time: 30 minutes

Ingredients

  • 9 oz cooked turkey breast
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 1/2 slices cooked turkey bacon
  • 6 cups chopped romaine lettuce
  • 3/4 cup halved grape tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup chopped cucumber
  • 1/3 cup shredded carrot
  • 6 tbsp 2% plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 1/2 tbsp lemon juice or mild vinegar
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp dried dill or parsley
  • 1/4 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp kosher or sea salt

Preparation Steps

  1. Place the eggs in a saucepan, cover with cold water, and bring just to a gentle boil. Turn off the heat, cover, and let sit for 10–12 minutes. Transfer to an ice bath until cool, then peel and cut each egg into quarters.
  2. Cook the turkey bacon in a skillet or oven until crisp. Let cool slightly, then chop. Measure about 1 1/2 slices for the salad.
  3. Use cooked turkey breast (leftover roasted turkey or deli turkey labeled lower-sodium). Chop into bite-size pieces and measure about 9 oz.
  4. Rinse and dry the romaine, tomatoes, cucumber, and carrot. Chop the romaine, halve the tomatoes, chop the cucumber, and shred the carrot.
  5. In a small bowl or jar, whisk together the Greek yogurt, olive oil, lemon juice or mild vinegar, Dijon mustard, garlic powder, dried dill or parsley, black pepper, and salt until smooth. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.
  6. In a large mixing bowl, add the chopped romaine, grape tomatoes, cucumber, and shredded carrot. Toss lightly.
  7. Add the chopped turkey breast and the quartered eggs to the bowl. Sprinkle the chopped turkey bacon over the top.
  8. Drizzle most of the yogurt ranch dressing over the salad (reserve a tablespoon or so). Toss gently to coat, keeping the egg pieces mostly intact.
  9. Add about 2 cups of the mixture to a large bowl or container for Frank, ensuring his portion has about 3 1/2 oz turkey, 1 egg, and roughly 1/2 slice worth of turkey bacon pieces. Add a small spoonful of any reserved dressing on top if desired.
  10. Divide the remaining salad between two containers for Susan and Elise, about 1 1/2 cups each, making sure each has about 2 1/2 oz turkey, 1 egg, and about 1/3 slice worth of turkey bacon. Drizzle with any remaining dressing.
  11. Serve immediately or refrigerate tightly covered for up to 24 hours. Keep chilled for work or school lunches. Treat this as an occasional full-flavor lunch in your rotation.
