White Bean Turkey Chowder with Greens

NOTES: This chowder is designed as a full meal in a bowl with lean protein, smart carbs, and 1–2 cups of vegetables per person. Use low-sodium broth and ingredients, avoid added sugars, and keep portions aligned with your goals. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total pot); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 12 oz lean ground turkey (93%+ lean)
  • 1 can (15 oz) no-salt-added cannellini or great northern beans, drained and rinsed
  • 3 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 3/4 cup 2% milk or unsweetened almond milk
  • 1/2 cup plain 2% Greek yogurt
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and diced
  • 2 cups chopped kale, ribs removed
  • 1/2 tsp dried Italian seasoning
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a soup pot over medium heat for 1–2 minutes.
  2. Add ground turkey and cook 6–7 minutes, breaking it into small pieces, until browned and cooked through.
  3. Add onion and carrots. Cook 4–5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until vegetables are softened.
  4. Stir in white beans, kale, chicken broth, Italian seasoning, garlic powder, and black pepper.
  5. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer 12–15 minutes until carrots are tender and kale is soft.
  6. Whisk milk and Greek yogurt together, then stir into the pot. Warm on low heat 3–4 minutes, stirring often, without boiling.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning. Ladle into three bowls, giving Frank the largest portion of turkey and beans.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~630 kcal, ~49 g protein, ~50 g carbs, ~18 g fat.
  • Susan: ~475 kcal, ~37 g protein, ~37 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
  • Elise: ~475 kcal, ~37 g protein, ~37 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
