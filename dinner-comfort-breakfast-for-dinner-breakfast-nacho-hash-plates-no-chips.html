Breakfast Nacho Hash Plates (No Chips)

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 650 kcal • P 55g • C 60g • F 18g • Sat 7g • Na 700mg • Fiber 12g
Susan/Elise (each): 488 kcal • P 41g • C 45g • F 14g • Sat 5g • Na 525mg • Fiber 9g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 6 each eggs
  • 1.50 lb baby potatoes
  • 1 can black beans
  • 1 each avocado
  • 1 jar salsa
  • 6 oz reduced-fat shredded cheddar cheese
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • garlic powder
  • onion powder
  • smoked paprika
  • Italian seasoning
  • hot sauce
  • Dijon mustard
  • extra-virgin olive oil
  • salsa
  • chili powder
  • ground cumin

Steps

  1. Oven: heat to 425°F.
  2. Hash: dice baby potatoes; toss with 2 tsp olive oil, chili powder, cumin, black pepper, modest kosher salt. Roast 22–26 minutes, stirring once.
  3. Beans: drain/rinse black beans (15 oz can). Warm 1 1/2 cups beans in a saucepan with salsa.
  4. Eggs: scramble or fry 6 eggs.
  5. Assemble: potatoes + beans + 2 oz cheddar total across plate + salsa + avocado slices.
  6. Serve per Notes.

Notes

Nacho breakfast vibe without chips: potatoes, beans, eggs, salsa, avocado. Portions (per person): Frank: 2 eggs + 1 cup hash + 1/2 cup beans + 1/3 avocado Susan: 2 eggs + 3/4 cup hash + 1/3 cup beans + 1/4 avocado Elise: 2 eggs + 3/4 cup hash + 1/3 cup beans + 1/4 avocado Tips (recipe-specific): Use salsa as the ‘sauce’ so you don’t need sour cream. Measure cheddar; the salsa + spices carry flavor.
