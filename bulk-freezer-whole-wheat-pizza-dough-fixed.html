Bulk Freezer Whole Wheat Pizza Dough

Make-ahead whole wheat pizza dough for the freezer: portion into 6 dough balls for fast weeknight pizzas.

Servings

6 dough balls.

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 0 minutes
  • Total time: 1 hour 50 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 bag whole wheat flour
  • 1 bag bread flour
  • 1 jar instant yeast
  • 1 canister kosher salt
  • 1 bottle extra-virgin olive oil

Preparation Steps

  1. Measure 2 3/4 cups warm water (650 g). Warm to 100–105°F (warm to the touch, not hot).
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together 5 3/4 cups whole wheat flour (700 g), 2 1/2 cups bread flour (300 g), 2 1/2 tsp instant yeast (8 g), and 12 g kosher salt (about 1 tbsp + 1 tsp Diamond Crystal OR about 2 1/2 tsp Morton kosher) until evenly combined.
  3. Add the warm water and 2 tbsp + 1 tsp extra-virgin olive oil (30 g). Stir with a sturdy spoon until a shaggy dough forms and no dry flour remains.
  4. Knead 8–10 minutes: either by hand on a lightly floured counter or in a stand mixer on low/medium-low until the dough is smooth, elastic, and slightly tacky (not wet).
  5. First rise: place dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover, and let rise at room temperature 60–90 minutes, until roughly doubled.
  6. Divide into 6 dough balls: turn dough onto the counter, shape into a log, and cut into 6 equal pieces. If using a scale, each piece should be about 10 oz (280–290 g). Shape each piece into a tight ball.
  7. Optional cold ferment (better flavor): place dough balls on a lightly oiled tray, cover, and refrigerate 12–48 hours.
  8. Freeze for bulk storage: lightly oil each dough ball, wrap tightly in plastic wrap, then place all wrapped balls into a freezer bag. Label with the date. Freeze up to 3 months.
  9. Thaw for pizza night: move 1 dough ball to the fridge 12–24 hours before using. About 60 minutes before baking, let the covered dough sit at room temperature so it relaxes for stretching.
  10. Bake use guideline (per dough ball): preheat oven to 475°F with a stone/steel or inverted sheet pan heating inside. Stretch dough to 12–14 inches on parchment, par-bake 5 minutes, add toppings, then bake 10–12 minutes more until browned.

Notes

Portions (per person):


Frank – For pizza night, use 1 dough ball to make 1 pizza; serve Frank 4 slices (about 40% of the pie).


Susan – For pizza night, serve Susan 3 slices (about 30% of the pie).


Elise – For pizza night, serve Elise 3 slices (about 30% of the pie).


Tips (recipe-specific):

Weigh flour and water for consistent dough balls and predictable bake results.


For best flavor, thaw a dough ball overnight in the fridge and let it sit covered 60 minutes at room temp before shaping.


To prevent freezer burn, oil each dough ball lightly, wrap tightly, then freeze in a labeled bag.
