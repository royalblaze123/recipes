Slow Cooker Sirloin Black Bean Quinoa Stew

Chefy sirloin stew with black beans and quinoa—thick, hearty, and calibrated as a full dinner bowl (no side).

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 22 minutes
  • Cook time: 420 minutes
  • Total time: 442 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 2 lb sirloin steak
  • 1 carton low sodium beef broth
  • 1 each yellow onion
  • 1 bunch celery
  • 1 lb bag carrots
  • 6 cloves garlic
  • 1 can black beans
  • 1 bag quinoa
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 bottle extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 jar chili powder
  • 1 jar ground cumin
  • 1 jar smoked paprika
  • 1 jar black pepper
  • 1 bunch cilantro
  • 1 jar cornstarch
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Prep beef: cut sirloin into 3/4-inch cubes.
    2. Prep veg: dice 1/2 the onion, slice 2 celery stalks, slice 3 carrots, and mince 3 garlic cloves. Chop cilantro (use about 1/4 cup; refrigerate remaining cilantro).
    3. Brown beef (recommended): heat a skillet over medium-high heat. Add 2 tsp olive oil and brown beef 3–4 minutes. Transfer beef to the slow cooker.
    4. Slow cooker base: add onion, celery, carrots, garlic, 2 tsp chili powder, 1 tsp cumin, 1/2 tsp smoked paprika, and black pepper.
    5. Liquid: add the carton of low sodium beef broth plus 2 cups water and the can of diced tomatoes. Stir once.
    6. Cook: cook on LOW for 6–7 hours or HIGH for 3–4 hours until beef is tender.
    7. Beans + quinoa late: drain and rinse black beans. Rinse 1/2 cup dry quinoa. Stir beans and quinoa into the stew and cook on HIGH for 25–30 minutes until quinoa is tender.
    8. Thicken: whisk 1 tbsp cornstarch with 2 tbsp water. Stir slurry into the stew and cook 5 minutes.
    9. Finish: stir in chopped cilantro and serve.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – About 3 1/2 cups stew.


      Susan – About 2 2/3 cups stew.


      Elise – About 2 2/3 cups stew.


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Add quinoa near the end so it keeps a clean texture and doesn’t overcook.


      Rinse beans and quinoa to improve flavor and reduce foam.


      Keep spices measured so it stays flavorful but not overly intense.
