Turkey Meatballs Marinara + Protein Pasta

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 645 kcal • P 60g • C 55g • F 18g • Sat 6g • Na 690mg • Fiber 10g
Susan/Elise (each): 484 kcal • P 45g • C 41g • F 14g • Sat 4g • Na 518mg • Fiber 8g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.75 lb 93% lean ground turkey
  • 1 package whole-wheat breadcrumbs
  • 2 each eggs
  • 1 jar marinara sauce
  • 1 box Barilla Protein+ pasta
  • 1 bag frozen broccoli florets
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • garlic powder
  • onion powder
  • Italian seasoning
  • smoked paprika
  • chili powder
  • ground cumin
  • crushed red pepper
  • extra-virgin olive oil

Steps

  1. Oven: heat to 425°F. Line a sheet pan and lightly oil.
  2. Meatballs: combine turkey with 1/2 cup breadcrumbs, 2 eggs, 2 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp onion powder, 1 tsp Italian seasoning, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper. Mix gently.
  3. Form 18–20 meatballs. Bake 12–14 minutes to 165°F.
  4. Pasta: cook Barilla Protein+ pasta al dente (about 8–10 minutes). Drain.
  5. Broccoli: roast 1 bag frozen broccoli florets at 425°F on a second sheet pan with 2 tsp olive oil for 16–20 minutes, stirring once.
  6. Sauce: warm 2 cups marinara. Add baked meatballs and simmer 3–4 minutes.
  7. Serve: portion pasta + meatballs + broccoli per Notes.

Notes

Classic comfort: meatballs + marinara, but structured and portioned. Portions (per person): Frank: 8–9 meatballs + 1 1/4 cups cooked protein pasta + 1 cup broccoli Susan: 6–7 meatballs + 1 cup cooked protein pasta + 3/4 cup broccoli Elise: 6–7 meatballs + 1 cup cooked protein pasta + 3/4 cup broccoli Tips (recipe-specific): Bake meatballs so they stay tender but not greasy. Keep pasta measured—comfort comes from sauce + meatballs, not huge noodles.
