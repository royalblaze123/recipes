Slow Cooker Turkey Taco Quinoa Chili

Lean turkey taco chili thickened with quinoa for slow carbs and a hearty, spoonable texture.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 25 minutes
  • Cook time: 330 minutes
  • Total time: 355 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 2 lb ground turkey
  • 1 bag quinoa
  • 1 can no salt added diced tomatoes
  • 1 can tomato sauce
  • 1 carton low sodium chicken broth
  • 2 cans black beans
  • 1 each yellow onion
  • 1 each bell pepper
  • 1 bottle extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 jar chili powder
  • 1 jar ground cumin
  • 1 jar oregano
  • 1 jar garlic powder
  • 1 jar black pepper
  • 1 container nonfat Greek yogurt
  • 1 each lime
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Brown turkey: heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a skillet. Brown ground turkey, breaking it up. Drain if needed (keeps the slow cooker from turning greasy).
    2. Veg: dice 1/2 onion and 1/2 bell pepper; store remaining.
    3. Slow cooker: add browned turkey, diced tomatoes, tomato sauce, and 1 cup low sodium broth. Stir in chili powder, cumin, oregano, garlic powder, and black pepper. Cook LOW 5 hours (or HIGH 2 1/2–3 hours).
    4. Beans: drain and rinse black beans. Stir 2 cups beans into the chili during the final 30 minutes. Refrigerate remaining beans.
    5. Quinoa thickener: stir in 1/2 cup dry quinoa during the final 30 minutes (quinoa absorbs liquid and keeps the chili thick, not soupy).
    6. Finish: squeeze lime juice into the pot and let rest 5 minutes with lid off.
    7. Serve: top with a small dollop of Greek yogurt and use the 40/30/30 split.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – About 2 1/2 cups chili with yogurt (40% of the full pot).


      Susan – About 2 cups chili with yogurt (30% of the full pot).


      Elise – About 2 cups chili with yogurt (30% of the full pot).


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Add quinoa late so it thickens without over-softening.


      Keep tomato products lower-sodium when possible to protect daily sodium targets.


      If you want extra crunch, serve with chopped romaine on top (use what’s already in the fridge).
