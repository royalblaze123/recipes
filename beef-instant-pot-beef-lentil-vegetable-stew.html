Instant Pot Beef & Lentil Vegetable Stew

NOTES: Pressure-cooked lean beef stew with green lentils, carrots, and celery. This is a full-plate beef dinner built around lean beef, smart carbs, and plenty of non-starchy vegetables. Uses olive oil or avocado oil only, keeps sodium moderate, and avoids added sugars to support A1c, cholesterol, blood pressure, and PPPD-friendly eating. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

PORTION SIZES (per person): Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 1 pound lean beef stew meat, trimmed and cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 3/4 cup dry green or brown lentils, rinsed
  • 2 medium carrots, sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 1/2 tbsp olive oil
  • 4 cups low-sodium beef or vegetable broth
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste (no sugar added)
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste, keep light)
  • 1 bay leaf

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set the Instant Pot Pro to Sauté. Add olive oil, then the beef cubes. Sear for 4–5 minutes, turning occasionally, until lightly browned on multiple sides.
  2. Add the diced onion, carrots, and celery. Sauté 3–4 minutes until the vegetables begin to soften.
  3. Stir in the garlic, tomato paste, thyme, oregano, smoked paprika, pepper, and salt. Cook 1 minute, stirring, to coat the beef and vegetables.
  4. Add the rinsed lentils and pour in the broth. Add the bay leaf and stir, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot.
  5. Cancel Sauté. Lock the lid, set the valve to Sealing, and cook on High Pressure for 18 minutes.
  6. Allow a 10-minute natural release, then carefully quick-release any remaining pressure.
  7. Remove the bay leaf, stir the stew, and let it sit on Warm for a few minutes to thicken slightly before serving.
  8. Taste and adjust seasoning with a pinch of salt or extra herbs if needed, keeping overall sodium moderate.
  9. Divide the finished meal into three plates or containers: give Frank roughly 1/3 of the total volume, and split the remaining 2/3 between Susan and Elise as slightly smaller but still generous portions.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~520 kcal, ~48 g protein, ~32 g carbs, ~18 g fat.
  • Susan: ~390 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
  • Elise: ~390 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
