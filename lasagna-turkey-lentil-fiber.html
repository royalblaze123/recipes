Fiber-Boosted Turkey & Lentil Lasagna

Lean turkey and red lentils layered with whole-wheat noodles and light cheese to boost fiber and keep carbs slow-burning.

PORTION SIZES (3 servings total): Frank = 1.0× (largest square), Susan = 0.75×, Elise = 0.75×. Cut the baked lasagna into 4 equal squares, serve 2 squares to Frank and 1 square each to Susan and Elise.

Ingredients

  • 8 whole-wheat lasagna noodles (dry)
  • 3/4 lb lean ground turkey (93% lean or better)
  • 1/2 cup dry red lentils, rinsed
  • 1 small yellow onion, finely diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups no-salt-added crushed tomatoes
  • 1 1/2 cups low-sodium vegetable broth
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 1/2 tsp dried thyme
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp kosher salt (optional, to taste)
  • 1 cup part-skim ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup part-skim shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 2 tbsp grated reduced-fat Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped

Step-by-step Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Lightly coat a 9×9-inch baking dish with nonstick spray.
  2. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Cook the whole-wheat lasagna noodles until just shy of al dente, about 7–8 minutes. Drain and lay flat on parchment to prevent sticking.
  3. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the diced onion and sauté 4–5 minutes until softened.
  4. Add the minced garlic and cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Add the lean ground turkey and cook 5–6 minutes, breaking it into small crumbles, until lightly browned.
  6. Stir in the dried oregano, dried basil, dried thyme, black pepper, and optional salt. Toast the herbs with the turkey for 1–2 minutes.
  7. Add the rinsed red lentils, crushed tomatoes, and vegetable broth to the skillet. Stir well and bring to a gentle simmer.
  8. Simmer the turkey and lentil mixture for 15–18 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the lentils are tender and the sauce is thickened.
  9. Spread a thin layer of the turkey and lentil sauce on the bottom of the prepared baking dish.
  10. Layer lasagna noodles over the sauce. Add dollops of ricotta, followed by more turkey and lentil sauce.
  11. Repeat the layers (noodles, ricotta, sauce) until all components are used, finishing with sauce on top.
  12. Sprinkle the shredded mozzarella and grated Parmesan evenly over the top.
  13. Cover the dish with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake an additional 10–15 minutes until bubbling and lightly browned.
  14. Rest the lasagna for 10–15 minutes. Sprinkle with chopped fresh parsley before serving.
  15. Cut into 4 equal squares. Serve 2 squares to Frank and 1 square each to Susan and Elise.

Nutrition (per person)

Frank (largest portion): approx 535 kcal, 47 g protein, 46 g carbs, 18 g fat.
Susan (medium portion): approx 400 kcal, 35 g protein, 34 g carbs, 14 g fat.
Elise (medium portion): approx 400 kcal, 35 g protein, 34 g carbs, 14 g fat.
