Turkey Pepperoni & Veggie Thin-Crust Pizza (Frank Household – Occasional)

Whole-wheat thin-crust pizza with turkey pepperoni, extra vegetables, and part-skim cheese for an occasional, full-flavor pizza night that still fits Frank Household targets.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 12 minutes
  • Cook time: 16 minutes
  • Total time: 28 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 large rectangular whole-wheat thin pizza crust
  • 2 oz turkey pepperoni
  • 8 oz cooked chicken breast
  • 2 cups thinly sliced bell peppers
  • 1 1/2 cups small broccoli florets
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup thinly sliced red onion
  • 3/4 cup no-salt-added tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
  • 4.5 oz part-skim mozzarella cheese
  • 1 oz finely grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp dried Italian seasoning
  • 1/4 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp kosher or sea salt
  • Olive oil spray

Preparation Steps

  1. Heat the oven to 425°F (about 220°C). Line a large sheet pan with parchment paper and lightly spray with olive oil.
  2. If the chicken is not yet cooked, season about 10 oz raw boneless skinless chicken breast lightly with salt, pepper, and garlic powder and bake or sauté until cooked through (165°F internal), then chop to make 8 oz cooked chicken breast. Set aside.
  3. Place the whole-wheat pizza crust on the prepared sheet pan. If using very thin lavash-style pieces, arrange them to cover the pan and press together slightly.
  4. In a medium bowl, toss the bell peppers, broccoli, mushrooms, and red onion (if using) with 1 tsp olive oil and a light sprinkle of salt and pepper. Spread the vegetables on a second parchment-lined pan and pre-roast for 6–8 minutes until just beginning to soften.
  5. In a small bowl, stir together the tomato sauce, garlic powder, dried Italian seasoning, and a pinch of salt and pepper.
  6. Spread the seasoned tomato sauce evenly over the pizza crust. Sprinkle about half of the shredded mozzarella over the sauce.
  7. Scatter the chopped cooked chicken evenly over the crust. Distribute the pre-roasted vegetables on top of the chicken. Arrange the turkey pepperoni slices evenly over the vegetables so each future square gets some.
  8. Sprinkle the remaining mozzarella and the grated Parmesan over the top. Drizzle the remaining 2 tsp olive oil very lightly across the pizza surface.
  9. Bake at 425°F for 10–12 minutes, or until the crust is crisp, the cheese is melted and lightly browned in spots, and the pepperoni edges are slightly crisp.
  10. Remove the pizza from the oven and let it rest 2–3 minutes. Cut into 10 equal squares.
  11. Place 4 of the 10 squares on a plate for Frank, making sure his portion includes a good mix of turkey pepperoni, chicken, and vegetables.
  12. Place 3 squares each on plates for Susan and Elise, redistributing any loose toppings evenly.

Portion Sizes (Per Person, Approximate)

  • Frank (F · 1.0×): 4 of 10 squares with ~3.5 oz turkey pepperoni and chicken combined, ~2.6 oz whole-wheat crust, ~1.7 oz part-skim mozzarella, and ~2 cups vegetables.
  • Susan (S · 0.75×): 3 of 10 squares with ~2.6 oz turkey pepperoni and chicken combined, ~2 oz whole-wheat crust, ~1.3 oz part-skim mozzarella, and ~1 1/2 cups vegetables.
  • Elise (E · 0.75×): 3 of 10 squares with ~2.6 oz turkey pepperoni and chicken combined, ~2 oz whole-wheat crust, ~1.3 oz part-skim mozzarella, and ~1 1/2 cups vegetables.
