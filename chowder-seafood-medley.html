Seafood Medley Chowder

NOTES: This chowder is designed as a full meal in a bowl with lean protein, smart carbs, and 1–2 cups of vegetables per person. Use low-sodium broth and ingredients, avoid added sugars, and keep portions aligned with your goals. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total pot); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 6 oz shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 6 oz mild white fish (cod or haddock), cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 6 oz sea scallops, cut in half if large
  • 3 cups low-sodium seafood or vegetable broth
  • 3/4 cup 2% milk or unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and diced small
  • 1 carrot, peeled and diced
  • 1 stalk celery, diced
  • 1/2 tsp dried thyme
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Prep seafood: pat shrimp, fish, and scallops dry and cut into bite-size pieces as needed.
  2. Heat olive oil in a soup pot over medium heat for 1–2 minutes.
  3. Add onion, carrot, and celery. Cook 5–6 minutes, stirring occasionally, until vegetables are softened.
  4. Stir in potatoes, broth, thyme, garlic powder, and black pepper.
  5. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer 10–12 minutes until potatoes are just tender.
  6. Add shrimp, fish, and scallops to the pot. Cook on low heat 5–6 minutes, stirring gently, until all seafood is opaque and cooked through.
  7. Stir in milk and warm for 2–3 minutes without boiling.
  8. Taste and adjust seasoning. Ladle into three bowls, giving Frank the largest portion of seafood and potatoes.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~620 kcal, ~49 g protein, ~50 g carbs, ~18 g fat.
  • Susan: ~465 kcal, ~37 g protein, ~37 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
  • Elise: ~465 kcal, ~37 g protein, ~37 g carbs, ~13 g fat.
