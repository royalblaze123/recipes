Turkey Broccoli Chickpea Bake

NOTES: Built as a complete dinner using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled starch portions. Use low-sodium broth and minimal added salt to support blood pressure and kidney health. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of pan); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of pan); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of pan).

Ingredients

  • 1 lb 93% lean ground turkey
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 cups small broccoli florets
  • 2 cans (15 oz each) no-salt-added chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried parsley
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1/3 cup plain 2% Greek yogurt
  • Olive oil spray for baking dish

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Lightly spray an 8x11-inch baking dish with olive oil spray.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  3. Add diced onion and cook 4–5 minutes until softened.
  4. Stir in minced garlic and cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Add ground turkey, breaking it into crumbles, and cook 6–8 minutes until no pink remains.
  6. Steam or microwave broccoli florets 2–3 minutes until bright green and crisp-tender; drain well.
  7. Stir chickpeas, steamed broccoli, low-sodium chicken broth, dried thyme, dried parsley, smoked paprika, salt, and black pepper into the skillet.
  8. Cook 4–5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the mixture thickens slightly and excess liquid reduces.
  9. Remove from heat and let cool 2–3 minutes, then stir in Greek yogurt until creamy and evenly distributed.
  10. Transfer mixture to the prepared baking dish and spread into an even layer.
  11. Bake uncovered 20–25 minutes until hot and lightly browned on top.
  12. Let casserole rest 5–10 minutes before cutting into portions for Frank, Susan, and Elise.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~550 kcal, ~47 g protein, ~48 g carbs, ~15 g fat.
  • Susan: ~410 kcal, ~35 g protein, ~36 g carbs, ~11 g fat.
  • Elise: ~410 kcal, ~35 g protein, ~36 g carbs, ~11 g fat.
