Spicy Shrimp Skillet Rice Bowls

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 620 kcal • P 60g • C 55g • F 16g • Sat 4g • Na 660mg • Fiber 12g
Susan/Elise (each): 465 kcal • P 45g • C 41g • F 12g • Sat 3g • Na 495mg • Fiber 9g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.75 lb raw shrimp
  • 1 bag frozen fajita-style pepper blend
  • 1 can black beans
  • 1 cup dry brown rice
  • 1 each avocado
  • 1/2 jar salsa
  • 1 each lime
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • chili powder
  • ground cumin
  • garlic powder
  • smoked paprika
  • extra-virgin olive oil

Steps

  1. Rice: cook 1 cup dry brown rice with 2 cups water (35–40 minutes), rest 5 minutes, fluff.
  2. Peppers: heat 2 tsp olive oil in skillet. Cook frozen fajita pepper blend 6–8 minutes until browned.
  3. Shrimp: pat dry. Add shrimp to skillet; cook 2–3 minutes total until opaque.
  4. Season with 2 tsp chili powder, 2 tsp garlic powder, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, and 1/2 tsp black pepper.
  5. Beans: use one 15 oz can black beans. Drain/rinse. Stir 1 1/2 cups beans into skillet; warm 2 minutes.
  6. Finish with 2 tsp lime juice.
  7. Bowls: divide rice + shrimp mix. Top with salsa and avocado. Portion per Notes.

Notes

Fast, craveable shrimp bowls that still stay in the dinner macro lane. Portions (per person): Frank: 1/3 rice + 1/3 skillet mix + 1/3 avocado + salsa Susan: 1/4 rice + 1/4 skillet mix + 1/4 avocado + salsa Elise: 1/4 rice + 1/4 skillet mix + 1/4 avocado + salsa Tips (recipe-specific): Cook peppers first so they caramelize; shrimp goes in last so it stays tender. If you want more heat, add more chili powder—not more salt.
