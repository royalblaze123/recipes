Pork Tenderloin Protein Nachos + Green Beans

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 635 kcal • P 58g • C 40g • F 22g
Susan/Elise (each): 476 kcal • P 44g • C 30g • F 16g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.50 lb pork tenderloin
  • 2/3 bag bean-based tortilla chips
  • 3/4 bag reduced-fat shredded Mexican cheese blend
  • 1/2 tub 2% plain Greek yogurt
  • 1/2 jar salsa
  • 2 each bell peppers
  • 1 each yellow onion
  • 12 oz fresh green beans
  • 6 tsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 tsp chili powder
  • 2 tsp cumin
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp kosher salt

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a large sheet pan with parchment.
  2. Pork: pat tenderloin dry and slice into 1/2-inch medallions. Season with 2 tsp chili powder, 2 tsp cumin, 2 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1 tsp black pepper, and 1 tsp kosher salt.
  3. Cook pork medallions in a hot nonstick skillet with 2 tsp olive oil for 3–4 minutes per side until 145°F. Rest 3 minutes, then slice into bite-size pieces.
  4. Veg: slice or dice the listed peppers and onion. Toss with the listed olive oil on the sheet pan and roast 10 minutes to start browning.
  5. Add the cooked protein (and any beans/corn listed) over the roasted vegetables and spread into an even layer.
  6. Cheese: sprinkle the listed shredded cheese evenly over the hot topping mix.
  7. Bake 4–6 minutes until the cheese is melted and bubbling at the edges.
  8. Chips: while the pan bakes, portion chips by weight: Frank 1.5 oz; Susan 1.125 oz; Elise 1.125 oz. (Keep chips separate so they stay crunchy.)
  9. Greek yogurt: use 6 tbsp as a drizzle across the finished nachos.
  10. Salsa: measure 1/2 jar salsa into a bowl for topping.
  11. Serve: spoon the hot cheesy topping mix over each person’s chips. Finish with the listed cold toppings. Portion per Notes.
  12. Green beans: toss green beans with remaining 2 tsp olive oil and a pinch of kosher salt. Roast on a second sheet pan at 425°F for 10–12 minutes until blistered.
  13. Serve green beans alongside the nachos. Portion per Notes.

Notes

Pork tenderloin fajita-style topping + a green bean side to keep the plate big and balanced. Portions (per person): Frank: Chips: 1.5 oz + 1/3 of the hot topping mix + 1/3 of the cold toppings + Green beans: 1/3 (F) or 1/4 (S/E) of the pan Susan: Chips: 1.125 oz + 1/4 of the hot topping mix + 1/4 of the cold toppings + Green beans: 1/3 (F) or 1/4 (S/E) of the pan Elise: Chips: 1.125 oz + 1/4 of the hot topping mix + 1/4 of the cold toppings + Green beans: 1/3 (F) or 1/4 (S/E) of the pan
