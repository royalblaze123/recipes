Air Fryer Garlic-Parmesan Salmon and Shrimp with Asparagus and Sweet Potato

A high-protein seafood plate: salmon and shrimp with a light garlic-Parmesan finish, plus asparagus and sweet potato.

Servings

3 servings (Frank, Susan, Elise).

Times

  • Prep time: 15 minutes
  • Cook time: 18 minutes
  • Total time: 33 minutes

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1 lb fresh salmon fillets
  • 1 lb raw shrimp
  • 6 tsp olive oil
  • 4 tsp lemon juice
  • 4 tsp garlic paste
  • 6 tsp grated Parmesan
  • 2 tsp Italian seasoning
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 bunch fresh asparagus
  • 1 large sweet potato

Preparation Steps

  1. Prep: peel 1 large sweet potato and cut into 1/2-inch wedges; trim 1 bunch fresh asparagus.
  2. Season seafood: in a bowl, toss 1 lb raw shrimp with 2 tsp olive oil, 2 tsp lemon juice, 2 tsp garlic paste, 1 tsp Italian seasoning, 1/2 tsp paprika, and 1/2 tsp black pepper.
  3. Season salmon: brush 1 lb fresh salmon with 2 tsp olive oil, then sprinkle with 2 tsp garlic paste, 1 tsp Italian seasoning, 6 tsp grated Parmesan, 1/2 tsp paprika, and 1/2 tsp black pepper.
  4. Cook sweet potato (air fryer): preheat air fryer to 390°F for 3 minutes; toss sweet potato wedges with 2 tsp olive oil and air fry at 390°F for 12–14 minutes, shaking at 7 minutes.
  5. Cook asparagus (stovetop or microwave): microwave asparagus with 2 tbsp water in a covered bowl for 3–4 minutes, then toss with 1 tsp olive oil and 1 tsp lemon juice.
  6. Cook salmon and shrimp (air fryer): place salmon in basket and air fry at 390°F for 8–10 minutes; add shrimp for the last 5–6 minutes (shrimp should be opaque and salmon should reach 145°F).
  7. Portion: divide salmon, shrimp, sweet potato, and asparagus into plates; use any pan juices as a light drizzle.

Notes

Portions (per person):

Frank — 5 oz salmon; 5 oz shrimp; 3/4 cup sweet potato wedges; 1 1/2 cups asparagus.

 

Susan — 4 oz salmon; 4 oz shrimp; 1/2 cup sweet potato wedges; 1 1/4 cups asparagus.

 

Elise — 4 oz salmon; 4 oz shrimp; 1/2 cup sweet potato wedges; 1 1/4 cups asparagus.

 

Tips (recipe-specific):

 

Timing trick: start sweet potatoes first so salmon and shrimp finish hot and juicy.

 

If your basket is small, cook salmon first, then shrimp; keep cooked seafood loosely tented for 3 minutes.
