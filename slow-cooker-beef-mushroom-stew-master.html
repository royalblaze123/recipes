Slow Cooker Beef & Mushroom Stew

Servings

3 bowls (Frank 1.0×, Susan 0.75×, Elise 0.75×).

Times

  • Prep time: 15 minutes
  • Cook time: 8 hours
  • Total time: 8 hours 15 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2 lb beef chuck roast
  • 12 oz mushrooms
  • 1 lb baby potatoes
  • 1 lb carrots
  • 2 cups low-sodium beef broth
  • 2 tsp olive oil
  • 2 tsp cornstarch

Recipe

  1. Heat 2 tsp olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Sear the 2 lb beef chuck roast for 3–4 minutes per side until well browned. Transfer to slow cooker.
  2. Add 12 oz mushrooms, 1 lb baby potatoes, and 1 lb carrots evenly around the beef.
  3. Pour in 2 cups low-sodium beef broth, ensuring vegetables are mostly submerged.
  4. Cover and cook on LOW for 8 hours until beef is fork-tender.
  5. In a small bowl, whisk 2 tsp cornstarch with 2 tbsp cold broth from the cooker until smooth. Stir slurry into stew.
  6. Cover and cook on HIGH for 20 minutes until thickened. Stir gently before serving.

Notes

Portions (per person):

Frank – 1 bowl (8 oz cooked beef, 1 cup vegetables, 1/2 cup broth).

Susan – 1 bowl (6 oz cooked beef, 3/4 cup vegetables, 1/3 cup broth).

Elise – 1 bowl (6 oz cooked beef, 3/4 cup vegetables, 1/3 cup broth).

Tips (recipe-specific):

Brown beef before slow cooking for deeper flavor.

Use the cornstarch slurry at the end to control thickness precisely.
