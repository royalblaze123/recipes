Brothy Turkey Chili Soup (Stovetop)

NOTES: Designed as a complete meal soup using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled starch. Use low-sodium broth and avoid extra salt when possible to support blood pressure and kidney health. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~2 cups); Susan: medium portion (~1.5 cups); Elise: medium portion (~1.5 cups).

Ingredients

  • 1 lb 93% lean ground turkey
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) no-salt-added diced tomatoes
  • 1 can (8 oz) no-salt-added tomato sauce
  • 4 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 can (15 oz) no-salt-added kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) no-salt-added pinto or black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large soup pot over medium heat.
  2. Add ground turkey and cook 6–8 minutes, breaking into crumbles, until no pink remains. Transfer to a plate and set aside.
  3. In the same pot, add diced onion and red bell pepper. Cook 5–6 minutes until softened.
  4. Stir in minced garlic and cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Add chili powder, ground cumin, smoked paprika, dried oregano, salt, and black pepper. Stir to coat vegetables.
  6. Pour in diced tomatoes with juices, tomato sauce, and low-sodium chicken broth.
  7. Add drained kidney beans and pinto or black beans and stir to combine.
  8. Return cooked turkey to the pot and stir evenly into the chili base.
  9. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer uncovered 25–30 minutes, stirring occasionally, to let flavors develop and broth thicken slightly.
  10. Taste and adjust salt and pepper as needed.
  11. Let soup rest 5 minutes off heat, then ladle into bowls for Frank, Susan, and Elise.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~520 kcal, ~45 g protein, ~55 g carbs, ~11 g fat.
  • Susan: ~390 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~41 g carbs, ~8 g fat.
  • Elise: ~390 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~41 g carbs, ~8 g fat.
