Slow Cooker Thick Turkey Chili with Beans and Peppers

Prep: 20 min   Cook: 360 min   Total: 380 min

Ingredients

  • 2.0 lb lean ground turkey 93%
  • 0.5 carton low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 can no-salt-added diced tomatoes
  • 0.5 can kidney beans
  • 0.5 can black beans
  • 1 onion
  • 2 bell peppers
  • 1 bag frozen corn
  • olive oil
  • tomato paste
  • chili powder
  • cumin
  • smoked paprika
  • oregano
  • black pepper
  • salt substitute

RBM rules: quantities appear before items; fresh meat/poultry/fish use weights. Condiments/spices/seasonings are name-only (no tsp/tbsp in Ingredients); all measurements are in Steps. Water is referenced in Steps only.

Step-by-step Instructions

  1. Brown turkey (stovetop for texture): heat a large skillet over medium-high with 4 tsp olive oil. Add ground turkey and cook 7–9 minutes, breaking into chunks, until browned. Drain any excess fat.
  2. Slow cooker: add browned turkey, diced onion, and diced bell peppers.
  3. Add 1 can diced tomatoes with juices, 0.5 carton broth (16 fl oz), and 2 tbsp tomato paste.
  4. Season: add 2 tsp chili powder + 2 tsp cumin + 1 tsp smoked paprika + 1 tsp oregano + 1 tsp black pepper + 2 tsp salt substitute.
  5. Cook on LOW 5–6 hours or HIGH 3–4 hours.
  6. Add beans and corn late to keep structure: add 0.5 can kidney beans + 0.5 can black beans (drained/rinsed) and 0.5 bag frozen corn (about 1 1/2 cups) for the last 35–45 minutes on HIGH.
  7. Thicken if needed: crack the lid for the last 20 minutes to reduce, or mash 1/2 cup beans and stir back in.
  8. Portion: Frank gets ~3 cups chili. Susan and Elise each get ~2 1/4 cups chili.
