Smoky Turkey Burger Plates + Sweet Potato Fries

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 645 kcal • P 60g • C 60g • F 16g • Sat 5g • Na 680mg • Fiber 10g
Susan/Elise (each): 484 kcal • P 45g • C 45g • F 12g • Sat 4g • Na 510mg • Fiber 8g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 2.00 lb 93% lean ground turkey
  • 4 each whole-wheat burger buns
  • 1.75 lb sweet potatoes
  • 1 bag shredded lettuce
  • 1 each onion
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • garlic powder
  • onion powder
  • smoked paprika
  • dried oregano
  • extra-virgin olive oil
  • mustard
  • ketchup
  • pickle relish
  • hot sauce
  • Worcestershire sauce
  • cayenne pepper

Steps

  1. Oven: heat to 425°F. Cut 1.75 lb sweet potatoes into fries. Toss with 2 tsp olive oil, 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1/2 tsp garlic powder, 1/4 tsp cayenne, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper. Roast 24–28 minutes, flipping once.
  2. Patties: mix turkey with 2 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp onion powder, 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper. Form 6 patties.
  3. Skillet: cook patties over medium heat 5–6 minutes per side to 165°F.
  4. Buns: toast buns lightly.
  5. Assemble: add sliced onion, lettuce, mustard/hot sauce. Serve with fries. Portion per Notes.

Notes

Turkey burgers that still taste like burger-night (smoke + spice carries it). Portions (per person): Frank: 2 patties + 1 cup fries side + toppings Susan: 1 patty + 3/4 cup fries side + toppings Elise: 1 patty + 3/4 cup fries side + toppings Tips (recipe-specific): Don’t press turkey burgers (dries them out); just flip once. Roast sweet potato until browned edges—caramelization is the flavor.
