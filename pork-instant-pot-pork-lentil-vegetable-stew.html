Instant Pot Pork & Lentil Vegetable Stew

NOTES: Pressure-cooked lean pork stew with green lentils, carrots, and celery. This is a full-plate pork dinner built around lean pork tenderloin/loin or 93% lean ground pork, smart carbs, and plenty of non-starchy vegetables. Uses olive or avocado oil only, keeps sodium moderate, and avoids added sugars to support A1c, cholesterol, blood pressure, and PPPD-friendly eating. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

PORTION SIZES (per person): Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 1 pound lean pork loin or shoulder, trimmed and cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 3/4 cup dry green or brown lentils, rinsed
  • 2 medium carrots, sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 1/2 tbsp olive oil
  • 4 cups low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste (no added sugar)
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • 1 bay leaf

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set the Instant Pot Pro to Sauté and add olive oil.
  2. Add pork cubes and sear 4–5 minutes, turning occasionally, until lightly browned.
  3. Add onion, carrots, and celery. Sauté 3–4 minutes until slightly softened.
  4. Stir in garlic, tomato paste, thyme, oregano, smoked paprika, pepper, and salt. Cook 1 minute, stirring.
  5. Add lentils and pour in broth. Add the bay leaf and stir, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot.
  6. Cancel Sauté. Lock the lid, set the valve to Sealing, and cook on High Pressure for 18 minutes.
  7. Allow a 10-minute natural release, then quick-release any remaining pressure. Remove bay leaf and let stew rest on Warm to thicken slightly.
  8. Taste and adjust seasoning with a pinch of salt or extra herbs if needed, keeping overall sodium moderate.
  9. Divide the finished meal into three plates or containers: give Frank roughly 1/3 of the total volume, and split the remaining 2/3 between Susan and Elise as slightly smaller but still generous portions.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~500 kcal, ~46 g protein, ~34 g carbs, ~16 g fat.
  • Susan: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
  • Elise: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
