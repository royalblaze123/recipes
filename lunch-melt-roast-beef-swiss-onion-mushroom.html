Open-Face Melt: Roast Beef Swiss with Onion-Mushroom Sauté

Prep: 18 min   Cook: 12 min   Total: 30 min

Ingredients

  • 6 slices whole wheat bread
  • 14 slices roast beef
  • 6 slices Swiss cheese
  • 1 onion
  • 1 8 oz package mushrooms
  • olive oil
  • black pepper
  • salt substitute
  • Dijon mustard

Rules enforced: quantities before items; fresh meat/poultry/fish use weights; packaged items use fractional containers when partial. Condiments/spices/seasonings have no measurements in Ingredients; all measurements are in Steps. No olives, no squash, no Brussels sprouts, no zucchini, no tofu/tempeh. Lunch validations included in Notes.

Step-by-step Instructions

  1. Skillet: sauté sliced 1 onion and 1 package mushrooms with 2 tsp olive oil 8–10 minutes until browned. Season with pepper/salt substitute.
  2. Broiler: set to high. Line a sheet pan.
  3. Bread: place 6 slices on pan; broil 1 minute to toast.
  4. Spread: Dijon measured (Frank 2 tsp total; Susan 1.5 tsp; Elise 1.5 tsp).
  5. Layer: add roast beef (Frank 6 slices; Susan 4; Elise 4).
  6. Top with onion-mushroom sauté (divide evenly).
  7. Cheese: add Swiss (1 slice per bread slice). Broil 2–3 minutes until melted; rest 2 minutes.

Notes

A steakhouse-style open-face melt: roast beef + browned onions/mushrooms with measured Swiss, broiler-finished. Portions (per person): Frank: - Plate: 1 serving (largest portion) Susan: - Plate: ~3/4 of Frank’s portion Elise: - Plate: ~3/4 of Frank’s portion Tips (recipe-specific): Brown the mushrooms hard for ‘steakhouse’ flavor without added fat. Resting 2 minutes keeps the melt from sliding.
