Japanese-Style Curry Chicken Bowls (Light, Thick Sauce)

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 650 kcal • P 58g • C 70g • F 14g • Sat 3g • Na 700mg • Fiber 10g
Susan/Elise (each): 488 kcal • P 44g • C 52g • F 10g • Sat 2g • Na 525mg • Fiber 8g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.75 lb boneless skinless chicken breast
  • 1 each onion
  • 2.00 lb sweet potatoes
  • 1 carton low-sodium chicken broth
  • cornstarch
  • curry powder
  • 1 cup dry brown rice
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • garlic powder
  • onion powder
  • smoked paprika
  • Italian seasoning
  • ground cumin
  • chili powder
  • red pepper flakes
  • extra-virgin olive oil
  • low-sodium chicken broth

Steps

  1. Rice: cook 1 cup dry brown rice with 2 cups water 35–40 minutes; rest 5 minutes.
  2. Chicken: sauté chicken with 2 tsp olive oil, black pepper, garlic powder, and 2 tsp curry powder 6–8 minutes.
  3. Veg: add diced onion and sweet potato cubes; sauté 4 minutes.
  4. Sauce: add 3 cups low-sodium broth. Simmer 12–14 minutes until sweet potato is tender.
  5. Thicken: whisk 2 tbsp cornstarch into 1/2 cup cold water; stir in. Simmer 2–3 minutes until thick.
  6. Serve over rice per Notes.

Notes

Japanese-curry comfort using a light broth base thickened to gravy texture. Portions (per person): Frank: About 2 1/2 cups (heaping bowl) Susan: About 2 cups (bowl) Elise: About 2 cups (bowl) Tips (recipe-specific): Thicken at the end so it tastes like curry gravy, not soup. Keep sweet potatoes in larger cubes so they don’t dissolve.
