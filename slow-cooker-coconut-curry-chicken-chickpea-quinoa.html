Slow Cooker Coconut Curry Chicken with Chickpeas and Quinoa

Mild coconut curry chicken with chickpeas and quinoa for slow carbs and a creamy texture without heavy dairy.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 360 minutes
  • Total time: 380 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 2 lb boneless skinless chicken breast
  • 1 can light coconut milk
  • 1 can chickpeas
  • 1 carton low sodium chicken broth
  • 1 each yellow onion
  • 1 each bell pepper
  • 1 each lime
  • 1 jar curry powder
  • 1 jar ground cumin
  • 1 jar garlic powder
  • 1 jar black pepper
  • 1 bottle extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 bag quinoa
  • 1 bag fresh baby spinach
  • 1 bunch cilantro
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Prep: dice 1/2 onion and 1/2 bell pepper; store the rest.
    2. Slow cooker base: add chicken, diced onion/pepper, 1 can light coconut milk, and 1/2 cup low sodium broth. Stir in curry powder, cumin, garlic powder, and black pepper.
    3. Cook: LOW 6 hours (or HIGH 3–4 hours) until chicken shreds easily.
    4. Shred chicken in the pot. Leave lid off 10 minutes on HIGH to slightly thicken.
    5. Chickpeas: drain and rinse chickpeas. Measure out 1 cup chickpeas and stir in for the final 20 minutes. Refrigerate remaining chickpeas.
    6. Quinoa side: cook 3/4 cup dry quinoa. Measure out 1 1/4 cups cooked quinoa for serving; refrigerate remaining quinoa.
    7. Finish: stir in a handful of spinach until wilted. Squeeze lime juice over the curry and top with chopped cilantro.
    8. Serve: spoon curry over quinoa using the 40/30/30 split.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – About 2 1/2 cups total (curry + quinoa) with a larger share of chicken (40% of the full recipe).


      Susan – About 2 cups total (curry + quinoa) (30% of the full recipe).


      Elise – About 2 cups total (curry + quinoa) (30% of the full recipe).


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Keep curry mild in the pot; add extra heat at the table to stay PPPD-friendly.


      Use light coconut milk to keep calories controlled while staying creamy.


      Letting it thicken with the lid off prevents a watery curry.
