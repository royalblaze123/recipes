Pork & Brown Rice Stuffed Bell Peppers

NOTES: Bell peppers stuffed with lean ground pork, brown rice, and vegetables. This is a full-plate pork dinner built around lean pork tenderloin/loin or 93% lean ground pork, smart carbs, and plenty of non-starchy vegetables. Uses olive or avocado oil only, keeps sodium moderate, and avoids added sugars to support A1c, cholesterol, blood pressure, and PPPD-friendly eating. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

PORTION SIZES (per person): Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 4 large bell peppers, tops cut off and seeds removed
  • 1 pound 93% lean ground pork
  • 1 cup cooked brown rice (from 1/2 cup dry)
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 1 medium carrot, finely diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried Italian seasoning
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/4 cup low-sodium chicken broth or water

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F and lightly oil a baking dish just large enough to hold peppers upright.
  2. Blanch hollowed peppers in boiling water 3–4 minutes until slightly softened, then drain and place in baking dish.
  3. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add onion and carrot and cook 4–5 minutes until softened.
  4. Add ground pork and cook 4–5 minutes, breaking it up, until no longer pink. Drain excess fat if needed.
  5. Stir in garlic, Italian seasoning, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Cook 1 minute.
  6. Add cooked brown rice and broth or water. Stir and simmer 2–3 minutes until mixture is moist but not soupy.
  7. Spoon pork and rice mixture tightly into each pepper. Cover dish with foil and bake 25 minutes.
  8. Remove foil and bake an additional 5–10 minutes until peppers are tender but still hold their shape.
  9. Taste and adjust seasoning with a pinch of salt or extra herbs if needed, keeping overall sodium moderate.
  10. Divide the finished meal into three plates or containers: give Frank roughly 1/3 of the total volume, and split the remaining 2/3 between Susan and Elise as slightly smaller but still generous portions.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~500 kcal, ~46 g protein, ~34 g carbs, ~16 g fat.
  • Susan: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
  • Elise: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
