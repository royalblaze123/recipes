Beef & Brown Rice Stuffed Bell Peppers

NOTES: Colorful bell peppers stuffed with lean beef, brown rice, and vegetables. This is a full-plate beef dinner built around lean beef, smart carbs, and plenty of non-starchy vegetables. Uses olive oil or avocado oil only, keeps sodium moderate, and avoids added sugars to support A1c, cholesterol, blood pressure, and PPPD-friendly eating. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

PORTION SIZES (per person): Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 4 large bell peppers, tops cut off and seeds removed
  • 1 pound 93% lean ground beef
  • 1 cup cooked brown rice (from 1/2 cup dry)
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 1 medium carrot, finely diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried Italian seasoning
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/4 cup low-sodium beef broth or water

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F. Lightly oil a baking dish just large enough to hold the peppers upright.
  2. Blanch the hollowed bell peppers in boiling water for 3–4 minutes until slightly softened, then drain and place in the baking dish.
  3. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add onion and carrot and cook 4–5 minutes until softened.
  4. Add the ground beef and cook, breaking it up, for 4–5 minutes until no longer pink. Drain any excess fat if needed.
  5. Stir in the garlic, Italian seasoning, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Cook 1 minute.
  6. Add the cooked brown rice and broth or water. Stir and simmer 2–3 minutes until the mixture is moist but not soupy.
  7. Spoon the beef and rice mixture tightly into each pepper. Cover the dish with foil and bake 25 minutes.
  8. Remove foil and bake an additional 5–10 minutes until peppers are tender but still hold their shape.
  9. Taste and adjust seasoning with a pinch of salt or extra herbs if needed, keeping overall sodium moderate.
  10. Divide the finished meal into three plates or containers: give Frank roughly 1/3 of the total volume, and split the remaining 2/3 between Susan and Elise as slightly smaller but still generous portions.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~520 kcal, ~48 g protein, ~32 g carbs, ~18 g fat.
  • Susan: ~390 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
  • Elise: ~390 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
