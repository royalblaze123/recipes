Turkey Taco Stuffed Poblanos with Cauliflower Rice (Sheet Pan)

Dinner • Sheet Pan • Cook time: 30 min

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 4 each fresh poblano peppers
  • 24 oz fresh 93% lean ground turkey
  • 11 oz 2% plain Greek yogurt
  • 3 oz reduced-fat shredded cheddar
  • 1.32 lb frozen cauliflower rice
  • 1 each fresh yellow onion
  • taco seasoning
  • garlic powder
  • cumin
  • black pepper
  • kosher salt
  • extra-virgin olive oil

Steps

  1. Heat oven to 425°F. Put a parchment-lined sheet pan in the oven to preheat (helps browning).
  2. Poblanos: Slice 4 poblano peppers lengthwise, leaving stems on. Remove seeds and ribs.
  3. Filling base: Dice 1 onion (use about 5.5 oz). In a skillet, heat 2 tsp olive oil. Cook the onion 4–5 minutes until soft.
  4. Turkey: Add 24 oz ground turkey to the skillet. Add 2 tsp kosher salt, 2 tsp garlic powder, 2 tsp cumin, and 2 tbsp taco seasoning (or to taste). Cook 7–9 minutes until no pink remains.
  5. Creamy taco binder: Off heat, stir 11 oz Greek yogurt into the turkey mixture. Stir in 2 oz cheddar.
  6. Stuff & bake: Place poblano halves on the hot sheet pan. Divide filling evenly into the 8 halves. Top with remaining 1 oz cheddar. Bake 14–18 minutes until peppers are tender and the centers are hot.
  7. Cauli rice side: While poblanos bake, cook 1.32 lb frozen cauliflower rice in a large nonstick skillet with 1 tsp olive oil, 1 tsp kosher salt, and 1 tsp garlic powder for 6–8 minutes until hot and moisture cooks off.
  8. Serve: Plate poblanos with cauliflower rice.

Notes

Sheet Pan dinner built to be high-protein and satisfying. Portions (per person): Frank: 3 poblano halves (1–2 poblanos) + 7 oz cauliflower rice Susan: 2 poblano halves (1 poblano) + 5.5 oz cauliflower rice Elise: 2 poblano halves (1 poblano) + 5.5 oz cauliflower rice Tips (recipe-specific): If you want more ‘taco’ pop without extra sodium, add 2 tsp lime zest into the filling (zest only). For a crispier top, broil 1–2 minutes after baking.
