Pan-Seared Chicken Cutlets with Low Sodium Soy Ginger Glaze and Lentils

Servings

3 plates (Frank 1.0×, Susan 0.75×, Elise 0.75×).

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 25 minutes
  • Total time: 45 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2.0 lb chicken breast
  • 1.0 cup dry lentils
  • 3 bell peppers
  • 10 tsp olive oil
  • 6 tsp minced garlic
  • 2 tsp black pepper
  • 2 tsp salt substitute
  • 2 tsp dried parsley
  • 5 tsp low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1/3 cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • 2 tsp cornstarch

Steps

  1. Lentils: Rinse 1 cup dry lentils. Simmer 18–22 minutes until tender; drain and keep warm.
  2. Peppers/onions: Slice peppers and onions. Sauté onions in 4 tsp olive oil 6–8 minutes to brown; add peppers + 2 tsp minced garlic + 1 tsp pepper + 1 tsp salt substitute and cook 6–8 minutes tender-crisp.
  3. Chicken/turkey: Pound to even thickness. Season with 1 tsp pepper + 1 tsp salt substitute. Sear in a skillet with 3 tsp olive oil 4–5 minutes per side to 165°F; rest 3 minutes, then slice.
  4. Pan sauce: Lower heat to medium. Add 1 tsp minced garlic and cook 30 seconds.
  5. Deglaze: Add the broth portion from Ingredients and scrape browned bits.
  6. Reduce: Simmer 2–4 minutes until slightly thickened and sauce clings.
  7. Finish: Simmer soy + broth, add cornstarch slurry, simmer 60–90 seconds to lightly thicken.
  8. Return protein to pan briefly to coat (30–60 seconds), then plate.
  9. Plate portions per Notes.

Notes

Portions (per person):

Frank – 1 plate (8 oz cooked protein, 3/4 cup cooked lentils, 1 1/2 cups peppers and onions, ~3 tbsp pan sauce).

Susan – 1 plate (6 oz cooked protein, 3/4 of Frank’s starch portion, 1 1/4 cups peppers and onions, ~2 tbsp pan sauce).

Elise – 1 plate (6 oz cooked protein, 3/4 of Frank’s starch portion, 1 1/4 cups peppers and onions, ~2 tbsp pan sauce).

Tips (recipe-specific):

Do not crowd the skillet—browning is your flavor engine without extra sodium.

Always reduce the sauce 2–4 minutes so it clings; watery sauce reads “cafeteria,” not gourmet.

Storage + Reheat

  • Cool promptly; store in airtight containers in the fridge up to 3–4 days (typical safety guidance).
  • Reheat protein + vegetables in a skillet with a splash of broth (best for sauces) or air fry at 350°F for 4–6 minutes; microwave starch 60–90 seconds with a splash of water.
