Beef and Quinoa Chili

NOTES: Each chili is designed as a full meal with lean protein, smart carbs, and plenty of vegetables. Use low-sodium, no-sugar-added canned goods, avoid added sugars, and keep portions aligned with your goals. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total pot); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 12 oz 90–93% lean ground beef
  • 1/3 cup uncooked quinoa, rinsed
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced or 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 can (15 oz) no-salt-added kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) no-salt-added crushed tomatoes
  • 2 cups low-sodium beef or chicken broth
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste (no sugar added)
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil (optional)
  • 1 1/2 tsp mild chili powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Rinse quinoa under cool water and drain.
  2. Dice onion and bell pepper into small pieces.
  3. Heat a soup pot over medium heat. Add olive oil if needed, then add lean ground beef.
  4. Cook beef 6–7 minutes, breaking it into crumbles, until browned and cooked through. Drain any excess fat if necessary.
  5. Add onion, bell pepper, and garlic. Cook 4–5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until vegetables soften.
  6. Stir in chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, and black pepper. Cook 1 minute.
  7. Add quinoa, kidney beans, crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, and broth. Stir to combine.
  8. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer 25–30 minutes, stirring once or twice, until quinoa is tender and chili is thick.
  9. Taste and adjust seasoning. Ladle into three bowls, giving Frank the largest portion. Serve with a side of steamed green beans or a small salad.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~650 kcal, ~50 g protein, ~60 g carbs, ~20 g fat.
  • Susan: ~490 kcal, ~38 g protein, ~45 g carbs, ~15 g fat.
  • Elise: ~490 kcal, ~38 g protein, ~45 g carbs, ~15 g fat.
