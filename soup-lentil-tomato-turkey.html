Tomato Herb Lentil & Turkey Soup (Stovetop)

NOTES: Designed as a complete meal soup using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled starch. Use low-sodium broth and avoid extra salt when possible to support blood pressure and kidney health. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~2 cups); Susan: medium portion (~1.5 cups); Elise: medium portion (~1.5 cups).

Ingredients

  • 1 lb 93% lean ground turkey
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1.5 cups dry green or brown lentils, rinsed
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) no-salt-added diced tomatoes
  • 5 cups low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Juice of 1/2 lemon (optional)
  • Chopped fresh parsley, for garnish (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large soup pot over medium heat.
  2. Add ground turkey and cook 6–8 minutes, breaking into crumbles, until no pink remains. Transfer to a plate and set aside.
  3. In the same pot, add diced onion, carrots, and celery. Cook 5–6 minutes until softened.
  4. Stir in minced garlic and cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Add rinsed lentils, diced tomatoes with juices, and broth. Stir to combine.
  6. Stir in dried oregano, dried thyme, smoked paprika, bay leaf, salt, and pepper.
  7. Return cooked turkey to the pot and stir evenly into lentil mixture.
  8. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low, cover partially, and simmer 30–35 minutes, stirring occasionally, until lentils are tender but not mushy.
  9. If soup is too thick, add a little extra broth or water 1/4 cup at a time to reach desired consistency.
  10. Remove bay leaf and discard.
  11. Stir in lemon juice (if using) and taste, adjusting salt and pepper as needed.
  12. Let rest 5 minutes off heat, then ladle into bowls and garnish with chopped parsley if desired.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~520 kcal, ~45 g protein, ~55 g carbs, ~10 g fat.
  • Susan: ~390 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~41 g carbs, ~7 g fat.
  • Elise: ~390 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~41 g carbs, ~7 g fat.
