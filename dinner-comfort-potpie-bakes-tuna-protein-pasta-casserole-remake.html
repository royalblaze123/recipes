Tuna Protein Pasta Casserole (Remake)

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 650 kcal • P 62g • C 60g • F 16g • Sat 6g • Na 700mg • Fiber 10g
Susan/Elise (each): 488 kcal • P 46g • C 45g • F 12g • Sat 4g • Na 525mg • Fiber 8g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1 box Barilla Protein+ pasta
  • 3 cans tuna
  • 1 bag frozen peas
  • 1 carton skim milk
  • cornstarch
  • 1 container reduced-fat shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 package whole-wheat panko breadcrumbs
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • garlic powder
  • onion powder
  • Italian seasoning
  • smoked paprika
  • cornstarch
  • extra-virgin olive oil
  • low-sodium chicken broth

Steps

  1. Oven: heat to 425°F.
  2. Pasta: cook Protein+ pasta al dente (8–10 minutes). Drain.
  3. Tuna: Can size note—use a standard 5 oz can tuna.. Drain 3 cans well.
  4. Peas: microwave peas; drain any water.
  5. Sauce: whisk 2 tbsp cornstarch into 2 cups skim milk. Simmer 3–5 minutes until thick.
  6. Cheese: melt 6 oz reduced-fat cheddar into sauce.
  7. Combine: fold pasta + tuna + peas into sauce. Transfer to baking dish.
  8. Top: mix 1/2 cup panko with 1 tsp olive oil and black pepper; sprinkle over casserole.
  9. Bake 10–12 minutes; broil 1 minute for crunch. Rest 5 minutes. Serve per Notes.

Notes

Classic tuna casserole feel—cheesy, crunchy top—without cream soup. Portions (per person): Frank: About 2 1/2 cups (1/3 of pan) + veg side Susan: About 2 cups (1/4 of pan) + veg side Elise: About 2 cups (1/4 of pan) + veg side Tips (recipe-specific): Drain tuna and peas well so the sauce stays thick. Broil 1 minute for a crunchy top without extra butter.
