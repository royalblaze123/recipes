Lentil Vegetable Chowder

NOTES: This chowder is designed as a full meal in a bowl with lean protein, smart carbs, and 1–2 cups of vegetables per person. Use low-sodium broth and ingredients, avoid added sugars, and keep portions aligned with your goals. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total pot); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup dry brown or green lentils, rinsed
  • 3 cups low-sodium vegetable broth
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and diced
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and diced small
  • 1 cup cut green beans, fresh or frozen
  • 3/4 cup 2% milk or unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried Italian seasoning
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a soup pot over medium heat for 1–2 minutes.
  2. Add diced onion and carrots. Cook 4–5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened.
  3. Stir in lentils, potatoes, green beans, and vegetable broth. Add Italian seasoning, garlic powder, and black pepper.
  4. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer 20–25 minutes, stirring once or twice, until lentils and potatoes are tender.
  5. Stir in milk and cook on low heat 3–4 minutes. The chowder will be thick and hearty.
  6. Taste and adjust pepper as needed. Ladle into three bowls, giving Frank the largest portion.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~590 kcal, ~35 g protein, ~85 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
  • Susan: ~445 kcal, ~26 g protein, ~64 g carbs, ~9 g fat.
  • Elise: ~445 kcal, ~26 g protein, ~64 g carbs, ~9 g fat.
