Protein Baked Ziti-Style Casserole (Barilla Protein+)

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 630 kcal • P 58g • C 55g • F 18g • Sat 6g • Na 680mg • Fiber 12g
Susan/Elise (each): 472 kcal • P 44g • C 41g • F 14g • Sat 4g • Na 510mg • Fiber 9g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.50 lb 93% lean ground turkey
  • 1 box Barilla Protein+ pasta
  • 1 jar marinara sauce
  • 1 bag baby spinach
  • 1 tub low-fat cottage cheese
  • 6 oz reduced-fat shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 2 oz grated Parmesan cheese
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • garlic powder
  • onion powder
  • smoked paprika
  • chili powder
  • ground cumin
  • Italian seasoning
  • extra-virgin olive oil

Steps

  1. Oven: heat to 400°F. Lightly oil a casserole dish.
  2. Pasta: cook Barilla Protein+ pasta until al dente (about 8–10 minutes). Drain, reserve 1/2 cup pasta water.
  3. Turkey: brown 1.50 lb lean ground turkey 7–9 minutes. Season with 2 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp onion powder, 1 tsp Italian seasoning, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper.
  4. Sauce: stir in 2 cups marinara + 1/4 cup reserved pasta water; simmer 3 minutes.
  5. Spinach: stir in 4 cups baby spinach until wilted (60–90 seconds).
  6. Cheese mix: stir 1 cup low-fat cottage cheese with 2 tbsp marinara to loosen.
  7. Assemble: fold pasta into sauce. Spread half into dish. Spoon cottage mixture over. Add remaining pasta.
  8. Top: sprinkle 6 oz reduced-fat mozzarella + 2 oz Parmesan evenly.
  9. Bake: 16–20 minutes until bubbling. Broil 1–2 minutes for light browning. Rest 10 minutes.
  10. Portion per Notes.

Notes

Baked-ziti comfort with protein pasta and a lighter cheese structure. Portions (per person): Frank: About 1/3 of pan Susan: About 1/4 of pan Elise: About 1/4 of pan Tips (recipe-specific): Al dente pasta prevents mush after baking and reheating. Cottage cheese makes it creamy without a heavy ricotta load.
