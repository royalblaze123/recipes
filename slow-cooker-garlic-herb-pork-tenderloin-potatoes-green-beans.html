Slow Cooker Garlic Herb Pork Tenderloin with Potatoes and Green Beans

Fork-tender pork tenderloin with chunky potatoes and crisp-tender green beans—built to stay structured, not mushy.

Servings

3 servings.

Times

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 360 minutes
  • Total time: 380 minutes

Ingredients

Main Dish

  • 2 lb pork tenderloin
  • 1 bag baby potatoes
  • 1 bag fresh green beans
  • 1 carton low sodium chicken broth
  • 1 each yellow onion
  • 6 cloves garlic
  • 1 bottle extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 jar Italian seasoning
  • 1 jar garlic powder
  • 1 jar black pepper
  • 1 each lemon
  • 1 bunch parsley
  • 1 container nonfat Greek yogurt
    • 

    Preparation Steps

    1. Prep potatoes: halve baby potatoes (keep pieces large so they hold texture). Slice 1/2 onion; store remaining onion.
    2. Slow cooker: add potatoes to the bottom. Place pork tenderloin on top. Add 3/4 cup low sodium broth and 1 tbsp olive oil.
    3. Season: sprinkle pork and potatoes with Italian seasoning, garlic powder, and black pepper. Add minced garlic.
    4. Cook: LOW 6 hours (or HIGH 3 hours) until pork reaches 145°F and potatoes are tender but still holding shape.
    5. Green beans late: trim green beans. Stir green beans into the slow cooker for the final 25 minutes on HIGH so they stay crisp-tender.
    6. Finish: squeeze lemon over everything and sprinkle chopped parsley.
    7. Serve: slice pork and serve with potatoes and green beans using the 40/30/30 split. Add a small yogurt dollop on the side if you want a creamy element.

      Notes

      Portions (per person):


      Frank – About 6 oz cooked pork plus potatoes and green beans totaling about 2 1/2 cups (40% of the full cooker).


      Susan – About 4–5 oz cooked pork plus potatoes and green beans totaling about 2 cups (30% of the full cooker).


      Elise – About 4–5 oz cooked pork plus potatoes and green beans totaling about 2 cups (30% of the full cooker).


      Tips (recipe-specific):


      Add green beans near the end so they stay crisp-tender (not limp).


      Keep potato pieces large so they don’t turn to mash in the slow cooker.


      If the sauce looks thin, leave the lid off 10 minutes on HIGH before serving.

      • Susan: 465 kcal; 42 g protein; 26 g carbs; 20 g fat; 4 g fiber; 360 mg sodium
      • Elise: 465 kcal; 42 g protein; 26 g carbs; 20 g fat; 4 g fiber; 360 mg sodium
