Chicken White Bean Pesto Salad

NOTES: Designed as a portable grab-and-go lunch using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled smart carbs. Use low-sodium ingredients and avoid added sugars where possible. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total volume); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total volume); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total volume) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 12 oz cooked boneless, skinless chicken breast, chopped
  • 1 can (15 oz) no-salt-added cannellini or great northern beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 1/2 cups halved cherry tomatoes
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1/4 small red onion, finely diced
  • 2 tbsp prepared basil pesto
  • 1/4 cup plain 2% Greek yogurt
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 3 cups chopped baby spinach or spring mix

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine chopped chicken, white beans, cherry tomatoes, diced bell pepper, and diced red onion.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together basil pesto, Greek yogurt, olive oil, lemon juice, and black pepper to make a light pesto dressing.
  3. Pour dressing over the salad and toss until everything is evenly coated.
  4. Place chopped spinach or spring mix into three containers and top with the chicken white bean pesto salad, giving Frank the largest portion.
  5. Cover and refrigerate up to 3 days. Toss gently before eating.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~570 kcal, ~48 g protein, ~40 g carbs, ~19 g fat.
  • Susan: ~430 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~30 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
  • Elise: ~430 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~30 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
