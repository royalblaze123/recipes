Pork Fajita Bowls with Quinoa

NOTES: Seared fajita-style pork with peppers and onions over quinoa with lettuce. This is a full-plate pork dinner built around lean pork tenderloin/loin or 93% lean ground pork, smart carbs, and plenty of non-starchy vegetables. Uses olive or avocado oil only, keeps sodium moderate, and avoids added sugars to support A1c, cholesterol, blood pressure, and PPPD-friendly eating. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

PORTION SIZES (per person): Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 1 pound pork loin or tenderloin, sliced into thin strips
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 1/2 tsp mild chili powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or less to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 3/4 cup uncooked quinoa, rinsed
  • 1 1/2 cups low-sodium broth or water
  • 2 cups shredded romaine or spring mix for serving

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Cook quinoa with broth or water according to package directions, then fluff and keep warm.
  2. In a bowl, toss pork strips with chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  3. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add seasoned pork and sear 3–4 minutes until just cooked through. Transfer to a plate.
  4. In the same skillet, add peppers and onion and cook 5–6 minutes until tender-crisp and lightly charred.
  5. Return pork and any juices to the skillet, add garlic, and cook 1–2 minutes, tossing to combine.
  6. Taste and adjust seasoning with a pinch of salt or extra herbs if needed, keeping overall sodium moderate.
  7. Divide the finished meal into three plates or containers: give Frank roughly 1/3 of the total volume, and split the remaining 2/3 between Susan and Elise as slightly smaller but still generous portions.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~500 kcal, ~46 g protein, ~34 g carbs, ~16 g fat.
  • Susan: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
  • Elise: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~26 g carbs, ~12 g fat.
