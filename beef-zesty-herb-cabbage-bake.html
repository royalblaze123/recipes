Zesty Herb Beef & Cabbage Bake

NOTES: Layered beef and cabbage bake with carrots and brown rice for a cabbage-roll flavor without the work. This is a full-plate beef dinner built around lean beef, smart carbs, and plenty of non-starchy vegetables. Uses olive oil or avocado oil only, keeps sodium moderate, and avoids added sugars to support A1c, cholesterol, blood pressure, and PPPD-friendly eating. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

PORTION SIZES (per person): Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total cooked meal); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the total cooked meal) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 1 pound 93% lean ground beef
  • 4 cups thinly sliced green cabbage
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1 medium carrot, shredded
  • 1/2 cup cooked brown rice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 cup low-sodium tomato sauce (no added sugar)
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (or to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F and lightly oil a medium casserole dish.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add onion and carrot and cook 3–4 minutes until softened.
  3. Add ground beef and cook 4–5 minutes, breaking it up, until no longer pink. Drain excess fat if needed.
  4. Stir in garlic, oregano, basil, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Cook 1 minute.
  5. Add tomato sauce, cooked brown rice, and sliced cabbage. Stir and cook 5–7 minutes until cabbage begins to wilt and mixture is evenly combined.
  6. Transfer mixture to the casserole dish, cover with foil, and bake 20 minutes. Remove foil and bake an additional 5–10 minutes until cabbage is tender and the top is slightly browned at the edges.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning with a pinch of salt or extra herbs if needed, keeping overall sodium moderate.
  8. Divide the finished meal into three plates or containers: give Frank roughly 1/3 of the total volume, and split the remaining 2/3 between Susan and Elise as slightly smaller but still generous portions.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~520 kcal, ~48 g protein, ~32 g carbs, ~18 g fat.
  • Susan: ~390 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
  • Elise: ~390 kcal, ~36 g protein, ~24 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
