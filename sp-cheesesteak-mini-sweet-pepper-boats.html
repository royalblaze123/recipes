Cheesesteak Mini Sweet Pepper Boats (Sheet Pan)

Dinner • Sheet Pan • Cook time: 25 min

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 18 each fresh mini sweet peppers
  • 28 oz fresh 93% lean beef sirloin (thin-sliced)
  • 3 oz provolone
  • 1 each fresh yellow onion
  • 2 each fresh bell peppers
  • 1/2 heart romaine hearts
  • 1/2 pint cherry tomatoes
  • garlic powder
  • black pepper
  • kosher salt
  • extra-virgin olive oil

Steps

  1. Heat oven to 425°F. Line a sheet pan with parchment.
  2. Peppers: Slice 18 mini sweet peppers lengthwise to make 36 boats. Remove seeds.
  3. Veg: Slice 1 onion and 2 bell peppers into thin strips. Sauté with 2 tsp olive oil, 1 tsp kosher salt, 1 tsp garlic powder, and 1/2 tsp black pepper for 6–8 minutes until softened.
  4. Steak: Add 28 oz thin-sliced lean beef to the skillet. Cook 3–5 minutes until just cooked through. Turn off heat.
  5. Assemble: Fill each pepper boat with a little beef/veg. Tear 3 oz provolone into 36 small pieces and place one piece on each boat.
  6. Bake: Arrange boats on the sheet pan and bake 10–12 minutes until peppers are tender-crisp and cheese is melted. Broil 1 minute if you want browning.
  7. Salad: Chop 1/2 heart romaine hearts (about 4 oz) and halve 1/2 pint cherry tomatoes. Toss with 1 tsp olive oil, 1 tsp vinegar (optional), 1/2 tsp kosher salt, and black pepper.
  8. Serve: Plate pepper boats with salad.

Notes

Sheet Pan dinner built to be high-protein and satisfying. Portions (per person): Frank: 14 pepper boats (7 mini peppers) + 1.5 cups salad Susan: 11 pepper boats (5–6 mini peppers) + 1 cup salad Elise: 11 pepper boats (5–6 mini peppers) + 1 cup salad Tips (recipe-specific): If using thicker steak, slice it very thin across the grain before cooking so it stays tender. To keep sodium controlled, season the steak lightly and let the peppers carry volume and sweetness.
