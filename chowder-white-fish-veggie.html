White Fish Veggie Chowder

NOTES: This chowder is designed as a full meal in a bowl with lean protein, smart carbs, and 1–2 cups of vegetables per person. Use low-sodium broth and ingredients, avoid added sugars, and keep portions aligned with your goals. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~1/3 of the total pot); Susan: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot); Elise: medium portion (~1/4 of the pot) (Frank = 1.0×, Susan/Elise ≈0.75× portions).

Ingredients

  • 12 oz mild white fish (cod, haddock, or pollock), cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 3 cups low-sodium vegetable or seafood broth
  • 3/4 cup 2% milk or unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and diced small
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and diced small
  • 1 cup cut green beans, fresh or frozen
  • 1/2 tsp dried thyme
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Prep vegetables: dice onion, carrots, and potatoes into small, even cubes; cut green beans into bite-size pieces if needed.
  2. Heat olive oil in a soup pot over medium heat for 1–2 minutes.
  3. Add onion and carrots. Cook 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until onion is translucent and carrots begin to soften.
  4. Stir in potatoes and green beans. Add broth, thyme, garlic powder, and black pepper.
  5. Bring to a gentle boil over medium-high heat, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer 12–15 minutes until potatoes are just tender.
  6. Add fish pieces to the pot, gently stirring to submerge. Simmer 5–6 minutes on low heat until fish is opaque and flakes easily.
  7. Stir in milk and warm for another 2–3 minutes without boiling.
  8. Taste and adjust pepper if needed. Ladle into three bowls, giving Frank the largest share of fish and potatoes.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~570 kcal, ~44 g protein, ~55 g carbs, ~15 g fat.
  • Susan: ~430 kcal, ~33 g protein, ~41 g carbs, ~11 g fat.
  • Elise: ~430 kcal, ~33 g protein, ~41 g carbs, ~11 g fat.
