Bowl: Pork Tenderloin + Green Beans + Peppers/Onions over Brown Rice with Soy Sesame Finish

Servings

3 bowls (Frank 1.0×, Susan 0.75×, Elise 0.75×).

Times

  • Prep time: 18 minutes
  • Cook time: 27 minutes
  • Total time: 45 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2 pork tenderloins
  • 1.0 cup dry brown rice
  • 1 bag frozen green beans
  • 3 bell peppers
  • 9 tsp olive oil
  • 4 tsp minced garlic
  • 2 tsp black pepper
  • 2 tsp salt substitute
  • 2 tsp dried parsley
  • 7 tsp low-sodium soy sauce
  • 2 tsp sesame oil
  • 2 tsp black pepper

Steps

  1. Base: Cook 1 cup dry brown rice to yield ~3 cups cooked. Keep warm.
  2. Vegetables: Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a sheet pan. Toss vegetables with oil + pepper + salt substitute; roast until browned-tender.
  3. Aromatics: Add minced garlic for the last 2 minutes of roasting (or it burns and ruins the vibe).
  4. Protein: Sear lean cutlets/strips with 3 tsp olive oil 2–4 minutes per side to brown; rest 3 minutes then slice.
  5. Sauce: Whisk; keep it light—this is a finish, not a bath.
  6. Build order (texture-first): Base → hot vegetables → sliced protein → drizzle sauce → parsley + black pepper last.
  7. Portions per Notes.

Notes

Portions (per person):

Frank – 1 bowl (8 oz cooked protein, 1 cup cooked brown rice, 2 cups vegetables, 2–3 tbsp drizzle).

Susan – 1 bowl (6 oz cooked protein, 3/4 of Frank’s base portion, 1 1/2 cups vegetables, 2 tbsp drizzle).

Elise – 1 bowl (6 oz cooked protein, 3/4 of Frank’s base portion, 1 1/2 cups vegetables, 2 tbsp drizzle).

Tips (recipe-specific):

Keep sauce as a finish. If the bowl is swimming, you built soup without the decency to admit it.

Roast vegetables hot (425°F) and don’t crowd the pan—browning is the whole point.

Storage + Reheat

  • Cool promptly; pack base, protein, and vegetables in separate compartments if possible; store in the fridge up to 3–4 days (typical safety guidance).
  • Reheat base + protein 60–90 seconds; reheat vegetables 4–6 minutes at 350°F in the air fryer for texture; add sauce after reheating to keep the bowl bright.
