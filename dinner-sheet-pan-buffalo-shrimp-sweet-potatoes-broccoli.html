Buffalo Shrimp + Sweet Potatoes + Broccoli

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 645 kcal • P 55g • C 55g • F 18g • Sat 3g • Na 700mg • Fiber 10g
Susan/Elise (each): 484 kcal • P 41g • C 41g • F 14g • Sat 2g • Na 525mg • Fiber 8g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.50 lb shrimp
  • 2.00 lb sweet potatoes
  • 1 bag frozen broccoli florets
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • garlic powder
  • onion powder
  • smoked paprika
  • Italian seasoning
  • ground cumin
  • chili powder
  • red pepper flakes
  • extra-virgin olive oil
  • Dijon mustard
  • balsamic vinegar
  • apple cider vinegar
  • low-sodium soy sauce
  • cornstarch
  • hot sauce

Steps

  1. Oven: heat to 450°F. Line sheet pan.
  2. Sweet potatoes: cube; toss with 2 tsp olive oil, smoked paprika, garlic powder, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, black pepper. Roast 14 minutes.
  3. Broccoli: microwave; drain/pat dry. Toss with 2 tsp olive oil, garlic powder, 1/2 tsp kosher salt, black pepper. Add; roast 10 minutes.
  4. Shrimp: toss shrimp with 2 tsp olive oil, 3 tbsp hot sauce (measured), garlic powder, black pepper, and a pinch of kosher salt.
  5. Add shrimp for last 6–8 minutes until pink.
  6. Serve per Notes.

Notes

Buffalo heat without fried food—shrimp goes on at the end so it stays juicy. Portions (per person): Frank: 6 oz shrimp + 1 cup sweet potatoes + 1 1/2 cups broccoli Susan: 5 oz protein + 3/4 cup starch + 1 cup veg Elise: 5 oz protein + 3/4 cup starch + 1 cup veg Tips (recipe-specific): Add shrimp last—overcooking is the only way this gets dry. Roast veg hard for browned edges = flavor.
