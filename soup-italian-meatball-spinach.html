Italian Turkey Meatball & Spinach Soup (Stovetop)

NOTES: Designed as a complete meal soup using lean protein, high-fiber vegetables, and controlled starch. Use low-sodium broth and avoid extra salt when possible to support blood pressure and kidney health. PORTION SIZES: Frank: largest portion (~2 cups); Susan: medium portion (~1.5 cups); Elise: medium portion (~1.5 cups).

Ingredients

  • 1 lb 93% lean ground turkey
  • 1/4 cup plain 2% Greek yogurt
  • 1/4 cup whole-wheat breadcrumbs
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tbsp grated Parmesan cheese (optional, reduced-fat)
  • 1 tsp dried Italian seasoning
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt (divided)
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 6 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 cup water
  • 3/4 cup small whole-wheat pasta (like ditalini or small shells)
  • 3 cups fresh baby spinach

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine ground turkey, Greek yogurt, breadcrumbs, egg, Parmesan (if using), Italian seasoning, garlic powder, onion powder, 1/4 teaspoon salt, and black pepper. Mix gently until just combined.
  2. Form small meatballs about 1 to 1.25 inches in diameter and place on a plate.
  3. Heat olive oil in a large soup pot over medium heat.
  4. Add diced onion and cook 4–5 minutes until softened.
  5. Stir in minced garlic and cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
  6. Pour in low-sodium chicken broth and water and bring to a gentle boil.
  7. Carefully drop turkey meatballs into the simmering broth one at a time. Reduce heat to a gentle simmer and cook 10 minutes.
  8. Add whole-wheat pasta and remaining 1/4 teaspoon salt. Simmer another 8–10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until pasta is al dente and meatballs are cooked through.
  9. Stir in baby spinach and cook 2–3 minutes until wilted and bright green.
  10. Taste and adjust seasoning with additional pepper or a small pinch of salt if needed.
  11. Remove from heat and let soup rest a few minutes before ladling portions for Frank, Susan, and Elise.

Nutrition per Person

  • Frank: ~500 kcal, ~45 g protein, ~45 g carbs, ~14 g fat.
  • Susan: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~34 g carbs, ~10 g fat.
  • Elise: ~375 kcal, ~34 g protein, ~34 g carbs, ~10 g fat.
