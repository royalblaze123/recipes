Open-Face Tuna Melt Plates (No Mayo) + Side Salad

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 640 kcal • P 55g • C 60g • F 18g
Susan/Elise (each): 480 kcal • P 41g • C 45g • F 14g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 3 cans tuna
  • 8 slices whole-wheat bread
  • 6 oz reduced-fat cheddar cheese
  • 1 bag spring mix
  • Dijon mustard
  • apple cider vinegar
  • extra-virgin olive oil
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • garlic powder
  • onion powder
  • smoked paprika
  • Italian seasoning
  • ground cumin
  • chili powder
  • red pepper flakes
  • extra-virgin olive oil
  • low-sodium chicken broth
  • low-sodium beef broth
  • low-sodium soy sauce
  • hot sauce
  • Dijon mustard
  • balsamic vinegar
  • apple cider vinegar
  • cornstarch

Steps (stovetop)

  1. Tuna mix: drain tuna (3 cans, 5 oz each). Mix with Dijon (2 tbsp), apple cider vinegar (2 tbsp), black pepper, garlic powder, and 2 tsp olive oil.
  2. Toast bread: toast slices until crisp.
  3. Assemble melts: spread tuna on bread; top with measured cheddar. Broil 2–3 minutes until melted.
  4. Side salad: toss spring mix with apple cider vinegar (2 tsp) + olive oil (2 tsp) + black pepper.
  5. Serve per Notes.

Notes

True tuna-melt comfort without mayo; mustard + vinegar gives punch. Portions (per person): Frank: 2 melts + 2 cups salad Susan: 2 melts + 1 1/2 cups salad Elise: 2 melts + 1 1/2 cups salad Tips (recipe-specific): Broil fast; keep bread crisp. Mix tuna with acid + a touch of oil for creaminess without mayo.
