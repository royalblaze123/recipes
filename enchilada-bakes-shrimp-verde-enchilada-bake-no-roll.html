Shrimp Verde Enchilada Bake (No-Roll)

Nutrition (approx)

Frank: 625 kcal • P 56g • C 48g • F 20g
Susan/Elise (each): 469 kcal • P 42g • C 36g • F 15g

Ingredients (purchase-clean)

  • 1.50 lb raw shrimp
  • 1 package corn tortillas
  • 1 bag reduced-fat shredded Mexican cheese blend
  • 1 each avocado
  • 1/2 jar salsa
  • 1 can salsa verde
  • 1 bag frozen fajita-style pepper blend
  • 6 tsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 tsp chili powder
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp kosher salt

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F.
  2. Prep a 9x13-inch baking dish with a light spray of cooking spray.
  3. Shrimp: pat shrimp dry. Season with chili powder, garlic powder, black pepper, and kosher salt.
  4. Cook shrimp in a hot nonstick skillet with 2 tsp olive oil for 2–3 minutes total, flipping once, just until opaque. (Do not overcook.)
  5. Veg: cook frozen fajita pepper blend with 4 tsp olive oil 6–8 minutes until browned at edges.
  6. Combine: fold cooked shrimp into the peppers and warm 1 minute.
  7. Verde: jar/can size note—use a standard 15 oz salsa verde. Use 1 1/2 cups as the layering sauce; refrigerate any remainder.
  8. Assemble: spread 1/2 cup enchilada sauce in the bottom of the dish. Layer 1/2 of the tortillas, then 1/2 of the filling, then 1/3 of the cheese. Repeat tortillas + filling + 1/3 cheese. Top with remaining sauce and the last 1/3 cheese.
  9. Bake 15–18 minutes until bubbling at edges and cheese is melted. Rest 10 minutes before slicing.
  10. Toppings: dice avocado and serve salsa on the side. Top each serving with measured avocado and salsa.

Notes

Shrimp enchilada bake is fast and surprisingly light—big payoff with avocado and salsa on top. Portions (per person): Frank: 1/3 of the casserole + 1/3 of the avocado + salsa topping Susan: 1/4 of the casserole + 1/4 of the avocado + salsa topping Elise: 1/4 of the casserole + 1/4 of the avocado + salsa topping
