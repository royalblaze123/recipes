Sheet-Pan Cod: Cabbage + Peppers/Onions + Cauliflower Rice with Smoky Tomato Dijon

Servings

3 plates (Frank 1.0×, Susan 0.75×, Elise 0.75×).

Times

  • Prep time: 18 minutes
  • Cook time: 27 minutes
  • Total time: 45 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2.0 lb cod fillets
  • 2 bags frozen cauliflower rice
  • 1 head green cabbage
  • 3 bell peppers
  • 9 tsp olive oil
  • 4 tsp minced garlic
  • 2 tsp black pepper
  • 2 tsp salt substitute
  • 2 tsp dried parsley
  • 4 tsp tomato paste
  • 3 tsp vinegar
  • 3 tsp Dijon mustard

Steps

  1. Base: Sauté cauliflower rice 6–7 minutes until hot and steam-dry. Keep warm.
  2. Oven: Heat to 425°F. Line a large sheet pan with parchment or foil for faster cleanup.
  3. Vegetables (Cabbage + Peppers/Onions): Toss with 4 tsp olive oil + pepper + salt substitute. Roast at 425°F on a sheet pan. Start tougher veg first (carrots/cabbage) 10 minutes; add quicker veg (broccoli/asparagus/green beans/mushrooms) later so everything stays browned-tender.
  4. Aromatics: Add minced garlic during the last 2 minutes of roasting (or it burns and turns bitter).
  5. Protein: Pat cod dry. Roast at 425°F for 10–12 minutes, until flaky. Add after the harder vegetables have a head start.
  6. Finish sauce: Whisk finishing sauce ingredients. Thin with 2–3 tbsp water to make a brushable glaze.
  7. Plate: Base first, then vegetables, then protein. Drizzle finishing sauce. Add parsley + black pepper last.
  8. Portions per Notes.

Notes

Portions (per person):

Frank – 1 plate (7 oz roasted protein, 2 cups cauliflower rice, 2 cups roasted vegetables, 2–3 tbsp finish sauce).

Susan – 1 plate (5 1/4 oz roasted protein, 3/4 of Frank’s base portion, 1 1/2 cups roasted vegetables, 2 tbsp finish sauce).

Elise – 1 plate (5 1/4 oz roasted protein, 3/4 of Frank’s base portion, 1 1/2 cups roasted vegetables, 2 tbsp finish sauce).

Tips (recipe-specific):

Stagger timing: tough veg first, quick veg later. If everything goes in at once, one thing will be mush and one thing will be raw.

Finish sauce goes on after roasting (or last 2 minutes max). That’s how you keep it ‘gourmet’ instead of ‘cafeteria’.

Storage + Reheat

  • Cool promptly; store in airtight containers in the fridge up to 3–4 days (typical safety guidance).
  • Reheat protein + veg at 350°F in the air fryer 4–6 minutes for texture; reheat base in microwave 60–90 seconds; add finishing sauce after reheating.
